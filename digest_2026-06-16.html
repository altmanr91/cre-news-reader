Stockdale’s Andrew Saba On Medical Real Estate’s Supply-Demand Imbalance

Commercial Observer — Mon, 15 Jun 2026 20:40:46 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Stockdale Capital Partners, a Los Angeles-based firm, is actively expanding its healthcare real estate portfolio through two distinct funds: an open-ended healthcare fund targeting stabilized assets and an opportunistic fund focused on buy-to-suit or office-to-medical conversions. The firm's strategy is driven by the inelastic demand for healthcare services, an aging U.S. population, and a significant supply-demand imbalance in medical outpatient facilities. Rising construction costs and labor expenses have curtailed new supply, leading to increased competition for existing properties, strong occupancy rates in the mid-90s, high tenant retention, and robust rent growth in the sector. Stockdale is primarily focused on the top 50 metro areas, with a preference for Sun Belt and West/Southwest U.S. markets experiencing patient migration.

Office-to-medical conversions are generally not sufficient to meet the needs of large office projects (e.g., 400,000-500,000+ SF buildings).

In the Miami-Area Condo Market, There Are Two Narratives

Commercial Observer — Tue, 16 Jun 2026 11:00:39 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The South Florida condo market is experiencing a bifurcated trend: a robust luxury segment driven by an influx of high-net-worth individuals and corporate relocations, contrasted with a struggling lower end facing pressure from new structural repair mandates. Developers of high-end projects, such as the Four Seasons-branded condo in Coconut Grove and the Nobu-branded tower in Brickell, are seeing strong pre-sales and securing significant construction financing, indicating sustained demand for premium products. However, the market for older, more affordable condos is softening due to costly special assessments mandated by post-Surfside reforms, leading to increased inventory and longer selling periods. This dynamic highlights the importance of product differentiation and amenity-rich offerings even within the luxury sector, as buyers are discerning despite high demand.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Florida's 2022 legislative reforms mandate structural health reports and reserve funding for condo buildings at least three stories tall and 30+ years old, impacting the older condo stock. Previously, only Miami-Dade and Broward counties required recertification, and boards often delayed or waived renovations.
