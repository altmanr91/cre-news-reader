Foreclosures jump as FHA safety net shrinks

The Real Deal — National — Mon, 04 May 2026 21:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Housing Distress

Foreclosure filings increased by 28% year-over-year in March, driven by stricter rules for the Federal Housing Administration's (FHA) "partial claim" program. This program, which allowed delinquent borrowers to defer missed payments, now has tighter restrictions, requiring homeowners to make three consecutive on-time payments and limiting access to once every two years. Analysts predict that up to half of seriously delinquent FHA borrowers may not meet these new requirements, potentially leading to approximately 250,000 distressed sales over the next 12 to 18 months. The impact on home prices will vary by market, with stronger markets potentially absorbing these sales due to existing equity, while weaker markets may see price reductions.

TRD PolicyPro: Eviction bill draws NYAA warning, Hochul revises pied-à-terre tax

The Real Deal — New York — Mon, 04 May 2026 22:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

New York's proposed "Clean Hands Act," which would prevent landlords from initiating evictions while building violations are active, is drawing strong opposition from the New York Apartment Association (NYAA). The NYAA argues that the bill's broad scope on violations makes compliance nearly impossible for building owners, including non-profit and rent-regulated housing providers, and could destabilize their finances. They also highlight the practical difficulties of accessing tenant units for repairs and the lengthy city certification process for completed work. As an alternative, the NYAA proposes a rent-escrow mechanism with court oversight and guaranteed owner access for repairs. Separately, Governor Kathy Hochul's office is revising the proposed pied-à-terre tax, moving away from using unreliable assessed values and instead considering a model based on comparable sales data for properties valued over $5 million.

Market Research / Condo Report

San Francisco's condominium market is experiencing a notable shift, with typical condo values increasing in eight Bay Area ZIP codes, all located within the city. Typical condo values in the Bay Area generally remain flat or are falling, burdened by high homeowner association dues, insurance costs, and tighter lending standards. Many owners are reluctant to sell at a loss after the pandemic-era downturn, contributing to limited inventory and increased competition for available units.
