U.S. Ranks First In Being 'Zillow For Money Launderers'

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 23 Jun 2026 14:13:55 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / Regulatory Report

A new report from the Tax Justice Network identifies the U.S. as the leading country globally for real estate money laundering, earning it the moniker "Zillow for money launderers." The Financial Secrecy Index, which ranks 141 countries, assesses complicity in hiding illicit funds through real estate based on factors like public access to ownership information, government access to centralized records, and requirements for beneficial ownership disclosure from foreign entities. The U.S. scored highest on this index, followed by Switzerland, Singapore, Hong Kong, and the UAE. Between 2015 and 2020, an estimated $2.3 billion was laundered through U.S. real estate, with 30% in commercial properties, largely due to fewer due diligence and reporting regulations compared to financial accounts. While the Biden administration attempted to tighten controls, recent actions under the Trump administration, such as halting enforcement of the Corporate Transparency Act and a federal judge blocking a rule requiring disclosure for all-cash residential deals, have weakened these efforts. However, broader anti-fraud measures by Freddie Mac and Fannie Mae in 2024, including enhanced financial reporting and KYC requirements for multifamily transactions, could indirectly make money laundering more challenging.
