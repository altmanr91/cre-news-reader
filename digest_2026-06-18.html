SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The Federal Reserve, under new Chairman Kevin Warsh, unanimously voted to maintain its benchmark interest rate between 3.5 percent and 3.75 percent for the fourth consecutive meeting, signaling a continued 'higher-for-longer' environment due to inflation uncertainty and geopolitical tensions. The updated 'dot plot' projections removed previous forecasts for a rate cut this year and indicated potential rate hikes, with the federal funds rate now projected at 3.8 percent by the end of 2026.
