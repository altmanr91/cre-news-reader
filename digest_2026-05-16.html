House knocks build-to-rent provision from amended Senate bill

The Real Deal — National — Fri, 15 May 2026 17:06:31 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The House of Representatives has released an amended version of the 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act, notably removing a controversial provision that would have required institutional investors to sell build-to-rent homes within seven years of construction. This change is seen as a potential victory for the build-to-rent sector, which had experienced a freeze in investment due to the original provision. MARKET: FLORIDA (STATEWIDE)

South Florida golf courses targeted in lawmakers’ latest land-use power grab

The Real Deal — Miami — Fri, 15 May 2026 14:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

Florida lawmakers have passed the Infill Redevelopment Act, a new piece of legislation that would remove local governments' power to block housing developments on recreational sites such as golf courses, tennis courts, and clubhouses. The act, which targets Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach counties, allows developers to proceed with staff-level administrative approval, bypassing public hearings, provided the sites have been closed for at least a year and developers pay double the standard impact fees. While intended to streamline development and potentially reduce costs, the legislation notably lacks an affordability mandate, a key difference from the state's earlier Live Local Act which requires 40% workforce housing.
