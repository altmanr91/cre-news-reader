The great mall divide: Coresight report finds that top-tier malls thrive while lower-tier properties struggle

REJournals — Thu, 04 Jun 2026 10:03:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Retail Report

A new report from Coresight Research highlights a significant bifurcation in the U.S. enclosed shopping mall sector, with top-tier properties thriving while lower-tier assets continue to struggle. The study, titled "The American Mall Renaissance: A Bifurcated Sector with Top-Tier Assets Leading the Way," indicates that metrics such as foot traffic, net operating income, rents, and occupancy are moving in opposite directions for these two categories of malls. Top-tier malls, often featuring luxury retailers in affluent areas, have nearly recovered to pre-pandemic foot traffic levels and boast high occupancy, while lower-tier malls lag significantly. Mall owners are increasingly adapting by replacing traditional department store vacancies with entertainment, fitness, and healthcare concepts to enhance the consumer experience and generate higher rents, a strategy that also benefits many mid-tier malls appealing to younger demographics. However, lower-tier malls face a challenging cycle of declining traffic and tenant departures, with redevelopment into alternative uses like distribution centers or mixed-use projects often being their most viable path forward.

Colliers multifamily report: “Stabilizing” the best way to describe national multifamily market

REJournals — Thu, 04 Jun 2026 10:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Multifamily Report

The U.S. multifamily sector is entering a phase of gradual stabilization, according to Colliers' Q1 2026 report. This rebalancing is driven by a slowdown in new construction and sustained renter demand, largely due to high single-family home costs and elevated mortgage rates. National apartment occupancy held steady at 95.1%, with effective monthly rents averaging $1,934. While Sun Belt markets still face high supply, Midwest and select coastal markets are seeing stronger occupancy due to more restrained construction activity. Investors are selectively targeting markets with easing supply pressures and strong long-term fundamentals, with cap rates stabilizing in the mid-5% range.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

National apartment occupancy stood at 95.1% in Q1 2026, almost unchanged from the previous quarter and slightly below 95.2% a year earlier. Effective monthly rents averaged $1,934 nationally, up from $1,894 a year ago. National absorption reached over 85,000 units in Q1, outpacing the roughly 64,500 new units delivered. 501,117 units were under construction nationally at the end of Q1 2026, down from over 573,000 units a year earlier. Chicago posted a 96.3% occupancy rate in Q1, with effective rents increasing 3.5% year-over-year to $2,240 per month. Milwaukee also recorded a 96.3% occupancy rate and 2.1% annual rent growth. Cap rates have largely stabilized in the mid-5% range nationally.
