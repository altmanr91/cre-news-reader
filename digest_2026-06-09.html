Housing Notes: Thoughts about eliminating Florida property taxes

The Real Deal — National — Mon, 08 Jun 2026 19:35:36 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Jonathan Miller, a prominent real estate market analyst, argues against Florida's proposed elimination of property taxes, asserting that such a move would be detrimental to the state's housing market and quality of life. He emphasizes that property taxes are the most efficient and stable revenue source for local governments, funding essential services like police, fire, and schools, and that their elimination without a clear replacement plan would lead to reduced affordability and increased uncertainty. Miller highlights that land is an ideal tax target due to its inelastic supply and immobility, making property taxes difficult to evade and closely linking payment to local service benefits, which in turn capitalizes into property values.
