Texas Grid Operator Approves New Rules And Processes For Data Centers

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 04 Jun 2026 17:35:41 -0400

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The Electric Reliability Council of Texas (ERCOT) has approved new rules for data center operators and developers, which could have significant financial implications if finalized by the Texas Public Utility Commission. These rules aim to manage the unprecedented 450 gigawatts of power demand requested by data centers and other large loads, a figure more than five times ERCOT's all-time high. The new regulations include a batch review process for large electricity users, requiring nonrefundable fees and proof of development milestones for projects to be considered in the initial 'Batch Zero' group. Additionally, data centers and crypto mining facilities will be mandated to remain online during brief power disruptions to prevent cascading blackouts, a requirement that industry supporters estimate could cost billions and take years to implement.
