Wall Street Celebrates Big Tech's AI Growth, But Concerns Bubble Under The Surface

Bisnow News Feed — Sun, 03 May 2026 08:12:19 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Wall Street celebrated strong first-quarter earnings from major tech companies, driven by significant investments in artificial intelligence. Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and Meta reported higher-than-expected revenue growth, with cloud services and generative AI products showing substantial gains. This performance is seen by some as validation for the unprecedented data center spending spree. However, other analysts caution that rising costs for AI infrastructure, including electricity, equipment, and labor, are creating headwinds. Additionally, concerns are emerging about the concentration of cloud revenue from a small number of AI model-making customers, particularly Microsoft's reliance on OpenAI, which some view as a significant counterparty risk.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The overall cloud market grew by 35% year-over-year in Q1 2026, marking the industry's highest quarterly growth rate since 2021. In 2021, the cloud market was 40% of its current size. The cost of building and leasing new data centers is increasing due to supply constraints affecting prices for electricity, critical equipment, optical fiber, and labor. Project timelines are also prolonged by rising community opposition and legal hurdles. Shortages of graphics processing units (GPUs) and other AI-specific chips and IT gear are driving up equipment costs.

MARKET: MINNEAPOLIS-ST. PAUL, MN

To succeed in today’s Twin Cities office sector? Landlords must work hard

REJournals — Mon, 04 May 2026 10:10:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Office Report

Newmark's Q1 2026 report on the Minneapolis-St. Paul office market indicates a challenging but potentially stabilizing environment. While overall vacancy slightly dipped to 19.9% and net absorption turned positive, much of this improvement is attributed to a shrinking inventory as obsolete office space is removed through conversions and demolitions. Leasing activity remains subdued, characterized by a "flight to quality" where tenants right-size their footprints into newer, amenity-rich buildings, creating a sharp divide between Class A and Class B properties. Landlords are responding by holding asking rents steady but increasing concessions, particularly in the struggling downtown cores where vacancy rates are significantly higher. Despite these challenges, the report highlights opportunities for opportunistic investors willing to acquire discounted assets and reposition them, supported by the region's diverse economy and educated workforce.

DATA POINTS

Q1 2026 Twin Cities Office Vacancy: 19.9%

End of 2025 Twin Cities Office Vacancy: 20.8%

Q1 2026 Twin Cities Office Net Absorption: 50,000 square feet

Office Space Removed from Inventory Since 2024: 3.4 million square feet

Q1 2026 Downtown Minneapolis Office Vacancy: 30%

Q1 2026 Downtown St. Paul Office Vacancy: 37%

Target Lease Exit at City Center: 900,000 square feet

Twin Cities Unemployment Rate End of 2025: 3.8%

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Overall office vacancy across the Twin Cities dipped to 19.9% in Q1 2026, down from 20.8% at the end of 2025. Office net absorption in the Minneapolis-St. Paul market turned positive, reaching nearly 50,000 square feet during the first quarter. Since 2024, more than 3.4 million square feet of office space has been taken out of the Twin Cities office inventory. In downtown Minneapolis, vacancy hovered just above 30% in Q1 2026, while St. Paul’s downtown office market posted a vacancy rate of about 37%. The region’s unemployment rate stood at 3.8% at the end of 2025.

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — New York — Mon, 04 May 2026 11:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

A partnership between Marty Burger and Andrew Heiberger acquired a 12-story office building at 29 West 35th Street in New York City's Garment District for $25 million. The 467m tax break for office-to-residential conversions is seen as a critical incentive. Asking rents for dated office buildings in the Garment District are around $60.49 per square foot, half the cost of trophy offices in Hudson Yards.
