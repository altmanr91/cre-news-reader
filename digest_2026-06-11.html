While approximately half of major asset classes are generating positive real yields above the 3.8% consumer inflation rate, the author cautions investors to consider payout sustainability, price volatility, and total return expectations. What’s new in Wisconsin TIF: Key changes under Acts 173 and 235

REJournals — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 09:19:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

Wisconsin has enacted significant changes to its tax incremental financing (TIF) laws through Acts 173 and 235, aiming to stimulate residential development and affordable housing. Key reforms include expanding the TIF district life by up to two years for affordable housing initiatives and clarifying the definition of "newly platted residential development." Additionally, a new Residential TID type has been authorized, specifically designed for owner-occupied, single-family or two-family residences, with distinct requirements for lot sizes and home square footage. These new TIDs will operate on a "pay-go" structure, with developers providing upfront financing for infrastructure and receiving reimbursement from generated increment, and are subject to a new three percent valuation test.
