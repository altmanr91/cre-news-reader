What the NY AG’s antitrust investigation could mean for Compass

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 06 Jun 2026 14:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

The New York Attorney General has launched an antitrust investigation into Compass following its $1.6 billion acquisition of Anywhere Real Estate, which created the nation's largest residential real estate firm. The probe, confirmed by the AG's office, is examining whether the combined entity holds too much market dominance in New York. Potential remedies could include divestitures, agent headcount reductions, or fines, though a legal expert suggests the company may have already taken steps to address concerns, such as a subsidiary ending a licensing agreement. The outcome of the New York investigation could influence other states like Illinois and California to initiate similar inquiries.

In Bay Ridge, impossible to build becomes impossible to stop

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 06 Jun 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article discusses how recent City Charter revisions in New York City have shifted the balance of power in real estate development, making it easier for developers to overcome local opposition to new housing projects. Developer Daniel Grinshteyn, through his firm BWH Group, acquired a 40,000-square-foot site in Bay Ridge, Brooklyn, in 2024, but initially lacked the leverage to pursue a high-density residential project due to NIMBYism and the unlikelihood of City Council rezoning approval. However, new charter reforms, approved by voters in November of last year, now allow developers to appeal City Council rejections to a mayoral-controlled board, or, starting in 2027, utilize an expedited approval process for districts with low housing production. These changes provide Grinshteyn with significant leverage, compelling the local Council member to negotiate in good faith for the rezoning of his proposed 292-unit project, Fort Hamilton Mews, which also includes commercial space and underground parking.

The Daily Dirt: Mamdani’s YIMBY Socialism

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 06 Jun 2026 11:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article analyzes New York City Mayor Zohran Mamdani's housing policy, which attempts to bridge the gap between YIMBY (Yes In My Backyard) advocates and socialist tenants' rights groups. The mayor's strategy involves supporting market liberalism for new housing development, including permitting reform and rezonings, while simultaneously advocating for aggressive socialist policies for existing housing, such as community land trusts and tenant protections. This approach aims to unite historically divergent political constituencies by addressing both housing supply and affordability concerns, though its long-term stability may be tested by future rezoning efforts, particularly in working-class neighborhoods.

George Armoyan wipes out big Loop office loans as Chicago’s roster of debt-free towers grows

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Chicago — Sat, 06 Jun 2026 12:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Canadian investor George Armoyan, through Clarke Incorporated, has consolidated control over three Chicago-area office properties, including 120 South LaSalle and 20 South Clark streets in the Loop and 275 North Field Drive in Lake Forest. This move is part of Clarke's $1.1 billion takeover of Ravelin Properties, formerly Slate Office REIT, which had been facing financial difficulties and defaulted on debentures. The transaction effectively wipes out significant office loans, allowing the properties, particularly 20 South Clark Street, to operate debt-free and pursue aggressive leasing strategies in a competitive market.
