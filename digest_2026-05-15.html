Understanding the Realities of Private Credit Stress

Connect CRE — Fri, 15 May 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Private Credit

A recent CBRE report indicates that while private credit is experiencing stress, these issues are primarily confined to Business Development Companies (BDCs) that have financed the software sector, with limited direct impact on commercial real estate (CRE) financing. The report clarifies that private credit is a broad term encompassing corporate direct lending, asset-backed lending, and CRE loans, with alternative lenders, including CRE private credit and mortgage REITs, constituting a significant portion of non-agency closings. While direct contagion is unlikely due to the limited link between BDCs and CRE, indirect impacts such as tightening bank credit lines, reduced confidence in limited partnerships affecting fundraising, and potential liquidation sequencing for illiquid assets warrant close monitoring. The report also highlights concerns about the 2026 maturity wall, suggesting that reduced lending capacity could lead to distress or value declines for maturing CRE loans.

House Cuts Build-To-Rent Ban From Latest Bill, Targets Vote Next Week

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 14 May 2026 08:29:57 -0400

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The U.S. House of Representatives has removed a controversial provision from the 21st Century Road to Housing Act that would have imposed a seven-year forced-sale requirement on build-to-rent (BTR) operators. This amendment is a significant win for the BTR industry, which had seen a slowdown in activity due to the proposed restrictions. The revised bill, expected to be voted on next week, still prohibits large institutional investors (those with at least 350 homes) from purchasing existing single-family homes, but it includes new exceptions for properties that have always been renter-occupied, manufactured homes, and those requiring significant renovation to meet building codes. The removal of forced-sale provisions and investment caps signals a more favorable regulatory environment for BTR, which has seen substantial growth in recent years.
