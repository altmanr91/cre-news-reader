Housing Notes: Land appreciates, homes depreciate

The Real Deal — National — Wed, 27 May 2026 19:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This opinion column argues that the appreciation in residential real estate value primarily stems from the land, not the physical structure. The author contends that a home's value, excluding the land, depreciates over time unless there is continuous investment in maintenance and renovations. Conversely, land values tend to rise over the long term in most housing markets, offsetting the structural depreciation. The author emphasizes that homeownership requires constant investment in the house to keep pace with market expectations, as neighbors are typically doing the same. The piece concludes by suggesting that understanding land's behavior as an asset class is crucial for addressing affordable housing challenges.

Banks Fueled The Rise Of Private Credit. Now They're 'Competing Against Ourselves'

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 27 May 2026 17:24:16 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Banks are facing a dilemma in the commercial real estate lending market, as their previous strategy of financing private credit funds has led to increased competition. After regulatory scrutiny and balance sheet issues constrained banks from 2023, they provided capital to debt funds, inadvertently empowering these alternative lenders. Now, as banks re-enter the market with a stronger position due to offloaded problem loans and potential policy changes, they find themselves competing against the very funds they helped capitalize. This dynamic has led banks to focus on smaller deals, largely avoiding construction lending, and continuing to act as servicers for private lenders by providing credit lines.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Alternative lenders have since retaken the lead, responsible for 53% of CRE debt originations in the first quarter, with debt fund deal activity surging 280%, while banks' market share fell to 22%. Committed credit lines by the largest U.S. banks to private credit vehicles increased 145% over five years, reaching $95B by Q4 2024, with $56B utilized.
