Andrew Heiberger, Howard Lev jump on boutique condo boom with Flatiron townhouse

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 20 Jun 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Development

Developers Andrew Heiberger and Howard Lev are planning a three-unit luxury condominium project in New York City's Flatiron District, capitalizing on the growing demand for boutique new developments and move-in-ready townhouses. The project, dubbed the London Clay Townhouse, is expected to launch sales in the summer with a projected sellout exceeding $20 million. This development aligns with a broader market trend where smaller, high-end projects are increasingly attracting wealthy buyers, as evidenced by recent high-priced penthouse sales and townhouse transactions in desirable Manhattan and Brooklyn neighborhoods.

Lawsuit casts Riverside, Nussbaum Lowinger as architects of sprawling scheme

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 20 Jun 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

A federal lawsuit has been filed against Riverside Abstract and the defunct law firm Nussbaum Lowinger, alleging a multi-year real estate fraud scheme involving fraudulent property flips, misappropriated escrow funds, and concealed kickbacks. The plaintiffs, Blueberry Funding and EADMK, are seeking to recover $87.5 million in escrow funds. The lawsuit claims the defendants orchestrated dozens of sham transactions since 2018, using inflated valuations to secure larger loans and pocket the difference, with one example cited being the Park at Crestview apartment complex in Austin.

Forget cash. These Bay Area sellers want AI stock

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Sat, 20 Jun 2026 12:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article explores the emerging trend of Bay Area homeowners attempting to sell luxury residential properties in exchange for pre-IPO shares of artificial intelligence companies like OpenAI and Anthropic. While several high-profile listings have garnered significant attention, the article highlights the challenges and complexities of such transactions, including valuation discrepancies, capital gains tax implications, and the unpredictable nature of IPOs. Despite the difficulties, some sellers are genuinely seeking to tap into the anticipated wealth creation from the AI boom, even if it means ultimately pursuing traditional cash deals or separate stock transactions.

MARKET: LAKE CHARLES, LA

The challenge of cheap markets

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 20 Jun 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Muss Development and Amesbury Companies have acquired the 270-unit Erdace Apartments in downtown Lake Charles, Louisiana, by purchasing a HUD-insured loan for $37 million, which equates to $137,000 per unit. The new owners plan to invest several million dollars in renovations to the lobby, facades, hallways, and balconies, and will rebrand the property as the Ryan Apartments. This acquisition marks Muss Development's entry into the Louisiana market, contrasting with its historical focus on more complex and expensive development in New York City.
