Mamdani’s housing plan picks winners and losers

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 30 May 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Mayor Zohran Mamdani's "Block by Block" housing plan aims to create and preserve 400,000 affordable housing units over the next decade through zoning reform, public financing, and increased intervention in distressed properties. The plan benefits private developers with billions in investment and streamlined reviews, and also supports tenant groups and nonprofits by giving them leverage in acquiring distressed multifamily buildings. Conversely, owners of troubled rent-stabilized buildings face expanded use of the city's 7A program, which allows courts to strip landlords of control over distressed properties, alongside new enforcement initiatives targeting chronic violators.

The Daily Dirt: Hotel workers just got a raise big enough to make bankers jealous

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 30 May 2026 12:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

A new eight-year contract between the Hotel and Gaming Trades Council and New York City hotel owners will significantly increase wages for nearly 30,000 hotel workers across more than 250 hotels. Housekeepers' hourly wages are projected to reach over $61, potentially making them six-figure earners by the sixth year of the agreement. Hotel owners, facing the threat of a strike during the 2026 FIFA World Cup and a proposed City Council bill that would limit housekeeper workloads, reluctantly agreed to the terms, viewing it as a necessary concession despite ongoing economic challenges for the industry.

“Terribly sloppy”: NYC brokers grapple with new pied-à-terre tax

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 30 May 2026 12:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

New York City has approved an annual pied-à-terre tax on luxury second homes valued at $5 million or more, set to take effect on July 1. The legislation, passed as part of the state budget, has generated mixed reactions among real estate brokers, with some expressing frustration over the rushed process and lack of detailed information prior to the vote. While some brokers anticipate a negative impact on the market, others believe the anxiety is overblown, noting that New York City's luxury market is accustomed to high transaction costs and that initial panic often subsides as details become clearer.
