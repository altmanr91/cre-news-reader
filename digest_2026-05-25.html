MARKET: NEW YORK CITY, NY

Is NYC’s proposed cash-buyer tax already dead on arrival?

The Real Deal — National — Mon, 25 May 2026 01:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

A proposed 1% tax on all-cash home purchases over $1 million in New York City, intended to generate $160 million annually to address the city's budget deficit, is reportedly facing strong opposition and may be withdrawn. The residential real estate industry has reacted negatively, arguing that cash transactions are a significant portion of the market, especially at the high end, and the tax would further burden an already challenged market. Critics suggest the tax could lead buyers to circumvent the rule by taking out small mortgages or restructuring deals, potentially slowing transactions and complicating development underwriting. The proposal, introduced late in the budget process, has drawn comparisons to other controversial measures like the pied-à-terre tax and Los Angeles's 'mansion tax,' which have shown mixed results and, in some cases, chilled luxury investment activity.

Keeping up with the Thakkars, the embattled Dallas developer family

SALE

The Real Deal — Texas — Sun, 24 May 2026 14:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

This article details the ongoing legal and development challenges faced by Dallas-based developer Poorvesh Thakkar. While he recently achieved a milestone with the sale of a portion of his Mustang Square mixed-use project in Plano, his development track record remains largely unproven due to numerous delayed or aborted projects. Concurrently, Thakkar is embroiled in a lawsuit in New York City, where the ground lease owner of a Midtown Manhattan office building he acquired is seeking his eviction for alleged unpaid property taxes and penalties totaling nearly $28 million. Thakkar, who purchased the building for a significant discount with plans for a residential conversion, has filed a countersuit, claiming he was hindered from his conversion project.
