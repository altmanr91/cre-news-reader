Balancing Value, Equity, and Debt: A Simple 1031 Equation

REJournals — Mon, 18 May 2026 09:58:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article provides a detailed guide for investors navigating 1031 exchanges, emphasizing that successful tax deferral hinges on meticulously matching the value, equity, and debt of the relinquished property with the replacement property. The author, an attorney and qualified intermediary, explains the "simple 1031 equation" for full deferral, which requires the replacement purchase price to be greater than or equal to the amount realized, equity invested to be greater than or equal to net equity from the sale, and debt replaced to be greater than or equal to debt paid off or released (or offset by additional cash). The piece clarifies key terms like "amount realized" and "net equity," distinguishing between transactional and operational expenses, and highlights common pitfalls such as neglecting debt replacement planning. It also briefly touches on how Delaware Statutory Trust (DST) offerings can assist in meeting debt replacement requirements.

The 'Whale Hunting' Era Of Data Center Development May Be Ending

Bisnow News Feed — Sun, 17 May 2026 09:47:22 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The data center industry is experiencing a shift from the development of remote, gigawatt-scale hyperscale campuses, often dubbed "bragawatts" due to their speculative nature, towards smaller facilities of 100 MW or less located closer to major population centers. This change is driven by the increasing demand for AI inference computing, which requires lower latency and proximity to end-users, as opposed to the training of large language models that fueled the initial boom. Industry leaders note that these smaller, "edge" data centers are more feasible to develop, often utilizing infill sites like former industrial plants with existing power infrastructure, allowing for quicker deployment and better grid connectivity.

Reactions to Zillow-MRED lawsuit expose fault lines in fight over listing data

The Real Deal — Chicago — Sun, 17 May 2026 14:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Zillow has filed a lawsuit against Midwest Real Estate Data (MRED), the Chicago-area Multiple Listing Service, and Compass, alleging that MRED colluded with Compass to force Zillow to display Compass's private listings nationwide or lose access to its Chicago data feed. This legal action highlights a broader industry debate over the control and transparency of listing data, questioning whether homesellers should be able to market properties privately or if a fully public market best serves consumers. Zillow argues that MRED, whose board includes Compass-affiliated brokers, is acting as a competitor rather than a neutral marketplace, potentially harming broader market access and consumer interests. The outcome of this lawsuit could significantly impact how MLSs nationwide interact with listing portals and brokerages, potentially leading to more fragmented listing data if Zillow loses, or setting a precedent for broader data distribution if Zillow prevails.
