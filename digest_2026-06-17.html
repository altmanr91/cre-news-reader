Big tech returning to the office — on its own terms

REJournals — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 09:47:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Office Strategy

A recent Newmark report, "Big Tech's U.S. Office Strategy Is Clear: Concentrate, Upgrade, Expand," indicates that major technology companies are no longer broadly reducing their office footprints. Instead, they are strategically expanding in key innovation markets, focusing on consolidating employees into premier buildings, and upgrading their work environments. This shift emphasizes quality over quantity, with tech firms favoring flagship campuses and trophy assets that offer deep labor pools and strong innovation ecosystems, particularly driven by the need for in-person collaboration in the AI sector. The report suggests that while space utilization per employee may be slightly less than pre-pandemic, the overall reduction is smaller than anticipated, as companies plan for peak attendance days, leading to continued demand for substantial footprints.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

High-quality Class A and trophy buildings continue to outperform lower-tier properties, with tenants gravitating toward buildings offering superior amenities, transit access, and collaborative environments. Older Class B properties continue to face elevated vacancy and leasing challenges. New office construction has slowed significantly, helping bring supply and demand into better balance.

Oil Crisis Highlights Plastics' Hidden Costs For Construction

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 16 Jun 2026 12:03:33 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The ongoing Middle East conflict has highlighted the construction industry's increasing reliance on petroleum-based plastics, which account for nearly 20% of global plastic production and are vital for various building components. While a weak residential market has somewhat mitigated the impact on finished plastic product prices, raw material costs for plastics like PVC, polypropylene, and polyethylene have surged due to rising oil prices. Experts note that while plastics are not yet the primary driver of overall construction costs compared to materials like steel or copper, their growing use, particularly in specialized applications like PVC membrane roofs for multifamily and data center projects, makes their cost a significant and increasing factor. Continued oil market uncertainty could lead to further price increases and potential supply shortages, underscoring the industry's vulnerability to petrochemical market fluctuations.

Senate Advances Housing Bill After Striking Deal With House Leaders

The Senate has advanced the 21st Century Road to Housing Act, a significant federal housing bill that has garnered bipartisan support and is expected to pass both chambers. Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 16 Jun 2026 18:26:05 -0400

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

New York's legislature has passed a bill proposing a one-year moratorium on new data center permits for facilities over 20 MW, which now awaits Governor Kathy Hochul's signature. This legislation, known as the Responsible Data Center Development Act, would also impose new energy efficiency goals, labor standards, and community benefit requirements for projects over 5 MW, and mandate public hearings for all new developments. The move comes despite Governor Hochul's prior initiatives to attract AI and advanced technology infrastructure, including $10 billion in tax credits for semiconductor manufacturing and a $10 billion nanotechnology research facility, which have spurred significant data center development interest. Industry experts note that the 20 MW threshold is low and would impact nearly all new data center projects, while proponents argue the pause is necessary to assess environmental impacts like energy and water usage, and to give communities a voice in development.
