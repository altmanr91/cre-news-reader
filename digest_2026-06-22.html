CMBS Distress Rate Climbed to 11.86% in May

Commercial Observer — Mon, 22 Jun 2026 13:06:19 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / CMBS Report

The overall commercial mortgage-backed securities (CMBS) distress rate climbed to 11.86% in May 2026, an increase from 11.08% in April, as both special servicing and delinquency rates rose across the conduit and single-asset, single-borrower universe. This reversal erased April's brief improvement and pushed distress back toward cyclical highs, more than doubling since mid-2022. The persistent gap between the special servicing rate and the delinquency rate indicates that a significant portion of distressed loans are being actively managed by servicers before or instead of becoming payment-delinquent, serving as a leading indicator for investors as upcoming maturities approach.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Self-storage (0.15%), industrial (1.04%), and manufactured housing (1.19%) show strong resilience.

Banks sweeten deals as they vie for private credit’s business

The Real Deal — National — Mon, 22 Jun 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Banks are increasingly competing to provide 'back leverage' to commercial real estate private credit lenders, resulting in more favorable terms for borrowers. This competition is driving down interest rates, improving leverage ratios, and offering more flexible recourse options on repurchase facilities and lines of credit. The shift is partly due to banks limiting direct CRE loan exposure under new regulations, making lending to intermediaries a more attractive, less capital-intensive option. This trend is significantly benefiting private credit firms by reducing their cost of capital and offering more advantageous accounting methods, such as non-mark-to-market facilities.

Real estate has a Ponzi problem

The Real Deal — National — Sun, 21 Jun 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

This article highlights a recurring theme in recent real estate fraud cases: the alleged sale of trust rather than tangible assets. It details several high-profile cases, including Ken Mattson's alleged $100 million Ponzi scheme and Josh Schuster's $10 million scheme, where investors were reportedly swayed by personal relationships and perceived credibility. The author argues that the private nature, complex ownership structures, and potential for outsized returns in real estate can make it particularly susceptible to such schemes, making it difficult for even experienced investors to distinguish legitimate opportunities from fraud. The article concludes by emphasizing that credibility can be as persuasive as collateral in real estate dealings.

Evolution never stops at Minneapolis’ 9th Street Center

LEASE

REJournals — Mon, 22 Jun 2026 10:04:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Hillcrest Development has secured two new tenants, Wyldwolf Games and Midwest Indoor Soccer, for its 9th Street Center in Minneapolis, continuing the property's transformation from an industrial site into a mixed-use experiential destination. Wyldwolf Games, a tabletop gaming retailer, will occupy 1,945 square feet, while Midwest Indoor Soccer will take approximately 28,000 square feet for two indoor soccer fields and youth programs. The additions reflect Hillcrest Development's strategy to curate a diverse tenant mix that includes retail, recreation, and community-focused businesses, leveraging the property's unique physical characteristics and central location.
