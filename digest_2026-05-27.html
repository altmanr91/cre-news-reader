Housing Notes: We co‑broke with everyone*

The Real Deal — National — Tue, 26 May 2026 20:04:24 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Jonathan Miller, a prominent residential real estate analyst, has partnered with The Real Deal to publish his "Housing Notes" column and quarterly New York City market report. Miller's column discusses the ongoing challenges faced by Multiple Listing Services (MLSs) and the broader real estate industry due to antitrust litigation and evolving commission structures. He argues that while the Department of Justice and class-action lawsuits aimed to increase transparency and shift buyer agent commission payments, the market has largely maintained the status quo, with sellers still bearing the cost. Miller contends that the economic impact of these changes has been misunderstood, as buyers would simply offer less for a property if they had to pay their agent's commission out-of-pocket, ultimately not saving sellers money. He also highlights the contentious issue of private listings, particularly by Compass, which he views as unethical and detrimental to price discovery, creating a dilemma for traditional MLS systems.

PolicyPro: Unpacking Mamdani’s housing plan

The Real Deal — New York — Tue, 26 May 2026 22:30:23 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Mayor Zohran Mamdani has unveiled a comprehensive housing plan for New York City, focusing on expanding affordable housing, increasing tenant protections, and addressing distressed properties. Key initiatives include pushing for expanded project labor agreements in affordable housing developments, which has drawn criticism from some industry stakeholders concerned about potential cost increases. The plan also aims to intensify the use of the city's 7A program to take control of troubled buildings from landlords with chronic violations and introduce a new preservation program allowing distressed building owners to pool reserves for capital and operating needs. Additionally, the city seeks to facilitate the conversion of more rental buildings into co-ops and is backing two City Council bills: the SAFER Homes Act, which overhauls the Third Party Transfer program for distressed properties, and the Community Opportunity to Purchase Act (COPA), which gives city-certified nonprofits priority in acquiring certain distressed multifamily properties.
