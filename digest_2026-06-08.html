CMBS Distress Rises in 17 of the 25 Largest U.S. Markets

Commercial Observer — Mon, 08 Jun 2026 11:30:24 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / CMBS Distress Report

A May 2026 analysis of commercial mortgage-backed securities (CMBS) distress by CRED iQ reveals a national distress rate of 12.7 percent across the 25 largest U.S. metropolitan statistical areas, an increase from 12.2 percent in June 2025. Seventeen of these markets experienced year-over-year increases, with Minneapolis, Denver, and Rochester, N.Y., showing the highest distress rates. The most significant escalations were observed in St. Louis, Oklahoma City, and Pittsburgh, largely due to concentrated office and mixed-use loan exposure. Conversely, Providence, Charlotte, and Austin saw improvements in their distress rates, reflecting successful loan resolutions and stronger market dynamics in some Sun Belt areas.

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The U.S. construction industry is experiencing a significant shift, with data center and advanced manufacturing projects increasingly dominating overall activity, while multifamily construction faces substantial headwinds. This disparity is so pronounced that excluding data center and manufacturing work would reveal a flat or declining pace of construction nationally. South Florida Dirt: Miami-Dade County vs. Fisher Island’s billionaires

The Real Deal — Miami — Sun, 07 Jun 2026 17:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Miami-Dade County has abandoned its proposed $400 million deal to acquire a nearly 10-acre fuel depot on Fisher Island from HRP Group, opting instead to pursue the property through eminent domain. HRP Group had previously acquired the site for $180 million in October, with plans to demolish the fuel facility, remediate the land, and develop luxury condominiums, conveying 4 acres to the Fisher Island Community Association. The county's decision to pursue eminent domain comes after a lawsuit filed by residents against HRP and the resignation of two county officials, highlighting a contentious battle over the site's future use and the county's need for continued fuel supply for PortMiami cruise ships.

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Multifamily owners in Texas are facing increased pressure to sell or refinance assets as capital markets continue to favor a "higher for longer" interest rate environment, despite earlier hopes for rate cuts. While operating fundamentals are showing uneven improvement across submarkets, refinancing challenges are primarily driving transaction activity, with many sales involving smoothly operating properties burdened by distressed capital stacks rather than operational distress. The market is characterized by significant submarket divergence, with some areas experiencing rent growth and strong absorption, while others, particularly in North Dallas, continue to offer substantial concessions. Buyers are increasingly focused on yield-on-cost frameworks and current asset production rather than theoretical stabilization values, with assumable low-rate debt emerging as a key selling point for properties.
