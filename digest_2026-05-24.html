Hotel union deal puts NYC owners on notice

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 23 May 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

New York City's hotel industry has reached a tentative eight-year labor agreement with the Hotel and Gaming Trades Council, covering nearly 30,000 workers across more than 250 properties. The deal, which is expected to be ratified, includes significant wage increases, with housekeepers projected to earn over $100,000 annually by the sixth year. While the agreement averts a strike ahead of the 2026 FIFA World Cup, hotel owners are concerned about the substantial increase in payroll obligations, which they believe will impact their bottom line amidst rising inflation, tariffs, and borrowing costs. Operators are expected to attempt to raise room rates to offset these increased expenses.

MARKET: SAN FRANCISCO, CA

Hoping to spur development, San Francisco wants to eliminate some affordable housing requirements

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Sat, 23 May 2026 16:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

San Francisco city leaders are proposing to reduce the required percentage of affordable housing units in new developments from 15% to 5% to stimulate housing construction, a move that would make the city's on-site affordable housing requirements among the lowest nationally. Projects with 25 or fewer units would be entirely exempt. This proposal, supported by Mayor Daniel Lurie and Supervisor Myrna Melgar, aims to address the city's struggle to build new housing despite a tech boom. Concurrently, a ballot initiative will seek to increase the city's annual contribution to its Housing Trust Fund from $52 million to $125 million to finance and preserve affordable units.
