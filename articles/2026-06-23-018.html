Unconstitutional? DeSantis plan excludes newcomers from property tax cuts

The Real Deal — Miami — Mon, 22 Jun 2026 22:50:28 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Florida Governor Ron DeSantis's proposed property tax amendment faces constitutional challenges due to a provision that would require new residents to wait five years before qualifying for an increased homestead exemption. The Miami Herald's analysis, citing a U.S. Supreme Court ruling, suggests this waiting period could be deemed discriminatory, as the Florida Supreme Court previously struck down a similar measure four decades ago. The proposal, which aims to increase the homestead exemption from $50,000 to $250,000 by 2028, is intended to appeal to voters concerned about population growth and to shift the tax burden to seasonal homeowners. However, critics also warn that eliminating property taxes could create a significant funding gap for public services.

DATA POINTS

Proposed homestead exemption increase: From $50,000 to $250,000

Year proposed exemption takes effect: 2028

Percentage of Floridians benefiting from lower tax bills: 60%

Exemption coverage at $500,000 peak: 92% of homeowners

Potential funding gap for public services: $55 billion
