Artist Romero Britto to Expand Compound in Miami’s Little River

ACQUISITION

Commercial Observer — Tue, 12 May 2026 20:15:34 +0000

SUMMARY

Artist Romero Britto has expanded his compound in Miami's Little River neighborhood with the acquisition of a 57,187-square-foot industrial building for $5.2 million. The property, located directly across from his existing Britto Palace studio and headquarters, was sold by Doug Levine, founder of Crunch Fitness. This purchase reflects Britto's belief in Little River's potential as a vibrant creative hub, drawing parallels to the early development of Lincoln Road and Wynwood.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Little River has attracted arts organizations and culinary establishments due to cheap rents and industrial space. Oolite Arts is developing a $30 million campus about a mile west on 72nd Street. AJ Capital acquired a portfolio in the neighborhood for $80 million in 2021 for repositioning. Miami-Dade County officials approved a 3,233-unit development from Pablo Castro last year under the Live Local Act.
