How the Fort Worth Stockyards became a chess piece in a “Succession”-level family drama

SALE

The Real Deal — Texas — Sat, 09 May 2026 14:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

This article details the dramatic exit of Craig Cavileer, the developer behind the successful redevelopment of the Fort Worth Stockyards, from the project's second phase. His departure, stemming from a "Succession"-level family drama involving Majestic Realty and the Hickman family, has cast uncertainty over the future of the planned expansion, which includes high-end shopping and a hotel. The article also covers the wrongful termination lawsuit filed by former Fermi CEO Toby Neugebauer against the data center REIT, citing the company's struggles with its flagship development and a significant stock price drop. Additionally, Crow Holdings secured a five-year, fixed-rate refinancing loan for six buildings within its Old Parkland office complex in Dallas. Finally, a long-foreclosed IHOP site in Downtown Austin, previously owned by Nate Paul, sold at auction for nearly $13 million to Travis County Exchange Corporation, representing a significant discount from its appraised value and signaling a prime redevelopment opportunity.
