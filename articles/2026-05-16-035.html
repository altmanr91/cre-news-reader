More Oakland offices seized by lender as East Bay distress grows

FORECLOSURE

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Fri, 15 May 2026 22:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Edgewater Park Plaza, a 206,000-square-foot office complex in Oakland, was seized by its lender, East West Bank, through foreclosure after the owner defaulted on $21.4 million in debt. The property, valued at $8.7 million in the foreclosure, represents a significant decline from its 2022 purchase price of $35.7 million, highlighting the severe distress in Oakland's office market due to high interest rates, weak leasing demand, and remote work trends.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE
