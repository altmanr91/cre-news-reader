PolicyPro: Upstate rent reg bill builds momentum, COPA secures Council support

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 21 May 2026 22:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

A bill known as the Rent Emergency Stabilization for Tenants (REST) Act is gaining momentum in the New York State Assembly, aiming to expand rent regulation beyond New York City. The proposed legislation, which has cleared two Assembly committees, would allow localities to declare a housing emergency using publicly available data, rather than requiring a vacancy study. It would also lower the threshold for regulated buildings to four units or more and include buildings substantially rehabilitated more than 15 years ago. Meanwhile, the Community Opportunity to Purchase Act (COPA) has secured majority support in the New York City Council, with 26 sponsors, making its passage likely if it reaches a floor vote.

DATA POINTS

Current rent stabilization vacancy rate threshold: 5% or lower

Current rent stabilization building unit threshold: 6 or more units

Proposed REST Act building unit threshold: 4 or more units

COPA City Council sponsors: 26
