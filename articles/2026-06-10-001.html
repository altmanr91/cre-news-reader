Industrial Real Estate Has Entered Its Fixer-Upper Phase

Commercial Observer — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 10:00:57 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The industrial real estate sector is experiencing a significant shift towards value-add investment strategies, driven by a confluence of market corrections and improving fundamentals. Despite a recent boom, a shortage of specific logistics real estate types has emerged, creating opportunities for investors to acquire and upgrade industrial assets. Experts predict strong rent growth in the coming years, with a notable 'flight to quality' observed, particularly in the Northeast. This trend is further supported by a shrinking supply pipeline, rising rents, and increased investment volume, making the acquisition and repositioning of industrial properties an attractive proposition.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

JLL data indicates that national industrial rents are inching up just under a percentage point, pulling out of a three-year plateau. BGO's machine learning algorithm predicts significant disparity in performance across 2,500 U.S. submarkets, with top quartile markets potentially seeing up to 30% rent growth over the next five years, while bottom quartile markets may experience flat or declining performance.
