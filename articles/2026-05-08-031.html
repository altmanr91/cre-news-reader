Amazon’s 1,300-acre land buy deepens Texas data center bench as Hut 8 inks a near $10B lease

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Texas — Thu, 07 May 2026 20:00:32 +0000

SUMMARY

Entities tied to Amazon have acquired over 1,300 acres in Bastrop County, Texas, for what appears to be a hyperscale data center development, marking one of the largest land acquisitions for this purpose in Central Texas. This transaction contributes to the region's growing data center market, driven by the demand for AI computing and the availability of affordable land and expanding power infrastructure. Separately, Hut 8 has signed a 15-year, $9.8 billion lease for the first phase of its Beacon Point campus in Nueces County, near Corpus Christi, for a large-scale AI training and operations facility. The lease is a triple-net agreement with a high-investment-grade counterparty, providing Hut 8 with long-term revenue certainty, and the project is designed to mitigate local water consumption concerns through a closed-loop cooling system.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: data center

Site Area: 1300 acres

Project Notes: The land was previously slated for the Creekside Master Planned Community and owned by Austin-based CTX Capital Partners.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: Amazon Data Services

SELLER: CTX Capital Partners

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Texas is experiencing an unprecedented data center buildout driven by AI demand, with over 30 identified projects in the region representing at least $50 billion in projected capital investment. Bastrop County is a hotspot for data center development, including EdgeConneX's four-building, 2.8 million-square-foot campus with an estimated $1.4 billion price tag. The Hut 8 lease covers 352 megawatts of computing capacity, with room to scale to a planned 1-gigawatt campus, and the first phases alone represent $17 billion in capital investment. Corpus Christi is facing severe drought conditions, with reservoirs below 8 percent capacity.
