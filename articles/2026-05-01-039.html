Financing New Hotels Is A 'Nightmare' Without Branded Condos, Hoteliers Say

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 30 Apr 2026 09:53:38 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The article discusses the growing trend of luxury hotels incorporating branded residential components to secure financing, a strategy that has tripled between 2015 and 2025. Hoteliers at Bisnow's National Hospitality Conference emphasized that a residential component is now almost a prerequisite for new luxury hotel developments in the U.S., as lenders are increasingly hesitant to finance standalone hotel projects due to rising construction and financing costs. Presales of these residential units help de-risk projects, lower the developer's equity requirement, and can even generate additional cash, making the overall development more viable in a challenging capital market environment. This trend is particularly strong in South Florida but is rapidly expanding to other major U.S. markets.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The number of rooms under construction in the U.S. decreased year-over-year for the 15th consecutive month in March, according to STR data. Miami has the second-most branded residential projects globally, and Fort Lauderdale ranks eighth. South Florida had 48 completed branded condo projects and 55 in the pipeline at the end of 2025.
