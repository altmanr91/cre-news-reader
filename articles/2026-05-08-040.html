Cushman & Wakefield Revenue Climbs As AI Leases Drive Dealmaking

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 07 May 2026 11:41:54 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / Earnings Report

Cushman & Wakefield reported a record-breaking first quarter revenue of $2.5 billion, a 9% increase year-over-year, primarily fueled by robust leasing activity driven by artificial intelligence demand in the office and industrial sectors. Despite a net loss of $12.6 million due to a pension buyout settlement, the firm's adjusted net income rose significantly. CEO Michelle MacKay highlighted AI's potential to expand the economy and generate substantial demand for high-quality office and modern industrial spaces, projecting an additional 330 million square feet of demand over the next decade.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Manhattan and San Francisco are noted as leading markets for office absorption in Q1, driven by strong tech ecosystems. Industrial demand is shifting towards modern, power-intensive facilities suitable for AI and automation.
