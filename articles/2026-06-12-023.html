Menin debuts 100% affordable housing initiative for city libraries

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 18:19:08 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

City Council Speaker Julie Menin has launched an initiative to develop 100% affordable housing on existing public library sites across New York City, targeting three initial locations in the Bronx, Queens, and Brooklyn. This proposal aims to introduce hundreds of new affordable units and replace aging library facilities through public-private partnerships, with the City Council seeking a $60 million investment from the Mayor's administration. The initiative builds on previous successful co-location projects and aligns with recent zoning amendments designed to streamline affordable housing development.

DATA POINTS

Mayor's housing plan goal: 200,000 affordable housing units built and 200,000 preserved over the next decade

City of Yes zoning amendments goal: 82,000 new homes over a 15-year period

Mayor's executive budget library funding: $31.7 million

City-owned underutilized buildings/vacant land: Close to 1,000

Small lots potential housing units: 35,000 units on nearly 3,000 small lots
