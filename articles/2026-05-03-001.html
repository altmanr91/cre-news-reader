Housing lotteries frustrate landlords, tenants. Here’s how to fix them

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 02 May 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This opinion piece argues that New York City's housing lottery system, designed to fill income-restricted apartments, is inefficient and creates unnecessary vacancies, harming both tenants and affordable housing operators. The author highlights that below-market units typically remain vacant for four to five months due to bureaucratic processes, overwhelming application volumes, and mismatches between applicants and available units. Drawing on recommendations from the NYU Furman Center, the article suggests reforms such as pre-screening applicants for eligibility, gathering more detailed preferences to avoid unwanted offers, and creating a unified document submission system across various government housing programs to streamline the process.
