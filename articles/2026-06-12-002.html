There’s Been a Big Shift in the Commercial Real Estate Capital Markets

Commercial Observer — Fri, 12 Jun 2026 09:39:40 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article argues that the conventional view of banks and private credit as competitors in commercial real estate lending is outdated. Instead, a structural shift has led to increased collaboration, with banks providing balance sheet capital and private lenders offering flexibility and creativity. This partnership has expanded the overall lending ecosystem, making more projects financeable and contributing to a robust credit environment despite mixed property fundamentals. The author invokes Jevons Paradox to explain how greater efficiency in financing has led to increased overall lending activity, rather than a mere transfer of market share. This collaborative approach is particularly crucial as a significant volume of commercial mortgages mature in 2026, and it has also contributed to a lower incidence of distressed asset sales compared to previous cycles.
