Boston Office Market's Big Problem? Not Enough Demolition, Execs Say

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 19 May 2026 15:13:54 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Boston's office market is performing better than its high vacancy rate suggests, according to market leaders, who argue that the overall vacancy figure is inflated by a large stock of obsolete buildings. Tenant demand for high-quality, Class A office space in downtown Boston and close-in suburbs remains robust, with some new office buildings achieving unprecedented rents. The market is also seeing a positive trend of adaptive reuse, with numerous office-to-residential conversions approved or underway, which is helping to reduce the supply of less desirable office space. This flight to quality and the redevelopment of older assets are expected to lead to a healthier market as companies continue to bring employees back to the office.
