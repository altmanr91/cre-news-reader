Kalthia buys another distressed Bay Area hotel months after foreclosed loan sale

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 15:07:39 +0000

SUMMARY

Kalthia Group Hotels, through an affiliate, has acquired the 142-room Hyatt House Pleasant Hill in Pleasant Hill, CA, for $3.3 million. This transaction follows the hotel's foreclosure by a Mount Street U.S. affiliate in June 2025, which subsequently auctioned off the loan in March for $3.5 million. The sale price of approximately $23,200 per room is significantly below the Bay Area's median hotel price per room, reflecting the continued distress in the region's hospitality market.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Northern California hotels traded at a median price of about $109,000 per room in 2025. San Jose's Hotel De Anza traded for $11.5 million, or about $115,000 per room, in 2024. Several Bay Area properties have entered foreclosure over the past year, including a 276-room dual-branded Marriott hotel at 1431 Jefferson Street in Oakland, the 541-room Signia by Hilton in downtown San Jose, and the 500-room Oakland Marriott City Center.
