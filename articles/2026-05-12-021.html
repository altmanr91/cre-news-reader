Brooklyn dealmaker Bert I. Dweck faces barrage of lawsuits over missing money, bad checks

The Real Deal — New York — Mon, 11 May 2026 22:30:24 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Brooklyn dealmaker Bert I. Dweck of Dweck Group is facing a barrage of seven lawsuits in New York state court, totaling at least $14.4 million in alleged unpaid loans, bounced checks, and misappropriated escrow funds. The lawsuits, filed by investors and lenders, describe a pattern where Dweck allegedly solicited funds for real estate deals that either stalled or never materialized, and failed to return escrow money. Some complaints also name Dweck's parents and his lawyer, Jacques Erdos, in connection with the alleged fraud. The legal actions highlight a severe cash crunch and alleged misconduct across multiple real estate transactions and personal lending relationships.
