Historic office, retail building latest Oakland property to fall into default

FORECLOSURE

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Wed, 06 May 2026 22:47:52 +0000

SUMMARY

A historic 11,800-square-foot office and retail building in Oakland's Uptown district has defaulted on a $2.4 million loan. The 1923-built property, known as "Radio Central" for its past as a radio station, is owned by an affiliate managed by developer Warren Malnick. This default is part of a broader trend of commercial property distress in Oakland, affecting various asset types including office, multifamily, and hotel properties.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: office

Address: 464 19th Street, Oakland, CA

Size: 11,800 SF

Loan Amount: $2.4M

Loan Per SF: $203/SF (calculated)

Year Built: 1923

Notable Features: Historic two-story building at the corner of 19th Street and Broadway, designed by Edward Foulkes, and formerly home to radio stations KROW and KABL.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

OWNER: Affiliate managed by Warren Malnick — Warren Malnick, Developer

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Tribune Tower at 409 13th Street, 1540–1544 Broadway, 1500 Broadway, the Financial Center Building, 2101 Webster Street, 1901 Harrison Street, and 2100 Franklin Street are also in default or foreclosure in Oakland. Hotel defaults are particularly increasing across the Bay Area.
