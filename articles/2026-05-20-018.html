How pied-à-terre, cash-buyer surcharges will change behavior

The Real Deal — New York — Tue, 19 May 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article analyzes the potential behavioral changes and market impacts resulting from New York's proposed pied-à-terre and cash-buyer taxes. The author argues that these new taxes, intended to generate revenue and fulfill political pledges, are likely to discourage luxury home purchases, potentially shifting demand towards high-end rentals and impacting development decisions. The piece highlights the counterintuitive nature of taxing a behavior (owning a pied-à-terre) that the government simultaneously seeks to encourage for revenue, and suggests that wealthy buyers will likely seek methods of tax avoidance. The author also criticizes the rushed and opaque legislative process behind these tax proposals.

DATA POINTS

Estimated annual pied-à-terre surcharge for an $18M unit: $200,000

Regular annual property tax for an $18M unit: $100,000

Proposed transfer tax on all-cash home purchases: 1%

Threshold for all-cash transfer tax: $1 million or more
