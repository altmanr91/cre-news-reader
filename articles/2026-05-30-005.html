Rezonings Are Now Driving Land Development in New York City

Commercial Observer — Fri, 29 May 2026 13:47:46 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

New York City's development market has shifted from being primarily location-driven to one where regulatory entitlements are the main catalyst for new residential development. The scarcity of prime development sites has intensified this trend, making zoning capacity a highly valued asset. This phenomenon, while not new, is more pronounced than ever, with developers and investors actively pursuing sites with the potential for increased density through rezonings and land use reforms. This entitlement-driven approach is evident in the significant multifamily unit deliveries in Brooklyn and Queens in 2025, largely spurred by recent neighborhood-wide rezonings and initiatives like 'City of Yes'.
