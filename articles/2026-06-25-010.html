Michigan Strategic Fund Approves $270.7M Plan for Redevelopment of Former Lakeside Mall

DEVELOPMENT

REBusinessOnline — Wed, 24 Jun 2026 14:33:13 +0000

SUMMARY

The Michigan Strategic Fund Board has approved a $270.7 million Transformational Brownfield Plan to facilitate the redevelopment of the former Lakeside Mall site in Sterling Heights. The project, led by Lakeside OOTB Ventures LLC, a subsidiary of Lionheart Capital, will feature 1,545 residential units across nine multifamily buildings, including a dedicated building for residents over 55. Additionally, the redevelopment will introduce 154,007 square feet of new retail space, a central community park, and various parking solutions, with an estimated total capital investment of $621 million upon completion.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: mixed-use

Size: 154,007 SF

Units: 1,545

Total Project Cost: $621.0M

Notable Features: Includes a building targeted at residents over 55 years of age, a central community park, and green space.

Project Notes: The Transformational Brownfield Plan incentive package of $270.7 million will be reimbursed over 30 years. The City of Sterling Heights plans a $27.6 million bond issuance for infrastructure costs. The former Lakeside Mall closed in July 2024.
