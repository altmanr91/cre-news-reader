It’s official: Fifth Third Bank will exit Downtown Dallas Comerica Tower after Comerica merger

LEASE

The Real Deal — Texas — Thu, 21 May 2026 16:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Fifth Third Bank is vacating its 200,000-square-foot lease at Comerica Bank Tower in Downtown Dallas ahead of schedule, following its merger with Comerica. The bank will relocate to a new 63,000-square-foot space at a mixed-use development in Preston Center, with a full move-in anticipated in late 2028. This early departure, along with the upcoming expiration of Comerica's naming rights, creates uncertainty for the future of the Comerica Bank Tower, which is slated for a significant redevelopment.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: office

Address: 8300 Douglas Avenue, Dallas, TX

Size: 63,000 SF

Completion: late 2028

Site Area: 2.5 acres

Notable Features: New mixed-use development featuring a 12-story, 300,000-square-foot office building and a 17-story residential tower.

Project Notes: Fifth Third Bank is moving from its current 200,000-square-foot lease at 1717 Main Street, Dallas, TX. The bank will operate out of its North Park location at 8850 Boedeker Street until the new office is ready.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

TENANT: Fifth Third Bank

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Lincoln Property Company

OWNER: Slate Asset Management

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Downtown Dallas' office market is struggling, with AT&T's planned headquarters move to Plano leaving a 2 million-square-foot vacancy. The Comerica Bank Tower is planned for redevelopment to include 600,000 square feet of modern offices, 240 apartments, a 242-key hotel, and 10,000 square feet of ground floor retail.
