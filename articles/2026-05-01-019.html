TRD PolicyPro: Pied-à-terre tax fluctuations, state bill targets condo lawsuit risk

Latest New York Real Estate News — Thu, 30 Apr 2026 22:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Government Policy / Market Commentary

New York City Comptroller Mark Levine's office released an analysis indicating a proposed pied-à-terre tax could generate between $340 million and $510 million annually, depending on its final structure and exemptions. This tax, aimed at non-primary residences valued at $5 million or more, is intended to help close the city's budget gap, though the Real Estate Board of New York (REBNY) expressed concerns that it might fall short of expectations and deter investment. Concurrently, state lawmakers are addressing the high litigation risks that have hindered condo development, with a new Assembly bill proposing clearer procedures for construction-defect claims and limits on damages to reduce insurance costs. Another bill aims to support the affordable housing sector by shielding certain low-income housing providers from some civil lawsuits and establishing a New York Housing Insurance Stabilization Association to address soaring insurance and liability costs.
