Which Miami-Dade office buildings are setting records? We mapped them

The Real Deal — Miami — Tue, 19 May 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Office Rents

Miami-Dade County's office market is experiencing record-setting rents, primarily driven by demand for smaller, high-end boutique office buildings rather than traditional corporate towers. These projects, often five or six stories, feature bespoke designs and luxury amenities, catering to ultra-high-net-worth individuals seeking discreet office space near their waterfront residences. While a major corporate tower, 830 Brickell, is finalizing a lease at $250 per square foot, most other record-breaking deals are occurring in these smaller, amenity-rich properties, with many letters of intent exceeding $200 per square foot and signed deals regularly surpassing $150 per square foot.

DATA POINTS

Highest known lease being finalized: $250 per square foot, gross, at 830 Brickell

Letters of intent at several boutique buildings: Over $200 per square foot

Letters of intent at many boutique buildings: Over $150 per square foot

Signed deals regularly exceeding: $150 per square foot
