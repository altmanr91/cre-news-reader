Expanding Floral Distributor Buys Facility in L.A. County

ACQUISITION

Commercial Observer — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 21:00:48 +0000

SUMMARY

Holland Flower Market, a flower importer and wholesaler, acquired a 91,010-square-foot Class A industrial facility in Commerce, CA, for $34.1 million to support its expanding operations and investment in automation. The buyer secured a $25 million acquisition loan from BMO Bank. This transaction highlights a trend in Southern California where owner-users are leveraging current market conditions, including softer leasing and a correction in infill rents, to acquire high-quality assets in prime locations.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The industrial market in Los Angeles has a vacancy rate of 4.2 percent, with leasing activity rebounding in Q1 2026. Infill rents have seen a 26 percent correction from peak levels, accompanied by elevated concessions, creating more favorable conditions for tenants and owner-users.
