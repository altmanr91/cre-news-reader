Camp Mystic files for bankruptcy one year after deadly Hill Country floods

The Real Deal — Texas — Wed, 24 Jun 2026 17:08:46 +0000

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Business News

Camp Mystic, a summer camp in Texas' Hill Country, has filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy following a deadly flash flood last summer that resulted in multiple fatalities. The bankruptcy filing, which includes affiliated entities, indicates assets valued between $100,000 and $500,000 against liabilities of $10 million to $50 million. The camp faces wrongful death lawsuits and withdrew its application to reopen after an investigative committee found it lacked adequate emergency preparedness and evacuation plans, violating state law.
