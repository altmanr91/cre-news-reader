Johnson digs in against Bears stadium tax break as Arlington Heights push advances

The Real Deal — Chicago — Wed, 06 May 2026 19:34:21 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Political & Government Policy

Chicago Mayor Brandon Johnson is opposing a proposed property tax break for the Chicago Bears' potential domed stadium in Arlington Heights, arguing it would divert public resources to a wealthy franchise at the expense of city residents and workers. He views the subsidy as a 'giveaway to billionaires' and is leveraging his political influence to block the 'megaprojects bill' in the Illinois Senate. Johnson contends that subsidizing a suburban stadium would undermine economic opportunities for Chicago workers, particularly minority contractors, and reiterated his interest in keeping the team within the city, despite the Bears' focus on suburban and out-of-state options.
