Hidden costs besiege Chicago’s Frank Lloyd Wright homes

SALE

The Real Deal — Chicago — Mon, 04 May 2026 11:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

The article highlights the unique and often challenging market for Frank Lloyd Wright-designed homes in the Chicago area, where well-maintained properties command premium prices while those with deferred maintenance face significant restoration costs. The Hills-DeCaro House in Oak Park recently sold for $2.3 million, exceeding its asking price, demonstrating strong demand for turnkey historic homes. In contrast, the J.J. Walser Jr. House in Chicago's Austin neighborhood, now owned by Fannie Mae after foreclosure, is valued at only $65,000 due to severe disrepair, with restoration costs estimated at over $2 million. This disparity underscores the high cost of preserving Wright's experimental architecture, which often features structural quirks and non-standard materials, making repairs complex and expensive.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The market for historic homes, especially Frank Lloyd Wright-designed properties, has 'exploded' in recent years. Well-maintained Wright homes command a premium, while those with deferred maintenance can require restoration costs double or more than the purchase price. The Wright-designed Winslow House in River Forest sold for $2.2 million in 2024. The J.J. Walser Jr. House in Chicago's Austin neighborhood is valued at $65,000, with an estimated $2 million needed for restoration. Wright's homes are known for structural quirks, leaky roofs, and unorthodox construction methods, making repairs difficult and expensive.
