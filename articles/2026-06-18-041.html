Dishelved San Jose site proposed for offices, then housing, falls into default

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 21:45:00 +0000

SUMMARY

REO / Foreclosure

A blighted commercial site in San Jose, previously slated for office and then residential development, has fallen into default on a $6.7 million loan. The property, which was acquired in 2018, had a 75-unit apartment project with ground-floor retail proposed in 2024, and was subsequently listed for sale. The site's deteriorating condition has drawn the attention of city code enforcement.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Commercial property defaults in San Jose have been increasing, particularly in the hotel sector. The 52-room Fontaine Inn in east San Jose defaulted on a $6.5 million loan, and a 204-room Motel 6 and Super 8 by Wyndham complex nearby is in default on a $21.7 million loan. The La Quinta Inn & Suites in San Jose also defaulted on a $16 million loan last month.
