Lending Reaches 5-Year High As Alternative Funds Flood In

Bisnow News Feed — Mon, 11 May 2026 11:41:03 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / Lending Report

Commercial real estate lending reached a five-year high in the first quarter of 2026, driven by a significant increase in capital from alternative lenders, particularly debt funds and mortgage REITs. These alternative sources accounted for 53% of nonagency loan closings, a substantial rise from the previous year, indicating improved market sentiment and stronger lending momentum. While banks and CMBS lenders saw their market share decline, overall acquisition activity is increasing, leading to more disciplined yet healthy lending conditions. The average loan size grew, and mortgage rates and spreads decreased, suggesting a slightly less conservative lending approach.
