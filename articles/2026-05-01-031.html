Blackstone hits Ashland Greene with $177M foreclosure on DFW apartment portfolio

LOAN

Texas - The Real Deal — Thu, 30 Apr 2026 15:38:41 +0000

SUMMARY

Blackstone has initiated foreclosure proceedings on a four-property multifamily portfolio in Dallas-Fort Worth owned by Ashland Greene, citing a default on a $177 million loan. The portfolio, totaling 1,530 units, includes properties in Arlington, Dallas, Irving, and Mesquite. Ashland Greene had utilized a "traveling" housing finance corporation program to secure property tax exemptions for three of the properties, a loophole that has since been closed by state legislation, although the firm claims its exemptions remain intact. The borrower is reportedly in discussions with Blackstone to modify the loan, with a foreclosure auction scheduled if an agreement is not reached.

Ashland Greene acquired the properties in January 2022.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

House Bill 21, signed in May 2025, closed a loophole allowing property tax exemptions for certain housing finance corporations, leading to distress among operators as appraisal districts began revoking exemptions. The Sun Belt apartment market saw peak prices in Q3-Q4 2021 and Q1-Q2 2022, with subsequent challenges from rising interest rates and new supply.
