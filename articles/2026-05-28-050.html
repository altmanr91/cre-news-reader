Charlotte Locals Speak Out Against Data Centers As City Moves Toward Moratorium Vote

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 27 May 2026 10:27:36 -0400

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

Charlotte residents are strongly advocating for a moratorium on new data center developments, citing concerns over environmental impact and resource strain. During a public hearing, citizens expressed anxieties about excessive water and power consumption by data centers, particularly in light of ongoing drought conditions and water restrictions in the city. While some argue for the economic benefits and tax revenue generated by these facilities, the overwhelming sentiment from the community is a call for a pause to reassess the long-term sustainability and planning for such projects.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Communities across the U.S., including the Carolinas, are experiencing backlash against data center developments, leading to an increase in moratoriums. Durham, Apex, and Chatham County have all recently passed moratoriums ranging from 60 days to one year.
