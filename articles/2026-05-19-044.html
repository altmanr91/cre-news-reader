Landlords Agree To Pay $218M To Settle Price-Fixing Claims In RealPage Antitrust Case

Bisnow News Feed — Mon, 18 May 2026 13:02:25 -0400

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Eleven major real estate firms have agreed to pay a combined $218 million to settle price-fixing claims in an antitrust class-action lawsuit against RealPage. The settlement, filed in the U.S. District Court for the Middle District of Tennessee, requires the landlords to cease using nonpublic rental data from RealPage's rent-setting software, formerly known as Yieldstar, and to stop using the software to set prices for their rental apartments. This agreement follows a previous $141.8 million settlement with 27 other firms and a separate settlement by RealPage with federal prosecutors, which mandated changes to its software's rent suggestion methods.
