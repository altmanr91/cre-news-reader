Marcus & Millichap: A bright future still ahead for Detroit’s industrial sector

REJournals — Fri, 22 May 2026 10:05:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Industrial Outlook

Detroit's industrial market is currently facing short-term challenges, including elevated vacancies and softer pricing, but is buoyed by its strong manufacturing heritage and significant corporate investments. Major manufacturers are injecting capital into advanced manufacturing and battery production, creating new opportunities despite ongoing restructuring in the auto industry. The Interstate-96 Corridor is a standout performer, experiencing robust tenant demand and investor interest, while a projected slowdown in new construction is expected to help stabilize the market. Despite a forecasted decline in average asking rents for 2026, the market is anticipated to maintain one of the highest industrial rent averages in the Midwest, positioning it for long-term stability and recovery.

DATA POINTS

Forecasted industrial vacancy rate (end of 2026): 5.3%

Projected industrial inventory growth (2026): 0.2%

Forecasted average asking industrial rent decline (2026): 2.4%

Projected average industrial rent (2026): $7.40 per square foot

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Interstate-96 Corridor in Detroit is outperforming the broader market, with tenant demand more than doubling the activity levels seen elsewhere. Investor interest is concentrated in Troy and around Detroit Metropolitan Airport. Oakland County is expected to account for much of the new industrial supply reaching the market in 2026.
