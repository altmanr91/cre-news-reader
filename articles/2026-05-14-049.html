Google, SpaceX In Talks To Launch Orbital Data Centers As Space Race Accelerates

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 13 May 2026 17:17:45 -0400

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Business News

Google and SpaceX are reportedly in discussions for a rocket-launch deal to deploy Google's Project Suncatcher, an AI data center in low Earth orbit. This potential partnership highlights the accelerating race to establish space-based AI infrastructure, driven by the promise of uninterrupted solar power. The emerging sector faces significant challenges, primarily high launch costs and limited rocket availability, which companies like Cowboy Space and Blue Origin are also attempting to address through integrated launch and data center systems or large satellite constellations. NASA's engagement with these proposals, despite raising concerns, indicates the agency views orbital data centers as a credible near-term possibility.
