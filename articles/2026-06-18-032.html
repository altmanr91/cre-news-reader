Brandon Johnson floats $425M taxpayer assist for Related Midwest’s The 78

The Real Deal — Chicago — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 21:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

Chicago Mayor Brandon Johnson has proposed a $425 million tax increment financing (TIF) package to fund infrastructure improvements at The 78, a 62-acre megadevelopment site in the South Loop. This public subsidy is intended to facilitate the construction of a $750 million, 22,000-seat soccer stadium for the Chicago Fire, which is planned as a privately financed project. The TIF funds would also prepare the broader site for future mixed-use development, including a significant portion allocated for a 1,200-space underground parking garage deemed crucial for the site's challenging topography and as a foundation for future high-rise construction.
