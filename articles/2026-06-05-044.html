Game On: Family-Friendly Entertainment Grows Retail

Connect CRE — Fri, 05 Jun 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Family-friendly entertainment destinations are increasingly occupying formerly vacant retail spaces nationwide, driven by a split in consumer spending habits. While higher-income households still seek premium experiences, budget-conscious families are opting for more affordable, local entertainment options. This trend is leading to the conversion of obsolete retail buildings into venues like indoor fun parks and roller skating rinks, with significant growth in categories such as kid zones, game show studios, and escape rooms. The rise of location-based entertainment is solidifying its role as a major player in retail real estate, with foot traffic exceeding pre-pandemic levels and a substantial pipeline of new entertainment square footage planned.

DATA POINTS

Foot traffic across 20 tracked entertainment concepts (2025): 217 million visits

Foot traffic increase (2025 vs. 2019): 12% above 2019 levels

Average dwell times per visit: 140 minutes

Kid zone locations in pipeline: 355

Kid zones' share of planned entertainment square footage: 61%

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Location-based entertainment is solidifying as a major player in retail real estate, with American families seeking closer-to-home and lower-cost activities. This trend is leading to the conversion of large vacant retail boxes, such as former theaters and department stores, into viable targets for entertainment expansion.
