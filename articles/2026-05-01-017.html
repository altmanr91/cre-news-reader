Brookfield affiliate abandons Virginia data center over administrative snafu

National - The Real Deal — Thu, 30 Apr 2026 20:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Development / Regulatory Issue

A Brookfield affiliate, Compass Datacenters, has withdrawn from a significant data center development in Prince William County, Virginia, following a legal dispute over improper public notices for a zoning approval meeting. The project, which was to span over 800 acres, faced strong public opposition and regulatory hurdles, ultimately leading to a judge invalidating the zoning approvals. While Compass will not appeal the ruling and is reportedly exiting Northern Virginia, co-developer QTS, backed by Blackstone, is expected to appeal, potentially keeping a portion of the project alive.
