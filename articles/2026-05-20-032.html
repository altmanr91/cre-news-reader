Cleveland Unveils 350-Acre Industrial Redevelopment Project

DEVELOPMENT

Connect CRE — Tue, 19 May 2026 20:38:42 +0000

SUMMARY

The City of Cleveland has unveiled "The Midline," a significant 350-acre industrial redevelopment project on the city's near east side. This initiative aims to transform long-vacant industrial land into a connected employment district, featuring new trails, parks, and enhanced transit access. The project, spearheaded by The Site Readiness for Good Jobs Fund, will consolidate fragmented parcels to create a unified district designed to attract large-scale employers in manufacturing, R&D, office, and service sectors. The master plan envisions at least 1.5 million square feet of new industrial and commercial space, projected to create over 2,500 direct jobs and generate up to $100 million in annual tax revenue for the city upon full build-out.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: industrial

Property Name: The Midline

Size: 1,500,000 SF

Site Area: 350 acres

Notable Features: Includes new trails, parks, and transit access; designed to attract large-scale employers across manufacturing, research and development, office and supporting service sectors.

Project Notes: The project is a district-wide strategy rather than a single-user project, driven by The Site Readiness for Good Jobs Fund (SRF).
