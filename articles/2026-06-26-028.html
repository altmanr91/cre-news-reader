1903 Frank Lloyd Wright registered landmark repairs will cost millions

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Chicago — Thu, 25 Jun 2026 16:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

A Frank Lloyd Wright-designed home on Chicago's West Side, known as the Walser House, is slated for a $3.5 million restoration after falling into disrepair. The property was recently acquired by Austin Coming Together, a nonprofit focused on community development, from Community Initiatives for $125,000. The extensive renovation will address significant damage, including the roof, windows, and moisture issues, with a commitment to preserving the home's original Prairie School design as a Chicago landmark and National Register of Historic Places property.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: single family

Property Name: Walser House

Address: 42 North Central Avenue, Chicago, IL

Sale Price: $125K

Total Project Cost: $3.5M

Year Built: 1903

Notable Features: Frank Lloyd Wright-designed home, Chicago landmark, part of the National Register of Historic Places, built in the Prairie School design style.

Project Notes: The acquisition price included $60,000 from the city's Troubled Buildings Initiative. The building has been vacant since 2019 and requires substantial repairs to the roof, windows, and interior due to moisture damage. The restoration must adhere to the original Prairie School design.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: Austin Coming Together — Darnell Shields, executive director

SELLER: Community Initiatives
