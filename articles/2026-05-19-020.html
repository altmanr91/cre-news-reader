Naftali, Blavatnik snag $374M refi for Williamsburg Wharf

REFINANCE

The Real Deal — New York — Mon, 18 May 2026 18:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Naftali Group and Access Industries, led by Miki Naftali and Len Blavatnik respectively, have secured a $374 million refinancing package from Barings for the first phase of their Williamsburg Wharf master-planned development. This new financing will retire existing debt from Bank OZK. The initial phase of the project, which includes 89 condominiums, over 500 rental apartments, and 15,000 square feet of retail, has seen strong market reception, with the condo units over 70 percent sold and the rental units over 90 percent occupied.

Notable Features: Master-planned development with nearly 525 feet of water frontage, featuring a rooftop pool deck, private outdoor ice-skating rink, and an indoor-outdoor fitness center.

Project Notes: The first phase includes 89 condos and over 500 apartments. The condo portion is over 70% sold, and the rental portion is over 90% occupied. The refinancing retires a previous $310 million senior loan from Bank OZK and $75 million in mezzanine financing from Barings, along with an additional $238 million loan from Bank OZK.
