Starwood freezes SREIT redemptions as liquidity crunch deepens

The Real Deal — National — Fri, 01 May 2026 19:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Starwood Capital Group has temporarily suspended redemptions from its $22 billion Starwood Real Estate Income Trust (SREIT) due to a surge in withdrawal requests. The firm's chairman, Barry Sternlicht, stated that selling assets in the current soft pricing environment, characterized by high interest rates and uneven demand, would mean unloading high-quality properties at a discount. This move is a defensive measure to preserve the fund's value, even as the REIT's net asset value per share has declined by 6 percent over the past year. The fund has also reduced its annualized distribution rate for Class I shares to 4.7 percent from 6.3 percent to conserve cash.
