Loan defaults pile up at hotels along stretch of San Jose thoroughfare

REO

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Fri, 05 Jun 2026 22:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

The Fontaine Inn, a 52-room hotel in San Jose, has defaulted on a $6.5 million loan from State Bank of Texas. This default is part of a broader trend of distress in the Bay Area's lodging market, with several other hotels in the region, including a nearby 204-room complex and another Super 8 by Wyndham in Livermore, also facing loan defaults or foreclosure. The ongoing challenges highlight the difficult operating environment for hotels in the area.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

A 204-room hotel complex at 2560 Fontaine Road in San Jose is in default on a $21.7 million loan. A Super 8 by Wyndham in Livermore defaulted on a $7.7 million State Bank of Texas loan in 2022. The 541-room Signia by Hilton hotel in San Jose was taken back by its lender in 2025. The 59-room La Quinta Inn & Suites in San Jose defaulted on a $16 million Central Valley Community Bank loan in May 2026. KSL Capital Partners purchased the Toll House Hotel in Los Gatos through a deed-in-lieu of foreclosure for an unpaid debt of $30 million.
