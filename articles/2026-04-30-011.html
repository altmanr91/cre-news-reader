McHugh Construction celebrates groundbreaking of 80-unit multifamily project in Chicago’s South Loop

CONSTRUCTION

REJournals — Thu, 30 Apr 2026 17:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

McHugh Construction and Powers & Sons Construction have broken ground on Southbridge 1C, an 80-unit multifamily project in Chicago's South Loop. This 12-story building, developed by The Community Builders, will offer a mix of market-rate and affordable rental units, along with ground-floor retail. The project is a significant phase in the redevelopment of the former Harold Ickes Homes, aiming to provide high-quality housing and support long-term neighborhood investment.

Notable Features: Adjacent to Cermak-McCormick Place CTA Green Line station; includes 1,500-square-foot retail space; 12th-floor amenities include fitness area and outdoor terrace.

Project Notes: The project will receive $11.9 million in Tax Increment Financing (TIF) assistance. 44 units (55%) will be affordable, with 15 for families earning 30-80% AMI and 29 for CHA households. The construction cost is approximately $36.5 million.
