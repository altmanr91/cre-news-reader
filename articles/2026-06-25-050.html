Korean Skincare Retailer Moves Forward With U.S. Expansion

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 24 Jun 2026 12:07:11 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / Retail Expansion

South Korean skincare retailer Olive Young has initiated its U.S. expansion with the opening of two new stores in the Los Angeles area, marking its first physical locations outside of South Korea. These openings in Pasadena and Westfield Century City are part of a broader strategy to aggressively grow its brick-and-mortar presence across California and eventually nationwide. The company has also established a 39,000 SF distribution warehouse in Bloomington, California, to support its North American logistics and plans for a second East Coast hub later this year. This retail rollout is complemented by a new U.S. online market and a partnership to launch K-beauty sections within Sephora stores globally, aiming for 90% of sales from overseas customers within a decade.
