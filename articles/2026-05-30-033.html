Big oil spike, little difference for Houston resi

The Real Deal — Texas — Fri, 29 May 2026 19:27:51 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Despite a significant spike in oil prices due to the Iran war, Houston's luxury residential market is unlikely to see a substantial impact, according to local real estate agents and economists. While middle-class oil and gas employees may receive large bonuses that could boost home sales in lower and middle-tier neighborhoods, the broader economic diversification of Houston and a more cautious approach by energy companies to capital expenditure mean that the city's high-end housing market is less sensitive to oil price fluctuations than in previous decades. Economists note that the industry's past experiences with busts have led to a reduction in speculative drilling and hiring, limiting the ripple effect on the wider economy and, consequently, on luxury real estate.
