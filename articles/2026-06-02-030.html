Panoramic joins Bay Area school-to-resi pipeline with Berkeley project

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 21:56:23 +0000

SUMMARY

Panoramic Interests has filed permits to redevelop a former school campus at 1130 Oxford Street in Berkeley into a residential community. The project, which plans for 32 three-story homes averaging 1,500 square feet each, seeks to utilize California Senate Bills 330 and 1123 to streamline the approval process. This development aligns with a broader Bay Area trend of converting underused school sites into housing to address local housing shortages.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: single family

Address: 1130 Oxford Street, Berkeley, CA

Units: 32

Notable Features: Each home will be three stories with a single-car garage.

Project Notes: The project utilizes Senate Bill 330 and Senate Bill 1123 for streamlined and ministerial approval. The site was formerly Oxford Elementary School, built in 1965 and remodeled three decades ago, with the school relocating in 2020.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Panoramic Interests

SPONSOR PIPELINE

2050 Kammerer Avenue, San Jose: 78-unit workforce housing project for Alum Rock Union Elementary School District teachers and staff, with half for lower-income households and the rest at below-market-rate rents.

760 Hillsdale Avenue, San Jose: 288-unit workforce housing complex on vacant land next to the Silicon Valley Education Campus.

5670 Camden Avenue, San Jose: 108 condominium units on Little League fields next to a school.

1314 Le Borgne Avenue, Baldwin Park: 104 townhomes at the former Florence Flanner School.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The City of Berkeley is mandated to plan for 8,934 new housing units by 2031 as part of state housing targets.
