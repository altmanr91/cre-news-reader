Dallas secures $19M worth of incentives for potential Morgan Stanley move

The Real Deal — Texas — Thu, 25 Jun 2026 17:15:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The Dallas City Council has approved an $18.5 million incentive package to support Morgan Stanley's potential relocation to Dallas. The financial services firm plans to initially occupy 255,000 square feet in Downtown Dallas before moving into a new 700,000-square-foot building at 2401 McKinney Avenue in Uptown by 2031. This move, which could bring 3,800 employees, is part of a larger $1.3 billion development by Trammell Crow Company, with Morgan Stanley committing $700 million towards its new offices. The project aims to solidify Dallas-Fort Worth as a major financial hub, though Morgan Stanley has not yet formally announced its decision.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Downtown Dallas is experiencing a decline in tenants, with AT&T vacating 2 million square feet (6% of downtown office space) when it moves to Plano. Uptown Dallas, however, is booming with new businesses and financial institutions, including the upcoming Texas stock exchange.
