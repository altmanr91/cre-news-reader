Arizona’s largest home sells for $9M after two years under contract

SALE

The Real Deal — National — Tue, 02 Jun 2026 14:29:39 +0000

SUMMARY

The largest private residence in Arizona, a 52,000-square-foot mansion on Sugar Loaf Mountain, has sold for $9 million to a Florida investor. The buyer plans extensive renovations totaling $40 million and intends to rename the estate. The property, which includes a main house and a guest house on a 6-acre lot, was previously owned by Jamie Hormel, wife of the late George "Geordie" Hormel.

DATA POINTS

Notable Features: The estate includes a 2,900-square-foot guest house, an Olympic-sized swimming pool, and dual locker rooms, and was once ranked as the 13th largest residence in the United States.

Project Notes: The buyer plans to invest $40 million in renovations. The property was originally known as McClure Mansion and was listed for $16 million in June 2022 before going under contract two and a half years ago.
