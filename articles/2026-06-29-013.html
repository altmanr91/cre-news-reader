Even as retail spending hits new heights, consumers are more selective with what they’re buying

REJournals — Mon, 29 Jun 2026 09:51:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Retail Report

Colliers' May U.S. Retail Foot Traffic & Sales Analysis indicates that while overall retail sales increased by 5.2% year-over-year in May, much of this growth was driven by inflation, particularly in gas station sales. Consumers are becoming more selective, prioritizing value-oriented purchases, apparel, and experiences like theaters and music venues, which saw significant foot traffic increases. Conversely, spending on larger discretionary items like furniture and home improvement, as well as visits to fast-food restaurants and banks, saw more modest gains or declines, reflecting increasing financial pressure on households.
