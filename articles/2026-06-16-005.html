Stockdale’s Andrew Saba On Medical Real Estate’s Supply-Demand Imbalance

Commercial Observer — Mon, 15 Jun 2026 20:40:46 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Stockdale Capital Partners, a Los Angeles-based firm, is actively expanding its healthcare real estate portfolio through two distinct funds: an open-ended healthcare fund targeting stabilized assets and an opportunistic fund focused on buy-to-suit or office-to-medical conversions. The firm's strategy is driven by the inelastic demand for healthcare services, an aging U.S. population, and a significant supply-demand imbalance in medical outpatient facilities. Rising construction costs and labor expenses have curtailed new supply, leading to increased competition for existing properties, strong occupancy rates in the mid-90s, high tenant retention, and robust rent growth in the sector. Stockdale is primarily focused on the top 50 metro areas, with a preference for Sun Belt and West/Southwest U.S. markets experiencing patient migration.

DATA POINTS

Healthcare spend after age 65: 60%

U.S. outpatient medical occupancy: mid-90s%

U.S. outpatient medical tenant retention: upper 80s-low 90s%

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The U.S. population is aging, with the peak expected over the next four to six years, driving continued demand for outpatient medical space. Construction deliveries for medical facilities have decreased over the last eight to twelve quarters due to rising material and labor costs. Office-to-medical conversions are generally not sufficient to meet the needs of large office projects (e.g., 400,000-500,000+ SF buildings).
