New York’s Renewed Fight Against Property Deed Theft Confronts Key Challenges

Commercial Observer — Mon, 04 May 2026 10:20:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Political & Crime News

Deed theft, formally illegal in New York state only since 2023, remains a persistent and growing problem for single-family homeowners in New York City, exacerbated by the hot housing market and difficulties in prosecution. Despite new laws in 2023 and 2024 aimed at curbing the trend, complaints surged to 517 in 2025, up from 149 in 2023. Experts note that fraudsters continually adapt to new regulations, making it a challenging issue to resolve. The lack of public transparency regarding beneficial owners of shell corporations, despite the LLC Transparency Act, further complicates efforts to combat these scams. Recent legislative efforts have focused on pausing evictions and foreclosures related to fraudulent deeds, voiding such deeds, and extending the statute of limitations for criminal charges.
