More investment sales in Cincinnati’s retail market? That’s what Marcus & Millichap predicts

REJournals — Thu, 14 May 2026 10:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Retail Forecast

Marcus & Millichap's 2026 retail Investment Forecast predicts a busier year for investment sales in Cincinnati's retail market, driven by the strength of downtown Cincinnati, limited new retail supply, and attractive lower sales prices with higher cap rates. Despite a moderate recovery expected after negative net absorption and rent dips in 2025, the metro area's vacancy rate is projected to reach 6.5% by year-end 2026 due to new supply outpacing demand. However, the report highlights strong retail sectors in Butler County and Northern Kentucky, with increased capital expected for single-tenant acquisitions.

DATA POINTS

Projected metro area asking rent growth 2026: $14.50 per square foot

Projected metro area vacancy rate end of 2026: 6.5%

Projected new retail space deliveries 2026: 450,000 square feet

Increase in trading activity last year: 33%

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Downtown Cincinnati's multi-tenant segment has one of the metropolitan area's lowest vacancy and highest rent areas. The downtown area's construction pipeline accounts for less than 1% of new retail supply in the region. The Northern Kentucky submarket saw a year-over-year drop in vacancy of nearly 120 basis points. Cincinnati ranks among the lowest-priced major metros with high cap rates.
