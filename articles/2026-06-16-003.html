In the Miami-Area Condo Market, There Are Two Narratives

Commercial Observer — Tue, 16 Jun 2026 11:00:39 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The South Florida condo market is experiencing a bifurcated trend: a robust luxury segment driven by an influx of high-net-worth individuals and corporate relocations, contrasted with a struggling lower end facing pressure from new structural repair mandates. Developers of high-end projects, such as the Four Seasons-branded condo in Coconut Grove and the Nobu-branded tower in Brickell, are seeing strong pre-sales and securing significant construction financing, indicating sustained demand for premium products. However, the market for older, more affordable condos is softening due to costly special assessments mandated by post-Surfside reforms, leading to increased inventory and longer selling periods. This dynamic highlights the importance of product differentiation and amenity-rich offerings even within the luxury sector, as buyers are discerning despite high demand.

DATA POINTS

Florida overall condo inventory increase since 2023: Active listings more than doubled to 68,757 units

Median closing period increase for Florida condos: From 71 days to 111 days

Median sale price change for South Florida condos (past year): Fallen by 1.5 percent to $310,000

Millionaire population surge in Miami (2014-2024): 94 percent

Millionaire population surge in West Palm Beach (2014-2024): 112 percent

South Florida transactions above $10 million (Q1 2026 YoY): Doubled

South Florida transactions above $30 million (Q1 2026 YoY): Doubled

Preconstruction units in South Florida above $2 million: 60 percent of 35,000 units

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Florida's 2022 legislative reforms mandate structural health reports and reserve funding for condo buildings at least three stories tall and 30+ years old, impacting the older condo stock. Previously, only Miami-Dade and Broward counties required recertification, and boards often delayed or waived renovations. The median sale price of a South Florida condo has fallen by 1.5 percent in the past year to $310,000. The millionaire population of Miami and West Palm Beach surged by 94% and 112% respectively between 2014 and 2024. Transactions in South Florida above $10 million and $30 million both doubled year-over-year in Q1 2026.
