Russian Hill manse with Gap connections goes quick at $17M

SALE

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Fri, 29 May 2026 20:01:10 +0000

SUMMARY

A Russian Hill mansion with historical ties to the Gap retail empire has sold for nearly $17.3 million, closing less than a month after being listed. The property, formerly owned by developer Robert Sydney Fisher, brother of Gap founder Donald Fisher, was sold by his children. This transaction highlights the robust luxury housing market in San Francisco, driven by the tech boom and a scarcity of high-end properties.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: single family

Address: 888 Francisco Street, San Francisco, CA

Size: 6,000 SF

Sale Price: $17.3M

$/SF: $2,883/SF (calculated)

Year Built: 1998

Notable Features: The home features three bedrooms, four full bathrooms, two half-baths, a wine cellar, a butler’s bar, a powder room, an elevator, and multiple entertaining spaces with views of Alcatraz Island and the Marin Headlands. It was designed by architect Sandy Walker.

Project Notes: The property was previously owned by developer Robert Sydney Fisher, brother of late Gap founder Donald Fisher. The existing house on the lot was torn down before the current home was built in 1998.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The San Francisco luxury housing market is experiencing a surge in activity, with a growing list of eight-figure deals, driven by the artificial intelligence boom and an influx of affluent buyers, leading to a 'mansion shortage' in the city. Other recent high-value sales include 2626 Larkin Street for $24 million, 2830 Pacific Avenue for $27.5 million, and the off-market sale of 2898 Vallejo Street for $56 million.
