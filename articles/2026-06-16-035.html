San Francisco’s mansion shortage claims two more trophy homes

SALE

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Mon, 15 Jun 2026 23:45:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Two trophy homes in San Francisco's Presidio Heights neighborhood recently traded for a combined $32 million, highlighting the city's tightening luxury housing market. The transactions included the $21 million sale of a five-bedroom mansion by Richard and Monene Bradley to 3501 Jackson LLC, and the $11 million purchase of another five-bedroom mansion by tech executive Woodrow Levin and his wife Gretchen from David Brailer and Matthew Rhoa. These sales contribute to a trend of increasing luxury home transactions in the area, fueled by wealth generated from the artificial intelligence industry.
