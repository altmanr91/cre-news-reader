Housing champion vs. Pelosi’s pick for SF seat in Congress

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Wed, 03 Jun 2026 14:26:20 +0000

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Political & Crime News

State Sen. Scott Wiener, a prominent advocate for increased housing construction, secured the top position in the primary election to represent San Francisco in Congress, advancing to a November runoff against Supervisor Connie Chan. Wiener's campaign, significantly supported by the real estate industry, champions the YIMBY (Yes In My Backyard) philosophy, asserting that housing shortages are the root cause of affordability crises. His platform includes a robust housing plan, proposing federal incentives of $10,000 per housing unit built by cities, funded by rolling back Republican tax cuts. Chan, while also a Democrat, offers a contrasting view on housing, expressing skepticism about the "all housing is good housing" argument favored by Wiener.

DATA POINTS
