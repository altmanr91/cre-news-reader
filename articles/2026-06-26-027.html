601W’s bad week gets worse with $43M foreclosure on Tesla-leased Chicago building

FORECLOSURE

The Real Deal — Chicago — Thu, 25 Jun 2026 17:57:14 +0000

SUMMARY

Washington Capital has filed a $42.6 million foreclosure lawsuit against 601W Companies, alleging default on a commercial loan for a Tesla-leased service center at 717 South Desplaines Street in Chicago. The loan, originated in July 2023, matured on May 1, 2026, and was used to finance construction costs for Tesla's 116,000-square-foot service and showroom facility. This foreclosure adds to a series of financial challenges for 601W, including defaults on other significant office property loans in Chicago.

The loan provided financing for construction costs tied to the tenant's lease, including a $1.2 million tenant improvement allowance and a $9.3 million approved project budget to be funded by 601W.
