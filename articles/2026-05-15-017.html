Inspector general probes ICE detention center deals

The Real Deal — National — Thu, 14 May 2026 19:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

The Department of Homeland Security's inspector general has launched an investigation into a $38 billion program initiated by former Secretary Kristi Noem, which involved the acquisition of warehouses for conversion into ICE detention centers. The probe will audit all warehouse purchases made by the agency under Noem's leadership, with approximately $1 billion already spent on nine facilities. The program has faced criticism due to zoning issues and the unsuitability of warehouses for detention center infrastructure, leading to complications in securing basic necessities like plumbing. The program was already paused by current Secretary Markwayne Mullin, and the investigation follows prior scrutiny of DHS contracts and alleged misuse of funds.
