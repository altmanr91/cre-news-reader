Quarterra offloads distressed Katy apartment complex

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Texas — Wed, 13 May 2026 14:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Atlas Real Estate Partners has acquired The Maddox, a 326-unit apartment complex in Katy, TX, from Quarterra, a subsidiary of Lennar. The property was purchased at a significant discount to replacement cost, reflecting the current market conditions for distressed multifamily assets. Atlas plans to renovate the exterior and amenities of the 2019-built complex, anticipating that current tenant concessions will diminish as demand in the desirable Katy submarket continues to grow.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Property Name: The Maddox

Address: 1330 Park West Green Drive, Katy, TX

Units: 326

Year Built: 2019

Notable Features: Five-building, three-story complex with good school district strength and freeway access.

Project Notes: Property was offering up to six weeks of free rent to new tenants at the time of sale.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: Atlas Real Estate Partners — Arvind Chary, Co-founder

SELLER: Quarterra

SELLER BROKER: Newmark

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Distressed multifamily properties have contributed to a surge in loans flagged for foreclosure, particularly for Houston apartments acquired in 2021 and 2022. Lennar recently sold its majority stake in Quarterra to TPG after Quarterra experienced losses due to rising construction costs and oversupply in the Sun Belt. KKR previously acquired over 5,200 units across 18 complexes from Quarterra for $2.1 billion.
