Tanger’s Justin Stein On Leveraging Data and Gnoshes to Grow a Retail Portfolio

Commercial Observer — Tue, 26 May 2026 19:05:18 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Tanger, a prominent REIT specializing in open-air outlet and lifestyle centers, is experiencing a strong resurgence in retail, marked by 97% occupancy and 3.4 million square feet of leases executed over the past year. The company is actively pursuing growth through ground-up development, acquisitions, and strategic re-merchandising of its existing 41-center portfolio. A key part of their strategy involves diversifying beyond traditional apparel and footwear to include more food, beverage, entertainment, home, and health and wellness offerings, which has increased dwell time and sales. Tanger is leveraging data analytics to inform its leasing decisions and attract new brands, with many retailers planning their real estate needs several years in advance due to limited new supply in the market.

DATA POINTS

Leases executed (trailing 12 months): 3.4 million square feet

Number of centers: 41

Number of stores: 3,000+

Footwear and apparel tenant mix (current): 70%

Footwear and apparel tenant mix (historical): 80%

SPONSOR PIPELINE

Nashville: Ground-up development opened in 2023

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

There has been a lack of new retail supply added around the country, leading brands to consider markets they historically may not have, such as Huntsville, AL, and Little Rock, AR.
