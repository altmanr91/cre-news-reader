Brookfield snaps up South Bay data center for $90M, adding to growing South Bay collection

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Fri, 15 May 2026 19:04:51 +0000

SUMMARY

Brookfield Properties has acquired a 120,000-square-foot data center in Sunnyvale, CA, for $90.3 million from DivcoWest. The property, located at 255 Caspian Drive, is fully leased to data center operator Equinix. This acquisition further expands Brookfield's growing portfolio of data centers in the South Bay, reflecting the increasing demand for AI-serving infrastructure in the region.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The South Bay is a hub for data center development, with San Jose identified as a hotspot. Prologis plans 1.7 million square feet of data centers across four buildings in San Jose, and Goodman Group plans two data centers totaling 414,000 square feet. Menlo Equities is pivoting a previously approved 400,000-square-foot office and life sciences campus at 888–894 Ross Drive in Sunnyvale to a 200,000-square-foot data center with an adjacent PG&E substation.
