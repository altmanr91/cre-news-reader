Evolution never stops at Minneapolis’ 9th Street Center

LEASE

REJournals — Mon, 22 Jun 2026 10:04:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Hillcrest Development has secured two new tenants, Wyldwolf Games and Midwest Indoor Soccer, for its 9th Street Center in Minneapolis, continuing the property's transformation from an industrial site into a mixed-use experiential destination. Wyldwolf Games, a tabletop gaming retailer, will occupy 1,945 square feet, while Midwest Indoor Soccer will take approximately 28,000 square feet for two indoor soccer fields and youth programs. The additions reflect Hillcrest Development's strategy to curate a diverse tenant mix that includes retail, recreation, and community-focused businesses, leveraging the property's unique physical characteristics and central location.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: mixed-use

Property Name: 9th Street Center

Address: 9th Street, Minneapolis, MN

Size: 224,000 SF

Notable Features: Consists of nine buildings built between 1910 and the 1950s, located in the Marcy-Holmes neighborhood near the University of Minnesota and downtown Minneapolis, offering ample parking and brick-and-timber spaces.

Project Notes: Property was acquired by Hillcrest Development in the late 1990s and has since been gradually transformed from a traditional industrial site into a diverse campus of businesses.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

There is a growing need for indoor sports facilities in the Minneapolis-St. Paul market, with a scarcity of indoor soccer facilities specifically in Minneapolis. The Twin Cities region is experiencing growing demand for experiential businesses that encourage in-person gathering.
