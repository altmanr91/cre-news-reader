JLL Sees Revenue Rise 11% As Iran Conflict Impact Remains Muted

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 30 Apr 2026 10:47:46 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / Earnings Report

JLL reported a robust first quarter, with revenue climbing 11% year-over-year to nearly $6.4 billion, driven by strong performance in its capital markets and leasing divisions. Despite global macroeconomic headwinds and the ongoing conflict in Iran, the company's adjusted EBITDA increased by 22%. While the U.S. market has shown resilience, JLL's CEO Christian Ulbrich noted that prolonged geopolitical tensions could increasingly impact the global economy, particularly in Europe, India, and other energy-dependent regions. The firm's capital markets business saw significant momentum across investment sales, debt advisory, and equity advisory, while office and industrial leasing activity benefited from increased deal size and volume, notably fueled by the artificial intelligence and financial services sectors.
