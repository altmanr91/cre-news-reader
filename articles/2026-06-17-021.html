HPD unveils fund for supportive housing preservation

The Real Deal — New York — Tue, 16 Jun 2026 20:45:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The New York City Department of Housing Preservation and Development (HPD) has launched a $1 billion Supportive Preservation Program aimed at preserving the city's existing supportive housing stock. This initiative will provide below-market loans, modify existing HPD loans, and offer residential real estate tax exemptions to encourage conventional lenders and address the financial needs of these properties. The program seeks to upgrade aging infrastructure and improve energy efficiency in buildings that have provided affordable housing and services for decades, ensuring their long-term stability and continued support for vulnerable tenants.
