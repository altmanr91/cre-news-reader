Impact Funds Lose Steam With Investors, But Study Shows Their Buildings Perform Better

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 20 May 2026 19:31:53 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Despite a record high in withdrawals from socially conscious investment funds in Q4, a new study indicates that multifamily impact investing, particularly in affordable housing, demonstrates superior financial performance and stability. The Multifamily Impact Council and NYU Stern School of Business found that strategies like rent-splitting and housing support plans increase tenant stability, reduce vacancy loss, and boost net operating income. This performance is particularly attractive in a softening rental market characterized by declining rents and rising vacancies, suggesting that impact-oriented strategies are proving to be a durable business model with both social and economic benefits.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The U.S. apartment market is currently experiencing a supply glut, leading to a 1.7% year-over-year decline in average rents in April and a 7.2% vacancy rate. New York City's pension funds plan to invest $4B in affordable homes over the next four years due to a record number of rent-burdened households. The expansion of the low-income housing tax credit is also driving investment in affordable units.
