“Floating lantern”: Kengo Kuma’s Design District project scores $85M construction loan

LOAN

The Real Deal — Miami — Tue, 02 Jun 2026 22:30:17 +0000

SUMMARY

A joint venture between Lionheart Capital, Leviathan Development, Well Duo, and the Lane Organization secured an $85 million construction loan from Monroe Capital for Mirai Design District, a mixed-use office and retail project in Miami. The development, designed by Japanese architect Kengo Kuma, will feature Class A office space, retail, restaurant, and hospitality components, along with a standalone architectural house. The project, which broke ground after a two-year delay due to design revisions and entitlements, is expected to be completed in 2028. Developers are optimistic about leasing demand for boutique office spaces in the Design District, targeting family offices, small funds, and design firms.

Notable Features: Designed by Kengo Kuma as a 'floating lantern' with a transparent ground floor, layered facade, and central garden; includes 41,000 SF of Class A office space and 16,000 SF of retail/restaurant/hospitality space, plus a 2,000 SF standalone sculptural house.

Project Notes: The development site includes 4218, 4200, and 4240 Northeast Second Avenue. Lionheart Capital acquired the remaining interest in 4218 Northeast Second Avenue in October 2023 for $5.5 million. Miami Design District Associates sold 4200 and 4240 Northeast Second Avenue to Lionheart Capital, Leviathan Development, and Well Duo in September 2025 for $20 million. The project was delayed by design revisions and a lengthy entitlement process.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Design District has nearly 1,000 residential units in the pipeline. Developers are seeing steady migration of financial firms and creative businesses to Miami, driving demand for boutique office options in the Design District over larger spaces in Wynwood and Brickell Financial District.
