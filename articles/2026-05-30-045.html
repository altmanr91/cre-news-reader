U.S. Industrial Markets Tilt Toward Landlords Despite Global Softness

Bisnow News Feed — Fri, 29 May 2026 11:54:35 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / Industrial Report

The U.S. industrial market is beginning to favor landlords, according to a new report from Cushman & Wakefield. Market conditions shifted in 16 out of 35 major U.S. markets, moving from tenant-friendly to more neutral or landlord-favorable. This trend contrasts with global industrial markets, where most are still shifting towards tenant-favorable conditions. Despite macroeconomic volatility and geopolitical events impacting leasing decisions, U.S. industrial vacancy is expected to decline further, and rents are projected to rise in over half of American markets in the coming years.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

U.S. industrial markets are experiencing a shift towards landlord-favorable conditions, contrasting with global trends. Vacancy in the Inland Empire inched up to 7.8% in Q1. U.S. industrial vacancy declined 10 basis points from its late 2025 peak of 7.0% in Q1. Total absorption over the year ending in March was up 35% from the prior year, and first-quarter leasing activity cleared 170M SF for the fourth consecutive quarter.
