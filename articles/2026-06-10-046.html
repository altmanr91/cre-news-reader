While U.S. Apartment Rents Stall, San Francisco Market Accelerates

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 09 Jun 2026 18:05:22 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

San Francisco's multifamily market is experiencing a significant reacceleration in rent growth, driven by a surge in AI-fueled hiring and a severe undersupply of housing. While national rent growth has largely flatlined due to increased supply and softer demand, San Francisco posted an 8.4% rent increase, far exceeding other major U.S. metros. The city's limited construction pipeline, with only 300 market-rate units currently underway, combined with high-wage job creation and significant development constraints, is tightening market conditions and pushing rents to historic highs. This dynamic contrasts sharply with Sun Belt markets, which are grappling with oversupply and declining rents.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

San Francisco's multifamily market is experiencing an 8.4% surge in rent growth, significantly outpacing the national average of 0.6% in Q1 2026. The city's average rent is $3,429 per month, with occupancy above 96%. This contrasts with Sun Belt markets like Austin and San Antonio, which saw rent declines of nearly 3.3% due to high vacancy rates (13-16%) and oversupply.
