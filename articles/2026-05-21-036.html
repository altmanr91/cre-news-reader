Foreclosures tick up in stubbornly expensive Austin

The Real Deal — Texas — Wed, 20 May 2026 20:49:26 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Foreclosure Report

Austin is experiencing a notable increase in home foreclosures, leading the nation last month with a 199% year-over-year spike in filings. This trend is attributed to employment issues and depreciation in home values, particularly impacting first-time homebuyers who purchased with low down payments in 2022 when prices peaked. Despite the rise, market experts suggest that foreclosures still represent a relatively small share of the overall market and reflect broader national housing trends rather than a localized crisis, as Austin remains one of the most overvalued housing markets in the country.

DATA POINTS

Foreclosure filings April 2026: 1 in every 2,000 homes

Year-over-year increase in foreclosure filings (April 2025 vs. April 2026): 199%

Peak average sale price of Austin home (2022): $625,800

Current average sale price of Austin home: $570,500

Decline in average sale price since 2022 peak: 8%

Foreclosure listings mid-March: Almost 2,500

Increase in foreclosure listings since September: 13%

Typical share of foreclosure listings in active listings (2007-2022): 0.15% to 0.50%

Average share of foreclosure listings in active listings (first five months of 2026): Approximately 1.4%

Median sale price of home in Austin proper (2025): $570,000

Median sale price of home in Austin proper (2024): $573,000

Year-over-year decline in median sale price in Austin proper: 0.6%

Median sale price in Williamson County (2025): $417,000

Year-over-year decline in median sale price in Williamson County: 2.8%

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Austin's housing market is considered one of the most overvalued in the country, trailing only Newark, NJ, and the Philadelphia area, according to Fitch Ratings. While some sub-sectors like condos, certain luxury neighborhoods, and outlying suburbs have seen significant distress, home prices within Austin city limits have largely held flat.
