Momentum building for downtown Chicago office leasing? JLL report says it’s coming

REJournals — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 10:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Forecast Report

JLL's 2026 Big Bets report forecasts a period of renewed momentum for Chicago's commercial real estate market, particularly in downtown office leasing. The report predicts a 10% increase in downtown office leasing volume this year, driven by a rising number of lease expirations and a complete absence of new office construction plans. This development slowdown is expected to lead to declining vacancy rates and potentially higher rents in top-tier and Class-A office buildings. The report also highlights the strength of Chicago's multifamily sector, with high urban apartment occupancy and significant rent growth, alongside impressive home value appreciation.

Construction jobs generated by major development projects: 14,000

Suburban office inventory decline from 2017 peak: 8.4%

Suburban annual leasing volume forecast: Exceed 4.3 million square feet

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

No new office developments are currently planned for Chicago's central business district, marking the first such pause in over a decade. Historically, similar development slowdowns have resulted in lower vacancy rates, higher rents, and reduced concession packages. Chicago's multifamily sector ranks among the strongest in the country, with urban apartment occupancy reaching a 10-year high of 96.1% in Q4 2025 and effective rents increasing 5.6% year-over-year. Chicago-area residential property values increased 5.34% in 2025, the highest growth rate among major U.S. metropolitan areas. Suburban office inventory has fallen 8.4% from its 2017 peak, with much of the space being adaptively reused for housing, warehouses, and data centers.
