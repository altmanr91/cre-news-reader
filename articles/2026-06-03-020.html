PMG’s Kevin Maloney on Billionaires’ Row supertall: a financial “disaster”

The Real Deal — New York — Tue, 02 Jun 2026 22:36:20 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Kevin Maloney, founder of Property Markets Group (PMG), described the 111 West 57th Street supertall in Manhattan as an architectural marvel but a financial "disaster" due to significant challenges. The project, developed in partnership with JDS Development, faced an 11-year development timeline, a difficult sales environment exacerbated by the pandemic, and a default on a $725 million loan in 2017 due to rising construction costs. Despite the initial struggles, recent sales activity, particularly since Sotheby's International Realty took over in July 2024, has generated $480 million in closings and contracts, helping to pay down a $200 million loan and leaving Apollo Global Management as the sole lender.
