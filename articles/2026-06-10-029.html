Yorkville data center developer seeks 18-month extension to lock up land

The Real Deal — Chicago — Tue, 09 Jun 2026 20:51:17 +0000

SUMMARY

Development

The developers of Project Cardinal, a large-scale data center in Yorkville, IL, are seeking an 18-month extension to acquire all necessary land parcels for the project. Pioneer Development and Matt McCarron requested to push the deadline from July 1 to December 31, 2027, citing "unforeseen circumstances" in securing the land. The city's $51 million incentive payment to the developer is contingent on the successful acquisition of all parcels, and failure to meet the extended deadline would void the annexation agreement crucial for the project. This development is part of a broader trend in Yorkville, which has seen several data center proposals approved despite local opposition and ongoing litigation from residents concerned about environmental impact, traffic, and property values.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Yorkville was considering approximately a dozen data center proposals across 3,000 acres simultaneously. Three data center projects have been approved in Yorkville: Project Cardinal, Project Steel, and the 228-acre Cyrus One development.
