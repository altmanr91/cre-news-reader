Housing Notes: IPhones replace boomer vices, but not drive for home buying

The Real Deal — National — Thu, 21 May 2026 20:02:09 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This opinion piece by Jonathan Miller explores the generational differences in housing market motivations, noting that while younger generations are more financially prudent than their Boomer predecessors, they face unprecedented housing affordability challenges. Miller argues that the Federal Reserve's prolonged low interest rates depleted housing inventory, leading to continued price increases despite higher mortgage rates. He proposes several macro-level solutions to address affordability, including zoning reform to increase density, legalizing accessory dwelling units, streamlining permitting processes, utilizing public land and air rights for affordable housing, supporting industrialized construction methods, and implementing land value taxation to discourage speculation.
