David and Michael Shabsels’ summer camp empire files for bankruptcy

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 05 Jun 2026 21:13:25 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Simad Holdings, an American summer camp operator, and its affiliates have filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in New Jersey, listing liabilities between $500 million and $1 billion. The filing comes after owners David and Michael Shabsels missed payments to Israeli bondholders and transferred $34 million to their own companies. The company owns approximately 30 summer camps and about 80 real estate assets in total, including office and retail properties. The bankruptcy aims to protect these assets and ensure continued operations under a restructuring officer.
