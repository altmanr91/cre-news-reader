Cushman & Wakefield, JLL negotiate sale of two-property self-storage portfolio in Chicago

ACQUISITION

REJournals — Tue, 19 May 2026 13:48:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Etude Storage Capital has acquired a two-property, Class A self-storage portfolio in Chicago from Self Storage 1. The portfolio, totaling 150,216 square feet and 2,043 units, is strategically located in the affluent and supply-constrained Lincoln Park/Bucktown and Wrigleyville/Lakeview neighborhoods. This transaction underscores the strong investor demand for high-quality, well-located self-storage assets in major gateway cities, driven by durable demand from high incomes, renter density, and limited new supply in Chicago's core urban infill locations.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: self-storage

Size: 150,216 SF

Notable Features: The portfolio consists of two Class A self-storage facilities located in supply-constrained, affluent neighborhoods, benefiting from proximity to a major mixed-use redevelopment and a prominent sports stadium.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: Etude Storage Capital

SELLER: Self Storage 1

SELLER BROKER: Cushman & Wakefield — Greg Wells, Executive Director; Mike Mele; Luke Elliott; Ross Bailey

SELLER BROKER: JLL — Steve Mellon, Senior Managing Director; Brian Somoza; Dan Reynolds; Tom Kirschbraun

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The average three-mile population across the portfolio exceeds 480,000 residents, with average household incomes above $164,000, indicating strong underlying submarket fundamentals.
