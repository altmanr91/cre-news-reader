Plano proposes $700M funding plan, total team control to get Dallas Stars to suburbs

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Texas — Tue, 09 Jun 2026 22:03:10 +0000

SUMMARY

The Plano City Council has approved a $700 million funding plan for a new $3 billion arena and entertainment district, aiming to attract the Dallas Stars NHL team to the suburbs. This approval grants the team full control over the grounds and all arena revenue, a key factor in their potential relocation from downtown Dallas. The project, developed by Centennial Real Estate and Cawley Partners, will include mixed-use retail and entertainment space along with significant road improvements.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: mixed-use

Property Name: The Shops at Willow Bend

Address: 6121 W Park Blvd, Plano, TX*

Total Project Cost: $3.00B

Notable Features: The project includes a mixed-use retail and entertainment space, as well as road improvements to assist with traffic flow.

Project Notes: Plano proposed contributing $700 million towards the project if its costs exceed $1 billion. The city would own the arena and land, then lease the facility to Dallas Sports & Entertainment for three decades, with the NHL franchise retaining all arena revenue.
