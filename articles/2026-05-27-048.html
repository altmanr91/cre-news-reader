'Clicking On All Cylinders': Chicago Multifamily Sales Up 117%

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 26 May 2026 16:17:44 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Chicago's commercial real estate investment market experienced a robust start to 2026, with overall sales volume increasing by 42.8% year-over-year to over $4 billion in Q1. This surge was primarily fueled by the multifamily sector, which saw a 116.6% increase in investment to approximately $1.9 billion, making Chicago the second-highest U.S. market for multifamily sales. The market is characterized by tight vacancy rates, limited new construction, and a perception of improving tax predictability under the incoming Cook County Assessor, Pat Hynes. These factors are making Chicago an attractive investment destination, with investors increasingly willing to overlook historical concerns about the tax environment due to strong revenue growth.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Chicago's multifamily vacancy rates are among the tightest in the country at 5.1%, trailing only New York and the San Francisco Bay Area. The city has just 1.6% of its current multifamily inventory under construction. Chicago has the lowest industrial vacancy rate of any of the top 15 metros.
