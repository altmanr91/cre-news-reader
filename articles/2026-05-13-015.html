Feds’ fast-track DC selloff sparks fears of fire sale chaos

SALE

The Real Deal — National — Tue, 12 May 2026 18:16:18 +0000

SUMMARY

The General Services Administration (GSA) is rapidly divesting a significant portfolio of aging federal office buildings in Washington D.C.'s Southwest corridor, aiming to modernize and rightsize its real estate holdings. This initiative, driven by an estimated $50 billion in deferred maintenance liabilities, has already seen the sale of the Regional Office Building to Dalian Development for $24.3 million and the Liberty Loan building placed under contract. However, this fast-track approach is raising concerns among local real estate stakeholders, who fear it could lead to a fragmented redevelopment lacking cohesive long-term planning and potentially attracting undercapitalized buyers. Proposals are emerging to establish a dedicated redevelopment authority or allow the District to acquire and master-plan the sites to ensure a more integrated transformation of the area.
