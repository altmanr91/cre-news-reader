Private School Shortage Is Still Miami Office Market's Biggest Hurdle

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 02 Jun 2026 13:49:54 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Miami's office market is facing a significant hurdle in attracting out-of-market businesses and their high-earning employees: a severe shortage of private school availability. Despite business-friendly policies and no state income tax, companies are hesitant to expand in the city because prospective employees with families cannot secure spots in top private schools. This high demand, fueled by an influx of ultra-wealthy individuals, has led to long waitlists and is impacting residential real estate in affluent neighborhoods. Developers and prominent figures are actively investing in new private and charter schools to address this critical infrastructure gap, with brokers noting intense interest from K-12 institutions seeking to establish a presence in Miami.

DATA POINTS

Florida private school enrollment (2024-25): nearly 500,000 students

Increase in Florida private school enrollment (year-over-year): more than 30,000 students

Number of private schools in Florida: more than 3,200

Miami-Dade County's share of Florida private schools: roughly 19%

Miami-Dade County public school student loss (2024-25): 13,200 students

Florida taxpayer-backed vouchers for private education: up to $8,000 per student
