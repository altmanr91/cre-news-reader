The Daily Dirt: Hochul’s budget delays keep NYC real estate in flux

SALE

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 23 May 2026 11:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Governor Kathy Hochul's delayed state budget rollout has created uncertainty in New York City's real estate market, particularly concerning two proposed taxes. The budget, which includes a controversial 1% tax on all-cash home sales over $1 million and a 'pied-à-terre' tax on second homes valued at $5 million or more annually, has yet to be fully approved. Industry players are frustrated by the lack of clarity and the potential for these taxes to negatively impact sales and fail to meet revenue projections. The delay is seen as a strategic move by the governor to pressure lawmakers into accepting her proposals.
