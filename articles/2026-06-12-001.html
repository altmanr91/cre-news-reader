Forget Tech — Law Firms Are Driving Manhattan Office Leasing

LEASE

Commercial Observer — Fri, 12 Jun 2026 12:00:37 +0000

SUMMARY

Manhattan's office leasing market is seeing a significant surge driven by law firms, with the legal services sector representing 17.1% of overall leasing volume in 2026, up from 11% in 2025. This momentum is highlighted by several major lease transactions, including Simpson Thacher & Bartlett's 916,000-square-foot deal at 570 Fifth Avenue, which is the largest legal sector transaction in six years. The trend is fueled by law firms' appetite for expansion, stricter in-office attendance policies, and a preference for high-quality, prestigious Class A spaces with ample windowed offices, leading to a tightening supply of suitable options. This has resulted in a high volume of renewals and expansions, with the Grand Central neighborhood emerging as a new hot submarket for legal tenants.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Manhattan's office market is experiencing tightening supply for high-end Class A spaces, particularly those suitable for law firms. The Grand Central submarket is emerging as a new preferred location for legal tenants due to limited availability in traditional law firm hubs like Avenue of the Americas.
