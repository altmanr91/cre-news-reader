PolicyPro: SEQRA reform lands in state budget, pied-à-terre tax still unsettled

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 07 May 2026 22:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

New York State's proposed $268 billion budget includes significant real estate policy changes, notably reforms to the State Environmental Quality Review Act (SEQRA) and a potential pied-à-terre tax for New York City. The SEQRA reforms aim to accelerate housing production by exempting certain residential developments from environmental review, with specific unit thresholds for different density districts. While housing advocates support these changes as crucial for addressing the affordability crisis, the real estate industry has expressed a more cautious optimism regarding SEQRA and significant concern about the proposed pied-à-terre tax. Additionally, new legislation, the Commercial Tenant Opportunity to Purchase Act (CTOPA), has been introduced to grant commercial tenants a right of first refusal to purchase their buildings, aiming to protect small businesses from displacement.

DATA POINTS

Proposed State Budget: $268 billion

SEQRA Exemption Threshold (medium/high-density): 500 units or fewer

SEQRA Exemption Threshold (low-density): 250 units or fewer

Environmental Impact Statement Deadline: Two years
