Houston syndicator hits $70M distress patch after 2025’s blockbuster refi deal

FORECLOSURE

The Real Deal — Texas — Tue, 02 Jun 2026 17:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Nitya Capital, a Houston-based syndicator, is facing foreclosure proceedings on three North Texas apartment complexes totaling 847 units, despite securing a significant refinancing deal last year. One William Street Capital Management provided the loans for these properties, which are owned by the Texas Essential Housing Public Facility Corporation, suggesting Nitya utilized a now-closed loophole for tax breaks. This distress follows a $700 million CMBS refinancing in 2025 for 18 multifamily properties and other recent loan delinquencies, indicating ongoing challenges for the firm.
