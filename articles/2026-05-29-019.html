Gary Barnett’s next Midtown move?

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 28 May 2026 20:15:04 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

A pre-foreclosure suit has been filed against the owner of 110 East 55th Street, an office building in Midtown Manhattan, by a construction company that recently acquired the mortgage. This action is seen as a strategic move by Extell Development, led by Gary Barnett, to assemble a large development site on Park Avenue. The building is considered one of the final pieces needed for Extell's expansive project, which already includes a recently acquired development site at 405-417 Park Avenue and air rights from Central Synagogue.

SPONSOR PIPELINE

405-417 Park Avenue: Development site acquired for $500 million in May 2026.

Central Synagogue: Air rights acquired for $20 million.

East 56th Street office building: Office building acquired in May 2026.

111 East 54th Street: Building being eyed for acquisition, home to The Brook private club.

57 East 55th Street: Former Friars Club building recently purchased for $19 million.

Boston development site: Five-acre development site acquired for $75 million via foreclosure auction in March 2026, in partnership with JT Magen.
