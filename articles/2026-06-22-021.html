Cook County foreclosure map: O’Hare office building tops May defaults

The Real Deal — Chicago — Mon, 22 Jun 2026 11:15:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Foreclosure Report

An affiliate of IB Parks & Entertainment defaulted on a $28 million mortgage for International Tower, an 11-story office building near O'Hare Airport in Chicago. Investor Gene Staples had acquired the distressed property in 2023, betting on a post-pandemic office market rebound. This default was the largest foreclosure filing in Cook County for May, though overall foreclosure lawsuits and total mortgaged value declined month-over-month in the Chicago area. Despite the local decrease, Illinois ranked fifth nationally for foreclosure starts, and Chicago led major metros in properties repossessed.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Cook County recorded 753 foreclosure lawsuits in May, down from 849 in April. The total mortgaged value behind May's foreclosure efforts was $189.3 million, a 15% decrease from $222.3 million in April. The median foreclosure value in May was $189,012. Nationwide, foreclosure filings in May were up 14% year-over-year. Illinois ranked fifth among U.S. states for foreclosure starts in May, and Chicago topped major metros for properties repossessed. In Q1, Cook County had 3,439 foreclosure filings, the most in the country.
