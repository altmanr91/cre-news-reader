'Talk About Leverage': NYC Hoteliers Pay Dearly To Dodge World Cup Strike

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 21 May 2026 14:40:47 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

New York City hotel owners have agreed to a new bargaining agreement with the Hotel and Gaming Trades Council, averting a potential strike during the upcoming World Cup and America 250 celebrations. The deal, which is the most generous hotel labor agreement in the country, will see wages for unionized hotel workers increase by approximately 52% over eight years, reaching $60 an hour by 2034, in addition to continued full healthcare coverage. While this agreement provides predictability for investors and potential buyers, it is expected to significantly increase operating costs for hotel owners, who already face rising expenses and high occupancy taxes, likely leading to further upward pressure on room rates in the city's already expensive hotel market.
