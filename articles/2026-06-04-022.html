A New York rent freeze is imminent. Bondholders still expect to be paid.

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 03 Jun 2026 21:17:13 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Rent Regulation Impact

A new analysis by Moody's Ratings estimates that a five-year rent freeze in New York City's stabilized apartments could lead to approximately 6% of multifamily loans defaulting by 2030. For buildings with at least one rent-stabilized unit, this figure rises to over 8%. The report highlights that while high rent growth in market-rate units might help some properties, many loans on rent-stabilized properties were already distressed, with only a portion of projected failures directly attributable to a rent freeze. The findings underscore the potential financial strain on landlords, particularly those with properties predominantly composed of rent-stabilized units, as the Rent Guidelines Board considers a rent freeze amidst high tenant rent burdens.

DATA POINTS

Projected multifamily loan defaults (all multifamily): 6% by 2030

Projected multifamily loan defaults (buildings with at least one rent-stabilized unit): More than 8% by 2030

Loans failing financial stress tests in high inflation scenario (rent-stabilized properties): More than 14% by 2030

Loans where failure is specifically attributed to rent freeze: 29 out of 51 (8.3% of loans in buildings with at least one rent-stabilized unit)

Average decline in debt service coverage ratios (properties >90% stabilized): 0.11x

Rent burden for bottom fifth of New Yorkers by income: Approximately 133% of income

Rent burden for 40th percentile of wage earners in New York: 62% of income
