America’s biggest housing push in decades sits in political limbo

The Real Deal — National — Sun, 28 Jun 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The article discusses the bipartisan 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act, a federal legislative effort aimed at addressing the national housing shortage and affordability crisis. The bill seeks to streamline environmental reviews, incentivize local housing production, expand manufactured housing definitions, and support accessory dwelling units. While the legislation faces political delays, its potential impact includes gradually increasing housing inventory and providing greater certainty for builders, developers, and investors by removing a controversial forced-sale provision for build-to-rent investors. The industry broadly supports the bill as a crucial step toward increasing housing supply, despite acknowledging that its effects will be gradual and local obstacles will persist.
