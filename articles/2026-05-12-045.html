NYC Tenant Unions Are Multiplying. Mayor Mamdani Is In Their Corner

Bisnow News Feed — Mon, 11 May 2026 15:22:20 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

New York City tenant unions are gaining momentum and political support, with Mayor Zohran Mamdani's administration actively backing their efforts to secure better living conditions. The Union of Pinnacle Tenants (UPT), formed during the bankruptcy proceedings of Pinnacle Group, has emerged as a significant force, representing residents across dozens of buildings now owned by Summit Properties USA. Despite failing to block the portfolio's sale, UPT's advocacy in bankruptcy court secured a commitment from Summit Properties to invest $30 million in repairs over five years, supplemented by a $3 million revolving credit line from Flagstar. The mayor's Office of Tenant Protection, led by Cea Weaver, is actively involved in discussions between tenants and landlords, signaling a new era of city-backed tenant organizing against large real estate companies.
