Rising Insurance Costs Are Pushing More Americans To Consider Moving

Bisnow News Feed — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 17:05:13 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Rising insurance costs, driven by increased extreme weather events and surging reconstruction expenses, are beginning to shift U.S. migration patterns and depress property values. Sun Belt states like Texas, Arizona, and Florida, previously popular for migration, are now experiencing negative net migration, while states less vulnerable to climate risks, such as Illinois, New York, and Michigan, are seeing accelerated inbound migration. Research indicates that a 10% increase in insurance premiums correlates with a 4.6% drop in property valuations, signaling a significant impact on real estate markets.
