Brokers, developers use the World Cup to pitch foreign buyers. Here’s where they’re coming from

SALE

The Real Deal — Miami — Fri, 22 May 2026 17:25:11 +0000

SUMMARY

The World Cup is serving as a catalyst for condo sales to foreign buyers in South Florida, particularly from Latin America, at a time when the residential market is experiencing a slowdown. Brokers and developers are leveraging the event to attract international investors, with some offering incentives like World Cup tickets and airline miles to sweeten deals. While not the sole driver, the event is accelerating purchasing decisions, especially for short-term rental-friendly condos that help buyers offset expenses. The weak dollar is also contributing to the increased international demand, with strong buyer pools from countries like Brazil, Colombia, Argentina, and Mexico, as well as continued interest from the United Kingdom, Spain, and Germany.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The World Cup is being used by brokers and developers to attract foreign buyers to Miami, similar to other major events like Art Basel, Formula 1, and the Miami Open. Miami is hosting seven FIFA World Cup 2026 matches at Hard Rock Stadium in Miami Gardens. The weakness of the dollar has helped fuel an uptick in international demand for real estate. Buyers from Colombia, Mexico, Argentina, and Brazil were the top countries represented by foreign purchases in South Florida from January 2024 through October 2025. Interest also continues from the United Kingdom, Spain, and Germany.
