Freeway-fronting hillside in Visitacion Valley poised for mixed-income multifamily development

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 23:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Development

A property owner in San Francisco's Visitacion Valley is planning a mixed-income multifamily development comprising 45 units across three buildings. The project, designed by Schaub Li Architects, will include 26 one-bedroom, 18 two-bedroom, and one three-bedroom units, with five designated as deed-restricted affordable housing. The development is projected to cost approximately $8.5 million for construction, excluding other development expenses, and will also provide parking for cars and bicycles.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

San Francisco is mandated by the state to plan for 82,069 new housing units by 2031. Over 20,000 approved units are stalled due to rising construction costs, federal tariffs, and material shortages. In the previous year, only 1,453 new units were completed, a significant drop from over 5,000 in 2020, and plans for only 2,541 new homes were filed.
