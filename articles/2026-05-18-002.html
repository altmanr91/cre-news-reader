Saks Global’s Fire Sale Might Help It Out of Bankruptcy — But Kill the Business

Commercial Observer — Mon, 18 May 2026 10:30:21 +0000

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Business News

Saks Global, formed by the 2024 merger of Saks Fifth Avenue and Neiman Marcus, has filed for bankruptcy after missing a $100 million bond payment in late 2025. The $7 billion luxury retail entity, which included Bergdorf Goodman and Saks Off 5th, was financed with $2.2 billion in loans, $1.5 billion from Apollo Global Management, and a $475 million equity injection from Amazon. Despite initial projections of $600 million in cost-saving synergies, the company struggled with integrating operations, extended vendor payment terms, and saw sales decline by 13% with a net loss of $288 million. The bankruptcy has led to the fire sale of numerous retail properties, significant job losses, and substantial debts owed to brand-name vendors.
