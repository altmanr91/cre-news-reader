King County ditches lease for ownership in mental health services strategy

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — National — Tue, 19 May 2026 15:33:46 +0000

SUMMARY

King County has acquired the Connections Kirkland crisis center in Kirkland, WA, for $32.6 million, transitioning from a long-term lease to direct ownership of the 67,000-square-foot facility. This acquisition is part of a broader strategy to expand the county's behavioral health network, funded by a voter-approved property tax levy. The move is expected to save the county nearly $2.3 million in future lease payments and provides flexibility for potential expansion of services.
