More infill resi slated for increasingly dense Outer Mission

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Fri, 29 May 2026 21:35:41 +0000

SUMMARY

Development

A single-family lot in San Francisco's Outer Mission is slated for infill residential development, with plans to add three new housing units behind an existing home at 490 Ellington Avenue. The project, designed by Yuflux Engineering, involves replacing the current backyard with a three-story structure comprising two apartments and an accessory dwelling unit. This development is part of a broader trend of infill housing proposals in the Outer Mission, as San Francisco seeks to address its state-mandated housing goals amid a constrained new apartment development pipeline.

SPONSOR PIPELINE

5500-5504 Mission Street: A four-story, 49-bed, 30-room condominium complex for elder care, spanning approximately 26,500 square feet, with an estimated construction cost of $3.5 million.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

San Francisco must plan for 82,069 new housing units by 2030 to meet state-mandated housing goals. The city's new apartment development pipeline is largely frozen due to rising construction costs and an uncertain economic outlook.
