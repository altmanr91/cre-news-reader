Reinsdorfs, Wirtzes break ground on $7B 1901 Project

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Chicago — Thu, 04 Jun 2026 20:46:56 +0000

SUMMARY

The Reinsdorf and Wirtz families have broken ground on the $7 billion 1901 Project, a massive mixed-use redevelopment surrounding the United Center in Chicago. The project, which has been two years in the making with over $110 million in land acquisitions, will transform 55 acres of parking lots into a vibrant community space. It will feature a music hall, hotel rooms, apartments with an affordable housing component, retail, and parking garages, with full completion anticipated by 2040 across six phases.

Notable Features: Includes a 6,000-seat music hall and rooftop green spaces on parking garages. Approximately 20% of apartment units will be affordable housing, with a focus on transit connectivity.

Project Notes: The project involved over $110 million in land parcel acquisitions over two years. It was jumpstarted by a $55 million Class 7(b) property tax incentive from the Chicago City Council, which will lower taxes for 12 years. Developers are requesting a new CTA Pink Line station by 2034.
