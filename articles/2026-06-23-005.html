Changes in Retail Real Estate Nationwide Have Accelerated: Forum

Commercial Observer — Mon, 22 Jun 2026 20:35:18 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article summarizes key discussions from Commercial Observer's Retail & Hospitality Forum, highlighting the accelerating convergence of commercial real estate asset classes into integrated, experiential environments. Panelists emphasized the importance of creating vibrant, mixed-use developments where retail, office, and multifamily components are symbiotic, driven by strong demand from retailers and institutional investors re-entering the market. The Upper East Side of Manhattan was cited as an example of a retail corridor experiencing a resurgence due to an influx of younger families, with a major department store investing hundreds of millions in renovations to enhance the physical retail experience through digital integration and modern design.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The retail market is repriced and in the early stages of recovery, with institutional investors re-entering the market and larger transactions occurring. Retail offers superior spreads relative to industrial and multifamily, with strong stability, rent growth, net operating income growth, and the best occupancy performance in 15 years. There has been virtually no new supply of open-air retail or retail broadly in the U.S., paired with strong retailer demand, particularly for grocery-anchored open-air shopping centers.
