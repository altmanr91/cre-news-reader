Chicago’s newer office towers? They’re still boasting lower vacancy rates

REJournals — Wed, 03 Jun 2026 09:43:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Office Report

Chicago's office market continues to exhibit a stark contrast between newer, amenity-rich Class-A towers and older stock, with the former significantly outperforming in terms of vacancy rates. A recent report by Transwestern for Q1 2026 highlights that the 20 newest Class-A buildings in the CBD, representing 11.4% of total inventory, maintained a 9.5% vacancy rate, substantially lower than the overall CBD rate of 23.2%. The addition of 919 W. Fulton St. to the index, despite delivering with significant direct vacancy, underscores Fulton Market's emergence as a premier office destination. The sustained demand for high-quality office environments suggests potential for future development, as limited large-block availability in newer buildings may prompt some large tenants to anchor new projects.

DATA POINTS

Vacancy rate for newest Class-A office buildings (Q1 2026): 9.5%

Vacancy rate for newest Class-A office buildings (Q4 2025): 8%

Overall Chicago CBD office vacancy rate (Q1 2026): 23.2%

Total square footage in Transwestern's index: 17.8 million SF

Percentage of Chicago's total CBD office inventory represented by the index: 11.4%

919 W. Fulton St. size: 369,007 SF

919 W. Fulton St. preleased upon delivery: 46.9%

Available sublease space within index buildings (end of Q1 2026): 631,247 SF

Percentage of index inventory as sublease space: 3.6%

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Chicago CBD office market is characterized by a significant performance gap between newer Class-A buildings and older stock. The newest generation of Class-A office buildings, tracked by Transwestern's index, had a vacancy rate of 9.5% in Q1 2026, a notable increase from 8% in Q4 2025 but still considerably lower than the overall CBD vacancy rate of 23.2%. Fulton Market is emerging as a dominant office destination, with seven of the 20 buildings in the index located there. Despite rising vacancy, large-block direct vacancies (over 100,000 SF) are limited in the newest inventory, with only four such opportunities identified, including two blocks at 300 N. LaSalle St. (over 300,000 SF) and a 178,708 SF block at 919 W. Fulton St. The sublease market within index buildings showed improvement, totaling 631,247 SF, or 3.6% of the index inventory.
