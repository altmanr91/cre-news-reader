Continental Battery is the latest company to ditch Downtown Dallas for the suburbs

LEASE

The Real Deal — Texas — Thu, 07 May 2026 18:41:20 +0000

SUMMARY

Continental Battery Systems, a battery products distributor, is relocating its headquarters from Dallas to Irving, Texas, signing a 10-year lease for 25,000 square feet at 750 West John Carpenter Freeway. The move is supported by a $75,000 economic incentive package from the City of Irving, contingent on the creation or relocation of at least 75 jobs with an average annual salary of $119,000. This relocation reflects a broader trend of companies moving from Dallas's urban core to its northern suburbs.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: office

Address: 750 West John Carpenter Freeway, Irving, TX

Size: 25,000 SF

Notable Features: The City of Irving approved a $75,000 economic incentives package for the relocation.

Project Notes: Continental Battery Systems must create or relocate at least 75 new jobs with an average annual salary of at least $119,000 by the end of the year and maintain them for 10 years. The company must also increase the appraised tax value of its new suite by at least $500,000 by January 1, 2027.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

North Texas leads the nation in business relocations, with a strong preference for northern suburbs over Dallas's urban core. The former CVS Health Tower at 750 West John Carpenter Freeway was acquired by Capital Commercial Investments in 2024 for $23 million ($73/SF), having previously sold for $49.6 million ($157/SF) in 2016. AT&T's planned move to Plano will create a 2 million-square-foot vacancy, representing almost 6 percent of Downtown Dallas's submarket space.
