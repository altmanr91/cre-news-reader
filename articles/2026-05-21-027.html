“We cannot fall so far behind”: Controversial 5-story jewelry store project sails past approval

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Miami — Wed, 20 May 2026 23:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Fort Lauderdale commissioners have unanimously approved the rezoning and site plan for Weston Jewelers' controversial five-story flagship store on Las Olas Boulevard. The Arquitectonica-designed glass and wood panel building, which will rise 74 feet, faced opposition from residents concerned about the rezoning of a residential lot to commercial, the building's height, and its lack of on-site parking. Despite these concerns, the project is seen by supporters and city officials as crucial for downtown Fort Lauderdale's growth and competitiveness with neighboring markets.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: retail

Property Name: Weston Jewelers Flagship Store

Address: 221-229 Southeast 12th Avenue and 1117-1121 East Las Olas Boulevard, Fort Lauderdale, FL

Original Plan: 90 feet (108 feet with mechanicals)

Site Area: 0.25 acres

Notable Features: Arquitectonica-designed glass and wood panel building; will include spaces for luxury brands like Rolex, Cartier, Omega, Breitling, and Chopard, as well as independent jewelers and a bridal section. The fourth floor will house a Dikes family office.

Project Notes: The project will have zero on-site parking spots, with 100 off-site spaces secured through letters of intent with First Presbyterian Church (40 spaces), Demko Parking (40 spaces), and Moss Construction (20 spaces). Staff members will be prohibited from parking in public lots. One of the two lots was rezoned from residential to commercial.
