Chicagoland home prices in the pressure cooker

The Real Deal — Chicago — Wed, 27 May 2026 22:34:48 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Residential Report

Chicagoland home prices reached record highs in April, with the median sale price in the city hitting over $411,000 and the nine-county metro area reaching $390,000. This surge, significantly outpacing national trends, is driven by strong demand and limited inventory, leading to intense bidding wars and faster sales, even for luxury properties. The market is characterized by a robust seller's advantage, with many homes selling above asking price.

DATA POINTS

Chicago median home sale price (April): $411,000

Chicago metro area median home sale price (April): $390,000

Year-over-year increase in Chicago median home sale price: 5%

National median home price increase (April): 0.9%

Reduction in average days on market (Chicago city): 4 days year-over-year

Reduction in average days on market (metro area): 1 day year-over-year

Percentage of homes sold above asking price (March): 70%

Average premium over asking price: just north of $60,000
