LeFrak beefs up portfolio with $180M riverfront tower in Fort Lauderdale

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Miami — Mon, 04 May 2026 18:06:45 +0000

SUMMARY

The LeFrak Organization has acquired the 36-story, 337-unit Harbour at New River Apartments in downtown Fort Lauderdale for $180 million from The Related Group and Rabina Properties. The transaction, financed with a $100 million loan from Northwestern Mutual, highlights continued deal activity in South Florida's multifamily market despite an ongoing oversupply of apartments. The property, completed in 2024, is part of a larger trend of investment in the region by LeFrak, which is also involved in other significant developments.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

South Florida's multifamily market has experienced an oversupply of apartments, leading to slower lease-ups, increased concessions, and a drop in rents. The average rent across South Florida fell 2.2 percent year over year to about $2,200 in March. A record 18,600 units were completed in South Florida in 2024, outpacing total leasing by 20 percent. Another 12,718 apartments were finished last year, still outpacing net leasing by about 300 units. Despite this, demand for South Florida rentals is expected to continue due to migration trends and the region's business-friendly climate.
