“It’s going to hurt”: NYC resi players push back on proposed cash sales tax

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 16 May 2026 12:39:11 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

New York state lawmakers are considering a new 1% tax on residential cash sales of $1 million or more in New York City, a proposal projected to generate $160 million annually to help fund the city's budget deficit. This potential tax, along with a recently announced annual levy on second homes valued at $5 million or more, has drawn significant criticism from real estate professionals. Industry experts express concern that the lack of clear details and the cumulative effect of these taxes could deter transactions, particularly given that cash deals accounted for 65% of all sales in the city last year, rising to 75% for sales over $1 million. Critics also suggest the projected revenue may be overstated, as it doesn't account for potential changes in buyer behavior or market activity.
