Warsh Wants A 'New Chapter' For The Fed. A Rate Hike Could Come First

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 15:29:44 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Federal Reserve Chairman Kevin Warsh, in his first press conference, outlined a comprehensive plan to reform the central bank's operations through five new task forces, signaling a "new chapter" for the Fed. This initiative comes as inflation significantly exceeds the Fed's target and the labor market shows strong but shallow job gains, leading to expectations of a potential rate hike this year. While the benchmark rate was held flat, Warsh's emphasis on reassessment and a more proactive policy approach, rather than a data-dependent and reactive one, suggests a significant shift in the Fed's strategy for navigating the current economic landscape.

DATA POINTS

Inflation rate in May: 4.2%

Fed governors projecting at least one rate hike this year: 9 out of 18

Fed governors expecting rates to remain flat this year: 8 out of 18

Fed governors forecasting a 25-basis-point reduction this year: 1 out of 18
