The real estate bills Albany passed, killed or kicked down the road

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 05 Jun 2026 22:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

The New York State Legislature concluded its session with several real estate-related bills passed, while others failed to advance. Key legislation approved includes a bill requiring seller or landlord consent for private listings, a one-year moratorium on new data centers due to energy and water concerns, and increased experience requirements for real estate broker licenses. Notably, a highly anticipated bill that would have eased the process for upstate communities to adopt rent stabilization failed to pass, with its sponsor citing limited time for consensus-building after protracted budget negotiations.

DATA POINTS

Broker license experience requirement increase: From 2 years to 4 consecutive years as a licensed salesperson, or 3 years equivalent experience

Private listing violation penalties increase: From $2,000 to $5,000

Data center moratorium duration: One year
