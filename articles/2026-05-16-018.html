What the state budget means for real estate

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 15 May 2026 22:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

New York State lawmakers are nearing a final agreement on a $268 billion budget, which includes several significant real estate-focused proposals. Key among these are a new pied-à-terre tax targeting luxury second homes and a tax on all-cash home purchases over $1 million in New York City. Additionally, the budget is expected to include reforms to the State Environmental Quality Review Act (SEQRA) aimed at streamlining housing and infrastructure development by exempting certain projects from environmental review. These measures are intended to generate revenue for the city's budget deficit and accelerate housing construction, though some proposals have drawn criticism from the real estate industry.

DATA POINTS

Proposed State Budget: $268 billion

Pied-à-terre tax projected annual revenue (Governor's office): $500 million

Pied-à-terre tax projected annual revenue (Comptroller's office): $340 million

SEQRA exemption threshold (NYC, medium/high-density): 500 or fewer units

SEQRA exemption threshold (NYC, low-density): 250 units

SEQRA exemption threshold (outside NYC): 100 or fewer units

SEQRA exemption threshold (urban areas outside NYC): 300 units

SEQRA exemption threshold (municipalities without zoning codes): 20 units
