More Oakland apartments up for sale after lender foreclosure

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Fri, 08 May 2026 22:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Property Listing / For Sale

An Oakland apartment building, the Ora, is now on the market for sale after its lender, Prime Finance, foreclosed on the property in late 2024. The 74-unit building was originally purchased by Vaughn Management in 2021 for $41 million, but faced challenges due to weakening tenant demand and difficulties in refinancing short-term construction debt. JLL is marketing the property as an opportunity to acquire an asset below its estimated replacement cost.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

JLL estimates replacement cost for comparable podium construction at roughly $700,000 per unit. The property's assessed value was $26 million in summer 2025. Recent Oakland apartment trades have ranged between $200,000 and $400,000 per unit. In April 2026, CWCapital seized Telegraph Arts at 471 26th Street for $31.1 million (50% below its $61.9 million assessed value) and The Moran at 570 21st Street for $20.3 million (53% below its most recent valuation) after defaults on $52.3 million in loans.
