Johnson Prepares Sweeping Rental Overhaul, Chicago CRE Is Skeptical

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 09 Jun 2026 18:13:56 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Chicago Mayor Brandon Johnson is preparing to introduce the Protecting Renters Ordinance, a sweeping overhaul of the city's rental rules that has drawn significant skepticism from the commercial real estate industry. The proposed ordinance includes a tenant bill of rights, citywide "just cause" eviction, a ban on move-in and move-out fees, and a framework for potential rent control. Industry stakeholders argue that the measure would make Chicago an unattractive market for institutional capital, increase litigation, and ultimately reduce housing supply and affordability by deterring investment in rental housing. Critics also point out that Chicago already faces a housing supply shortage, building significantly fewer units per capita than peer cities, which exacerbates affordability issues.

DATA POINTS

Estimated annual revenue from rental registry: $20M

Chicago new housing units built per capita (2025) compared to peer cities: Roughly a fourth

Chicago multifamily vacancy rate: 5%

Nationwide multifamily vacancy rate: 8.5%

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Chicago's multifamily vacancy rate of 5% is significantly lower than the nationwide average of 8.5%, indicating a tight rental market. The city's new housing construction in 2025 was roughly a fourth of that in peer cities like New York, Los Angeles, Houston, and Phoenix, contributing to a diminished overall housing supply and broken affordability process.
