Great Central Brewing owners face second foreclosure, this time for nearly $5M on Near West Side

FORECLOSURE

The Real Deal — Chicago — Thu, 21 May 2026 19:04:22 +0000

SUMMARY

The owners of Great Central Brewing Company are facing a $4.7 million foreclosure lawsuit on their Near West Side facility in Chicago, IL. Harvest Commercial Capital filed the suit against 221 Wood Holdings LLC, alleging default on a $4.75 million loan originated in December 2024. This marks the second foreclosure attempt on the property in recent years, highlighting the financial challenges within the craft brewery industry.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: industrial

Property Name: Great Central Brewery

Address: 221 North Wood Street, Chicago, IL

Loan Amount: $4.8M

Project Notes: The property was previously subject to a $12 million foreclosure lawsuit in 2023 by T2 Capital, which was later dismissed after the ownership recapitalized the asset. T2 Capital is still owed nearly $2 million and is named as a defendant in the current complaint. The Metropolitan Water Reclamation District of Greater Chicago holds over $52,000 in unresolved liens.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

LENDER: Harvest Commercial Capital

SPONSOR: 221 Wood Holdings LLC — David Avram

OWNER: Great Central Brewing Company

LENDER: T2 Capital — Jeff Brown, CEO

LENDER: U.S. Small Business Administration

LENDER: Byline Bank

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The craft brewery business in Chicagoland and nationally has faced challenges due to shifting consumer habits, rising material costs, and an oversaturated market, leading to downsizing, equipment sales, or closures for several local brewers.
