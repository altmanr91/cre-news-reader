Developers: doing it for the fee

The Real Deal — National — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 11:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article examines the controversial practice of developers and sponsors collecting substantial fees even when their real estate investments result in significant losses for investors. It highlights how the "2 and 20" compensation model, common in finance, is applied in commercial real estate with additional construction and property management fees. Critics argue this structure can incentivize sponsors to prioritize asset accumulation for fee generation over investor profits, while proponents contend that fees are for services rendered, regardless of investment outcomes. The piece cites the Transamerica Pyramid sale, where the developer received an $80 million fee package despite the German pension funds losing over $200 million, and the case of a multifamily syndicator accused of overcharging fees amidst investor losses, as examples of this contentious dynamic.
