“Nonsensical”: Miami-Dade votes to pursue Fisher Island site via eminent domain

The Real Deal — Miami — Tue, 16 Jun 2026 22:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Miami-Dade County Commission has voted to proceed with acquiring the Fisher Island fuel terminal property through eminent domain, despite a previous $400 million negotiation with the current owner, a partnership including HRP Group, The Related Group, and Russell Galbut, having failed. The county aims to secure the nearly 10-acre site, which is critical infrastructure for PortMiami, after having missed an earlier opportunity to purchase it for $180 million. The current owners had planned to demolish the fuel bunk, remediate the land, and develop luxury condominiums, including conveying 4 acres to the Fisher Island Community Association. The eminent domain process is expected to be contentious and potentially costly, with legal experts suggesting the county may pay significantly more than the previously negotiated price.
