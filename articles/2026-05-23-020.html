Modified COPA nears passage despite real estate pushback

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 22 May 2026 22:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

A revised version of New York City's Community Opportunity to Purchase Act (COPA) is nearing passage, despite continued skepticism from the real estate industry. The bill, which grants city-certified nonprofits or joint ventures a right of first refusal on certain distressed multifamily buildings, includes concessions such as a shortened timeline for offers and stricter eligibility criteria for properties. While the current mayor supports the bill, real estate professionals argue that the changes are marginal and will still complicate financing, deter investors, and depress sale prices for affected properties.
