Top CBRE Researcher Ebere Anokute: E-Commerce’s Impact Is ‘Overblown’

Commercial Observer — Thu, 21 May 2026 21:55:47 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

CBRE's head of retail research for the Americas, Ebere Anokute, argues that the impact of e-commerce on physical retail is often overstated, with brick-and-mortar stores still accounting for the vast majority of retail sales. He highlights that the retail market is currently experiencing a positive moment due to a supply-constrained environment and outsized demand, particularly from service tenants, grocery stores, and fitness concepts. Anokute points to the Sun Belt region, including cities like Dallas, Phoenix, Houston, and various parts of Florida, as outperforming markets driven by population growth and new retail construction. He emphasizes consumer spending as a leading indicator for physical retail demand and notes the growth of discount retailers in response to economic uncertainty, while also stressing that 'value' doesn't always equate to 'cheap'.

DATA POINTS

U.S. retail availability Q1: 4.9%

Construction completions Q1: 4.7 million square feet

E-commerce share of overall retail sales: 16-16.5%

Physical store share of overall retail sales: 83-84%
