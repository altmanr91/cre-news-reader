PolicyPro: Housing chief details NYC landlord court crackdown

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 03 Jun 2026 23:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

New York City's Department of Housing Preservation and Development (HPD) is implementing a new strategy to expedite enforcement cases against negligent landlords in housing court. The initiative, led by Commissioner Dina Levy, aims to fast-track cases involving chronic neglect, properties under vacate orders, comprehensive litigation for outstanding violations, and Class C housing code violations. HPD is collaborating with the state Office of Court Administration to create expedited calendars for these cases, initially focusing on at least 10 housing portfolios with severe and persistent code violations. Additionally, a state bill targeting 'zombie homes' is close to passing, which would compel lenders of abandoned multifamily properties to address hazardous conditions, a move opposed by the banking industry.
