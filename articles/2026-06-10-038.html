Washington Holdings continues Silicon Valley sell-off with four-building office complex

SALE

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Tue, 09 Jun 2026 23:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Washington Holdings has sold four office buildings within the Mission Park complex in Santa Clara, CA, to an affiliate of BKM Capital Partners for $32.9 million. This transaction is part of Washington Holdings' broader strategy to divest its Silicon Valley office holdings, following a previous sale in San Jose. The buildings are currently occupied by a mix of tech companies and Washington Holdings' local offices.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: office

Property Name: Mission Park

Address: 1500-1800 Wyatt Drive, Santa Clara, CA

Sale Price: $32.9M

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

SELLER: Washington Holdings

BUYER: BKM Capital Partners

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Washington Holdings controls approximately 2.5 million square feet of office, retail, and R&D space in Silicon Valley with about 90% occupancy rates across its holdings. In April, Washington Holdings sold the seven-building Rio Tech Office Park in San Jose for $164.3 million. Other recent office sales in Santa Clara include Rubicon Point Partners' $87.2 million acquisition of 201,100 square feet at 3003 Bunker Hill Lane and 5303, 5353, 5403 Betsy Ross Drive, and Ellis Partners and Baupost Group's $310 million purchase of the Great America Commons office complex at 4555 and 4655 Great America Parkway.
