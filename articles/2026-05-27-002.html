The Window for Seizing on New York’s Class B and C Office Rebound Is Closing

Commercial Observer — Wed, 27 May 2026 10:20:46 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The author argues that New York City's Class B and C office market has already passed its bottom, likely by six months, driven by the success of the 467m office-to-residential conversion tax abatement program. This program has led to the removal of approximately 26 million square feet of office inventory, significantly reducing oversupply. Concurrently, leasing activity has steadily strengthened, leading to positive absorption and a faster-than-expected approach to market equilibrium. The author notes a disconnect between improving market realities and lingering investor pessimism, suggesting that the window for buying at the bottom has closed and sophisticated investors are now recognizing the changed risk-reward equation.

DATA POINTS

Office-to-residential conversions: Over 80 buildings in Manhattan actively pursuing residential conversion

Office inventory removed: Around 26 million square feet

Class B and C office pricing (historical): $800-$900 per square foot

Class B and C office pricing (recent bottom): Low $200s and high $100s per square foot
