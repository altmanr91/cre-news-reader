Whole Foods poised to end Fishtown grocery drought

LEASE

The Real Deal — National — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 20:51:05 +0000

SUMMARY

Whole Foods Market is reportedly planning to lease 9,400 square feet for a new grocery store at Fishtown Collective's 1716 Frankford Avenue development in Philadelphia. The store would occupy the ground floor of a 40-unit mixed-use building currently under construction. The proposed 24-hour loading zone for deliveries has been a point of discussion with local residents, though a neighborhood association has voted in favor of the plan, which now awaits city council review. This development aims to address the lack of a large-scale grocery store in the rapidly growing Fishtown neighborhood.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: retail

Address: 1716 Frankford Avenue, Philadelphia, PA

Size: 9,400 SF

Completion: mid-2027

Notable Features: Ground-floor retail in a 40-unit residential development.

Project Notes: Construction began early 2026 and is expected to complete within 12 months. The building includes over 12,000 square feet of retail space, with additional space for two more tenants.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Fishtown and Northern Liberties have seen thousands of new residential units delivered in recent years. Northern Liberties has several major supermarkets, including Acme Supermarket, Sprouts Farmers Market, and Giant Heirloom. Philadelphia currently has two standard-format Whole Foods stores and a smaller-format Daily Shop is planned for South Street. Six Amazon Fresh stores in the city closed earlier in 2026.
