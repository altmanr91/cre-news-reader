Newly built affordable housing community outside Bay Area trades for $84M

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Wed, 27 May 2026 22:30:57 +0000

SUMMARY

Hudson Valley Property Group has acquired Mosa Apartment Homes, a 387-unit affordable multifamily community in Elk Grove, for $83.6 million. The transaction involved the assumption of permanent debt and was financed through Freddie Mac loans, with LIHTC equity and public finance support. The acquisition secures affordability protections for the property for an additional 30 years, aligning with the city's housing goals.

Notable Features: Garden-style property with rooftop solar panels, a fitness center, and a library. Income restrictions range from 30% to 80% of area median income.

Project Notes: The property is structured across three low-income housing tax credit partnerships. Approximately 8% of units are supported by tenant-based vouchers. Affordability protections are locked in for another 30 years.

FINANCING

The acquisition included the assumption of permanent debt and was financed through three Freddie Mac loans, with LIHTC equity provided by Red Stone Equity Partners and public finance support from the California Municipal Finance Authority and the City of Elk Grove.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The City of Elk Grove is mandated to plan for 8,263 new housing units by 2029, with 4,265 designated as affordable.
