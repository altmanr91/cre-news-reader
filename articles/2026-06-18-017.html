Fed’s real estate portfolio needs more than $25B of maintenance

The Real Deal — National — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 17:49:15 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Government Real Estate

The federal government's real estate portfolio, managed by the General Services Administration (GSA), faces a significant deferred maintenance challenge, estimated at $25.8 billion. This includes 62 buildings requiring over $100 million in repairs, with two Washington D.C. buildings needing more than $1 billion each. The poor condition of these properties hinders the government's efforts to sell them, while legislative hurdles, such as the $4 million prospectus threshold requiring congressional approval for repairs, cause delays and increased costs. Additionally, Congress has diverted over $15 billion from the Federal Buildings Fund, further exacerbating the maintenance funding shortfall.

DATA POINTS

Total deferred maintenance: $25.8 billion

Buildings needing over $100M in repairs: 62

Prospectus threshold for congressional approval: $4 million

Average time for congressional approval: 435 days

Funds diverted from Federal Buildings Fund: $15 billion

Federal office space utilization: Less than a third

Estimated annual cost of unused federal office space: $2 billion
