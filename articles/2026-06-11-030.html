Bridge fund in place for delayed Cook County property tax bills

The Real Deal — Chicago — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 15:32:11 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

Cook County Board President Toni Preckwinkle has announced a $300 million bridge loan fund to assist local governments impacted by delayed property tax bills. This initiative aims to provide short-term financial relief, particularly for suburban tax districts heavily reliant on property tax revenue, and will also consider disinvested communities. The ongoing delays, attributed to a computer system overhaul, pandemic effects, and individual office struggles, have forced some districts, especially schools and libraries, to incur significant costs by taking out their own loans or using reserves.
