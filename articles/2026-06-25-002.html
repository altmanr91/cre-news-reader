D.C. Department of Veterans Affairs Building’s CMBS Loan Enters Special Servicing

LOAN

Commercial Observer — Wed, 24 Jun 2026 19:52:03 +0000

SUMMARY

The $102 million CMBS loan on 425 Eye Street NW, a Washington D.C. office building primarily leased to the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, has entered special servicing due to an imminent monetary default. The default was triggered by the tenant's failure to renew its lease 12 months prior to expiration, with 64% of the space expiring this month. The owner, PED Investments, is currently negotiating with the GSA regarding the lease renewal.

Project Notes: The seven-year loan was originated in December 2021 with a 2.94% interest rate, retiring $89 million in existing debt and allowing PED to recoup $9.6 million in equity. The loan had a 56.8% loan-to-value ratio based on a $180 million valuation. The building was previously sold in October 2018 for $157 million.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

A KLNB Washington, D.C., office report from Q3 2021 cited the Department of Veterans Affairs' lease of 283,242 square feet at the property among its major deals. A recent leasing ad by Colliers puts the building's rents at $52 per square foot.
