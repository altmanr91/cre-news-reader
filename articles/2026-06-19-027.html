Chicago’s top construction permits: Week ending June 16, 2026

The Real Deal — Chicago — Thu, 18 Jun 2026 18:39:53 +0000

SUMMARY

Construction / Permits

This article summarizes the top construction permits issued in Chicago for the week ending June 16, 2026, highlighting several significant alteration projects. The largest permit was for elevator installations at the Thompson Center, a joint venture between The Prime Group and Capri Investment Group, with Google also securing an electrical permit for the building. Other notable projects include a commercial renovation at 10 South Riverside Plaza, an interior renovation at Washington Irving Elementary School, a tenant improvement for Amwins Insurance Brokerage at 10 South La Salle Street, a plumbing infrastructure upgrade at a condo building at 5801 North Sheridan Road, and a data center upgrade for Netratlity Data Centers at 717 South Wells Street.

DATA POINTS

Thompson Center elevator installations: $19,000,000

Thompson Center electrical permit: $1,000,000

10 South Riverside Plaza commercial renovation: $6,000,000

Washington Irving Elementary School interior renovation: $4,500,000

Amwins Insurance Brokerage tenant improvement: $4,500,000

5801 North Sheridan Road plumbing infrastructure project: $4,170,000

Netratlity Data Centers upgrade: $3,000,000
