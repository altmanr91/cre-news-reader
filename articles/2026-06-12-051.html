As San Francisco's AI Surge Lifts Leasing, Oakland Scrambles To Prep Space For Spillover

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 14:43:20 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Oakland's office market is struggling with high vacancy rates and declining rents, prompting landlords to proactively reposition properties with spec suites and amenities to attract future spillover demand from San Francisco's AI-driven leasing surge. Despite Oakland's Q1 vacancy rate of 27.4% being lower than San Francisco's 31.6%, the East Bay city experienced negative absorption and a 28% drop in CBD rents from their peak. Landlords are focusing on having move-in-ready spaces and pre-approved permits to capitalize on the rapid occupancy needs of AI and tech tenants, a strategy that has proven successful in San Francisco where a building saw occupancy jump from 25% to over 90% by offering smaller, ready-to-occupy suites.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Oakland's Q1 2026 office vacancy rate was 27.4%, up 3% year-over-year due to negative net absorption of 110,000 SF. Asking rates in Oakland's central business district were $3.92 per SF, nearly 28% down from their peak. San Francisco's office vacancy rate at the end of Q1 2026 was 31.6%, an improvement from 34.4% a year earlier. New office leases in San Francisco accounted for 3.7M SF in Q1 2026, the highest level since Q2 2018.
