Energy Shocks and Occupier Impact

Connect CRE — Fri, 12 Jun 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

A recent CBRE article highlights that rising energy costs are significantly impacting commercial real estate occupiers across the United States, Europe, and parts of Asia-Pacific, with Europe and Asia-Pacific experiencing more pronounced effects due to their reliance on imported gas. The increased costs are affecting building operations and capital planning decisions, particularly for energy-sensitive materials in construction, where costs are projected to rise by 6.6% to 10.7%. The authors forecast a prolonged period of energy market volatility, with the full impact on facilities management budgets potentially lagging wholesale price spikes by 6 to 12 months, suggesting that budgets set before February 2026 are likely to be under-costed.

DATA POINTS

Projected increase in energy-sensitive material costs: 6.6% to 10.7%

Lag time for facilities management budget impacts: 6 to 12 months
