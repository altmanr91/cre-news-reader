Feds Look For Deal With 1,500 Landlords Suing Over Pandemic-Era Eviction Ban

Bisnow News Feed — Mon, 04 May 2026 12:02:41 -0400

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Government Policy

A class-action lawsuit brought by over 1,500 landlords against the U.S. government, challenging the constitutionality of the pandemic-era eviction moratorium, has entered settlement negotiations. The landlords, who claim the moratorium violated their Fifth Amendment rights by denying them compensation for lost rent, are seeking up to $1.5 billion in damages. This development follows a successful appeal in June 2025 that allowed the federal lawsuit to proceed, potentially opening the door for thousands of similar claims against the government.

DATA POINTS

Number of landlords suing: 1,500+

Claimed damages: $1.5B

Largest named landlord portfolio: 15,015 units

Eviction moratorium duration: September 2020 - July 2021
