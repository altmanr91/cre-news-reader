The great mall divide: Coresight report finds that top-tier malls thrive while lower-tier properties struggle

REJournals — Thu, 04 Jun 2026 10:03:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Retail Report

A new report from Coresight Research highlights a significant bifurcation in the U.S. enclosed shopping mall sector, with top-tier properties thriving while lower-tier assets continue to struggle. The study, titled "The American Mall Renaissance: A Bifurcated Sector with Top-Tier Assets Leading the Way," indicates that metrics such as foot traffic, net operating income, rents, and occupancy are moving in opposite directions for these two categories of malls. Top-tier malls, often featuring luxury retailers in affluent areas, have nearly recovered to pre-pandemic foot traffic levels and boast high occupancy, while lower-tier malls lag significantly. Mall owners are increasingly adapting by replacing traditional department store vacancies with entertainment, fitness, and healthcare concepts to enhance the consumer experience and generate higher rents, a strategy that also benefits many mid-tier malls appealing to younger demographics. However, lower-tier malls face a challenging cycle of declining traffic and tenant departures, with redevelopment into alternative uses like distribution centers or mixed-use projects often being their most viable path forward.

DATA POINTS

Top-tier mall foot traffic vs. 2019: 0.1% below

Lower-tier mall foot traffic vs. 2019: 6.8% below

Top-tier mall average occupancy: 95.5%

Lower-tier mall average occupancy: 89%
