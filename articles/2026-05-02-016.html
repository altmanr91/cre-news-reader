Denver leads nation in home value losses after post-pandemic surge

The Real Deal — National — Fri, 01 May 2026 23:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Housing Report

Denver's housing market experienced the sharpest year-over-year decline in home values among major U.S. metros in February, falling 2.2 percent, according to Realtor.com citing the S&P CoreLogic Case-Shiller Index. This contrasts with a national average increase of 0.7 percent and marks a significant shift from its pandemic-era boom. The downturn is attributed to a surge in inventory, rising insurance costs, and a notable pullback in demand for condominiums and townhomes, which are significant segments of the Denver market. This trend reflects a broader recalibration in previously high-flying secondary markets, with the Northeast and Midwest showing the strongest growth.
