Hard Rock Cafe lets Downtown Miami lease lapse after 30-plus years

The Real Deal — Miami — Wed, 24 Jun 2026 17:29:20 +0000

SUMMARY

Lease Expiration / Closure

Hard Rock Cafe's Bayside Marketplace location in Downtown Miami will close on August 19 after its lease with the city was not renewed. The restaurant, which has operated for over 30 years, will vacate the space, which is slated for redevelopment by the ground leaseholder, Ashkenazy Acquisition Corporation. The closure will result in over 100 job losses. The downtown retail market is experiencing some softening, with increased vacancy, though developers remain optimistic about new construction.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Downtown Miami's retail market is showing signs of softening, with retailers shedding 44,430 square feet of space and vacancy reaching 6.3 percent. Despite this, developers remain bullish, with nearly 64,000 square feet of retail space under construction and asking rents averaging $52.50 per square foot. The Miami Worldcenter has been a significant driver of recent retail development and leasing in the area.
