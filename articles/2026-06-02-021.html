Billionaire David Steward’s son pays $17M for Wellington horse farm

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Miami — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 21:00:45 +0000

SUMMARY

David Steward II, CEO of Lion Forge Entertainment, acquired a 7.82-acre horse farm in Wellington's Grand Prix Farms community for $17 million. The property, which includes 38 horse stalls, staff housing, and extensive equestrian facilities, was sold by an entity tied to Darin S. Goldstein. The transaction highlights the significant appreciation of equestrian properties in this sought-after enclave, with the seller realizing a substantial gain over their original purchase price.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: equestrian

Address: 3351 Grand Prix Farms Drive, Wellington, FL

Sale Price: $17.0M

Year Built: 2002

Site Area: 7.82 acres

Notable Features: One of the larger properties in Grand Prix Farms, includes 38 horse stalls, staff housing, manager's residence, groom's quarters, covered six-horse hot walker, grass Grand Prix field, and a large sand jumping ring. Located near Wellington International showgrounds.

Project Notes: Seller acquired the property in 1999 for $480,000. The farm generated seasonal rental income from its stalls, leasing for $23,000-$25,000 per stall during the Winter Equestrian Festival.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: Lion Forge Entertainment — David Steward II, CEO

SELLER: Darin S. Goldstein

SELLER BROKER: BitRealty — Ryan Beckett

BUYER BROKER: Engel & Volkers Wellington — Robert Scott Fitton
