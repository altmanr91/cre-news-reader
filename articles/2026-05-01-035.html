Q1 Office: Leasing Activity Increases, As the Pipeline Shrinks

Connect CRE — Fri, 01 May 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Office Report

The Q1 2026 office market reports indicate a stabilizing environment driven by shrinking inventory and increasing demand, particularly from large occupiers. Net absorption has largely remained positive across various reports, with vacancy rates consistently decreasing. While new office development continues to diminish from its 2019 peak, there is a growing appetite for new projects, though construction timelines may be extended due to labor demand. Analysts are also considering the long-term impact of artificial intelligence on the office sector, with a focus on task decomposition rather than widespread job displacement. Looking ahead, macroeconomic factors and inflationary pressures are expected to constrain supply further, leading to increased costs for top-tier space and a projected decline in net inventory by 2030.

Projected decline in net inventory by 2030: 50 million square feet
