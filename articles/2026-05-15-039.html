Cow Hollow home sells for biggest gap between asking and closing price in decades

SALE

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Thu, 14 May 2026 23:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

A six-bedroom home in San Francisco's Cow Hollow neighborhood recently sold for $15 million, nearly double its original asking price of approximately $8 million. This transaction represents the largest gap between asking and closing price for a San Francisco home above $5 million in at least 26 years, highlighting the intense bidding wars for trophy properties in the city. The quick sale, just over two weeks after listing, suggests an all-cash transaction, driven by a shrinking inventory of high-end homes and strong demand from ultrawealthy buyers, particularly those in the booming artificial intelligence sector.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Inventory of single-family homes in San Francisco remains thin, particularly at the high end, even as demand rebounds sharply. This scarcity has led to a 'mansion shortage' fueled by wealthy buyers, especially from the artificial intelligence sector. In April, 85% of San Francisco homes sold above asking, with the median sale closing 24.6% over list, and the city recorded 26 home sales above $5 million, including four over $10 million, all new records.
