Retail Q1: Low Availability and Construction Slow Absorption

Connect CRE — Fri, 15 May 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Retail Report

Retail market reports for Q1 2026 indicate negative net absorption rates across the board, primarily due to a wave of store closures from bankruptcies, though these spaces are quickly being re-leased due to steady demand. The construction pipeline continues to shrink, with new supply barely offsetting demolitions of obsolete retail properties. Flat rent growth and negative absorption are also attributed to market uncertainty, lingering inflation, and concerns about the job market and fuel costs. Despite these headwinds, retail sales and consumer spending remain positive, and analysts expect continued supply shortages to pressure rent growth, with Sun Belt markets outperforming due to population growth.

DATA POINTS

Gross deliveries Q1 2026: 7.8 million square feet

Demolitions Q1 2026: 2.6 million square feet

Net new supply Q1 2026: 5.2 million square feet

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Retail reports for Q1 2026 show negative net absorption rates, with the exception of CBRE's U.S. Retail Figures. A clear bifurcation persists in demand, with tight availability for small spaces and more modest availability for large anchor boxes. The construction pipeline is dwindling, with gross deliveries of 7.8 million square feet partially offset by 2.6 million square feet of demolitions, resulting in 5.2 million square feet of net new supply. Sun Belt markets are outpacing the rest of the country in retail metrics, driven by population growth.
