Resi players on the rise of luxury in the outer boroughs

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 07 May 2026 19:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Developers are increasingly focusing on luxury residential projects in New York City's outer boroughs, drawing buyers who prioritize neighborhood evolution and value over Manhattan's traditional appeal. The shift is driven by a desire for more square footage and evolving amenity preferences, with wellness offerings becoming standard. Developers are also navigating the challenges posed by the city's new 485-x tax abatement, which they argue makes all-affordable projects with prevailing wages economically unfeasible, leading to inefficient building designs.
