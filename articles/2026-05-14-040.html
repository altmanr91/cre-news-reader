The German pension fund that lost $1B on a risky U.S. real estate bet doesn’t want to talk about it

SALE

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Wed, 13 May 2026 15:59:50 +0000

SUMMARY

Germany's largest public pension fund, Bayerische Versorgungskammer (BVK), is embroiled in a legal dispute over its contentious exit from a U.S. real estate portfolio, including the Transamerica Pyramid in San Francisco. BVK is rejecting accusations in a lawsuit filed by Deutsche Finance America, which claims the pension fund stiffed it out of a $31.3 million early termination fee after selling the Transamerica Pyramid in March. Deutsche Finance America alleges that BVK paid its other partner, Michael Shvo, $79 million despite similar termination fee structures, raising questions about the deal's fairness. BVK, which invested nearly $2 billion into seven U.S. properties, estimates losses on these investments could exceed $1 billion and is attempting to distance itself from direct responsibility by citing a "fund-of-funds" investment structure.
