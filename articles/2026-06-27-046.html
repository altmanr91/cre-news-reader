Lawmakers Push To Make Data Center Developers Pay For Grid Upgrades

Bisnow News Feed — Fri, 26 Jun 2026 11:34:35 -0400

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

Lawmakers are debating the Ratepayer Protection Act, a bipartisan bill that would require data center developers to cover the costs of power grid upgrades necessitated by their projects. This initiative, backed by major tech companies, aims to prevent consumers from bearing the financial burden of increased energy demands from data center expansion. Concurrently, some progressive lawmakers are advocating for a temporary moratorium on all new data center construction until national safeguards and consumer protections are established, highlighting growing concerns over rising energy costs and infrastructure strain.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Moody's Ratings analysts warn that rapid infrastructure development for AI integration poses new credit risks for state and local governments, noting that tax policies for data centers may not deliver material revenue benefits and long-term economic benefits have typically been modest. Local moratoriums are pushing projects to shovel-ready locations, but power cost, internet connectivity, and natural disaster exposure remain key drivers for development decisions.
