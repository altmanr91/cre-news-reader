Pappas says taxpayer benefits “murky” for wherever Bears land new stadium

The Real Deal — Chicago — Wed, 27 May 2026 16:12:47 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

A report from the Cook County Treasurer's office casts doubt on the taxpayer benefits of proposed property tax incentives for a new Chicago Bears stadium, calling the benefits "murky." The analysis suggests that a payment in lieu of property taxes (PILOT) system, which the Bears have requested, would divert funds from schools and limit the expansion of the property tax base. The report estimates the team would receive disproportionate tax benefits compared to economic impact, despite the Bears' commitment to cover $2 billion in development costs. Governor JB Pritzker has defended the PILOT structure, emphasizing taxpayer protection in discussions.
