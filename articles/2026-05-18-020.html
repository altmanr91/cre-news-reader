The 'Whale Hunting' Era Of Data Center Development May Be Ending

Bisnow News Feed — Sun, 17 May 2026 09:47:22 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The data center industry is experiencing a shift from the development of remote, gigawatt-scale hyperscale campuses, often dubbed "bragawatts" due to their speculative nature, towards smaller facilities of 100 MW or less located closer to major population centers. This change is driven by the increasing demand for AI inference computing, which requires lower latency and proximity to end-users, as opposed to the training of large language models that fueled the initial boom. Industry leaders note that these smaller, "edge" data centers are more feasible to develop, often utilizing infill sites like former industrial plants with existing power infrastructure, allowing for quicker deployment and better grid connectivity.
