Plano greenlights “mini Reunion Tower” plan on future AT&T campus

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Texas — Wed, 27 May 2026 19:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

The Plano City Council has approved zoning for a 280-foot communication tower, designed to resemble Dallas's iconic Reunion Tower, at AT&T's new global headquarters campus in Plano. This approval is for a small portion of the 54-acre site, which will feature 2.3 million square feet of building space and parking. The telecom giant is targeting a move-in date in the second half of 2028, relocating from its current downtown Dallas lease.

Notable Features: Campus will include a 280-foot communication tower designed as a 'mini Reunion Tower' replica. Buildings will be no more than eight stories.

Project Notes: Plano offered a $20 million incentive package, including a 65% property tax rebate for 25 years, requiring AT&T to build amenity and retail space and occupy the building for the rebate duration. The proposed tower requires FAA approval and will not use overnight light effects. AT&T will vacate Whitacre Tower at 208 South Akard Street in Dallas, where its lease runs through 2031.
