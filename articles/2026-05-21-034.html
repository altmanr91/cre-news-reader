Master-planned community in store for Howe

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Texas — Wed, 20 May 2026 23:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Cherukuru Investments, led by Westlake investor Ravi Cherukuru, has acquired a 180-acre undeveloped tract in Howe, TX, with plans to develop a mixed-use retail and residential master-planned community. The land, located at the northeast corner of FM 902 and Bennett Road, was sold by the Hightower Family Trust, which had owned the property for 140 years. This acquisition continues the trend of significant development along the US-75 corridor in Dallas' far north, driven by factors such as the Texas Instruments semiconductor facility expansion in nearby Sherman.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: mixed-use

Site Area: 180 acres

Notable Features: Unimproved tract located outside Howe city limits but within the Howe Independent School District, approximately 23 miles south of Downtown McKinney and 58 miles from Downtown Dallas.

Project Notes: The land's tax appraisal value doubled from $1.9 million in 2022 to $3.8 million. A municipal utility district petition was filed on April 28 for the development.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: Cherukuru Investments — Ravi Cherukuru, Owner

SELLER: Hightower Family Trust

SELLER BROKER: Younger Partners — John St. Clair; Ethan Peck

SPONSOR PIPELINE

Bennett Road (south of current acquisition): 140 acres acquired last year

Van Zandt County: Acreage owned

Kaufman County: Acreage owned

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Greenfield sites along the US-75 corridor have experienced significant appreciation in recent years. Commercial development in the area includes Texas Instruments' $30 billion semiconductor facility expansion in Sherman, which is attracting residential investment throughout Grayson County.
