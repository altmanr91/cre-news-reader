SummerHill, Cypress plot San Jose office-to-resi redev with affordable apartments

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Thu, 18 Jun 2026 19:16:19 +0000

SUMMARY

SummerHill Homes and Cypress Equity Investments are under contract to acquire a 5.9-acre site in San Jose from LBA Realty, with plans to redevelop a vacant office building into a mixed-income residential community. The project will feature 373 affordable apartments and 73 market-rate townhouse-style condominiums, addressing the growing demand for housing in Silicon Valley driven by the influx of tech and AI workers. The affordable units will target households earning 60% of the area median income.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Address: 3550 North First Street, San Jose, CA

Units: 446

Site Area: 5.9 acres

Notable Features: Redevelopment of a vacant office building into a mixed-income residential community.

Project Notes: The 100% affordable apartment component will have an affordability target of 60% of the area median income. The site was previously acquired by an LBA affiliate in February 2025 for $18.5 million.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: SummerHill Homes — Kevin Ebrahimi, Senior Vice President of Development

BUYER: Cypress Equity Investments

SELLER: LBA Realty

SPONSOR PIPELINE

945 West Julian Street: Proposed eight-story building with 136 units on a half-acre site in San Jose.

1655 Berryessa Road: 260-unit affordable project known as Berryessa Family Apartments in San Jose.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Santa Clara County area median income is $143,850 for one person or $205,500 for a household of four. 60% of the area median income works out to roughly $86,310 for an individual renter or $123,300 for a four-person home. The site's assessed value was $32.4 million in January 2025.
