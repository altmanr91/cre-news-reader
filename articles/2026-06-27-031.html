Elme Communities liquidation roiled by scuttled sale

The Real Deal — Chicago — Fri, 26 Jun 2026 17:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Company News

Elme Communities' plan to liquidate its assets and dissolve has been complicated by the scuttled sale of its largest remaining property, the 1,222-unit Riverside Apartments in Alexandria, VA. The $280 million deal with a Beitel Group subsidiary fell through, prompting Elme to restart marketing the property. This setback raises concerns about Elme's ability to repay a $520 million term loan from Goldman Sachs, which was intended to finance the firm's dissolution. Elme has been liquidating its portfolio since August, including a significant sale to Cortland and the recent disposition of the Watergate 600 office building in Washington, DC, for $52.5 million.
