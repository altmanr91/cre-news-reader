Landlords tied to deadly Inwood fire amassed 1,300 violations across city

The Real Deal — New York — Tue, 12 May 2026 16:25:59 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

The owners of an Upper Manhattan apartment building where a fatal fire occurred last week have accumulated over 1,300 open housing violations across their New York City portfolio, prompting increased scrutiny on the city's enforcement against repeat-offender landlords. An investigation revealed 1,343 open violations at 10 buildings in Manhattan, Brooklyn, and Queens, with 406 classified as "immediately hazardous," including fire safety issues like non-functioning self-closing apartment doors, a condition cited at the Inwood property days before the blaze. The Department of Housing Preservation and Development has sued the landlords 16 times since 2020 for failing to correct dangerous conditions, with the Dyckman Street building already part of an enforcement program for persistently high violations. This controversy highlights the challenges of aging multifamily housing stock and fire safety compliance, as the city administration pledges tougher housing enforcement.
