If You Cannot Explain the Water, You Cannot Build the Data Center

Commercial Observer — Mon, 22 Jun 2026 13:00:41 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article argues that the data center industry must adopt greater transparency regarding water usage to overcome community opposition and regulatory scrutiny. The author contends that generalized water consumption figures are insufficient; instead, developers need to provide specific, localized data on water sources, expected consumption, and mitigation strategies in an easily understandable format. This transparency is crucial for building trust, securing project approvals, and driving better, more sustainable project design that aligns with local water conditions, rather than relying on broad claims or off-site offsets.
