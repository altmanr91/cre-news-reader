CBRE’s Scott Schnuckel On Why Retail Is Back in Favor

Commercial Observer — Wed, 20 May 2026 18:45:28 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

CBRE's managing director of retail for the Americas, Scott Schnuckel, discusses the resurgence of the retail real estate sector, attributing its strength to record-low vacancy rates and minimal new construction. He notes that the landlord community is benefiting from a long-term trend of limited new shopping center development, coupled with the demolition of older malls and a growth in retail brands, particularly value-oriented mass merchants, grocers, off-price retailers, and food and beverage concepts. While rent growth has been modest due to long-term lease contracts, investor demand is increasing, with retail becoming a favored sector for large funds seeking stability, especially for grocery-anchored and well-executed mixed-use projects. Schnuckel highlights that new construction is primarily occurring in high-growth Southern and West Coast markets like Phoenix, Texas, and Florida, where population expansion supports the development of new suburbs and incremental business for retailers.
