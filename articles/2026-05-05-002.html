Trinity and Sculptor Buy Oceanfront Resort on Florida’s Gulf Coast for $835M

ACQUISITION

Commercial Observer — Mon, 04 May 2026 22:00:52 +0000

SUMMARY

Trinity Investments and Sculptor Real Estate have acquired the JW Marriott Marco Island Beach Resort on Florida's Gulf Coast for $835 million. The transaction was financed with a $690 million CMBS loan originated by Wells Fargo and J.P. Morgan Chase. The resort features 809 hotel rooms, extensive event space, multiple restaurants, a spa, and two championship golf courses, representing a highly sought-after luxury beachfront asset with stable cash flows and significant upside potential.

Project Notes: The seller completed a $320 million renovation in 2018, adding an adults-only tower and rebranding the resort as a JW Marriott.
