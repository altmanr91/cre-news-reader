L3 Capital reportedly scooping up Palmolive Building retail space

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Chicago — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 18:07:47 +0000

SUMMARY

L3 Capital is reportedly acquiring the retail condominium portion of the Palmolive Building in Chicago's Gold Coast from Nuveen. The transaction includes the space currently occupied by Louis Vuitton and other retail tenants. Nuveen had been marketing the 51,795-square-foot retail space, which it acquired in 2012 for $90 million. This acquisition aligns with L3 Capital's strategy of transforming Gold Coast retail properties, as evidenced by their recent redevelopment of a former bank branch into a Skims store.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Gold Coast retail market is shifting from exclusive luxury brands to more accessible luxury. The Magnificent Mile retail market is rebounding, with new tenants moving into North Michigan Avenue. The former John Hancock Center is being redeveloped into a Marriott International Edition-branded hotel, and Water Tower Place is being converted into office space.
