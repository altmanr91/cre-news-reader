The Yield Is Real but So Is the Risk Premium You Are Being Asked to Accept

Connect CRE — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 21:47:54 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article analyzes the current landscape of U.S. high-yield bonds, noting their leading position in trailing one-year yields among major asset classes, with JNK posting 6.59%. While approximately half of major asset classes are generating positive real yields above the 3.8% consumer inflation rate, the author cautions investors to consider payout sustainability, price volatility, and total return expectations. Historically, high yields have often preceded strong total return periods, but the credit cycle and default rates are critical variables. The current modest risk premium of 160 basis points between high-yield bonds and 30-year Treasuries suggests a narrower margin of safety, as the market may not have fully priced in slowing growth, sticky inflation, and geopolitical impacts. The author recommends a barbell approach within fixed income, favoring investment-grade corporate credit and agency mortgage-backed securities, alongside a selective, quality-biased allocation to high yield, emphasizing BB-rated issues.
