In Business — and in Life — Persistence Is a Major Competitive Advantage

Commercial Observer — Thu, 04 Jun 2026 11:02:57 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Editorial

This opinion piece argues that persistence is a crucial competitive advantage in business and life, particularly in commercial real estate. The author uses the "Stonecutter's Creed" analogy to illustrate that success often appears sudden to observers but is, in fact, the cumulative result of countless small, consistent efforts over time. The central argument is that many people abandon sound processes too soon, mistaking a lack of visible progress for a lack of actual progress. The author emphasizes that identifying the right activities and committing to a sound process, followed by consistent execution, is key to achieving extraordinary results.
