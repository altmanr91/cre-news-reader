BGO Gives Up On Nontraded Industrial REIT That Never Drew Buyers

Bisnow News Feed — Fri, 15 May 2026 10:54:55 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / REIT Performance

BGO has terminated its nontraded REIT, BGO Industrial Real Estate Income Trust (IREIT), nearly three years after its launch, having failed to attract any outside investors. The IREIT, seeded with a 56.5% interest in a 9.4M SF industrial portfolio, had $197.3M in total assets at the end of 2025 but incurred over $3M in losses in each of the last two years. The decision to terminate comes as the portfolio's assets face upcoming loan maturity dates and amid a challenging environment for nontraded REITs, which saw redemption caps by major players like Blackstone and Starwood.

DATA POINTS

BGO IREIT Total Assets (End of 2025): $197.3M

BGO IREIT Portfolio Valuation: $971M

BGO IREIT Annual Losses (Last Two Years): Over $3M each year

Blackstone Real Estate Income Trust Q1 Capital Raise: $1.2B

Starwood Real Estate Income Trust Outstanding Redemption Requests (End of 2025): Nearly $1B

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Nontraded REIT fundraising experienced a period of decline, with major players like Blackstone Real Estate Income Trust (BREIT) and Starwood Real Estate Income Trust (SREIT) capping redemptions due to investor concerns over commercial real estate valuations. BREIT has since cleared its backlog and saw its highest quarterly capital raise in three years in Q1, while SREIT continues to face significant outstanding redemption requests.
