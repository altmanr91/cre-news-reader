Dinner and a show: Bill Fuller buys Fort Lauderdale’s Jungle Queen, Tropical Isle

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Miami — Mon, 29 Jun 2026 18:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Hospitality entrepreneur and developer Bill Fuller has acquired the historic Jungle Queen Riverboat and Tropical Isle attraction in Fort Lauderdale, FL, from the Faber family, who operated it for nearly 70 years. The transaction, valued at over $5 million, includes the Jungle Queen and River Queen boats, along with the Tropical Isle waterfront property which features a restaurant and performance space. Fuller, known for his commitment to historic preservation, plans to upgrade and enhance the attraction while maintaining its legacy as a key part of South Florida's tourism landscape.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: hospitality

Property Name: Jungle Queen Riverboat and Tropical Isle

Sale Price: $5.0M

Notable Features: Iconic tourist attraction launched in 1935, offering daily sightseeing tours, dinner cruises, live shows, and wildlife access. Departs from Marina Village at Bahia Mar.

Project Notes: The Faber family operated the attraction for nearly 70 years. The acquisition includes the Jungle Queen and River Queen boats, as well as the Tropical Isle waterfront property.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: Barlington Group — Bill Fuller, Co-founder and Managing Partner

BUYER: Mad Room Hospitality — Bill Fuller, Co-founder and Managing Partner

SELLER: Faber family
