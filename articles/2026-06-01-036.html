In the AI boom, even San Francisco’s Class A buildings need renovations

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Sun, 31 May 2026 16:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Lone Star Funds, which acquired the debt and subsequently the ownership of the 20-story, 360,000-square-foot office tower at 600 California Street in San Francisco, has partnered with Harvest Properties. Harvest Properties has purchased an equity stake in the building and plans significant renovations, including a full-floor amenity space spec buildout, to attract tenants in the burgeoning artificial intelligence sector. The building was only 25 percent occupied at the time of the partnership announcement, highlighting the need for upgrades to compete in the current market.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The AI leasing boom is driving demand for pre-furnished offices with extensive amenity spaces in San Francisco, prompting landlords of even trophy assets like Salesforce Tower to consider spec suite buildouts. Competitors are reportedly spending up to $40 million on full-floor renovations to attract new tenants. The AI frenzy has also tightened the San Francisco apartment market, with median one-bedroom rents crossing $4,000 per month and increasing 21% year-over-year, while national rents remained flat or slightly decreased. California Assembly Bill 1903, which aims to reform condo defect liability laws to encourage more condo development, has passed the Assembly and is moving to the Senate.
