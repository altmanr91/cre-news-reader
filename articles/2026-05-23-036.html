Sutter Health to build tallest tower in Emeryville with updated hospital plan

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Fri, 22 May 2026 19:39:29 +0000

SUMMARY

Sutter Health has unveiled an updated, larger vision for its planned Emeryville Medical Center, a $1 billion project that will feature a 17-story, 780,000-square-foot hospital with 325 beds. This development, which will become Emeryville's tallest building, is part of Sutter's strategy to transform the 12-acre former Emery Yards biotech campus into a regional medical hub. The project is intended to replace the aging Alta Bates Summit Medical Center campus in Berkeley, which is slated to close in 2030 due to seismic standards. Construction is expected to begin in 2029 with an opening targeted for 2033.
