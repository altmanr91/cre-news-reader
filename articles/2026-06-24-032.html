Austin lands another AI tenant as Partly moves HQ from New Zealand

The Real Deal — Texas — Tue, 23 Jun 2026 20:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Relocation

Partly, an AI company specializing in the automotive sector, is relocating its headquarters from New Zealand to Austin, TX. The company plans to significantly expand its workforce in the coming years and is actively seeking 20,000 to 30,000 square feet of downtown office space. The move is supported by a recent $50 million Series B funding round and is driven by Austin's proximity to "real world businesses" and a strong talent pool, including graduates from the University of Texas and Bay Area transplants.

DATA POINTS

Austin office space planned or under construction (early 2026): 1.7 million square feet

Proportion of pre-leased Austin construction pipeline tenants that are tech companies (early 2026): One-third

TENANTS AT PROPERTY

Partly

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

While the influx of tech companies to Texas has slowed, AI-based companies may be an exception, bolstered by the presence of tech giants like Google and Nvidia. Austin's office market had 1.7 million square feet of space planned or under construction at the beginning of the year, with tech companies accounting for one-third of pre-leased projects.
