Can the Hamptons keep its hot streak?

SALE

The Real Deal — New York — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 11:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

The Hamptons luxury residential market experienced a strong start to 2026, building on momentum from late 2025, with transactions above $5 million achieving a record share of deals. The median sale price for luxury homes in the first quarter of 2026 jumped 30 percent to $13 million, and sales of $10 million or more generated $560 million in volume. Notable transactions included the $58 million sale of an oceanfront mansion in Bridgehampton to Boston Celtics co-owner Wyc Grousbeck and his wife, Emilia Fazzalari, and the $72 million sale of an East Hampton estate by Ann Tenenbaum, marking the most expensive closing of the year so far. Despite some broker skepticism about sustained pace due to macroeconomic factors, the market continues to see significant high-value deals, with the rental market also showing signs of recovery after two slower summers.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Transactions above $5 million scored a record share of deals in the Hamptons during the first three months of 2026. The Hamptons rental market is showing signs of regaining momentum after two sluggish summers, with some agents reporting increased lease activity for the upcoming summer season.
