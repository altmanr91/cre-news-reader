65-Year-Old FREIT To Liquidate

Bisnow News Feed — Fri, 15 May 2026 11:30:21 -0400

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

The First Real Estate Investment Trust of New Jersey (FREIT), a REIT incorporated in 1961, has announced a plan of voluntary liquidation, which will involve selling off its entire portfolio of assets. The portfolio includes six apartment complexes, five shopping centers, and three parcels of vacant land in New Jersey, as well as a residential property in Middletown, New York. After liabilities are paid, net proceeds will be distributed to stockholders, with payments estimated between $24.44 and $30.03 per share, representing a significant premium over its recent stock price. The company's CEO noted pride in its legacy of consistent returns and expressed optimism about returning capital to stockholders in the current real estate environment.

DATA POINTS

Estimated per-share payout range: $24.44 - $30.03

Closing stock price on May 13: $15.25 per share

FFO in 2025: $7M ($0.94 per share)

FFO in 2024: $4.6M ($0.61 per share)

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Other publicly traded landlords, including Elme Communities and Stratus Properties, have also announced liquidation plans, citing undervaluation in the current market. Many public real estate companies are being taken private or merging with competitors, while fewer companies are pursuing IPOs due to sustained private capital growth.
