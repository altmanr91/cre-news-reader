Mamdani housing official calls RGB “very blunt tool”

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 06 May 2026 20:54:29 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

A top housing official in the Mamdani administration, Leila Bozorg, acknowledged that New York City's Rent Guidelines Board (RGB) uses a "very blunt tool" by applying uniform rent increases across the diverse rent-stabilized housing stock. While not directly endorsing differentiated rent increases based on building characteristics like age, Bozorg indicated a need for a more holistic approach, including city investment in programs like an insurance initiative for affordable and rent-stabilized buildings, to support owners in covering rising costs. This comes as both the administration and landlord groups are recognizing the financial distress in a growing portion of older rent-stabilized buildings, with landlord associations advocating for split rent orders to address the higher maintenance needs of pre-1974 properties.
