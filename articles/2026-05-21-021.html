Hotel union deal puts cleaners on track for $100K salaries

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 20 May 2026 21:44:56 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

A tentative eight-year labor agreement between the Hotel and Gaming Trades Council and the New York City hotel industry is set to significantly increase wages for nearly 30,000 workers across more than 250 hotels. The deal, which includes over 50 percent average wage increases, will see housekeepers' hourly pay rise from approximately $40 to over $61 by 2034, enabling them to earn over $100,000 annually by the sixth year of the contract. This agreement comes as hotel owners already face rising operating costs from the Safe Hotels Act and a constrained new supply pipeline due to strict permitting rules, with industry leaders acknowledging the deal's substantial impact on their bottom line but deeming it necessary to avert a strike before the 2026 FIFA World Cup.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Manhattan's average hotel room rates were almost $334 in 2025, the highest among major U.S. markets. New York City had 204 union hotels out of 785 total in 2025. Hotel occupancy rates are in the 85 percent range.
