SF condo prices in tony neighborhoods buck wider Bay Area value slump

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Mon, 04 May 2026 19:20:47 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Condo Report

San Francisco's condominium market is experiencing a notable shift, with typical condo values increasing in eight Bay Area ZIP codes, all located within the city. This marks a reversal after approximately five years of declines, driven by a new wave of wealth from the artificial intelligence sector and constrained supply. While gains are modest, topping out at nearly 4 percent in areas like Noe Valley and the Marina District, they signal a change in momentum, with investors also acquiring units for rental conversions as rents rise. This demand is creating sharper competition for available units, particularly in higher-end segments and properties with direct street access.

DATA POINTS

Bay Area ZIP codes with condo value increases (March 2024-March 2025): 8 (all in San Francisco)

Highest year-over-year condo value gain (94114 ZIP code): 3.9%

Second highest year-over-year condo value gain (94123 ZIP code): 3.8%

Condo value decline in some Oakland neighborhoods (past year): Up to 18%

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Typical condo values in the Bay Area generally remain flat or are falling, burdened by high homeowner association dues, insurance costs, and tighter lending standards. San Francisco is an exception due to an influx of high-earning tech workers from the AI boom, leading to increased demand and a 'mansion shortage' in the single-family market. Many owners are reluctant to sell at a loss after the pandemic-era downturn, contributing to limited inventory and increased competition for available units.
