Developers piece together opportunity in NYC’s rezeoned Garment District

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — New York — Mon, 04 May 2026 11:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

A partnership between Marty Burger and Andrew Heiberger acquired a 12-story office building at 29 West 35th Street in New York City's Garment District for $25 million. The developers plan to convert the property into a 107-unit apartment complex, with 27 units designated as affordable. The acquisition and planned conversion were contingent on the Midtown South rezoning, which passed in August 2025, allowing for increased residential density in the area. The project is also leveraging the 467m tax break, which is crucial for the financial viability of office-to-residential conversions in the current market.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Midtown South rezoning, passed in August 2025, transformed 42 blocks in central Manhattan to allow for 10,000 units of housing, increasing floor-area ratios from 12 to up to 18. However, developers face challenges due to construction costs, tight margins, and higher wage requirements under the 485x state tax program for multifamily projects over 100 units. The 467m tax break for office-to-residential conversions is seen as a critical incentive. Asking rents for dated office buildings in the Garment District are around $60.49 per square foot, half the cost of trophy offices in Hudson Yards.
