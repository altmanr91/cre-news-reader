Supply skeptic advances in SF congressional race amid developer pushback

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 20:01:20 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article analyzes the ongoing debate in San Francisco's congressional race regarding housing affordability, highlighting the differing approaches of candidates Connie Chan and Scott Wiener. Chan, a supply skeptic, advocates for subsidized housing and federal tax credits, arguing that market-rate development fails to address affordability for working families. Wiener, conversely, supports supply-and-demand economics and deregulation to streamline housing production, a stance echoed by several nonprofit affordable housing developers. These developers emphasize the need for an "all-of-the-above" strategy, acknowledging the necessity of subsidized housing while also stressing that increased overall housing supply is crucial to mitigate rising prices, especially given the Bay Area's economic growth.
