National Juneteenth Museum is finally coming to Fort Worth, home of civil rights icon Opal Lee

CONSTRUCTION

The Real Deal — Texas — Sun, 21 Jun 2026 14:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

The National Juneteenth Museum in Fort Worth, dedicated to civil rights icon Opal Lee, is moving forward with construction slated to begin in late 2026 or early 2027. The $70 million project, which has secured $50 million in funding including significant pledges from the city and state, will feature galleries, a theater, a business incubator, and a food hall. The museum aims to honor the legacy of Juneteenth and its "grandmother," Opal Lee, who tirelessly campaigned for its recognition as a national holiday.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: museum

Property Name: National Juneteenth Museum

Total Project Cost: $70.0M

Completion: late 2026 or early 2027

Notable Features: Will include 10,000-square-foot galleries, a 250-seat theater, a business incubator, and a food hall.

Project Notes: The Southside Community Center will be demolished on October 7 to make way for the museum. $50 million has been raised for the project, including a $15 million pledge from the city and a $10 million pledge from the state.
