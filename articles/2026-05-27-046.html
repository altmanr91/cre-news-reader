Canada Used To Send The U.S. Half Its Real Estate Money. No More

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 26 May 2026 15:29:27 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Canadian institutional investment in U.S. commercial real estate has significantly declined, with transaction volume falling 32% year-over-year to $5 billion for the 12 months ending in March. This represents less than half the average annual investment from Canada over the past five years. The shift is attributed to political tensions, including tariffs and rhetoric from the White House, which have encouraged Canadian investors to diversify their global portfolios away from the U.S. While the U.S. remains an attractive market for its relative stability compared to other global regions, the push for diversification among Canadian investors is expected to be a lasting trend.

DATA POINTS

Canada-to-U.S. CRE transaction volume (12 months ending March): $5B

Year-over-year decline in Canada-to-U.S. CRE transaction volume: 32%

Canadian capital allocation to U.S. assets (last year): 37%

Canadian capital allocation to U.S. assets (prior year): 54%

Total Canadian capital raised to invest abroad (last year): $13.4B

Canadian investment in American assets (2019-early 2025): $73B

Total foreign investment in U.S. real estate (year-over-year through March): 24% increase to $25.9B
