From adviser to adversary: A timeline of Floyd Mayweather’s relationship with Jona Rechnitz

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 28 May 2026 17:49:29 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Floyd Mayweather Jr. has filed a lawsuit against his former investment manager, Jona Rechnitz, alleging fraud and the diversion of $175 million. The lawsuit claims Rechnitz misrepresented the terms of several real estate deals Mayweather pursued in New York City, Chicago, and Miami, including a purported $402 million acquisition of a 1,000-unit affordable rental portfolio in New York City and a $100 million investment in luxury Manhattan rentals. Mayweather alleges he only acquired a minority interest in the affordable housing portfolio and made a small, later liquidated, equity investment in the luxury rentals, contrary to Rechnitz's assurances. The lawsuit also claims Mayweather failed to close on a stake in the former Versace Mansion in Miami Beach and that Rechnitz diverted $15 million related to an investment in SL Green.
