Multifamily Starts Tumble 40% As Housing Construction Hits 6-Year Low

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 11:05:12 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / Housing Starts Report

New multifamily construction starts experienced a significant decline last month, falling 40.2% from April and reaching an annualized pace of 295,000 units, a 14.2% decrease from May 2025. This contributed to overall housing starts hitting a six-year low at less than 1.2 million units annually, far below economists' projections. The downturn is attributed to persistent financing challenges, elevated interest rates, and rising construction and property insurance costs, leading builders to remain cautious despite some resilience in the Midwest.
