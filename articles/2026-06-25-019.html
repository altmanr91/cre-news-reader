The primary winners poised to shake up real estate

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 24 Jun 2026 21:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Political & Policy

The recent primary elections in New York's state Senate and Assembly saw a significant shift towards progressive candidates, particularly those endorsed by the Democratic Socialists of America and the Working Families Party. These wins are expected to profoundly influence city and state real estate policy, with a strong emphasis on tenants' rights and housing affordability. Key outcomes include David Orkin's victory in Assembly District 38 in Queens, where he campaigned on expanding good cause eviction protections and supporting a Tenant Opportunity to Purchase Act. Similarly, Eon Huntley's win in Assembly District 58 in Brooklyn, despite significant real estate industry backing for his opponent, signals a push for social housing initiatives and further tenant protections. These results indicate a growing political momentum against developer influence and a focus on addressing housing affordability challenges across New York City.

DATA POINTS

Assembly District 38 Election Result: David Orkin won with nearly 59% of the vote, beating incumbent Jenifer Rajkumar by 19 points.

Assembly District 58 Election Result: Eon Huntley beat incumbent Stefani Zinerman by 22 points.
