Westsider trails SF’s Kim for insurance commissioner, with ripples for resi market likely

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Wed, 03 Jun 2026 18:46:27 +0000

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Political & Crime News

Preliminary election results indicate that former San Francisco Supervisor Jane Kim is likely to advance to the November runoff for California insurance commissioner, while real estate industry-backed candidate Patrick Wolff appears to be out of the running. Kim, who received nearly $30,000 from real estate industry players, campaigned on penalizing insurance carriers for delayed payments and advocating for single-payer statewide disaster insurance policies. The race has gained significant attention due to the 2025 fires in Los Angeles, which severely impacted California's insurance market, leading to concerns over the cost and availability of homeowners insurance.
