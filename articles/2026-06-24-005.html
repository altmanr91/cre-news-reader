New York Developer Seeks to Build 296 Units in Flushing, Queens

DEVELOPMENT

Commercial Observer — Tue, 23 Jun 2026 19:59:29 +0000

SUMMARY

Developer Gary Chen has submitted a zoning change application to the New York City Department of City Planning for a proposed 10-story, mixed-use project in Flushing, Queens. The development would feature 296 residential units, with 75 designated as permanently affordable, alongside ground-floor commercial space and parking. Chen, who acquired the four parcels for the project in 2024 for $26.5 million, aims to transform an underutilized site into much-needed housing and employment opportunities in a transit-accessible area.

DATA POINTS
