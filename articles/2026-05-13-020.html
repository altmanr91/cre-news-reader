Resort project in Catskills roiled by environmental review

The Real Deal — New York — Tue, 12 May 2026 19:07:35 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

A New York State Supreme Court Justice has overturned the Cairo Planning Board's "negative declaration" for a proposed resort development at Blackhead Mountain, ruling in favor of residents and environmental groups who argued for a more thorough environmental review. The decision invalidates the final site plan approval for the project, which aims to transform the existing property into a 66,000-square-foot resort and spa with 127 units. Opponents have consistently raised concerns about the project's scale, its potential impact on water and energy resources, and its effect on the local hamlet's character.
