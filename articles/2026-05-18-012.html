A Brooklyn dealmaker’s legal avalanche

The Real Deal — New York — Sun, 17 May 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Brooklyn dealmaker Bert I. Dweck of Dweck Group is facing a series of seven lawsuits in New York state court, seeking at least $14.4 million. The lawsuits allege a pattern of unpaid loans, diverted escrow funds, and checks tied to frozen or nonexistent accounts, painting a picture of a slow-motion collapse rather than routine business disputes. Investors claim Dweck pitched acquisitions, collected deposits, or partnership capital, but then failed to close deals or return funds, with some allegations including fraudulent documents and improper release of escrow money. Lenders also allege defaults on significant loans and attempts at repayment with bounced checks and failed transfers.
