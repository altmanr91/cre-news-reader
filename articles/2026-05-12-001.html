Jeremy Moss of Silverstein Properties: 5 Questions

Commercial Observer — Mon, 11 May 2026 19:35:57 +0000

SUMMARY

Feature / Profile

This article is an interview with Jeremy Moss, Executive Vice President at Silverstein Properties, discussing the company's recent leasing activity and the broader office market in Lower Manhattan. Moss highlights the significant lease deal with American Express for the entire 2 World Trade Center building, emphasizing Lower Manhattan's advantages for corporate headquarters due to its extensive public transportation network and diverse tenant mix. He also notes the positive trends in the Lower Manhattan office market, including decreasing vacancy rates and increasing rents, driven by an influx of young professionals and the appeal of new construction.

DATA POINTS

Lower Manhattan office vacancy (post-pandemic peak): above 20 percent

Lower Manhattan office vacancy (current): high teens

Lower Manhattan Class A and B office rent increase: 5 to 10 percent

World Trade Center office rent increase since pandemic: 30 percent

World Trade Center current office rental rates: over $100 per square foot

World Trade Center campus occupancy: 95 percent leased

TENANTS AT PROPERTY

American Express

SPONSOR PIPELINE

55 Broad Street: Office-to-residential conversion project

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Lower Manhattan's office market has diversified from predominantly financial services and law tenants to include technology, professional services, and creative companies. The area benefits from its extensive public transportation network, offering the shortest commute for the most neighborhoods in New York City. Office-to-residential conversions have contributed to the increased value of remaining office space in Lower Manhattan.
