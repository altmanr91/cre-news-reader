Cornell sues Graduate hotel after lease default on Roosevelt Island

Latest New York Real Estate News — Thu, 30 Apr 2026 20:03:12 +0000

SUMMARY

Legal Dispute / Lease Default

Cornell University has filed a lawsuit against the Graduate by Hilton hotel on Roosevelt Island, alleging default on its 65-year ground lease and refusal to vacate the premises. The university claims the hotel ceased operations in November 2025, violating a lease stipulation requiring continuous operation, and failed to pay utility services. Cornell is seeking to eject the hotel and recover monetary damages, including $605,000 in lost conference space rental revenue. This legal action follows a separate lawsuit filed by lender ACRES Capital against the hotel's owner, AJ Capital, to recover $79 million.
