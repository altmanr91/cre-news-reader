Inside San Francisco’s biggest developer unraveling

The Real Deal — National — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 12:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

A prominent San Francisco Bay Area developer, Ken Mattson, is expected to plead guilty to wire fraud in connection with a $100 million Ponzi scheme that defrauded hundreds of investors, many of them retirees and fellow churchgoers. Mattson allegedly raised money for property acquisitions through off-book investments in subsidiaries that owned apartment buildings, promising regular returns. Instead, he diverted funds for personal use, including luxury homes and cars. The scheme unraveled when his business partner, Timothy LeFever, alerted authorities, leading to Mattson's arrest and a proposed 12-year prison sentence.
