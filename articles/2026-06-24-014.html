Rent control measure slashed from Massachusetts ballot

The Real Deal — National — Tue, 23 Jun 2026 19:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

The Massachusetts Supreme Judicial Court has blocked a statewide rent control ballot measure from appearing before voters in November, citing a religious exemption within the proposal as unconstitutional. This decision marks a victory for landlord-backed coalitions who argued against the measure, which would have allowed municipalities to cap annual rent increases at 5 percent. Opponents of rent control celebrated the ruling, while proponents indicated they might attempt to reintroduce the measure in the future. The proposed initiative, supported by housing advocates, faced criticism from industry groups and an economic analysis suggesting it could significantly reduce property values.
