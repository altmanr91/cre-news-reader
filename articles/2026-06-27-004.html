Manhattan Subleasing Tightens as AI Firms Fuel Office Growth in Q2

Commercial Observer — Fri, 26 Jun 2026 17:16:13 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Office Report

Manhattan's office market is experiencing a significant tightening of sublease inventory, driven primarily by robust demand from artificial intelligence and technology companies. Preliminary Q2 2026 data from JLL indicates that sublease availability has dropped to below 11 million square feet, a substantial reduction from its late-2022 peak. This absorption is concentrated in high-quality spaces, particularly in Midtown South, which is emerging as a tech and innovation hub. The increased leasing activity by AI firms, alongside financial services and law firms, is prompting landlords to reclaim sublease space for direct deals at higher rents, contributing to a quarter-over-quarter dip in overall office availability.

DATA POINTS

Manhattan sublease inventory: below 11 million square feet

Late-2022 peak sublease inventory: approximately 23 million square feet

Manhattan leasing volume (year-to-date 2026): 17 million square feet
