Housing Notes: Greenspan made the Fed famous

The Real Deal — National — Tue, 23 Jun 2026 15:42:41 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This opinion piece reflects on the legacy of former Federal Reserve Chairman Alan Greenspan, who recently passed away at 100. The author argues that Greenspan's policy mix of low short-term interest rates, a hands-off regulatory approach, and public encouragement of complex mortgage products significantly fueled the 2000s housing boom, increased leverage, and exacerbated the subsequent bust. The author also critiques the over-obsession with interest rates as the primary gauge of monetary policy, a trend Greenspan helped cement, and suggests that the lack of regulatory oversight during his tenure continues to distort the U.S. housing market.
