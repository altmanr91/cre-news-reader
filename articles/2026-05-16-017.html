House knocks build-to-rent provision from amended Senate bill

The Real Deal — National — Fri, 15 May 2026 17:06:31 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The House of Representatives has released an amended version of the 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act, notably removing a controversial provision that would have required institutional investors to sell build-to-rent homes within seven years of construction. This change is seen as a potential victory for the build-to-rent sector, which had experienced a freeze in investment due to the original provision. While restrictions on institutional investors in the single-family housing market remain, the definition of what constitutes such a home has been narrowed. The bill now faces a floor vote in the House, with its ultimate fate dependent on Senate approval.
