The Can Gets Heavier: Texas multifamily hopes for shift in capital

REJournals — Mon, 08 Jun 2026 09:57:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Multifamily owners in Texas are facing increased pressure to sell or refinance assets as capital markets continue to favor a "higher for longer" interest rate environment, despite earlier hopes for rate cuts. While operating fundamentals are showing uneven improvement across submarkets, refinancing challenges are primarily driving transaction activity, with many sales involving smoothly operating properties burdened by distressed capital stacks rather than operational distress. The market is characterized by significant submarket divergence, with some areas experiencing rent growth and strong absorption, while others, particularly in North Dallas, continue to offer substantial concessions. Buyers are increasingly focused on yield-on-cost frameworks and current asset production rather than theoretical stabilization values, with assumable low-rate debt emerging as a key selling point for properties.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Austin reported its first quarter of rent growth since Q2 2023. San Antonio's annual absorption has outpaced deliveries for two straight years. San Antonio cap rates are running approximately 25 basis points higher than Austin. North Dallas, which accounted for roughly half of DFW units under construction, has six to eight weeks of free rent quoted as standard across northern Collin County and North Fort Worth.
