Blackstone hits Ashland Greene with $177M foreclosure on DFW apartment portfolio

LOAN

Texas - The Real Deal — Thu, 30 Apr 2026 15:38:41 +0000

SUMMARY

Blackstone has initiated foreclosure proceedings on a four-property multifamily portfolio in Dallas-Fort Worth owned by Ashland Greene, citing a default on a $177 million loan. The portfolio, totaling 1,530 units across four apartment communities, is scheduled for auction if a loan modification agreement is not reached. Ashland Greene acquired these properties in January 2022, near the market peak, and has been impacted by rising interest rates and increased supply, despite utilizing a now-closed tax exemption program for three of the assets.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Size: 1,530 units

Loan Amount: $177.0M

Loan Per Unit: $115,686/unit (calculated)

Notable Features: Portfolio includes Mateo Apartment Homes, Birch Apartment Homes, Hawk Apartment Homes, and Knowlton Apartment Homes.

Project Notes: Ashland Greene acquired the properties in January 2022. Three properties (Birch, Hawk, Knowlton) utilized a 'traveling' housing finance corporation loophole for property tax exemptions, which was closed by House Bill 21 in May 2025.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

SPONSOR: Ashland Greene — Shakti C’Ganti

LENDER: Blackstone

OWNER: Pecos Housing Finance Corporation
