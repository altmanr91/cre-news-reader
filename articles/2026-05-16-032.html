As Texas data center gold rush reaches fever pitch, so does local resentment

The Real Deal — Texas — Fri, 15 May 2026 19:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The rapid expansion of data center development in Texas, particularly in Caldwell County, is generating significant local opposition despite an estimated $20 billion in investment over the next five years. Local officials and residents are concerned about the strain on water and the state's electrical grid, with data centers accounting for 87% of new large load connection requests to ERCOT. While some local governments are pushing back, the state legislature, which will not reconvene until next year, shows little inclination to regulate the AI-driven development boom, leaving communities with limited recourse.

DATA POINTS

Estimated investment in Caldwell County data centers: $20 billion

Number of confirmed data centers in Caldwell County: 4

Large loads seeking new ERCOT connections: 410 gigawatts

Percentage of new ERCOT connection requests for data centers: 87%
