Lee & Associates’ Report: Industrial and Multifamily Slow, Office Recovers, Retail Demand Holds

REBusinessOnline — Fri, 29 May 2026 16:52:19 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Q1 Report

Lee & Associates' Q1 2026 North America Market report indicates a nuanced commercial real estate landscape. Industrial and multifamily sectors are experiencing slow absorption due to a surge in new supply, leading to near-zero rent growth in industrial. The office market is showing a modest, albeit geographically uneven, recovery driven by demand for premium space, while retail continues to face a shortage of quality inventory. The report highlights divergent market performances, with some Sun Belt and Midwest industrial markets facing prolonged high availability, while specific cities like New York, Dallas, Houston, and San Francisco are seeing positive office absorption.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

US industrial net absorption in Q1 2026 was the lowest in over a decade, aside from Q2 2020. The overall industrial vacancy rate has nearly doubled since 2022. Leasing volume for industrial, excluding renewals, has risen above the prior two-year average in only about a third of markets surveyed. Small industrial spaces (under 50,000 SF) have a sub-5% vacancy rate, near pre-pandemic levels, and were listed for less than five months last year. US office net absorption in Q1 2026 was weak historically but validated a surge in demand in H2 2025, halting a six-year COVID slide that saw 215 million SF of negative net absorption. Divergent performance across major office markets is unusual, as typically at least 75% of major cities trend in the same direction.
