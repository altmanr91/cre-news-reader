How Mamdani cut $78B from housing plan, yet added units

The Real Deal — New York — Tue, 16 Jun 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Mayor Zohran Mamdani's updated housing plan aims to create and preserve 400,000 affordable housing units over 10 years with a five-year spending commitment of $22 billion, a significant reduction from his initial $100 billion campaign promise. The revised strategy marks a pivot towards leveraging the private sector for financing and development, relying on market-rate units to subsidize affordable housing rather than extensive public borrowing and union labor. The plan emphasizes upzoning, office conversions, and redeveloping city-owned and publicly controlled sites, while also continuing the use of for-profit firms for public housing renovation and management. The mayor's approach acknowledges that housing scarcity drives gentrification and displacement, aligning with a supply-side strategy to address the city's affordability crisis.
