Housing Notes: Rent history now matters more than your plastic

The Real Deal — National — Tue, 12 May 2026 15:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article discusses the ongoing credit score modernization initiative by the FHFA, which introduces new models like VantageScore 4.0 and FICO Score 10T to replace the Classic FICO model. The author, Jonathan Miller, argues that these new models, by incorporating rent payment history and trended credit data, will make mortgage lending more inclusive and potentially open the market to 5 to 10 million new borrowers. He highlights the significant cost difference, with VantageScore being substantially cheaper, and suggests this could lead to VantageScore dominating the market. While acknowledging the potential benefits of modernization, Miller expresses skepticism about other recent FHFA initiatives, viewing them as poorly conceived or politically motivated.
