Sterling’s 144K sf retail-to-industrial conversion nabs Fremont city approval

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 19:59:45 +0000

SUMMARY

Sterling Organization received unanimous approval from the Fremont City Council to redevelop a former Fry's Electronics store at 43800 Osgood Road into a 143,800-square-foot industrial facility. The Florida-based firm plans an $18 million renovation, with total investment, including the 2024 acquisition of the 11-acre property for $36 million, expected to reach nearly $65 million. This conversion addresses the strong demand for manufacturing space in Fremont, a key industrial hub in the Bay Area.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Fremont accounts for 75% of advanced manufacturing leasing activity in Silicon Valley. Fremont and Newark combined have 38 million square feet of industrial space, nearly three times that of Santa Clara. In Q1, Fremont and Newark saw over 600,000 square feet of leasing activity, with technology and AI companies driving 53% of active tenant requirements in Silicon Valley, primarily for spaces over 100,000 square feet.
