East Bay hotel finds buyer after foreclosure in region’s latest distressed lodging sale

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Fri, 08 May 2026 21:15:00 +0000

SUMMARY

A group led by Santa Clara-based investor Ashok Goyal acquired the 112-room Hyatt Place Newark/Silicon Valley hotel in Newark, CA for nearly $19.8 million. The property was seized by State Bank of Texas in October 2025 through foreclosure due to unpaid debt from the previous owner, Shivam Real Estate. The sale highlights the ongoing trend of distressed hotel assets in the Bay Area finding buyers at reduced prices, offering experienced operators an opportunity to capitalize on operational upside and the long-term strength of the Silicon Valley lodging market.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Hotels across the Bay Area have experienced defaults and foreclosures in recent years as the local lodging market recovers from a post-pandemic slump. This has created opportunities for investors to acquire properties at lower prices. Other recent distressed hotel sales include the 142-room Hyatt House Pleasant Hill for $3.5 million and the 128-key Hyatt House Pleasanton for $800,000, both acquired at foreclosure auction in late March 2026.
