Housing bill sails through House with bipartisan support

The Real Deal — National — Thu, 21 May 2026 18:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The House of Representatives has passed the amended 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act with bipartisan support, moving a housing affordability bill closer to becoming law. A key change in the House version, which received White House approval, removes a controversial provision that would have required build-to-rent investors to sell rental homes within seven years. This amendment is expected to provide comfort to the build-to-rent industry, which had seen investment frozen due to the previous mandate. The bill also narrows the definition of a single-family home to exclude manufactured housing and renovated homes for sale, drawing support from industry groups like the National Association of Home Builders and the National Association of Realtors.
