Illinois Municipal League pitches alternative housing plan, drawing Realtor backlash

The Real Deal — Chicago — Fri, 01 May 2026 23:09:52 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The Illinois Municipal League has introduced its "Real Housing Act" as an alternative to Governor JB Pritzker's "Build plan," aiming to address housing affordability while preserving local zoning authority. The proposal suggests voluntary incentives for local governments to adopt denser housing options and includes controversial measures such as capping residential real estate commissions at 3% and removing the state ban on local rent stabilization. The Illinois Realtors trade association has strongly criticized the proposal, arguing that the commission cap could violate antitrust laws and that other measures might limit housing supply and increase costs. The Municipal League contends its plan prioritizes housing affordability over real estate industry profits.
