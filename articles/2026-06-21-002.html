Andrew Heiberger, Howard Lev jump on boutique condo boom with Flatiron townhouse

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 20 Jun 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Development

Developers Andrew Heiberger and Howard Lev are planning a three-unit luxury condominium project in New York City's Flatiron District, capitalizing on the growing demand for boutique new developments and move-in-ready townhouses. The project, dubbed the London Clay Townhouse, is expected to launch sales in the summer with a projected sellout exceeding $20 million. This development aligns with a broader market trend where smaller, high-end projects are increasingly attracting wealthy buyers, as evidenced by recent high-priced penthouse sales and townhouse transactions in desirable Manhattan and Brooklyn neighborhoods.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Luxury buyers in New York City are increasingly favoring boutique new developments and move-in-ready townhouses. Buildings with fewer than 40 units now account for 32% of new units coming to market, up from 14% between 2016 and 2020. Newly renovated townhouses and new developments are commanding top-dollar in Downtown Manhattan and Brownstone Brooklyn.
