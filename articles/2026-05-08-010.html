Some welcome stability? It might be coming in the U.S. office market

REJournals — Fri, 08 May 2026 10:07:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Office Report

The U.S. office market is showing signs of stability and recovery, with national net absorption reaching 6.2 million square feet in Q1 2026, marking the seventh consecutive quarter of positive absorption. Demand for office space is now exceeding new supply, a significant improvement from two years prior. However, the recovery remains uneven, with gateway and innovation-driven markets outperforming the Midwest, which continues to experience negative absorption and rising vacancy rates in some cities. The flight to quality persists, as tenants favor newer Class-A buildings with amenities, while older properties are being repositioned or converted to other uses, contributing to a shrinking national office inventory.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

National net absorption reached 6.2 million square feet in Q1 2026, marking the seventh consecutive quarter of positive absorption. Trailing 12-month net absorption totaled 23.2 million square feet nationally, a significant increase from negative 64.6 million square feet in early 2024. Manhattan posted nearly 2 million square feet of positive absorption in Q1 2026, and San Francisco added nearly 1.4 million square feet. The Midwest region recorded negative absorption of 156,476 square feet in Q1 2026, with its office vacancy rate increasing 40 basis points year-over-year to 18.5%. Chicago posted negative absorption of nearly 693,000 square feet, and its vacancy climbed to 25.5%. Class-A office properties accounted for 7.7 million square feet of positive absorption, and the national Class-A vacancy rate fell by 10 basis points to 21.1%. The national office construction pipeline totaled 23.6 million square feet in Q1 2026, down from 158 million square feet at the end of 2019. The average office asking rent increased 2.4% year-over-year to $38.08 per square foot.
