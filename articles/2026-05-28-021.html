PolicyPro: SEQRA reform goes live, pied-à-terre tax set for July start

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 27 May 2026 22:45:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

New York State has enacted significant reforms to the State Environmental Quality Review Act (SEQRA), streamlining the approval process for various housing projects. The changes, effective immediately, exempt projects up to 500 units in high-density areas of New York City and smaller developments in other urban and rural communities from lengthy environmental reviews, a move praised by developers and housing advocates for reducing costs and delays. Concurrently, a new pied-à-terre tax is set to take effect on July 1, targeting one- to three-family homes valued at $5 million or more, and condos/co-ops assessed at $1 million or more, with phased-in rates. This tax, which includes exemptions for primary residences and full-time rentals, is designed to sunset in five years, while a proposed cash transfer tax was dropped.

DATA POINTS

SEQRA Exemption - NYC Medium/High-Density: Up to 500 units

SEQRA Exemption - NYC Low-Density: Up to 250 units

SEQRA Exemption - Other Urban Areas: Up to 300 units

SEQRA Exemption - Rural Communities: Up to 100 units

SEQRA Exemption - Areas without Zoning Codes: Up to 20 units

Pied-à-terre Tax Start Date: July 1

Pied-à-terre Tax - 1-3 Family Homes ($5M+): 0.8% to 1.3% for first two years

Pied-à-terre Tax - Condos/Co-ops (Assessed $1M+): 4% to 6.5% for first two years

Pied-à-terre Tax - Homes $5M-$15M (after 2028): 0.8%

Pied-à-terre Tax - Homes $15M-$25M (after 2028): 1.05%

Pied-à-terre Tax - Homes $25M+ (after 2028): 1.3%

New Solar Incentives: $200 million
