Pritzker’s affordable housing package stalls as lawmakers shelve zoning overhaul

The Real Deal — Chicago — Tue, 02 Jun 2026 19:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

Illinois Governor JB Pritzker's comprehensive six-bill package, known as Building Up Illinois Developments, failed to pass in the state legislature's spring session. The proposed legislation, which aimed to streamline zoning and parking regulations to encourage new construction, particularly affordable housing, faced significant opposition from local municipalities concerned about losing control over local zoning decisions. Despite the setback, Governor Pritzker reiterated his commitment to advancing affordable housing initiatives in the state, acknowledging that such legislative efforts often require multiple attempts over several years. While the zoning overhaul stalled, the state's new $60 billion budget includes $250 million for housing initiatives, with $20 million allocated for middle-income housing development and $50 million for down payment assistance.
