South Florida cities aim to fight housing crisis with controversial free land deals

South Florida - The Real Deal — Thu, 30 Apr 2026 13:45:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Government Policy / Affordable Housing

South Florida cities are increasingly utilizing controversial land deals, giving public land to private developers at no cost, to address the region's housing crisis. These initiatives, exemplified by projects in Miami's Little Havana and West Palm Beach, aim to facilitate the creation of affordable housing by offsetting high construction costs. While proponents argue these land-for-affordability swaps are essential, the lack of competitive bidding processes has drawn criticism regarding transparency and accountability.
