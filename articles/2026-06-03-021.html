PolicyPro: Lawmakers pass bills to curb private listings, raise broker licensing bar

The Real Deal — New York — Tue, 02 Jun 2026 22:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

New York lawmakers have passed two significant real estate bills. One bill aims to curb private listings by requiring agents to post listings on an MLS or public website in a timely manner, unless the seller opts out in writing with a disclosure. This measure applies to both sales and rental listings, increases penalties for violations, and offers liability protection for agents acting in good faith. The second bill tightens broker licensing requirements, doubling the required experience as a licensed salesperson from two to four consecutive years, or requiring three years of equivalent real estate industry experience, in addition to 152 hours of coursework. These bills now await the governor's decision.

DATA POINTS

Private Listing Bill Penalty Increase: From $2,000 to $5,000

Broker Licensing Salesperson Experience Requirement: From 2 years to 4 consecutive years

Broker Licensing Equivalent Experience Requirement: 3 years

Broker Licensing Coursework Requirement: 152 hours
