Chicago MLS goes nuclear: MRED plans to sever Zillow feed

The Real Deal — Chicago — Mon, 18 May 2026 21:19:46 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Midwest Real Estate Data (MRED), the MLS operator for northern Illinois, plans to suspend its data feed to Zillow if the home search giant does not comply with MRED's rules regarding the display of listings marketed privately before public release. This dispute, which has escalated into a federal lawsuit filed by Zillow, centers on Zillow's refusal to display listings pre-marketed through channels like MRED's Private Listing Network or Compass's "Private Exclusives." MRED argues that Zillow's filtering of listings based on the attached broker or brokerage violates its licensing agreement, while Zillow contends that MRED is using its market dominance to protect Compass listings nationwide. The potential data blackout would significantly reduce Zillow's listing volume in the Chicago area, impacting both sellers' exposure and buyers' access to available homes.
