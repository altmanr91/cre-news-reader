$25M Pac Heights Victorian trades after less than two weeks on market

SALE

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Tue, 16 Jun 2026 23:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

A historic Queen Anne Victorian home in San Francisco's Pacific Heights neighborhood sold for approximately $25 million in an all-cash transaction. The property, which had been owned by the same family for nearly 60 years, was on the market for less than two weeks before going under contract. The sale highlights the tight luxury housing market in San Francisco, driven by increasing wealth from the tech sector and a limited supply of high-end, move-in ready homes.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: single family

Address: 2315 Broadway Street, San Francisco, CA

Sale Price: $22.2M

Notable Features: Nine-bed, eight-bath Queen Anne Victorian home, 149 years old, well-preserved, chosen as the exhibition house for San Francisco's 2026 Decorator Showcase.

Project Notes: The buyer agreed to pay both sides of the broker commission and the real estate transfer tax, bringing the total cost to approximately $25 million. The property was last acquired in 1969 for $100,000.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: 2315 Broadway Holdings LLC

SELLER: Private Individual — Susan McLaughlin; Joseph Hartman

SELLER BROKER: Compass — Erin Thompson, Listing Agent

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

San Francisco's inventory of homes for sale fell to 900 in May, down by 500 homes year-over-year, marking the first time since 2019 that available homes in May dropped below 1,000. Sales from January to May are up 8 percent year-over-year in the city, fueled by wealth created by the artificial intelligence industry. The median home price in the city neared $2.2 million earlier this year, an all-time high.
