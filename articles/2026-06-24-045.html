Virginia Budget Keeps Data Center Tax Breaks, Adds $600M Energy Levy

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 23 Jun 2026 14:57:11 -0400

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

Virginia lawmakers have passed a $250 billion state budget that retains the sales and use tax exemption for data centers, a key incentive credited with establishing the state as a global data center hub. However, the new budget introduces a significant levy on data center energy consumption, projected to generate up to $600 million annually. This compromise legislation, which avoids a government shutdown, also mandates environmental studies and noise abatement regulations for data centers, signaling a shift towards balancing industry growth with environmental and community impacts. Analysts suggest this new energy tax, while not existential for large operators, could increase electricity costs by approximately 11% for data centers in the state and may foreshadow similar regulatory trends in other markets.

DATA POINTS

State Budget Amount: $250B

New Data Center Energy Tax Revenue: $600M annually

Energy Tax Rate: 1 cent per kilowatt-hour used per month

Estimated Annual Tax Burden for 100MW Data Center: $9.6M

Estimated Increase in Average Electricity Rates for Data Centers: 11%

Sales and Use Tax Exemption Rate: 5.3%

Original Sunset Date for Tax Exemption: 2035

Proposed Early Sunset Date for Tax Exemption (Senate Bill): 2027

Noise Abatement Regulations Deadline: 2030

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Virginia has the world's densest concentration of data center facilities. The state was the first to implement sales and use tax exemptions to promote data center development. The combination of rising fiscal pressures and anxiety about the socioeconomic fallout from AI adoption is likely to make data centers a ripe target for new revenue proposals in other markets.
