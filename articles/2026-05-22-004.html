Publix Buys Another South Florida Retail Center

ACQUISITION

Commercial Observer — Thu, 21 May 2026 16:45:21 +0000

SUMMARY

Publix has acquired the Fountains of Boynton Shopping Center, a 178,726-square-foot retail property in Boynton Beach, FL, for $78 million. The grocery chain is the anchor tenant at the center, which it purchased from a partnership between Bolder Group and Union Investment Real Estate. This acquisition is part of Publix's strategy to buy properties it occupies, a trend observed among retailers in the current high-interest-rate environment.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Publix has spent over $400 million since 2024 acquiring real estate it occupies. In December, Publix purchased a 137,780-square-foot retail center in Boca Raton for $83 million from Jamestown. Two months prior, it acquired a shopping center near Kendall for $72 million.
