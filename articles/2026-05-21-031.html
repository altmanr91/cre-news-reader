Yitzy Klor pursuing third condo deconversion attempt at 200 North Dearborn with $98M offer

The Real Deal — Chicago — Wed, 20 May 2026 17:18:10 +0000

SUMMARY

Property Listing / For Sale

A group of condo owners at 200 North Dearborn Street in Chicago are considering a $98 million bulk buyout offer from YK Investments, led by investor Yitzy Klor. This marks Klor's third attempt to deconvert the 310-unit building into rentals, following two previous failed offers through his firm, Strategic Properties of North America, due to financing issues. The current proposal, while higher than a recent offer, is being met with skepticism by some owners who experienced prolonged uncertainty during the prior failed attempts.

FINANCING

Proposed financing includes a $108 million principal loan from Capital Funding Group at a 7.7% interest rate and $13 million in preferred equity from Crest Capital at a 15% interest rate, both with 36-month terms. The total investment, including closing costs and units already acquired, is estimated to exceed $136 million.

SPONSOR PIPELINE

Ontario Place: A River North condo building where Strategic Properties of North America's $190 million deconversion offer failed to close after three years due to funding issues.
