L.A.’s ULA Transfer Tax Further Hamstrings Investment: Report

Commercial Observer — Tue, 02 Jun 2026 23:50:18 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Tax Impact Report

A new report by the RAND Corporation indicates that Los Angeles' Measure ULA transfer tax has significantly hampered commercial real estate investment and development since its implementation in April 2023. The tax, which levies 4% on transactions over $5 million and 5.5% on those over $10 million, has generated less than half of its projected revenue for affordable housing and tenant assistance. The study found a 31% reduction in high-value real estate deals, with multifamily and commercial transactions falling over 46%, and a 30% decrease in large multifamily development production. These declines have led to substantial losses in housing units and construction jobs, alongside significant revenue shortfalls for local governments.
