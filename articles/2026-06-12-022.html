PolicyPro: Council eyes heat protections for construction workers

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 23:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

New York City lawmakers are advancing a bill to protect construction workers from extreme heat, requiring building site permit holders to implement a "heat action plan." The proposed legislation mandates provisions like drinking water, shaded rest areas, and first-aid resources when temperatures reach 85 degrees Fahrenheit or higher. Work would be suspended entirely if temperatures hit 95 degrees Fahrenheit or more for an hour, with exceptions only for emergency repairs. The bill has garnered support from construction unions and City Hall, with the New York Building Congress also expressing backing for the sensible worker safety provisions.

DATA POINTS

Heat Stress Threshold: 85 degrees Fahrenheit for an hour or more

Extreme Heat Stress Threshold: 95 degrees Fahrenheit or higher for an hour or more

Drinking Water Requirement: At least one quart per worker per hour, kept at 60 degrees Fahrenheit or below

States with Heat Standards: 8 (including California, Washington, Maryland)
