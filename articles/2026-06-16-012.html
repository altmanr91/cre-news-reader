Not all industrial markets are created equal: The country’s top 25 industrial hubs are gaining strength

REJournals — Tue, 16 Jun 2026 10:04:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Industrial Report

A new report from Colliers indicates that the U.S. industrial sector is transitioning into a more balanced phase, characterized by improving fundamentals and moderating supply. The post-pandemic construction boom has subsided, with new deliveries falling 24% year-over-year and construction activity down 60% from its 2022 peak. This shift is leading to stronger demand and stabilizing vacancy rates, particularly in the top 25 industrial markets which account for 76% of the nation's inventory. While Sun Belt markets like Dallas-Fort Worth and Houston continue to lead in inventory growth and net absorption, several Midwest markets, including Indianapolis, are demonstrating robust fundamentals with demand outpacing supply.
