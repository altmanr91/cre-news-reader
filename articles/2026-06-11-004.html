Kings Capital Files Plans to Convert FiDi’s 61 Gold Street Into 108 Apartments

DEVELOPMENT

Commercial Observer — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 20:25:54 +0000

SUMMARY

Kings Capital has filed new plans with the New York City Department of Buildings to convert the vacant 17-story office building at 61 Gold Street in Manhattan's Financial District into 108 residential units. The updated proposal includes 1,536 square feet of ground-floor retail space, a new lobby, and other amenities. This project is part of a broader trend of office-to-residential conversions in the Financial District.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Address: 61 Gold Street, New York City, NY

Units: 108

Original Plan: 90 residential units

Notable Features: Includes 1,536 square feet of ground-floor retail space, a new lobby, mail room, and package room.

Project Notes: The building was previously a nursing facility and has been vacant since 2020. New York-Presbyterian Hospital's Royal Charter Properties acquired the building in 2014.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Kings Capital — Thomas Giannopoulos, Managing Partner

ARCHITECT: Issac & Stern Architects — Ralph Kowalczyk

OWNER: New York-Presbyterian Hospital's Royal Charter Properties
