Reschke, Primo seeking $50M loan to remake portion of BMO Harris Bank building into a hotel

LOAN

The Real Deal — Chicago — Thu, 14 May 2026 21:15:28 +0000

SUMMARY

Developers Mike Reschke and Quentin Primo are seeking a $50 million tax-increment financing (TIF) loan from the City of Chicago to convert the lower 10 floors of the BMO Harris Bank building into a luxury W Hotel. This follows a previous $40 million city subsidy for the conversion of the upper floors into 345 apartments. The TIF loan, which is repayable, would cover a significant portion of the $166.5 million renovation cost, with the developers in negotiations with Marriott International for the W Hotel brand. This project is part of a broader effort to repurpose vacant office space in downtown Chicago.
