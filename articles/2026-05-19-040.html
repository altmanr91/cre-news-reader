Small Colleges Are Dying. Developers See Opportunity

Bisnow News Feed — Mon, 18 May 2026 15:18:17 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The closure of small colleges across the U.S. is creating significant redevelopment opportunities for real estate developers and larger educational institutions, as nearly 300 colleges closed between 2008 and 2023, with hundreds more projected to shutter. These vacated campuses, often comprising thousands of acres with residential, academic, and faculty buildings, are being eyed for adaptive reuse into housing, office, or community services, or acquired by well-endowed universities seeking expansion. While some campuses face challenges due to remote locations or deferred maintenance, others in desirable areas are attracting buyers, though local political pushback can sometimes complicate redevelopment plans.

DATA POINTS

College closures (2008-2023): Nearly 300

Projected college closures (next decade): More than 400 private, nonprofit four-year colleges

College closures in New England (past decade): Roughly 32

Students impacted by recent closures (Hampshire College, Anna Maria College): Roughly 1,900
