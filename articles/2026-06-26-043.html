Federal Board Critiques 'Piecemeal' Approach To Selling D.C. Buildings

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 25 Jun 2026 13:54:47 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The Public Buildings Reform Board (PBRB) has criticized the federal government's "piecemeal" approach to selling millions of square feet of federal properties in Washington D.C.'s southwest corridor. Board members argue that selling individual properties without a coordinated master plan for the area's future use will not maximize taxpayer returns or attract significant investment. They emphasized the need for a governing entity to lead development planning and secure an anchor tenant to drive demand for the substantial amount of available property. While acknowledging the quick sales of two initial properties, the board believes a comprehensive strategy is essential for the long-term success of the 230-acre federal land disposition.
