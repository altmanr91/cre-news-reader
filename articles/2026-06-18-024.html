Council members: No CityFHEPS expansion? No budget

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 18:45:29 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

New York City Council members are threatening to reject any budget deal that does not include an expansion of the CityFHEPS housing voucher program. Despite campaign promises, Mayor Zohran Mamdani's budget proposal actually cuts $500 million from the program, primarily by reducing payouts to brokers. Council members argue that expanding CityFHEPS, which helps prevent homelessness by covering a portion of eligible tenants' rent, would ultimately save the city money by reducing shelter costs. The program's costs have tripled in the last three years, projected to reach $1.7 billion this fiscal year, with a full expansion estimated to cost an additional $6 billion to $20 billion over five years.

DATA POINTS

CityFHEPS program current households served: 70,000

CityFHEPS projected cost for current fiscal year: $1.7 billion

Estimated cost of full CityFHEPS expansion over next five years: $6 billion to $20 billion

Mayor Mamdani's proposed budget cut to CityFHEPS: $500 million
