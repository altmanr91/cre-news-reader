“So much wealth”: Why the rush for waterfront homes rolls on in South Florida

SALE

The Real Deal — Miami — Fri, 05 Jun 2026 19:41:05 +0000

SUMMARY

A waterfront home on North Bay Road in Miami Beach, previously renovated by developer Keith Menin and Rockstar Energy founder Russell Savage, sold for $27 million to a land trust. The property, originally purchased for $16.1 million in April 2024, was on the market for $28 million. This transaction highlights the continued strong demand for luxury waterfront homes in South Florida, driven by a limited inventory of updated properties and an influx of wealth, particularly from California buyers. Brokers note that proposed property tax cuts in Florida are also expected to further stimulate the market.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Florida Legislature recently voted to send a proposed constitutional amendment to voters in November to raise the homestead exemption on primary residences from $50,000 to $150,000 in 2027 and $250,000 in 2028. Billionaire Google co-founders Larry Page and Sergey Brin made significant acquisitions in Coconut Grove and Miami Beach, respectively, for a combined $188 million and $51 million. The proposed wealth tax in California is cited as a factor driving tech buyers to South Florida, with up to 80% of some brokers' deals coming from California.
