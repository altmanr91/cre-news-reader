Heitman seizes downtown Oakland office building from KKR, TMG

FORECLOSURE

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Tue, 23 Jun 2026 19:07:54 +0000

SUMMARY

Heitman Capital Management has foreclosed on a $239.6 million loan, seizing the 535,000-square-foot office building at 1221 Broadway in downtown Oakland from previous owners KKR and TMG Partners. TMG Partners, a former co-owner, will remain as the operating partner for the 24-story tower, with plans to renovate the property and attract new tenants. The foreclosure highlights the ongoing challenges in Oakland's office market, where rising vacancies and declining property values have led to several landlords defaulting on their loans.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Oakland office vacancies have risen and property values have plummeted since the pandemic, leading to numerous foreclosures and properties being sold at distressed prices. Last month, the 206,000-square-foot Edgewater Park Plaza office park went into foreclosure. In January, Deutsche Bank seized three office buildings from Starwood Capital at 2100 Franklin Street, 2101 Webster Street, and 1901 Harrison Street.
