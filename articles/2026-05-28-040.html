Brookfield lands Concord City Council approval for 12K-unit Navy station redevelopment

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Wed, 27 May 2026 19:36:20 +0000

SUMMARY

Brookfield Properties has secured a crucial approval from the Concord City Council for the redevelopment of the former Concord Naval Weapons Station. The unanimous vote greenlights a financial agreement with the U.S. Navy, paving the way for a $6 billion master-planned community. This significant step follows years of stalled efforts due to labor disputes and disagreements with the Navy, and allows Brookfield to proceed with the extensive permitting and entitlement process for the multi-decade project.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: mixed-use

Property Name: Concord Naval Weapons Station

Size: 6,000,000 SF

Units: 12,272

Total Project Cost: $6.00B

$/Acre: $2,696,629/acre (calculated)

$/Land SF: $61.91/land SF (calculated)

Site Area: 2225 acres

Notable Features: The master-planned community will include over 800 acres of parks and 25% of the residential units (3,068) will be designated as affordable housing.

Project Notes: Brookfield Properties will pay the U.S. Navy $628 million over nearly 30 years for the site. The development is expected to unfold in five phases over approximately three decades, with the first phase (1,696 market-rate units and 562 affordable units) likely breaking ground in 2030.
