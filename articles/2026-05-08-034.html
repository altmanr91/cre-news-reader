Alameda apartment building up for sale for first time this century

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Thu, 07 May 2026 21:20:16 +0000

SUMMARY

Property Listing / For Sale

A 93-unit apartment building in downtown Alameda, known as the Alameda Hotel Apartments, has been listed for sale at an asking price of $18.6 million. This Spanish Colonial Revival property, built in 1926, is being marketed for the first time in over three decades. The listing price is notably below the Alameda County Tax Assessor's estimated value of $21.3 million for 2025, reflecting the softening apartment values in the East Bay due to elevated interest rates and challenging refinancing conditions. The property includes a mix of sleeping rooms, studios, and one-bedroom apartments, along with ground-floor retail and an event venue.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Apartment values across the East Bay have softened due to elevated interest rates and difficult refinancing conditions. Older properties have generally performed better than newer construction. Alameda is required to plan for 5,353 new homes by 2031 but has only permitted 663. At the start of 2025, there were no multifamily properties under construction in Alameda, and none were delivered in 2024.
