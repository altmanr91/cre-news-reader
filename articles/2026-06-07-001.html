What the NY AG’s antitrust investigation could mean for Compass

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 06 Jun 2026 14:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

The New York Attorney General has launched an antitrust investigation into Compass following its $1.6 billion acquisition of Anywhere Real Estate, which created the nation's largest residential real estate firm. The probe, confirmed by the AG's office, is examining whether the combined entity holds too much market dominance in New York. Potential remedies could include divestitures, agent headcount reductions, or fines, though a legal expert suggests the company may have already taken steps to address concerns, such as a subsidiary ending a licensing agreement. The outcome of the New York investigation could influence other states like Illinois and California to initiate similar inquiries.
