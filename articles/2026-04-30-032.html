Second time’s the charm? UBS trying to offload Market Street office tower again

SALE

San Francisco - The Real Deal — Wed, 29 Apr 2026 20:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

UBS Realty Investors has relisted the 23-story office tower at 455 Market Street in San Francisco, seeking a sale price in the upper-$300-per-square-foot range, which would value the property between $130 million and $139 million. This marks the second attempt by UBS to sell the building, following an unsuccessful listing in 2022 with an asking price of approximately $280 million. The current offering comes as San Francisco's office market shows signs of an upswing in demand, despite persistent high vacancy rates.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

San Francisco's office market saw net absorption of 2.3 million square feet in Q1, indicating strong demand. The city's office vacancy rate is 30.4 percent, down from 32.8 percent in Q4 2025. Institutional investors and private capital spent nearly $1.3 billion on 2.5 million square feet of office space in the city in Q1, representing the fastest sales pace for office properties in years. This includes Yoda PLC's acquisition of the Transamerica Pyramid for $692 million.
