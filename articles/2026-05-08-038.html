Industrial Q1: Supply-Demand Gap Narrows

Connect CRE — Fri, 08 May 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Industrial Report

The U.S. industrial real estate market experienced robust absorption in Q1, narrowing the supply-demand gap for the first time in years, according to multiple market reports. This shift is attributed to strong leasing momentum and the lowest quarterly delivery volume since Q2 2017, signaling a transition from an oversupplied environment to a more balanced market. Key trends include a flight to quality, with demand for modern, automation-ready facilities, and a resurgence in large-block leasing driven by 3PL providers and larger users. While build-to-suit projects continue to dominate the development pipeline, speculative development is showing a modest comeback.

DATA POINTS

Speculative deliveries in Q1: 73%

Speculative deliveries in 2025: 71%

Build-to-suit projects as % of space underway: 40%

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The U.S. industrial market saw its lowest quarterly delivery volume since Q2 2017. Bulk leasing increased, reflecting stronger demand by large suppliers of 100,000 square feet-plus. New construction remains largely concentrated in build-to-suit projects and those with tenant pre-commitments. Approximately 73% of deliveries in Q1 were speculative, an increase from 71% in 2025. Completions are expected to remain modest through 2026, but renewed groundbreakings in markets with healthy demand lifted the national development pipeline for a third straight quarter.
