Construction costs pile onto housing crunch as copper, lumber spike

The Real Deal — National — Wed, 27 May 2026 18:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article analyzes how surging construction material costs, including copper, lumber, diesel, and aluminum, are exacerbating the housing affordability crisis. Geopolitical events, tariffs, and supply chain disruptions are driving up expenses for homebuilders, leading to increased uncertainty and fewer options for affordable homeownership. The National Association of Home Builders reports that material cost volatility is making it difficult for 70% of builders to price homes, with executives from major homebuilders anticipating higher prices later in the year.
