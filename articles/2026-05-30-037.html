Harvest Properties, Lone Star launch JV to recapitalize former WeWork HQ

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Fri, 29 May 2026 17:46:21 +0000

SUMMARY

Harvest Properties and an affiliate of Lone Star Funds have formed a joint venture to recapitalize and reposition 600 California Street, the former WeWork headquarters in San Francisco's Financial District. Lone Star Funds acquired the 20-story, 360,000-square-foot office building through a credit bid after purchasing a nonperforming CMBS loan tied to the property. Harvest Properties will serve as the operating partner, overseeing leasing and management, with plans to renovate the lobby, plaza, and add a roof deck and amenity floor. The building is largely vacant, presenting an opportunity for the new owners to capitalize on a recovering San Francisco office market.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

San Francisco's office vacancy rate was 28.7 percent at the end of Q1, but the market recorded its largest quarterly net occupancy gain in over six years with 1.4 million square feet. This marks the third consecutive quarter of positive net absorption, a trend not seen since 2018. Q1 leasing activity reached 3.5 million square feet, the best performance since 2000, driven by demand from artificial intelligence firms.
