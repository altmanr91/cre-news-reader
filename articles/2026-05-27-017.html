Housing Notes: We co‑broke with everyone*

The Real Deal — National — Tue, 26 May 2026 20:04:24 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Jonathan Miller, a prominent residential real estate analyst, has partnered with The Real Deal to publish his "Housing Notes" column and quarterly New York City market report. Miller's column discusses the ongoing challenges faced by Multiple Listing Services (MLSs) and the broader real estate industry due to antitrust litigation and evolving commission structures. He argues that while the Department of Justice and class-action lawsuits aimed to increase transparency and shift buyer agent commission payments, the market has largely maintained the status quo, with sellers still bearing the cost. Miller contends that the economic impact of these changes has been misunderstood, as buyers would simply offer less for a property if they had to pay their agent's commission out-of-pocket, ultimately not saving sellers money. He also highlights the contentious issue of private listings, particularly by Compass, which he views as unethical and detrimental to price discovery, creating a dilemma for traditional MLS systems.
