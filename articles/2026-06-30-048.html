Pencils Down For Boston Condo Developers As Buyers Stay On The Sidelines

Bisnow News Feed — Mon, 29 Jun 2026 15:47:05 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Boston's luxury condominium market is experiencing a significant slowdown, with only one major tower currently under construction and a dramatic drop in new projects coming to market. Developers face challenges from higher interest rates, increased development costs, and sluggish sales of existing inventory. While luxury condo sales saw an 11% year-over-year increase in Q1, the median sales price decreased by 7% to $3M, and the average price per square foot fell by 4% to $1,698. The number of sales has also declined, with 100 luxury sales in Q1 compared to 146 in Q1 2022. Experts attribute the slowdown to macroeconomic uncertainty, rising mortgage rates, and a shift to a buyer's market where cash buyers have significant leverage and developers are offering concessions to maintain sales momentum.
