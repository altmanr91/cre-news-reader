Rockpoint Scores Mid-Rise Apartment Property in Westwood

ACQUISITION

Connect CRE — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 21:20:44 +0000

SUMMARY

Rockpoint, a Boston-based real estate private equity firm, has acquired Wilshire Margot, a 97-unit multifamily property in Los Angeles's Westwood neighborhood. The mid-rise building, constructed in 2007, is strategically located along the Wilshire corridor, offering residents convenient access to major employment centers like Century City and Beverly Hills, as well as UCLA. Rockpoint plans to implement a repositioning strategy to enhance the property's value, capitalizing on the area's strong demographics and limited multifamily supply.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Property Name: Wilshire Margot

Address: 10599 Wilshire Blvd., Los Angeles, CA

Units: 97

Year Built: 2007

Notable Features: Mid-rise, type-I apartment building located along the Wilshire corridor, benefiting from proximity to major employment hubs and UCLA.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: Rockpoint — Aric Shalev, Managing Member and Co-CEO

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

West Los Angeles is identified as one of the most supply-constrained multifamily markets in the country, characterized by strong demographics, proximity to employment centers, and a deep base of affluent renters.
