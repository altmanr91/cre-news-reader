Big Shift In Retail Space As Institutional Capital Moves Into Laundromats

Commercial Observer — Thu, 14 May 2026 04:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The laundromat industry, traditionally fragmented and overlooked by institutional investors, is poised for a significant shift as sophisticated capital begins to enter the sector. This article argues that laundromats offer a compelling investment opportunity due to their resilient, non-discretionary demand, predictable cash flow, and long-term lease potential, mirroring the consolidation trends previously seen in self-storage, car washes, and gas stations. The author highlights the emergence of dedicated investment vehicles and a changing investor profile, with private equity and family offices now exploring laundromats, signaling a move towards professionalization and systematic portfolio construction. This shift presents new opportunities for retail landlords seeking stable tenants and investors looking for recession-resistant assets, provided the industry develops the necessary infrastructure for institutional-grade evaluation and deployment of capital.

DATA POINTS

Number of laundromats in the US: 35,000+

Annual revenue of the laundromat industry: $6.8 billion

Percentage of laundromats that are single-location operations: 80%

Increase in institutional inquiries for laundromats (Q2 2024 to Q4 2025): Fivefold
