Ted Turner, Who Left A Mark On Downtown Atlanta CRE, Dies At 87

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 06 May 2026 16:43:14 -0400

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Obituary

Ted Turner, the founder of Turner Broadcasting, passed away at 87, leaving a significant mark on Downtown Atlanta's commercial real estate landscape. While not a developer, Turner's acquisition of a majority stake in the struggling 1.2M SF Omni International complex in 1985 from Cousins Properties transformed it into the iconic CNN Center, revitalizing the area. He later sold the property in 1988 for $170M, nearly tripling his initial investment. Turner also owned and resided in the historic Bona Allen Building, which housed Turner Enterprises and Ted's Montana Grill corporate offices, further cementing his presence in the city's core.
