Distressed San Jose resi tower sells for $175M, shifts to mixed-income

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Thu, 28 May 2026 23:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

A business entity led by developers Andrew Jacobson and Gary Dillabough, with involvement from Flow, has acquired The Fay residential tower in downtown San Jose for $175 million. The 23-story, 336-unit property, which had previously fallen into default, will be rebranded and renovated, with a focus on activating ground-floor commercial spaces. The acquisition marks Flow's entry into the West Coast market. Notably, the City of San Jose has leased 197 units within the building to provide affordable housing for middle-income households, including public servants, with subsidies for residents earning 80% to 110% of the area median income.
