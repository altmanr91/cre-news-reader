DeSantis pushes to kill property taxes for most Floridians in special session

The Real Deal — Miami — Wed, 27 May 2026 22:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

Florida Governor Ron DeSantis is advocating for a special legislative session to consider a significant increase in the homestead exemption for primary residences, aiming to reduce property tax burdens for most Floridians. The proposal would raise the exemption from $50,000 to $250,000, eventually reaching $500,000, and would require approval from both state lawmakers and voters. DeSantis frames this as a measure to alleviate financial pressure on working residents by shifting the tax burden towards wealthier seasonal homeowners. The initiative is part of a broader trend among states exploring property tax reductions due to rising housing costs and inflation, though it faces challenges given property taxes are a major funding source for local public services.

DATA POINTS

Proposed homestead exemption increase: From $50,000 to $250,000, gradually increasing to $500,000

Percentage of Floridians benefiting from $250,000 exemption: 60%

Percentage of Floridians benefiting from $500,000 exemption: 92%

Required approval for measure: 60% of state lawmakers and 60% of Florida voters

Estimated funding gap if property taxes eliminated: $55 billion
