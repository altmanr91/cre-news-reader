Housing bill bounces back to House after Senate passage

The Real Deal — National — Tue, 23 Jun 2026 16:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The Senate has passed the 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act with an 85-5 vote, sending the housing affordability bill back to the House for a final vote this week. A key provision of the bill is a ban on large institutional investors from acquiring single-family homes, a measure that has been a point of contention but gained significant support. Notably, the legislation does not include a previously debated mandate that would have required build-to-rent investors to divest rental homes within seven years, a provision that had caused significant concern and deterred investment in the sector. The bill also features programs to aid communities in identifying housing opportunities, provides grants and loans for property rehabilitation, expands the definition of manufactured housing to potentially circumvent local zoning challenges, links federal grants to local housing construction, and streamlines environmental reviews for development projects.
