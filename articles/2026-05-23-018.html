Floyd Mayweather hits Jona Rechnitz with fraud lawsuit, alleges diversion of $175M

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 22 May 2026 23:14:38 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Floyd Mayweather Jr. has filed a lawsuit against his former investment manager, Jona Rechnitz, alleging a multi-year fraud scheme that diverted $175 million in cash, jewelry, and real estate funds. The lawsuit claims Rechnitz misrepresented Mayweather's acquisition of a 1,000-unit affordable housing portfolio in New York City, where Mayweather allegedly received only a 5% stake in one LLC instead of the entire portfolio. Additionally, Rechnitz is accused of diverting $15 million from a settlement with SL Green and $8.8 million in loan proceeds from Hankey Capital, secured by four of Mayweather's properties, to an associate's company.
