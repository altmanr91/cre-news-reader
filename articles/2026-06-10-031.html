Pritzker’s zoning overhaul stalls as BUILD housing package fizzles in Springfield

The Real Deal — Chicago — Tue, 09 Jun 2026 17:01:12 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

Illinois Governor JB Pritzker's comprehensive housing plan, the Building Up Illinois Developments (BUILD) proposal, has failed to pass the state legislature and is being sent back for revisions. The plan aimed to address the state's housing shortage by implementing statewide zoning standards, easing residential density restrictions, and reducing minimum lot sizes. While the real estate industry, including Illinois Realtors, supported the initiative to streamline development, municipal leaders opposed it due to concerns over losing local control. Despite the current setback, proponents anticipate that components of the BUILD act or similar legislation will be reintroduced to tackle the state's housing deficit.

DATA POINTS

Illinois housing unit deficit (2025): 142,000 units

Illinois housing units needed to keep up with demand: 227,000 units
