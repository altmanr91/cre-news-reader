Transit-oriented housing gains ground nationwide

The Real Deal — National — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 18:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Housing & Transit

A national analysis by the Urban Institute, highlighted by Bloomberg CityLab, reveals that transit-oriented development (TOD) has significantly increased nationwide, with nearly 67 million housing units (45% of the national stock) located within a half-mile of transit stops in 2022, a 36% rise over two decades. This growth reflects widespread state and local efforts to integrate zoning reforms with transit investments, promoting denser residential projects near public transportation. However, the report also highlights limitations, as only 10% of U.S. housing is near transit with frequent service (15 minutes or better). While cities like Seattle, Salt Lake City, and New York have seen substantial TOD growth, many Sun Belt markets continue to prioritize sprawl, with less than 5% of their housing growth being transit-oriented over the last four decades.

DATA POINTS

Housing units within half-mile of transit (2022): 67 million

Percentage of national housing stock near transit (2022): 45%

Increase in housing near transit over two decades: 36%

Percentage of U.S. housing near frequent transit service: 10%

Housing supply near rail stations increase (Seattle & Salt Lake City, 1980-2022): More than doubled

Housing units added near rail stations (New York, 1980-2022): More than 682,000

Transit-oriented housing as percentage of overall housing growth (Atlanta, Dallas, Houston over four decades): Less than 5%
