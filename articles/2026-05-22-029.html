“Tampa we love”: Jon Paul, Nick Pérez say some investors “not comfortable” with gulfside city

The Real Deal — Miami — Thu, 21 May 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Related Group executives Jon Paul Pérez and Nick Pérez discussed the challenges of attracting international investors to Tampa, despite strong job growth and competitive rents. They noted that while luxury home sales and rentals are robust, the broader housing market is flat due to high property taxes and insurance. The firm remains a significant developer in the Tampa Bay market, with billions invested in projects like the Ritz-Carlton Residences. The executives also highlighted a shift towards domestic buyers in South Florida, particularly from the Northeast, Midwest, and West Coast, driven by tax considerations. They emphasized the growing trend of branded and wellness-focused condo developments, with plans for a new wellness-branded project. West Palm Beach was identified as their fastest-selling market, with strong demand for luxury condos and high occupancy rates for rental projects.
