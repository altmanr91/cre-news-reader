Policy Pro: DSA wins could revive REST Act, Council strikes ULURP deals

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 25 Jun 2026 23:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

The Democratic Socialists of America (DSA) caucus in the New York Legislature is projected to expand, potentially bolstering efforts to pass the Rent Emergency Stabilization for Tenants (REST) Act. This legislation aims to enable municipalities outside New York City to implement rent stabilization, a prospect concerning to landlord groups. The article also reports on the Gotham Organization securing approval for its Monitor Point project in Brooklyn after increasing the affordable housing component to 50% of the units, following negotiations with the City Council. Additionally, the Dewey Clinton Park North project advanced despite some council member opposition.
