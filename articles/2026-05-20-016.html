Housing Notes: When cash is king, Albany wants a cut

The Real Deal — New York — Tue, 19 May 2026 16:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article analyzes a proposed 1% tax in New York on all-cash real estate purchases over $1 million, arguing that it is likely a legislative effort to recover lost mortgage recording tax revenue due to a surge in cash transactions. The author notes that approximately 75% of Manhattan purchases over $1 million are all-cash, representing an estimated $17.4 billion in 2025, and that this tax would affect a significant portion of high-end buyers. While the real estate industry views it as another tax burden that could slow sales, the author suggests it's a corrective measure to offset revenue declines from the mortgage recording tax, especially given the rise in cash transactions between 2022 and 2025. The article also highlights that co-op purchases, typically exempt from mortgage recording tax, would be subject to this new cash tax.

DATA POINTS

Estimated percentage of Manhattan purchases over $1M that are cash: 75%

Estimated value of cash purchases over $1M in Manhattan in 2025: $17.4 billion

Proposed cash tax rate: 1% of purchase price

Average market share of Manhattan cash sales over the past dozen years: 52.1%

Current market share of Manhattan cash sales: 65%
