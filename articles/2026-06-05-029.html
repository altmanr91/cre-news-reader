South Florida’s top warehouse deals reach over $100M this week

SALE

The Real Deal — Miami — Thu, 04 Jun 2026 20:07:56 +0000

SUMMARY

Two significant industrial transactions closed in South Florida this week, totaling over $116 million. An affiliate of Reyes Holdings acquired a 440,700-square-foot distribution warehouse in Deerfield Beach from Republic National Distributing Company for $84.1 million. Separately, Woodhill Real Estate purchased a 10-building industrial portfolio in East Hialeah, spanning approximately 165,000 square feet, from the Richard Kronenberg Family Trust for $32 million. The Hialeah portfolio was fully leased at closing, with the buyer planning renovations and anticipating rent growth as leases roll over.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

South Florida's industrial market is entering a more balanced phase. Vacancy rates in Miami and Fort Lauderdale metropolitan areas rose to 5.8 percent in Q1 due to new warehouse space, with 1.1 million square feet of negative absorption. Despite this, demand from logistics, distribution, and e-commerce tenants remains strong, and asking rents increased 1.2 percent year-over-year to $18.64 per square foot. Smaller industrial properties are particularly competitive.
