Sunday Summary: Retail, Hospitality, ICSC and a Big Merger

Commercial Observer — Sun, 24 May 2026 13:00:14 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Conference Summary

This article summarizes key discussions and insights from the ICSC conference in Las Vegas, focusing on the retail and hospitality sectors. Experts debunked myths about e-commerce penetration, noting it has remained around 16% of total retail sales for the past two years, which is positive news for physical retail. The market is experiencing a turnaround with low availability and positive absorption, driven by limited new construction, demolition of older malls, and a resurgence of retail brands, particularly in value-oriented segments like mass merchants, grocers, and off-price retailers. The article also highlights the growing trend of entertainment spaces, including trampoline parks, fitting into a 'barbell economy' where both high-net-worth individuals and budget-conscious consumers seek value. Additionally, it touches on the struggles of anchor stores like Saks Fifth Avenue and the flourishing dollar store segment benefiting from consumers seeking value amidst inflation.

DATA POINTS

E-commerce share of retail sales: around 16% for the last two years

Retail availability: 4.9%

Entertainment space planned in U.S. and Canada: 16.5 million square feet

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The retail market has seen three straight quarters of positive absorption. Record-low vacancy rates in retail are leading to growing rents, driven by a long trend of limited new shopping center construction, demolition of older malls, and growth in retail brands, particularly mass merchants, grocers, off-price, and food and beverage.
