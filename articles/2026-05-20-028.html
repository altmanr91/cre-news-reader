Align beefs up Safeway redevelopment plans in SF’s Outer Richmond

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Tue, 19 May 2026 19:13:18 +0000

SUMMARY

Align Real Estate has submitted revised plans for the redevelopment of a Safeway grocery store in San Francisco's Outer Richmond neighborhood. The updated proposal calls for a larger mixed-use project featuring 562 residential units, an increase from the previously planned 526 units, along with expanded retail space. The project incorporates affordable housing units and utilizes state density bonus laws to facilitate the development.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: mixed-use

Address: 850 La Playa Street, San Francisco, CA

Size: 820,000 SF

Units: 562

Site Area: 3.3 acres

Notable Features: The project includes 113 deed-restricted affordable housing units for residents earning 50-80% of the area's median income.

Project Notes: The application invokes Senate Bill 330 and the state's density bonus law to increase residential capacity and streamline the approval process.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Align Real Estate

OWNER: Albertsons Companies

SPONSOR PIPELINE

Five Safeway locations in the Bay Area: Redevelopment with new grocery stores and housing.

Trader Joe's in Oakland's Rockridge neighborhood: Redevelopment into a senior housing tower without a supermarket.
