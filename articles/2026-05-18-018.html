California’s housing debate breaks in a new direction

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Sun, 17 May 2026 16:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

California's gubernatorial race is seeing the state's housing crisis reshape political discourse, with candidates proposing diverse and sometimes radical solutions. One leading candidate has pledged to declare a state of emergency over the housing shortage, granting the governor broad powers to suspend regulations and allocate funds, while another has suggested expanding developed land to accommodate millions of new homes. The debate also highlights growing tensions over the high cost of union labor in residential construction, with some industry leaders now considering non-union alternatives to make projects financially viable amidst rising costs.

Proposed increase in developed land: From 6% to 7% of state land
