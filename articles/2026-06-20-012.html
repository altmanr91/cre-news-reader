NYC DOT Modernizes Willets Point Offices and Yard with $121M Redevelopment

CONSTRUCTION

Connect CRE — Fri, 19 Jun 2026 20:26:53 +0000

SUMMARY

The New York City Department of Transportation (DOT) is undertaking a $121 million redevelopment of its Harper Street administration building and maintenance yard in Willets Point, Queens. The project involves replacing obsolete 1934 facilities with three new structures: a 72,820-square-foot administration and warehouse building, a washbay, and a security booth. The modernized facility aims to improve operational efficiency, enhance safety for personnel and vehicles, and incorporate sustainable strategies to achieve LEED Gold certification.

DATA POINTS

Address: 32-11 Harper St, Queens, NY

$/SF: $1,662/SF (calculated)

Year Built: 1934

Notable Features: The project includes a five-story administration and personnel building and warehouse, a two-story washbay structure, and a precast concrete flood- and storm-resistant security booth. The facility is designed to meet LEED Gold certification.

Project Notes: The redevelopment replaces obsolete and deteriorated facilities that no longer met modern operational, safety, or resiliency standards. The existing 160,000-square-foot Queens Roadway Repair and Maintenance & Fleet Services maintenance yard operates 24/7 and houses over 300 field employees.
