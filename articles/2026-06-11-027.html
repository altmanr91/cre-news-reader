Greystone smacks Lowensteins with second Bronzeville foreclosure as CMBS collapse deepens to $54M

FORECLOSURE

The Real Deal — Chicago — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 20:51:11 +0000

SUMMARY

Greystone, acting as special servicer for CMBS bondholders, has filed a foreclosure lawsuit against a 68-unit multifamily building in Chicago's Bronzeville neighborhood, owned by an entity controlled by Raphael and John Lowenstein. The lawsuit seeks to recover over $19.4 million, including significant default interest and fees, on an original $15.4 million loan. This action follows a rapid decline in the property's value, which appraised at $23.6 million in 2022 but fell to $15.6 million by March, alongside plummeting occupancy and mounting operating expenses. The property has also accumulated multiple city code violations and mechanics liens, indicating severe neglect and financial distress.

Total payoff amount sought is over $19.4 million due to contractual penalties, including $1.6 million in default interest and $1.6 million for yield maintenance prepayment premium. The property's debt service coverage ratio fell to 0.98 by September 2024. City of Chicago filed five building code violation liens between October 2022 and April. Mechanics liens were filed in May 2024 for unpaid rehab work.
