NYC’s top construction permits: Week ending May 22, 2026

The Real Deal — New York — Tue, 26 May 2026 23:08:47 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Construction Permits

This article provides a roundup of the top construction permits filed in New York City during the week ending May 22, 2026, highlighting new building applications, major alterations, and demolitions. The largest new building permit was for a 105,697-square-foot residential project in Brownsville, planned to deliver 165 units. Other notable filings include a 91,404-square-foot residential building in Elmhurst and an 81,582-square-foot residential project in Gowanus. The largest alteration permit was for 316,832 square feet of space at 10 Astor Place in Noho, while the top demolition permit was for a 13,000-square-foot building in Fort George.

DATA POINTS

Largest New Building Permit (SF): 105,697

Largest New Building Permit (Units): 165

Largest Alteration Permit (SF): 316,832

Largest Demolition Permit (SF): 13,000
