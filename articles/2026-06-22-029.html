New Deadline Means £600B Of Property Needs An Energy Upgrade By 2031

Bisnow News Feed — Sun, 21 Jun 2026 09:45:52 -0400

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The UK government has set a new deadline of 2031 for commercial properties to achieve an Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) rating of B or higher, extending the previous target by one year. This decision, following a five-year consultation, also removes an interim 2027 deadline for an EPC C rating, providing landlords with more flexibility. The new regulations, which are expected to become law within the next year, will exempt buildings under 11,000 SF. Industry data indicates that approximately £600 billion to £800 billion worth of commercial assets currently fall below the new minimum energy efficiency standards.

DATA POINTS
