Chicago brokerages navigate Zillow access after listing purge

The Real Deal — Chicago — Fri, 22 May 2026 17:45:46 +0000

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Business News

More than half of Chicago-area home listings vanished from Zillow after Midwest Real Estate Data (MRED), the MLS operator for northern Illinois, cut off its listing feed. MRED cited Zillow's refusal to display privately marketed listings as a violation of MLS rules. Zillow has sued MRED and Compass, alleging collusion to pressure Zillow into displaying these listings. While some brokerages like Baird & Warner and Keller Williams OneChicago have established direct feeds to Zillow to maintain visibility, Compass International Holdings and its brands have ceased their direct feeds, embracing the strategy of offering exclusive listings not found on Zillow. Brokers express mixed feelings about the impact, with some seeing an advantage in increased prominence for their listings on Zillow, while others are concerned about lead generation.
