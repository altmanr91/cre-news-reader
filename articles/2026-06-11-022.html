FARE Act generates thousands of complaints, tenant refunds in first year

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 18:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

New York City's Fairness in Apartment Rental Expenses (FARE) Act, which shifted the responsibility for broker fees from tenants to landlords, has generated over 2,000 complaints and inquiries in its first year. The Department of Consumer and Worker Protection has issued 74 summonses for 100 violations, resulting in approximately $27,000 in penalties and $15,000 in tenant refunds. While supporters like Councilmember Chi Ossé highlight reduced upfront costs for renters, the real estate industry, represented by the Real Estate Board of New York, argues the law has led to tighter inventory, higher rents, and market confusion, and continues to challenge it in court. Researchers note that a lack of reliable data makes it difficult to fully assess the law's impact amidst broader housing market pressures.
