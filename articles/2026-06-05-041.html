Kaiser’s plans for new 761K sf hospital in SF’s Anza Vista come into focus

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Thu, 04 Jun 2026 21:45:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Kaiser Permanente has filed permits to develop a new 760,900-square-foot hospital and a 534,300-square-foot parking garage in San Francisco's Anza Vista neighborhood. The new facility, which will include over 300 inpatient beds and a full-floor emergency center, is intended to replace its existing San Francisco Medical Center campus. The project is driven by California's 2030 seismic safety deadline, which mandates upgrades or replacement of certain hospital buildings.

Notable Features: The new hospital will feature a full-floor emergency center, an ambulance bay, a blood bank facility, and more than 300 inpatient beds. It will also include a 12-floor parking structure with space for 1,003 cars and 63 bicycles.

Project Notes: The project is a replacement hospital for the existing 1954-built San Francisco Medical Center campus at 2190 O’Farrell Street, which will be converted to medical offices. The development is in response to California's 2030 seismic safety deadline.
