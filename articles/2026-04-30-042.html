CyrusOne Lines Up $1B CMBS Loan For 2 Data Centers In Allen

REFINANCE

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 30 Apr 2026 14:33:53 -0400

SUMMARY

CyrusOne, a Dallas-based data center developer and operator, has secured a $1.05 billion CMBS refinancing loan for its DFW3 and DFW4 data centers located in Allen, Texas. The five-year, fixed-rate loan, arranged by a consortium of major financial institutions, will be used to refinance existing debt and cover closing costs. The facilities, developed between 2017 and 2025, are nearly fully leased to two hyperscale tenants and numerous smaller users, providing 76.5 megawatts of capacity. The refinancing highlights the growing role of CMBS in funding data center expansion, despite concerns about tenant concentration and valuation transparency in the rapidly evolving sector.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: data center

Property Name: DFW3 and DFW4 Data Centers

Loan Amount: $1.05B

Completion: 2025

Notable Features: Provides 76.5 megawatts of capacity; nearly fully leased to two hyperscale tenants and nearly three dozen smaller users.

Project Notes: Proceeds will refinance approximately $1 billion of existing debt and pay closing costs. The data centers were developed between 2017 and 2025. The loan-to-value ratio is 66.8% with a debt yield of 9.3% based on a March appraisal valuing the properties at nearly $1.6 billion.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Data centers accounted for 13% of CMBS transactions last year. Special servicers have raised concerns about the difficulty in evaluating data centers due to lack of transparency, quickly evolving technology, and surging supply.
