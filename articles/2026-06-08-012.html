Developer seizes on affordable, workforce market with condos, townhomes

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Miami — Mon, 08 Jun 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Prospera Real Estate Collective, led by Ellen Buckley, is developing two affordable and workforce housing projects in Miami-Dade County, Florida. The projects include Grand Bahamas Place, a 56-unit condo building in Coconut Grove, and Faith Place Village, a 37-unit townhome community near North Miami. Grand Bahamas Place will offer units restricted to households earning up to 80 percent of the area median income, while Faith Place Village will cater to households earning up to 120 percent of the AMI. These developments address South Florida's critical need for affordable and workforce housing, with Prospera partnering with local churches and community groups to unlock value from underutilized land.

DATA POINTS

$/Unit: $410,714/unit (calculated)

Completion: early 2029

Notable Features: Condos will range from studios to two bedrooms and are restricted for households earning up to 80 percent of the area median income. The project includes nearly 3,000 square feet of retail and a 4,000-square-foot religious and community space.

Project Notes: The partnership paid $2.2 million for one lot in 2025, and Believers of Authority Ministries transferred the other lot for $100 in 2025. Condo prices will range from $305,000 to $450,000. The project is named in homage to the area's earliest settlers from the Bahamas.

FINANCING

Grand Bahamas Place received $4.2 million in Miami Forever bond financing and $700,000 from Florida Community Loan Fund. Prospera is working to obtain a construction loan for both projects.

SPONSOR PIPELINE

Faith Place Village: 37 for-sale townhomes in 14 buildings near North Miami, for households earning up to 120 percent of the AMI, with a total cost of $15.3 million and expected completion by end of 2027.

Pipeline of more than 1,800 residential units: Spanning affordable, workforce and luxury homes, across Florida, Arkansas, Georgia and North Carolina, consisting of for-sale and for-rent units.
