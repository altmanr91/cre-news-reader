South Florida by the numbers: The World Cup Effect

The Real Deal — Miami — Thu, 18 Jun 2026 18:15:49 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Economic Impact

The FIFA World Cup is expected to bring between 600,000 and 1 million visitors to South Florida, generating an estimated financial impact of $1.3 billion for the region. This influx of international attention is anticipated to significantly boost the local real estate market, with developers actively targeting buyers from Latin American countries. The tournament is also projected to create 9,000 local jobs and lead to a 200 percent increase in Broward County hotel room rates during peak periods, demonstrating a substantial economic uplift across various sectors.

DATA POINTS

Expected visitors during tournament: 600,000 to 1 million

Estimated financial impact in South Florida: $1.3 Billion

Expected increase in Broward County hotel room rates during peak periods: 200 percent

Estimated economic impact from World Cup guests using Airbnb: $384 Million

Number of local jobs expected to be created: 9,000

Projected tournament-related spending for greater Ft. Lauderdale alone: $150 million to $175 million
