Data Centers Are Plugging a Big Hole in the U.S. Construction Industry

Commercial Observer — Mon, 08 Jun 2026 10:30:15 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The U.S. construction industry is experiencing a significant shift, with data center and advanced manufacturing projects increasingly dominating overall activity, while multifamily construction faces substantial headwinds. This disparity is so pronounced that excluding data center and manufacturing work would reveal a flat or declining pace of construction nationally. Factors such as high interest rates, elevated material and labor costs, and a slowdown in population and employment growth are hindering multifamily development, leading to a shrinking pipeline of new projects and impacting contractor revenues. While some markets like South Florida continue to see multifamily activity, the national outlook suggests ongoing challenges for financing and delivering new rental housing units.
