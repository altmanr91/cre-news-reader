How Supply Chain Volatility Will Impact Industrial Real Estate

Connect CRE — Fri, 29 May 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article argues that ongoing supply chain volatility, driven by structural factors like geopolitical realignment, energy transition, labor constraints, and higher capital costs, is fundamentally reshaping the industrial real estate sector. The author contends that companies can no longer rely on temporary fixes for supply chain disruptions and are instead pursuing long-term network redesigns for greater resilience and operational flexibility. This shift necessitates that industrial owners and developers adapt by underwriting operations rather than just market comps, prioritizing supply chain-critical locations, and building flexibility into assets to accommodate evolving occupier needs such as increased trailer storage, automation, and light manufacturing capabilities.
