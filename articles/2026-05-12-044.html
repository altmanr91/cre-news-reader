Iran War's 'Storm Clouds' Threaten To Derail Hotels' Strong Start To 2026

Bisnow News Feed — Mon, 11 May 2026 14:30:26 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Major hotel companies experienced a strong start to 2026, with healthy U.S. demand leading many to raise their full-year outlooks. However, the ongoing conflict in the Middle East is casting a shadow over future expectations, impacting Middle East revenues, disrupting air routes, and increasing jet fuel costs. While some executives express caution regarding geopolitical risks and potential rate hikes, others remain optimistic about domestic demand and the long-term growth drivers like infrastructure investment and AI. Companies like Marriott International are anticipating a significant reduction in RevPAR for their Middle East properties and have adjusted global RevPAR growth forecasts accordingly, while Hilton expects continued strong performance in the U.S. across all segments.
