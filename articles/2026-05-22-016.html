Centre Square bidding war erupts as NYC developer scoops local offer

The Real Deal — National — Thu, 21 May 2026 21:09:26 +0000

SUMMARY

Property Listing / For Sale

A bidding war has erupted for Centre Square, Philadelphia's largest office property, which is currently in foreclosure. New York-based CSC Coliving submitted an $80 million bid to acquire the 1.76 million-square-foot complex, surpassing a previous $70 million agreement by local developers Dean Adler and PMC Property Group. Another developer, Universal Group, has also intervened in the foreclosure case, alleging its $75 million all-cash offer was improperly rejected. The property, which last traded for $328 million in 2017, has struggled with high vacancy, and bidders are proposing adaptive reuse plans including residential, hotel, and retail conversions.
