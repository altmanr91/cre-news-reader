Austin’s multifamily and commercial fundamentals show growth

The Real Deal — Texas — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 11:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Despite recent narratives suggesting a mass exodus, Austin's commercial real estate fundamentals show resilience and continued growth, according to market experts. The city's population surpassed 1 million in 2025, growing 4.2% since 2020, and it continues to attract corporate relocations to the Sun Belt. While the multifamily market experienced a significant oversupply with 107,000 units delivered in the past six years, leading to a peak vacancy rate of 16% in 2024, absorption rates have been historically high, and the development pipeline is now shrinking. This, combined with strong job growth, suggests a positive outlook for rent growth by 2027.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Austin's multifamily vacancy rate peaked at 16% in 2024, significantly higher than the national average of 9.3%. Rent growth is projected to turn positive in 2027. Austin's job growth between April 2025 and April 2026 was 1.2%, outpacing the statewide 0.8% and nationwide 0.2% figures. The city has added at least 4,000 residents each year in the first half of the decade.
