Hilton-branded hotels account for outsized share of CMBS distress

The Real Deal — National — Mon, 22 Jun 2026 16:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / CMBS Distress Report

A new analysis by KBRA, reported by Bisnow, reveals that Hilton-branded hotels account for a disproportionately high share of distressed commercial mortgage-backed securities (CMBS) debt. Nearly 16% of all CMBS debt tied to Hilton properties, totaling approximately $2.5 billion, is delinquent, which is more than double the overall market average for major hotel flags. This distress is largely concentrated in high-profile assets in challenging urban markets, such as the Hilton Union Square and Parc 55 in San Francisco, and the Palmer House Hilton in Chicago, which have faced foreclosure and significant value depreciation since the pandemic.
