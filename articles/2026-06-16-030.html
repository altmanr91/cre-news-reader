Fairmont Austin’s $430M CMBS loan flagged for special servicing amid foreclosure flurry

LOAN

The Real Deal — Texas — Tue, 16 Jun 2026 13:02:24 +0000

SUMMARY

The $430 million CMBS loan for the Fairmont Austin, owned by Manchester Financial Group, has been transferred to special servicing due to a significant decline in cash flow and occupancy. This luxury hotel, which opened in 2018 and was refinanced in 2024, is facing financial distress, exacerbated by the ongoing closure of the adjacent Austin Convention Center for renovations. The situation at Fairmont Austin is part of a broader trend of distress among Downtown Austin hotels, with two other prominent properties recently facing foreclosure or handing keys back to lenders.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Downtown Austin's luxury hotel market is experiencing distress, with the Fairmont Austin being the third hotel in two months to face financial challenges. The Line Austin, a 428-key hotel at 111 East Cesar Chavez Street, was handed back to its lender after an alleged default on a $172 million loan provided by JP Morgan in 2023. The Hyatt Centric also lost control during a foreclosure auction after defaulting on a $50.5 million loan. The Austin Convention Center, located at 500 East Cesar Chavez Street, closed for a $1.6 million overhaul in 2025, doubling its meeting space to 700,000 square feet, and is expected to reopen in 2029.
