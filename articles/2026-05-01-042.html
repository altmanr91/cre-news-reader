Newmark Posts 27% Revenue Growth Driven By More Office Leasing

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 30 Apr 2026 12:44:22 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / Earnings Report

Newmark reported robust first-quarter earnings, with total revenue increasing over 27% year-over-year to $846.5 million, driven by significant growth in office leasing, capital markets, and management/servicing fees. The company's leasing fees reached an all-time first-quarter high, primarily due to higher office volumes, including activity from artificial intelligence and technology companies in key markets like New York City, San Francisco Bay Area, and Texas. Capital markets revenue also saw a substantial jump, marking its tenth consecutive quarter of double-digit growth, largely attributed to increased client activity in the senior housing sector. Newmark's CEO expressed optimism for continued growth, citing normalizing return-to-office trends and improving industrial leasing fundamentals, leading the company to raise its full-year revenue outlook.
