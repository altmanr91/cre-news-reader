Quarterra plans apartment project amid rent growth in Snohomish

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Seattle — Wed, 27 May 2026 15:41:11 +0000

SUMMARY

Quarterra is planning a new 294-unit apartment complex, Emblem Snohomish, along with three commercial buildings and five townhomes at 2502 Bickford Avenue in Snohomish, Washington. The project will include 15,000 square feet of commercial space, featuring a drive-thru coffee and doughnut shop. This development aligns with strong market fundamentals in Snohomish County, which recorded the lowest multifamily vacancy rate in the Puget Sound region at 6.1% in Q1, alongside a 2% year-over-year rent increase.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Property Name: Emblem Snohomish

Address: 2502 Bickford Avenue, Snohomish, WA

Size: 15,000 SF

Units: 294

Site Area: 17.4 acres

Notable Features: The project includes three commercial buildings and five townhomes, with a creek separating the apartment and townhome segments. One commercial building will house a 2,500-square-foot coffee and doughnut shop with a drive-thru.

Project Notes: The development will replace five existing single-family homes and a shop. The properties have a combined assessed value of over $4 million, but Quarterra has not yet acquired them.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Quarterra

EQUITY/FINANCING: TPG

SPONSOR PIPELINE

The Whittaker Apartments: A 2016-built apartment property in west Seattle.

The Piper: A 284-unit luxury rental community in Redmond, opened in 2024.

Tavin apartment property: A 194-unit apartment property in Crown Hill, began leasing a year ago.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Snohomish County had the lowest multifamily vacancy rate in the Puget Sound region in the first quarter at 6.1%, according to Kidder Mathews data. Average rent in the county increased 2% year-over-year to $1,532.
