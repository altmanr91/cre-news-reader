Michael Levitt, affordable housing pioneer, dies at 94

The Real Deal — National — Tue, 16 Jun 2026 17:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Obituary

Michael Levitt, a pioneer in affordable housing development and founder of The Michaels Organization, recently passed away at the age of 94. Levitt was instrumental in embracing government-assisted housing programs after the 1974 Housing and Community Development Act, utilizing tax credits and public housing revitalization initiatives. His company grew to manage a portfolio of over 75,000 units and $12 billion in assets, spanning various housing types across the nation. Levitt was also recognized for establishing the first scholarship program and on-site supportive services for affordable housing residents.
