Welltower’s $821M CEO payday sparks backlash despite blockbuster run

The Real Deal — National — Mon, 29 Jun 2026 19:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Welltower CEO Shankh Mitra received an $821 million long-term compensation package, sparking significant backlash from shareholders and proxy advisers despite the company's successful turnaround in the senior housing sector. The compensation, the second-largest CEO pay award in the U.S. last year, followed Welltower's aggressive acquisition strategy of over $40 billion in senior housing properties during the pandemic, a move that transformed the company into the world's largest publicly traded real estate firm with a market capitalization of approximately $160 billion. Critics argue the package is excessive, rewards market growth driven by demographics, and is not sufficiently tied to long-term performance, leading to 80% of shareholders voting against the advisory say-on-pay resolution.

DATA POINTS

CEO Compensation Package: $821 million

Senior Housing Acquisitions (during pandemic): Over $40 billion

Welltower Market Capitalization (since 2020): Approximately $160 billion

Welltower Portfolio Size: More than 2,500 senior living communities

Shareholder Vote Against Say-on-Pay: Roughly 80%

Senior Housing Occupancy (Q1): 89.9%

Occupied Senior Housing Units Nationwide: 1.05 million

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Senior housing occupancy hit 89.9 percent in the first quarter, with a record 1.05 million occupied units nationwide, driven by accelerating demographic demand from aging baby boomers.
