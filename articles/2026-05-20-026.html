Franklin family looks to ease parking pressure on Fort Worth’s Stockyards with $14M garage

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Texas — Tue, 19 May 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

The Franklin family, owners of M.L. Leddy's, received zoning approval for a new four-story parking garage in Fort Worth's historic Stockyards. The $14 million project aims to alleviate significant parking shortages in the popular tourist area, which a 2023 study found to be operating at over 100% parking capacity on key streets. The proposed garage will include 246 spaces along with ground-floor retail and office space, with construction potentially starting after the FIFA World Cup in July.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: parking garage

Address: 115 NW 25th Street and 2458 Ellis Avenue, Fort Worth, TX

Total Project Cost: $14.0M

Notable Features: Four-story parking garage with 246 spaces and ground-floor retail and office space.

Project Notes: The project is a fraction of a previously paused, larger Stockyards expansion plan that included 1,300 underground parking spaces.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Franklin Leddy Corporation
