Ken Mattson reverses course, will plead not guilty in Sonoma Ponzi scheme case

The Real Deal — National — Fri, 12 Jun 2026 20:14:59 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Ken Mattson, a Sonoma-based investor and developer, has reversed his decision to plead guilty to federal charges in an alleged $100 million Ponzi scheme. Mattson, who was arrested in May 2025, is accused of defrauding hundreds of investors by misrepresenting their ownership stakes in properties managed by his companies, LeFever Mattson Property Management and KS Mattson LP. Prosecutors allege that investor funds were diverted for Mattson's personal use, including luxury real estate and vehicles, with returns paid from new investors or other sources rather than property distributions. He will now proceed with a not guilty plea, leading to a jury trial.
