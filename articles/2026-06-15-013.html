Tarlton overseeing construction on first phase of national memorial in Grafton

CONSTRUCTION

REJournals — Mon, 15 Jun 2026 14:18:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Tarlton, a St. Louis-based general contracting and construction management firm, has completed the first phase of a $6 million national memorial in Grafton, Illinois. The project involved installing 12 life-size bronze statues of U.S. Army Provisional Ranger Group Second Battalion members on 100-foot limestone bluffs overlooking the Mississippi River, commemorating their historic ascent of Pointe du Hoc on D-Day. A replica German "pillbox" bunker was also constructed at the bluff's summit, adding to the memorial's authenticity and historical significance.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: memorial

Property Name: National Memorial of Military Ascent

Address: 950 E Main St, Grafton, IL*

Total Project Cost: $6.0M

Phase I: completed

Notable Features: Features 12 life-size bronze statues installed on 100-foot limestone bluffs overlooking the Mississippi River, and a replica German 'pillbox' bunker.

Project Notes: The memorial commemorates the U.S. Army Rangers' ascent of Pointe du Hoc on D-Day, June 6, 1944. A public dedication was held on June 6.
