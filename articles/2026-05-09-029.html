Chicago zoning panel clears nearly 2,000 apartments after months-long freeze

The Real Deal — Chicago — Fri, 08 May 2026 21:41:14 +0000

SUMMARY

Development / Zoning Approval

Chicago's City Council zoning committee has resumed operations after a three-month freeze, advancing projects that would bring nearly 2,000 housing units to the city. The committee, now chaired by Ald. Gilbert Villegas, approved several significant developments, including three West Loop apartment projects totaling over 700 units, an office-to-residential conversion downtown, and other residential developments across various neighborhoods. This restart addresses a substantial backlog of over 100 zoning items, signaling a renewed push for residential growth and adaptive reuse initiatives in Chicago.
