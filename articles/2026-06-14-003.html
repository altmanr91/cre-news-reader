How New York’s proposed ban stacks up in states’ fight over private listings

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 13 Jun 2026 12:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

New York is poised to enact legislation that would require brokers to add sale and rental listings to a public-facing website in a timely manner after marketing begins, unless a seller signs a disclosure agreement. This move follows Washington state's recent ban on exclusive "pocket listings," which prohibits agents from marketing properties to exclusive groups without making them publicly available. The debate over private listings is intensifying in states like New York and Washington, framed as a fight for access amidst housing and affordability crises, with other states like Wisconsin, Connecticut, Illinois, and Hawaii also considering similar measures. The New York Attorney General's office is also investigating Compass for antitrust concerns related to its merger with Anywhere Real Estate, as Compass has been a vocal opponent of rules requiring MLS uploads and has developed its own private exclusive network.
