TRD PolicyPro: Eviction bill draws NYAA warning, Hochul revises pied-à-terre tax

The Real Deal — New York — Mon, 04 May 2026 22:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

New York's proposed "Clean Hands Act," which would prevent landlords from initiating evictions while building violations are active, is drawing strong opposition from the New York Apartment Association (NYAA). The NYAA argues that the bill's broad scope on violations makes compliance nearly impossible for building owners, including non-profit and rent-regulated housing providers, and could destabilize their finances. They also highlight the practical difficulties of accessing tenant units for repairs and the lengthy city certification process for completed work. As an alternative, the NYAA proposes a rent-escrow mechanism with court oversight and guaranteed owner access for repairs. Separately, Governor Kathy Hochul's office is revising the proposed pied-à-terre tax, moving away from using unreliable assessed values and instead considering a model based on comparable sales data for properties valued over $5 million.
