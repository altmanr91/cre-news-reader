The Legal Side of Data Center Development: Q&A with Cox & Castle’s Margo Bradish and Arielle Harris

Connect CRE — Fri, 08 May 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Feature / Profile

This article features a Q&A with Margo Bradish and Arielle Harris, partners at Cox, Castle & Nicholson, who discuss the legal and regulatory challenges associated with data center development. They highlight that data centers differ from traditional industrial developments due to their significant tax revenue potential and minimal traffic generation. Key hurdles include complex state-level regulatory processes for power plants and grid upgrades, particularly in California, as well as community opposition often fueled by misinformation. The attorneys emphasize the importance of clear communication from developers about project benefits and sustainability features to address community concerns, especially regarding water usage and environmental impacts.
