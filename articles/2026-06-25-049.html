May CRE Sales Reached $42B, Boosted By A 205% Leap In M&A

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 24 Jun 2026 11:51:36 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / Sales Volume Report

Commercial real estate sales volume reached $42 billion in May, a 15% increase year-over-year, primarily driven by a significant 205% surge in mergers and acquisitions and portfolio deals. This M&A activity helped offset a 4% decline in single-asset transactions and substantial drops in office and development site sales. Analysts attribute the recent slowdown in individual asset sales to the macroeconomic impact of the U.S. war with Iran, which caused a pause in market activity due to rising interest rates. Despite this, pricing momentum remains positive, with the RCA CPPI U.S. National All-Property Index showing a 1.6% increase from a year ago, the strongest growth since February.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The average cap rate for transactions increased 5 basis points from April to 6.6% in May. Office trades saw an average cap rate of 7.2% (up 1 basis point), industrial at 6.8% (up 26 basis points), and apartments at 5.8% (up 3 basis points). Yields on 10-year U.S. Treasury bonds crossed the 4.5% threshold in May and remained above it for approximately a week.
