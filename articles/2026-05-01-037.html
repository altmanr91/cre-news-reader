Consumer Spending Increases, Retail Fundamentals Remain Intact

Connect CRE — Fri, 01 May 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Retail Report

Despite rising inflation and increased gas and food prices, consumer spending has shown an uptick, with core retail sales (excluding auto and gasoline) increasing by 0.6% in March and 4.2% year-over-year. This growth is largely driven by households purchasing necessity and household items in bulk to save money, benefiting general merchandise stores and warehouse clubs. While overall retail vacancy saw a slight increase, it remains below 5.1%, and deal flow for retail properties has risen significantly, particularly for single-tenant assets, indicating growing investor interest in less management-intensive properties. A projected pullback in new construction is expected to further support the leasing of vacant retail spaces.

DATA POINTS

Core retail sales increase (March): 0.6%

Year-over-year spending growth: 4.2%

Sales increase at general merchandise stores and warehouse clubs: 1%

Retail vacancy rate (March 2026): below 5.1%

Deal flow increase (12 months ending March): 21%

U.S. wage growth (March, year-over-year, inflation-adjusted): 0.3%

Core CPI movement (last month): up 0.2%

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Warehouse clubs are planning significant expansion, with each aiming to open 15 to 25 new locations this year, primarily by backfilling existing spaces, which is expected to boost single-tenant leasing activity for spaces over 100,000 square feet. Retail vacancy for single- and multitenant spaces is at least 70 basis points below the long-term average. A broad pullback in construction is anticipated to limit supply-side pressure for the remainder of the year.
