NYC Developers File Plans for 21M SF of New Construction in Q1: REBNY

Commercial Observer — Fri, 22 May 2026 15:36:12 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Development Filings

New York City developers filed plans for 577 new buildings in Q1 2026, totaling 21.2 million square feet, a 74% increase year-over-year. The majority of these filings, 75% or 15.5 million square feet, were for multifamily projects, proposing 16,815 new units across 281 buildings. This surge in activity is seen as encouraging for the city's economy and housing shortage, especially following the expiration of the 421a tax abatement program in 2022 and the subsequent decline in housing filings. Despite a national downturn in multifamily construction starts, New York City maintains a robust construction pipeline.

DATA POINTS

National Multifamily Construction Starts decrease from early 2022 peak: 73%

NYC Units Under Construction (start of 2026): 43,000 units

Dallas-Fort Worth Units Under Construction (start of 2026): 31,000 units

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The national multifamily market saw construction starts hit a low in Q1 2026, reaching 55,000 units nationwide, a 73% decrease from the early 2022 peak. In contrast, New York City had 43,000 units under construction at the start of the year, outperforming markets like Dallas-Fort Worth (31,000 units).
