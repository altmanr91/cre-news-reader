Developer looks to bring almost 300 units to Flushing

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 25 Jun 2026 20:11:38 +0000

SUMMARY

Developer Gary Chen has filed an application with the New York City Department of City Planning for a zoning change to construct a 10-story, 296-unit mixed-use project in Flushing, Queens. The proposed development, which would include 33,000 square feet of commercial space and nearly 200 parking spaces, aims to provide approximately 25 percent affordable housing. Chen assembled the development site, comprising multiple parcels, for a total of $26.5 million in 2024.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: mixed-use

Address: 32-02 Linden Place, New York City, NY

Size: 33,000 SF

Units: 296

Notable Features: Approximately 25% of units would be designated for affordable housing.

Project Notes: The development site includes another property on Linden Place and two industrial buildings on Farrington Street, which were acquired for a total of $26.5 million in 2024.
