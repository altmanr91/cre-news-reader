Vacancy Has Peaked For Life Sciences. The Good News Ends There

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 23 Jun 2026 17:55:04 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

While lab leasing shows modest improvement in select U.S. markets, overall conditions for life sciences landlords remain challenging due to declining rents and increasing concessions. Availability has contracted by approximately 2M SF in the top 12 lab markets over the past nine months, but this has come at the cost of an 18% rent decrease since 2023 and longer free rent periods. The market is seeing a shift where non-life sciences tenants are increasingly absorbing lab space, and developers are pivoting properties to attract AI, robotics, and advanced manufacturing users, particularly in the Bay Area. Despite some positive signs in biotech funding and stock performance, the real estate sector faces continued tenant-favorable conditions and uncertainty from past federal research funding cuts.
