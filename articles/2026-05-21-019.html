HUD hands over $130M construction loan for Denver VA hospital adaptive reuse

LOAN

The Real Deal — National — Wed, 20 May 2026 15:56:39 +0000

SUMMARY

GM Development secured a $130 million construction loan from the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) for the adaptive reuse of the former Veterans Affairs hospital campus in Denver. The financing, arranged by Walker & Dunlop, will facilitate the conversion of a 10-story hospital building into 493 apartments with ground-floor retail. This project is part of a larger mixed-use redevelopment that will ultimately include over 1,400 housing units, additional retail, and office space, with a portion of the initial residential units designated as affordable housing.

Project Notes: The 8.3-acre campus was previously acquired by GM Development in 2022 for approximately $41 million. About 8% of the 493 adapted hospital units will be reserved for tenants earning 60% of area median income or below. The hospital conversion is expected to be the first phase of the project.
