Judge halts Dallas City Hall vote, putting redevelopment plans on ice

The Real Deal — Texas — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 21:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

A Dallas County District Judge has temporarily halted a planned vote by the Dallas City Council regarding the relocation or redevelopment of the Dallas City Hall building. The ruling, prompted by a petition from several council members, found that the city violated the Texas Open Meetings Act by not providing sufficient public notice for the special meeting. This temporary restraining order prevents any vote on the future of the I.M. Pei-designed landmark for 14 days, impacting ongoing discussions about the building's obsolescence and potential redevelopment, including a previous proposal to offer the site to the Dallas Mavericks for a new stadium.
