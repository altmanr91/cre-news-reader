JPMorgan pulls plug on $1.4B real estate fund after years of losses

The Real Deal — National — Tue, 26 May 2026 21:13:54 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Fund Liquidation

JPMorgan Asset Management is liquidating its $1.4 billion US Real Estate Income and Growth Fund, a 24-year-old core-plus vehicle, due to years of deteriorating performance. The fund, which held a diversified portfolio of industrial, multifamily, and office properties, was particularly impacted by its office exposure amid higher interest rates, soft office demand, and a challenging transaction market. The liquidation process is anticipated to take up to three years, reflecting the current difficulty in aligning buyer and seller pricing expectations in the commercial real estate market.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Investor appetite for global real estate funds has started to rebound, with $172 billion raised last year, a 13% increase year-over-year. However, capital markets face challenges from elevated Treasury yields, high financing costs, and shaky transaction momentum. Commercial property sales saw a 25% year-over-year jump in the first quarter, but April volume subsequently fell 33% to $24.7 billion.
