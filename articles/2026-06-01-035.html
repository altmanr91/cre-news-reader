New generation of AI tenants rewrites culture of office leasing

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 11:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The rapid expansion of artificial intelligence firms is significantly reshaping the office leasing landscape in New York City and San Francisco, creating a new class of tenants with distinct demands and accelerated timelines. These AI startups, often flush with venture capital, are driving a frenetic pace of leasing, with deals closing in weeks rather than months, and prioritizing speed and certainty over protracted negotiations. Landlords are adapting to these cash-rich but potentially volatile tenants by requesting substantial security deposits, while brokers are navigating unconventional requests and a compressed deal-making process. This shift is particularly evident in San Francisco, where AI companies accounted for nearly 40% of Q1 2026 deal volume, and in New York City, where they represented over a third of tech sector demand.
