UBS offloads mostly vacant Downtown Houston office building ripe for conversion

SALE

The Real Deal — Texas — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 18:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

UBS Realty Investors has sold a largely vacant 24-story office building in Downtown Houston to Tampa-based apartment developer Zhukovskyi Development. The 542,000-square-foot property at 919 Milam Street, built in 1956 and last renovated in 2023, is less than 20 percent occupied and is considered a prime candidate for residential conversion. While sale terms were not disclosed, the property was last valued at $48.3 million for tax purposes. This transaction highlights the ongoing trend of office-to-residential conversions aimed at addressing high office vacancy rates in cities like Houston.

Project Notes: Property was last renovated in 2023. The building was previously acquired in 2025 by 3L Real Estate from special servicers Midland, with plans for a $26.5 million apartment conversion.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Houston's office vacancy rate remains elevated at about 27 percent. Residential conversion is lauded as a solution for older office space, but financial and logistical challenges have limited successful project executions. Texas Senate Bill 840, passed in 2025, aims to ease conversions by removing zoning change requirements.
