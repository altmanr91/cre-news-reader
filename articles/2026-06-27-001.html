California Voters Will Decide If Local Taxes Should Be Harder to Approve

Commercial Observer — Fri, 26 Jun 2026 19:15:32 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

California voters will decide in November on a ballot measure that would require certain local special taxes, including those initiated by citizens, to pass with a two-thirds majority rather than a simple majority. This proposal is a compromise between state lawmakers and anti-tax advocates, aiming to address a perceived loophole in Proposition 218 regarding citizen-led tax initiatives. The measure, if passed, could significantly impact future local revenue measures affecting property values, development costs, and transaction activity, though it will not retroactively apply to existing taxes like Los Angeles' Measure ULA.
