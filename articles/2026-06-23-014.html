PolicyPro: Manhattan gets a tenant czar, ZD Jasper tests SEQRA reforms

The Real Deal — New York — Mon, 22 Jun 2026 23:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Manhattan's Borough President has appointed a new Director for Tenant Organizing, Alejandro Coriat, to advocate for tenant rights and educate residents on housing laws, fulfilling a campaign promise. This initiative aims to ensure tenant protections are enforced and to help tenants organize, though some landlord groups have expressed concerns about its one-sided nature. Separately, ZD Jasper Realty is the first developer in New York City to utilize recent State Environmental Quality Review Act (SEQRA) reforms, allowing its 143-unit residential project in Sunnyside to proceed without additional environmental review. This reform is intended to reduce costs and accelerate housing development, particularly for projects up to 500 units, as the city seeks to address its housing needs.
