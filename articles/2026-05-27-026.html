“Calculated scheme”: Brickell Key condo owners sue Swire over $32M seawall and baywalk assessment

The Real Deal — Miami — Tue, 26 May 2026 23:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Condominium associations on Brickell Key have filed a lawsuit against developer Swire Properties, alleging the company is illegally attempting to shift the financial burden of a $32.3 million seawall replacement and baywalk repair onto condo owners. The lawsuit claims that Swire, which developed the island, is violating state law by maintaining control of the master association for 44 years past the legal turnover threshold, and has used this control to improperly assess unit owners for work on property they do not own. The associations contend that the seawall is primarily owned by Swire and the City of Miami, and that the assessment was imposed without the required unit owner approval. Swire denies the allegations, stating it has not been served with the complaint and has always complied with all laws.
