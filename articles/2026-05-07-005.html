Continental Realty Acquires 14-Property U.S. Shopping Center Portfolio

ACQUISITION

Commercial Observer — Wed, 06 May 2026 20:14:42 +0000

SUMMARY

Continental Realty has acquired a portfolio of 14 shopping centers totaling over 2 million square feet across seven states in the Southeast and Midwest from U.S. Properties Group for approximately $200 million. This off-market transaction represents one of the largest U.S. retail portfolio deals this year, expanding Continental Realty's national shopping center portfolio to over 10.5 million square feet. The acquisition aligns with Continental Realty's strategy to invest in necessity-based retail in secondary markets, driven by population growth and affordability trends.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: retail

Size: 2,000,000 SF

Sale Price: $200.0M

Occupancy at Sale: 93.0%

Notable Features: Portfolio includes necessity-based retail centers with strong tenant mixes including major retailers.

Project Notes: The portfolio spans Georgia, Illinois, Tennessee, Ohio, North Carolina, South Carolina, and Virginia. The largest center in the portfolio is the 300,000-square-foot Streets of Indian Lake in Nashville, TN. This acquisition marks Continental Realty's first entry into Ohio with four shopping centers totaling 435,000 square feet.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: Continental Realty — Josh Dinstein, Co-Chief Investment Officer; JM Schapiro, CEO

SELLER: U.S. Properties Group

SELLER BROKER: CBRE — Chris Decouflé; Kevin Hurley

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Retail occupancy in the secondary markets covered by the portfolio averages 97 percent. CoStar Group data indicates this acquisition is among the largest U.S. retail portfolio transactions of the year so far.
