Proprietary Data Obsession Is Holding Real Estate Back From AI

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 13 May 2026 15:57:46 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Real estate firms are hesitant to fully embrace artificial intelligence due to concerns about proprietary data, despite the fact that large language models already access vast amounts of public and brokerage data. This 'data obsession' is hindering adoption, with only 5% of professionals trusting AI enough for critical decisions, according to a survey. Experts suggest that companies should focus on becoming 'data-smart' rather than 'data-secretive,' as employees are often using 'shadow AI' with company data anyway. To mitigate risks and leverage AI effectively, firms are advised to invest in company-wide systems, establish clear data usage contracts with third-party developers, and foster internal AI champions through training and collaborative initiatives.
