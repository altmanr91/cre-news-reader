Austin’s St. Johns multifamily project to cost $58M

CONSTRUCTION

The Real Deal — Texas — Tue, 26 May 2026 21:20:27 +0000

SUMMARY

Greystar is set to begin construction next month on the residential component of the St. Johns mixed-use project in Austin, TX. The multifamily development, comprising five buildings and 526 units, is estimated to cost $58.3 million. Over two-thirds of the units will be reserved for tenants earning below the area's median family income, reflecting a commitment to affordability in a market experiencing declining rents due to a supply boom.

Project Notes: The 19-acre site, formerly a Home Depot and Chrysler dealership, was acquired by the City of Austin in 2008 and 2013 using bond funds. The project will be divided into St. Johns South and St. Johns North, with the South Congress Public Facilities Corporation owning and ground leasing the parcels to Greystar. The project was halved to access more funding due to challenging market conditions.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Apartment rents in Austin have declined for 10 consecutive quarters following a supply boom during the pandemic. The city experienced a 4.8% year-over-year decline in average rent at the start of the year, the greatest drop among major metros.
