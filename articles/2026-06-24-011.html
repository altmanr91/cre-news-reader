High gas prices still putting a hurt on hotel industry

REJournals — Wed, 24 Jun 2026 09:58:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Hospitality Report

The U.S. hospitality sector is currently navigating a challenging summer travel season marked by cautious consumer spending due to high energy costs, increased airfare, and softer employment conditions, according to a report by Marcus & Millichap. Despite these short-term pressures, the industry is poised for a brighter future, supported by a significant shortage of new hotel construction and improving investment activity. The report highlights that limited-service hotels are experiencing the greatest slowdown in demand, while select-service, upscale, and luxury properties are demonstrating more resilience. The constrained development pipeline, which remains 34% below 2019 levels, is preventing oversupply and bolstering hotel fundamentals, while investment activity has seen a 19% increase in transaction volume over the past year.

DATA POINTS

Consumers altering vacation plans due to gasoline prices: 70%

Consumers planning to stay in paid lodging this summer: 45%

Limited-service hotel occupancy (May 2026): low-54% range

Select-service hotel occupancy vs. 2019 peak (May 2026): 1% below

Hotel additions vs. 2019 levels: 34% below

Hotel transaction volume increase (12 months ending March): 19%

Average hotel pricing per room key: $113,000

Average hotel capitalization rates: 8.7%

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Consumer sentiment has fallen to historically low levels. Occupancy in the limited-service segment has trended downward since 2019, falling from over 58% before the pandemic to the low-54% range by late 2025. Total room nights sold in the select-service segment have remained relatively stable since 2023. Hotel transaction volume has recovered to levels consistent with 2016. Pricing has remained relatively stable since 2023. Delinquency rates are relatively modest.
