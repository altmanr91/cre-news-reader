Beverly Hills nonprofit snags 262-unit apartment complex in San Jose for $88M

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Tue, 23 Jun 2026 14:18:22 +0000

SUMMARY

The Bedford Affordable Housing Foundation, a newly formed Beverly Hills-based nonprofit, has acquired the 262-unit Almaden Terrace apartments in San Jose for $87.7 million. The transaction was financed by Fannie Mae and the California Statewide Communities Development Authority, which issued $71.4 million in revenue bonds. The acquisition includes affordability covenants, requiring at least 40% of units to be reserved for low-income residents and 75% for those earning up to 80% of the area median income, addressing the rising rental costs in the San Jose market.
