Hotter prices are defrosting Miami’s spec market

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Miami — Mon, 11 May 2026 23:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Miami's spec mansion market is experiencing a resurgence, driven by soaring prices for ultra-luxury, move-in-ready homes. Developers who had previously paused new construction are now re-entering the market, acquiring prime sites to build high-end residences. The strong demand from affluent buyers, coupled with significant profit margins on finished homes, is making spec development an attractive investment despite rising construction costs and land prices. However, success hinges on careful cost management and the ability of luxury submarkets to sustain unprecedented pricing levels.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Prices for finished homes in Miami's ultra-luxury market range from $4,500 to over $6,000 per square foot. A waterfront Coral Gables estate recently flipped for $55 million, or $4,400 per square foot, representing a 62 percent gain. Construction costs have risen significantly, with cinderblock prices increasing from $0.80 to $2.00.
