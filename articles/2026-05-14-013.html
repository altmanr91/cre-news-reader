All that new supply? It’s suppressing apartment rent growth

REJournals — Thu, 14 May 2026 09:56:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Multifamily Report

The U.S. multifamily sector is experiencing sluggish rent growth due to a historic wave of new supply, particularly in Sun Belt and Mountain West markets, coupled with moderating demand. While national average advertised rents increased slightly in April to $1,758, they remain down 0.2% year-over-year, according to Yardi Matrix. Some previously struggling markets are showing signs of stabilization, but gateway and Midwest markets like New York City, San Francisco, and Chicago are outperforming the rest of the country in annual rent growth. The Twin Cities market, in particular, is projected for strong gains due to restrained new supply and solid demand.
