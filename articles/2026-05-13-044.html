Data Center Moratorium Will Come Before Charlotte City Council

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 12 May 2026 14:47:58 -0400

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The Charlotte City Council is preparing to vote on a proposed 150-day moratorium on new data center development, following a public hearing scheduled for May 26 and a potential vote on June 8. The moratorium aims to allow city officials to assess the impacts and risks of data centers, including concerns over rising electricity costs, noise, and pollution, and to develop new regulations. This local action reflects a growing trend of community backlash against data center development across the Carolinas, with other municipalities like Durham, Apex, and Chatham County having recently enacted similar moratoriums.
