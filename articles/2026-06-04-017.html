White House attempts to bring down construction costs

The Real Deal — National — Wed, 03 Jun 2026 18:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The White House has implemented temporary tariff adjustments on imported steel, aluminum, and copper, reducing levies on construction-related equipment from 25 percent to 15 percent until the end of next year. This measure aims to alleviate the burden of soaring material costs that have been impacting homebuilders, driven by geopolitical events and supply chain disruptions. The administration also introduced a future incentive for imports with high domestic content. However, it remains uncertain if these changes will significantly accelerate construction, particularly in the residential sector, which has seen a decline in single-family home starts due to material cost volatility.

DATA POINTS

Tariff reduction on construction equipment: from 25% to 15%

Future tariff eligibility threshold for domestic content: 85% (down from 95%) for 10% tariff

NAHB April confidence survey respondents reporting difficulty pricing homes due to material cost volatility: 70%

Diesel price jump since late February: approximately 50%

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Record-high copper prices and a roughly 50 percent jump in diesel prices since late February have contributed to soaring construction material costs. Single-family home construction fell across the country in the first quarter, while multifamily construction largely bucked that trend.
