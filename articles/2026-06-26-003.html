Al Adelson of the Berkeley Palm Beach: 5 Questions

CONSTRUCTION

Commercial Observer — Thu, 25 Jun 2026 19:15:32 +0000

SUMMARY

Developer Al Adelson is constructing The Berkeley Palm Beach, a luxury condominium tower in West Palm Beach, FL, with units priced from $2 million to $10 million. This project follows the success of his previous development, The Bristol, which demonstrated strong demand for high-end condos west of the Intracoastal Waterway. Adelson is leveraging new construction materials and technology to offer amenities and finishes comparable to The Bristol, but at a lower cost. The developer notes that significant infrastructure improvements and new institutional developments in the immediate vicinity, including a Vanderbilt University graduate school and a Cleveland Clinic hospital, are enhancing the site's appeal and driving sales momentum.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: condo

Property Name: The Berkeley Palm Beach

Address: 500 South Australian Avenue, West Palm Beach, FL

Current Occupancy: 20.0%

Notable Features: Located on Clear Lake, which serves as the water supply for West Palm Beach and Palm Beach Island, ensuring no recreational use. Proximity to a new Vanderbilt University graduate school and a 200-bed Cleveland Clinic hospital.

Project Notes: Units are priced from $2 million to $10 million. Construction has commenced. Fern Street is approved to cross the railroad track to Australian Avenue, improving connectivity to I-95.

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Al Adelson — Al Adelson, Developer

SPONSOR PIPELINE
