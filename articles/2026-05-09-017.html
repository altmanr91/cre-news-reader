“Icing on the cake”: Trump’s “big, beautiful” tax law sweetens deals amid surge in owner-user offices

The Real Deal — National — Fri, 08 May 2026 14:39:22 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

A new federal tax law, the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, has permanently reinstated 100 percent bonus depreciation, significantly incentivizing companies to purchase their own office spaces. This legislation allows owner-users to fully deduct the value of certain building components and operational necessities in the year of purchase, rather than spreading deductions over decades. This tax benefit, combined with declining office values and the desire for customized spaces in a hybrid work environment, is contributing to a surge in owner-user office acquisitions nationwide. Experts note that while bonus depreciation is not the sole driver, it acts as a significant 'icing on the cake' for firms already considering ownership.

DATA POINTS

Owner-user purchases last year: $8.9 billion

Owner-user share of office deal volume last year: 15.6%

Eligible building portions for deductions: Roughly 30% of an office's value

Bonus depreciation effective date: Properties bought and opened after January 19, 2025
