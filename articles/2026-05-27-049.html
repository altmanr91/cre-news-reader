Canary Wharf's £1.5B BTR Bet Pays Off

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 26 May 2026 11:21:32 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / BTR Portfolio

Canary Wharf Group's build-to-rent division, Vertus, has successfully established one of London's largest BTR platforms, nearing the completion of its build-out phase. Despite launching its first building, 10 George Street, in February 2020 at the onset of the pandemic, the portfolio now includes three stabilized properties and two new towers at 50-60 Charter Street, with another at 40 Charter Street completing in January. The residential expansion is a key part of Canary Wharf Group's strategy to diversify beyond corporate offices, with the BTR portfolio expected to total 2,445 units, contributing to nearly 4,000 homes built on the estate. The stabilized assets boast a 95% occupancy rate and a 60% tenant retention rate, attracting a diverse occupier profile beyond initial expectations of solely Canary Wharf workers.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Rental growth has slowed to low single digits annually after a post-pandemic boom. The new Renters Rights Act, which removes fixed tenancies, has not impacted occupancy rates. The occupier profile shows 25-35% of residents work in Canary Wharf, a lower figure than initially expected, indicating success in attracting residents from other parts of London.
