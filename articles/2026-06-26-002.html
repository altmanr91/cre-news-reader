Habitat for Humanity to Build 179 Co-op Units in Brooklyn and the Bronx

DEVELOPMENT

Commercial Observer — Thu, 25 Jun 2026 19:39:26 +0000

SUMMARY

Habitat for Humanity is developing two cooperative housing projects totaling 179 units across eight sites in Brooklyn and one building in the Bronx, representing the organization's largest construction pipeline to date. The projects, named Constellation in Brooklyn and Claremont House in the Bronx, are designed to provide affordable homeownership opportunities for working New Yorkers, addressing the "missing middle" demographic. These developments leverage various city, state, and federal subsidy programs, including the Open Door Program and the New Infill Housing Opportunity Program from the New York City Department of Housing Preservation and Development, and are supported by recent pro-development reforms and significant affordable housing investments from the city.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Property Name: Constellation / Claremont House

Units: 179

Original Plan: Constellation: 77 units; Claremont House: 40 units

Notable Features: The buildings will sit on a 99-year ground lease from the Community Land Trust.

Project Notes: The Constellation development in Brooklyn was expanded from 77 to 108 units, and the Claremont House in the Bronx was expanded from 40 to 71 units. Both expansions were enabled by the City of Yes for Housing Opportunity reforms.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Habitat for Humanity — Sabrina Lippman, CEO, New York City and Westchester County

OWNER: Community Land Trust

FINANCING

Utilizes city, state, and federal programs such as the Open Door Program and the New Infill Housing Opportunity Program from the New York City Department of Housing Preservation and Development, and the state’s Homes and Community Renewal program. Additional tax abatements for homeowners through Housing Development Fund Corporation (HDFC) co-ops.
