Hostage to the HOA: How some developers keep a tight grip on owner associations

The Real Deal — Miami — Tue, 23 Jun 2026 18:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

This article examines how some residential developers in Florida are allegedly retaining control of homeowners' associations (HOAs) and condominium associations long after state law dictates they should cede power to residents. The practice, which experts say goes against the spirit of the law, allows developers to maintain contractor relationships, delay construction defect lawsuits, and potentially profit from in-house management contracts. Homeowners in several complexes, including Portofino Vista in St. Cloud and Brickell Key in Miami, claim developer-controlled boards neglect property upkeep, lack financial transparency, and levy significant assessments for projects that may primarily benefit the developer's future plans. While developers often assert they are operating within their rights, critics argue this prolonged control grants immense power, enabling them to assess fees and pursue legal action against homeowners who resist.
