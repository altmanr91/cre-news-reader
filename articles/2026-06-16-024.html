South Florida real estate bets on World Cup for slow season boost

The Real Deal — Miami — Mon, 15 Jun 2026 22:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

South Florida's real estate and hospitality sectors are anticipating a significant boost from the FIFA World Cup, which is expected to draw 700,000 visitors and generate $1.3 billion in spending. With seven matches scheduled at Hard Rock Stadium, developers, hoteliers, and retailers across Miami-Dade and Broward counties are implementing various fan activations and marketing campaigns to capitalize on the event. This influx of visitors is projected to extend the typically slow summer season, with residential developers noting an uptick in foreign buyer interest and hoteliers reporting increased bookings and average daily rates compared to previous years.
