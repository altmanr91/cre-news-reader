Texas mall teardown revives long-stalled Dallas-to-Houston bullet train plans

DEVELOPMENT

Texas - The Real Deal — Wed, 29 Apr 2026 22:22:43 +0000

SUMMARY

Demolition has begun on the 800,000-square-foot Northwest Mall in Houston, clearing the way for the proposed Houston terminal of the Dallas-to-Houston high-speed rail line. This action by Texas High Speed Rail Holdings signals a potential revival for the long-stalled project, despite recent setbacks including the withdrawal of a significant federal grant. The site, strategically located near major interchanges, is envisioned as a future transit-oriented development with retail, dining, and public spaces.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: retail

Property Name: Northwest Mall

Address: 9500 Hempstead Road, Houston, Texas

Size: 800,000 SF

Site Area: 45 acres

Notable Features: Located close to the I-610 Loop and US 290 interchange, designated as the Houston terminus for a proposed high-speed rail line.

Project Notes: Demolition of the mall began in early April 2026 and is expected to take about a year. The site is being prepared for a future transit-oriented hub with retail, restaurants, public gathering space, and structured parking.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Texas High Speed Rail Holdings
