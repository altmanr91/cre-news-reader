“Equity investors should expect a full loss of capital,” warns feeder fund for S2 Capital’s REIT

The Real Deal — Texas — Fri, 08 May 2026 15:45:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Trinity Investors, a private equity firm that helped establish S2 Capital's multifamily REIT in 2024, has warned its equity investors to expect a full loss of capital. The REIT, which owns over 9,000 units across North Texas, Houston, and Phoenix, is reportedly shifting its focus to maximizing value for mezzanine investors. This update follows a capital call in January for $70 million in preferred equity, of which S2 Capital ultimately raised $30 million, providing a short runway for an orderly wind-down of the REIT. The firm attributes these challenges to a 32 percent drop in rents and a 26 percent increase in expenses since October 2022, compounded by elevated interest rates.
