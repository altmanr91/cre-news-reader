Entitled SF high-rise resi sites fall into default before shovels hit dirt

LOAN

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Thu, 14 May 2026 21:38:37 +0000

SUMMARY

Arixa Capital has issued notices of default to Forma Development Design Management for two planned residential high-rise sites in San Francisco, totaling over $17 million in outstanding debt. The developer reportedly failed to repay loans tied to 896 Folsom Street and 777 Sutter Street upon maturity. Both projects, which received city approvals in 2025 for apartment towers with ground-floor retail, have not yet broken ground, reflecting the broader challenges developers face in San Francisco's high-cost construction environment.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Loan Amount: $15.1M

Notable Features: Both planned towers include ground-floor retail.

Project Notes: The 896 Folsom Street site was previously acquired for just over $2 million in 2008. The developer resolved a prior default on the Folsom Street site in 2022 by refinancing with the current Arixa Capital loan. The Sutter Street project faced neighborhood opposition over its height.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

LENDER: Arixa Capital

SPONSOR: Forma Development Design Management — Walid Mando, Founder

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

San Francisco's development pipeline has largely frozen due to rents and condo prices lagging behind construction costs, despite rebounding downtown foot traffic and improving investor sentiment.
