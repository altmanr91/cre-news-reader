S3 Capital Lends $102M for Hell’s Kitchen Office-to-Resi Conversion

LOAN

Commercial Observer — Tue, 23 Jun 2026 20:08:30 +0000

SUMMARY

S3 Capital has provided $102 million in construction financing to Hershy Silberstein for the conversion of the Press Building in Hell's Kitchen, Manhattan, from office to residential use. The project will create 160 apartments and 39,712 square feet of commercial space. This transaction marks S3 Capital's first office-to-residential conversion loan, attracted by the sponsor's strong relationship with the lender, the property's prime location in a supply-constrained submarket, and its suitable layout for conversion. The development will benefit from the 467-m tax abatement program, with 25% of units designated for affordable housing.

Project Notes: 25% of units will be set aside for people earning an average of 80% of area median income. The 15-story building was previously acquired by DivcoWest Real Estate Investments in December 2018 for $131 million. Hershy Silberstein recently acquired the property for an undisclosed price after a prior deal with David Werner for around $40 million fell through.
