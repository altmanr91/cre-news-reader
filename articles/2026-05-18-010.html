What to expect at ICSC 2026: Dealmaking, flaunting and partying in Vegas

The Real Deal — National — Sun, 17 May 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The International Council of Shopping Centers (ICSC) conference in Las Vegas is anticipated to draw over 25,000 attendees for days of dealmaking and networking. This year's event features a "Professional Development Day" at the Wynn Las Vegas, followed by the main exhibition. The retail sector is experiencing stability, with national asking rents increasing by over 2% year-over-year to approximately $25 per square foot, driven by tight supply and positive net absorption. While the retail availability rate saw a slight increase to 5% due to some bankruptcies, grocery-anchored centers and suburban retail continue to outperform urban markets. Industry executives are attending to gauge market sentiment, explore new retail and food & beverage concepts for developments, discuss debt strategies amid upcoming loan maturities, and delve into the practical implementation of AI in commercial real estate.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Retail availability rate rose slightly to 5% due to retailer bankruptcies and store closures. Grocery-anchored centers are performing better than older malls, and suburban retail is outperforming downtown retail. Manhattan and Los Angeles had higher availability rates compared to markets in the Sun Belt.
