Vornado, Stellar reveal plans for Independence Plaza expansion

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 14 May 2026 18:26:25 +0000

SUMMARY

Vornado Realty Trust and Stellar Management are moving forward with plans for a significant expansion of Independence Plaza in Tribeca, proposing a 72-story, 976-unit building. The development, which will include a substantial affordable housing component, is designed to avoid the Uniform Land Use Review Procedure (ULURP) by being structured as a modification to the existing site plan. The developers have committed to further community benefits and upgrades to the complex, addressing resident concerns about construction impacts and the project's integration into the neighborhood.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Property Name: Independence Plaza

Address: 310 Greenwich Street, New York City, NY

Units: 976

Notable Features: The proposed building will be 72 stories tall and include 251 affordable units or units built elsewhere under the Universal Affordability Preference program. The existing complex has 1,328 units across three buildings, with 40% being rent-regulated or affordable, and includes 55,000 square feet of retail space, a fitness center, swimming pool, children's playroom, and laundry facilities.

Project Notes: The developers spent $33 million on upgrades in the last five years and plan to spend another $15 million on community benefits in the next two years. The project is structured as a modification of the larger site plan to avoid ULURP, but will require an environmental review and other approvals. The complex was previously rent-regulated under the Mitchell-Lama program.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE
