West Dundee pitches 100-acre Spring Hill Mall site for redevelopment

The Real Deal — Chicago — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 21:02:33 +0000

SUMMARY

Property Listing / For Sale

West Dundee is actively seeking developers for the 100-acre former Spring Hill Mall site, presenting two conceptual mixed-use models to attract interest. The village invested $13 million to acquire various mall parcels, including those previously owned by Kohan Retail Investment Group, and subsequently demolished the 1980s-era shopping center to prepare the land for redevelopment. The proposed concepts prioritize housing density and retail, with one model suggesting 778 housing units and 320,000 square feet of retail, and another featuring 1,326 residential units and 400,000 square feet of retail, both incorporating pedestrian-focused streetscapes and park space. Village officials anticipate a redevelopment timeline of up to 10 years, emphasizing that the models are starting points for discussion with potential developers.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Municipalities across Chicagoland are taking direct control of distressed retail hubs for redevelopment, such as Bloomingdale acquiring Stratford Square Mall for $17 million. Private players are also active, with Xroads Real Estate Advisors purchasing Lincolnwood Town Center for $12.3 million for mixed-use redevelopment. Other projects include the proposed $440 million overhaul of Golf Mill Town Center in Niles and residential additions at Westfield Old Orchard in Skokie and Yorktown Center in Lombard. Ford City Mall in Chicago is slated for a retail-to-industrial conversion, with Kurv Industrial under contract to acquire the 66-acre property for $150 million to build a 913,000 SF logistics campus.
