The $100M office: Ranking Chicago’s priciest tenant buildouts

The Real Deal — Chicago — Fri, 01 May 2026 11:15:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Chicago's office market is experiencing a surge in tenant buildout spending, driven by high vacancy rates and the need for landlords to attract and retain tenants in renovated Class A towers. Over the past three years, the top 11 spenders on office interiors in Chicago invested over $600 million, with costs per square foot for elite spaces sometimes exceeding $300, significantly higher than pre-2020 levels. This increased spending has led to more complex lease negotiations, often involving lenders, especially for financially distressed buildings. Tenant brokers are increasingly structuring tri-party agreements with owners, receivers, and special servicers to ensure buildout funds are secured, even in cases of potential foreclosure. While tenants are generally taking less space, they are investing heavily in creating sophisticated, collaborative environments to entice employees back to the office, raising the bar for quality and amenities across the market.
