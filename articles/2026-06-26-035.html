Presidio Bay opens the curtains on 17-acre urban village in Menlo Park

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Thu, 25 Jun 2026 19:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Presidio Bay Ventures has unveiled new renderings for its 17.75-acre urban village development in Menlo Park, which will feature nearly 700 residential units, three office buildings, and retail space. The project, which replaces former federal buildings, is designed to include a mix of housing, offices, shops, restaurants, and public parks, with a focus on warm tones and natural materials. The development is a significant undertaking for the city, contributing substantially to its state-mandated housing goals.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: mixed-use

Address: 345 Middlefield Road, Menlo Park, CA

Size: 713,000 SF

Units: 670

Total Project Cost: $1.00B

$/Acre: $56,338,028/acre (calculated)

$/Land SF: $1,293.34/land SF (calculated)

Site Area: 17.75 acres

Notable Features: The project includes 43,000 square feet of shops and restaurants, a 10,800-square-foot childcare center, and 1.5 million square feet of parking for over 1,700 cars and 1,200 bicycles. It will also feature a sports court, a two-story pavilion, parks, plazas, and lawns.

Project Notes: The 17.75-acre development replaces 16 former federal buildings, including a 20,000-square-foot office lab. The site was previously acquired by Presidio Bay from the U.S. government for $137 million. Of the 670 residential units, 118 will be designated as affordable.
