PolicyPro: Mamdani faces political referendum, housing shapes Assembly race

The Real Deal — New York — Tue, 23 Jun 2026 23:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Political & Crime News

Today's Democratic primaries in New York City are poised to significantly impact the real estate industry, testing the extent of the Democratic Party's leftward shift. The elections will gauge Mayor Zohran Mamdani's political influence, particularly through his endorsed candidates in high-stakes congressional races. A key Lower Manhattan Assembly race, replacing long-time incumbent Deborah Glick, is seen as a referendum on housing policy, pitting a candidate aligned with anti-development sentiment against a pro-growth advocate. The results will indicate whether the district remains resistant to new development or is open to candidates supporting increased housing construction.
