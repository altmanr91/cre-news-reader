Freddie Mac to auction foreclosed Austin apartment complex with $9.5M opening bid

The Real Deal — Texas — Mon, 08 Jun 2026 20:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Property Listing / For Sale

Freddie Mac is auctioning a foreclosed 526-unit apartment complex at 1601 Royal Crest Drive in Austin, TX, with an opening bid of $9.5 million. The mortgage giant foreclosed on the property after the previous owner, Mia Riverside, allegedly defaulted on a $50.2 million loan. Freddie Mac acquired the vacant property at a January foreclosure auction for $50 million. The 20.2-acre site is zoned for neighborhood mixed-use, allowing for residential, small employers, and neighborhood retail, with specific building height and setback requirements.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Austin's housing market experienced an increase in foreclosures in April, leading the nation year-over-year, primarily affecting luxury condos and high-priced areas. Other high-profile Austin properties, including The Line Austin and Hyatt Centric, also recently went to foreclosure auctions, returning to their lenders for a combined $222.5 million.
