Pocket listings face another setback as New York advances crackdown

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 12 Jun 2026 23:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

New York's state Legislature has passed the Fair and Transparent Real Estate Listings Act, a bill aimed at increasing transparency in property marketing by requiring agents to publicly market properties unless sellers or landlords explicitly opt out. This legislation, which mandates public listing on widely accessible platforms, is a response to evolving practices in private listings and seeks to ensure broader exposure and competition for properties. If signed into law by Governor Kathy Hochul, it could serve as a model for other states, following similar measures adopted in Washington, Wisconsin, and Connecticut, with Illinois and Hawaii also considering such regulations. Proponents argue that greater transparency benefits consumers by potentially leading to higher sale prices and more offers, while also deterring agents from exclusively pushing private listing networks.
