PolicyPro: City Council revives COPA with “marginal changes”

The Real Deal — New York — Mon, 18 May 2026 22:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

The New York City Council has reintroduced a revised version of the Community Opportunity to Purchase Act (COPA), a bill designed to give city-approved nonprofits and their joint ventures the first right to purchase distressed multifamily buildings or properties with expiring affordability restrictions. The updated bill, championed by Council member Sandy Nurse, aims to be more targeted by narrowing eligibility criteria for properties and shortening procedural timelines for nonprofits to express interest and make offers. While some changes have been made, critics argue that the core impact of the bill, which could delay property sales, remains largely the same. The revised bill also introduces a clearer penalty structure for non-compliant owners, imposing fines of up to 15 percent of a property's sale price.
