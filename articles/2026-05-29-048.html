Mori Building Buys Stake In SL Green's Next Trophy As Japan's U.S. CRE Interest Deepens

ACQUISITION

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 28 May 2026 11:44:00 -0400

SUMMARY

Mori Building Co., a Tokyo-based developer, has acquired a 49% equity stake in SL Green's planned trophy office tower at 346 Madison Avenue in Midtown Manhattan. The deal values the asset at $175 million and marks Mori Building Co.'s first development project in New York City, with SL Green serving as development and leasing manager. This partnership highlights the increasing interest of Japanese firms in U.S. real estate, particularly in high-quality office assets, as they seek diversification and higher returns amidst a slowing domestic economy.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Japanese investors were the second-biggest international investors in U.S. real estate in 2025, behind Canada. Trophy office owners in Manhattan were able to charge three figures per square foot in rents in Q1. Japanese firms are diversifying into other geographies and asset classes due to a slowing domestic economy and rising cost of borrowing.
