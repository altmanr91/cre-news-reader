Embattled Fulton Market property back on the market

The Real Deal — Chicago — Thu, 14 May 2026 17:44:01 +0000

SUMMARY

Property Listing / For Sale

A half-acre property in Chicago's Fulton Market, previously slated for an office development, is back on the market with a new strategy to rezone for residential construction. The site, owned by Grey Bemis-Kelley, has a history of failed transactions, including a litigious dispute with a previous buyer, Fortem Voluntas, over a proposed 17-story office project. Brokers are now marketing the property for multifamily use, citing the area's strong performance and existing zoning density entitlements.
