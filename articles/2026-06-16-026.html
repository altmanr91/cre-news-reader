Highgate Hotels sells downtown Chicago La Quinta for $31M

SALE

The Real Deal — Chicago — Tue, 16 Jun 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Highgate Hotels, in a joint venture with Cerberus Capital Management, sold the 239-room La Quinta hotel in downtown Chicago for $30.5 million to JAMP Hotels. This sale is part of Highgate's strategy to offload properties from a $1.5 billion portfolio acquired in 2021, particularly as a $625 million loan against 29 of these hotels faced imminent monetary default. JAMP Hotels plans to invest in revamping the property's amenities, expressing strong confidence in the future of Chicago's downtown hospitality sector, which has shown promising tourism recovery post-pandemic.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Chicago's hospitality sector saw 3.6 million room nights filled downtown in June, July, and August of 2025, a 4% increase over 2024 and higher than the pre-pandemic record set in 2019. The city's tourism board is implementing a 1.5% surcharge to double the budget of tourism agency Choose Chicago, a measure self-imposed by downtown hotel owners.
