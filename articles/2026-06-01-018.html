Will AI kill commercial real estate?

The Real Deal — National — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 11:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article presents a nuanced debate on the potential impact of artificial intelligence on commercial real estate, particularly the office sector. Sarah Mancuso of JLL argues that while AI will displace some workflows, it will ultimately create new categories of work and drive demand for specialized real estate, particularly from AI-native companies and data centers. She emphasizes that human judgment and relationships remain crucial in CRE, which AI cannot replicate. Conversely, Vikram Malhotra of Mizuho Americas suggests that AI poses a significant risk to office demand by potentially slowing "office-using" job growth from historical rates to a much lower pace, impacting office, retail, and residential sectors as companies prioritize efficiency.

DATA POINTS

Meta 2026 data center spending forecast: $125 billion to $145 billion

Bay Area AI-occupied space: 17 million square feet

Projected additional AI-occupied space by 2030 (JLL Research): 20 to 35 million square feet

Morgan Stanley estimated operating efficiencies from AI in CRE by 2030: $34 billion

Historical office-using job growth rate: 2% to 3% per year

Projected office-using job growth rate with AI impact: 0.5% per year

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Construction spending on offices is down from a 2020 peak, while outlays on data centers have increased and are now level with general office spending, according to U.S. Census Bureau data analyzed by Paul Kedrosky.
