Domus’ Fulton Market apartment tower plan hits setback

The Real Deal — Chicago — Thu, 21 May 2026 19:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Development

Chicago's City Council has postponed a vote on Domus Group's proposed 29-story, 347-unit residential tower in Fulton Market due to the developer's failure to finalize the purchase of land designated for a public park. The $150 million project, which seeks a zoning designation typically reserved for downtown skyscrapers, has faced controversy over its intensity and the precedent it might set for future developments in the area. The public park was a critical component that allowed the Chicago Plan Commission to advance the project despite initial zoning administrator recommendations against it.
