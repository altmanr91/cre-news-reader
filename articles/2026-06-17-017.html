NYC’s top construction permits: Week ending June 12, 2026

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 12:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Construction / Permits

The Gotham Organization has filed plans for a significant mixed-use development in Brooklyn's East New York, comprising three buildings with 599 residential units and over 533,000 square feet of space. Other notable new building permits include a 23-story mixed-use tower in Long Island City by ZD Jasper and a 99-unit project in Greenpoint by Mendy Schwimmer. Major alteration permits were filed for St. Catherine Academy in the Bronx and two East Village properties by L&M Development. Demolition permits were also issued for a large property in Jamaica, Queens, and multiple structures in the Bronx and East Harlem.

DATA POINTS

Largest New Building Permit (Brooklyn): 533,487 SF, 599 units, three buildings (12-14 stories) at 12040 Flatlands Avenue, 395 Louisiana Avenue, 12030 Flatlands Avenue

New Building Permit (Long Island City): 120,490 SF, 99 apartments, 23-story mixed-use building at 35-25 Steinway Street

New Building Permit (Greenpoint): 105,210 SF, 99 units, nine-story mixed-use building at 70 West Street

Top Major Alteration Permit (Bronx): 24,952 SF gym addition at St. Catherine Academy, 2250 Williamsbridge Road

Major Alteration Permit (East Village): 14,100 SF alterations at 641 East 13th Street

Major Alteration Permit (East Village): 13,008 SF alterations at 612 East 14th Street

Top Demolition Permit (Jamaica, Queens): 46,918 SF one-story property at 89-41 164th Street

Demolition Permit (East Tremont, Bronx): 13,179 SF combined (9,027 SF at 761 East Tremont Avenue, 3,217 SF at 1970 Prospect Avenue, 935 SF at 1974 Prospect Avenue)

Demolition Permit (East Harlem): 11,000 SF, five-story, 13-unit building at 2 East 116th Street
