PolicyPro: City quietly drops pied-à-terre tax rules, comptroller bullish on revenue

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 23:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

New York City tax officials have released proposed rules for implementing the contentious pied-à-terre tax, which is set to take effect in July. The 17-page proposal outlines how the Department of Finance will distinguish primary residents, who are exempt, from part-time owners subject to the tax, primarily through annual reviews of tax returns and residency-based benefits. The rules also address properties held by LLCs, corporations, and partnerships, requiring full ownership of the property interest or co-op shares for exemption eligibility. The city comptroller's office has revised its revenue projections, now expecting the tax to meet or exceed its annual $500 million target, a significant increase from earlier estimates, based on a clearer understanding of the tax base which includes over 13,586 units.

DATA POINTS

Annual revenue target for pied-à-terre tax: $500 million

Estimated units subject to pied-à-terre tax: 13,586

Potential surcharge for a $5.1 million condo/co-op in first two years: $325,650
