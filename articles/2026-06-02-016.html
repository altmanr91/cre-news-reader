Manhattan office market on pace for best year since Y2K

The Real Deal — New York — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 20:13:12 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Office Report

Manhattan's office market is on track for its strongest year since 2000, driven by robust leasing activity in May. The month saw 4.2 million square feet leased, a 35% increase year-over-year, bringing the year-to-date total to 19.6 million square feet. This surge was propelled by several large transactions, including a significant lease by a law firm at a new development and major renewals by prominent tech and financial tenants. The strong demand has led to a decrease in the availability rate to 13.2% and a rise in average asking rents to $77.76 per square foot, the highest since August 2020.

DATA POINTS

May 2026 Leasing Volume: 4.2 million square feet

Year-to-Date Leasing Volume: 19.6 million square feet

Manhattan Availability Rate: 13.2%

Available Inventory: 69.2 million square feet

Manhattan Average Asking Rent: $77.76 per square foot

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Manhattan's available office inventory has fallen to 69.2 million square feet, the lowest level since October 2020 and nearly 30% below its post-pandemic peak of 98 million square feet. Sublease space has decreased by 25% year-over-year. Midtown accounted for over half of all leasing activity in May, with its leasing volume nearly doubling from April and the previous year. Midtown South's leasing volume, while down from April, remained above its ten-year average.
