Airport-adjacent tract snapped up near Sherman

SALE

The Real Deal — Texas — Thu, 28 May 2026 22:27:13 +0000

SUMMARY

A 209-acre agricultural tract near the North Texas Regional Airport in Sherman, TX, was sold to OTW Preston 209 Limited, an entity linked to Jim Hazard of Cresa. The land, previously listed for $9.4 million, was acquired from individual investor Sreeramulu Bokkisham. This transaction highlights the increasing investment in the Sherman-Denison area, driven by the airport's expansion plans and significant industrial developments like Texas Instruments' semiconductor plant.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: land

Site Area: 209 acres

Notable Features: Located across from North Texas Regional Airport, listed for $45,000 per acre.

Project Notes: The seller, Sreeramulu Bokkisham, acquired the land in 2022.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: OTW Preston 209 Limited — Jim Hazard, Executive Managing Principal

SELLER: Point Break Real Estate Investments — Sreeramulu Bokkisham, Individual Investor

SELLER BROKER: Butch Fife Realtors — Butch Fife

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The North Texas Regional Airport is preparing to update its lighting and install a new taxiway to meet spillover demand from Dallas-area air travel. McKinney National Airport plans to complete a commercial terminal with passenger service by the end of 2026. Texas Instruments is developing a $40 billion semiconductor manufacturing plant on a 1,200-acre site in Sherman. Apple is also planning a laser equipment manufacturing plant in Sherman. Craig International is developing Preston Harbor, a 3,100-acre project in Denison.
