Braemar’s biggest shareholder demands shakeup over potential $480M payout

The Real Deal — National — Mon, 11 May 2026 15:06:30 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Braemar Hotels & Resorts' largest shareholder, Al Shams Investments, is demanding a board overhaul, alleging that the luxury hotel REIT's strategic review and potential hotel dispositions are structured to benefit an adviser tied to Chair Monty Bennett at the expense of public investors. Al Shams claims that a series of hotel sales could trigger a "change of control" clause in Braemar's advisory agreement, leading to a termination payment exceeding $480 million to Ashford Inc., an external adviser controlled by Bennett. This payout, the investor argues, would occur before shareholders realize any proceeds from asset sales, effectively wiping out value. Al Shams is preparing to nominate an alternative slate of directors at Braemar's 2026 annual meeting.

DATA POINTS

Potential termination payout to Ashford Inc.: $480 million

Threshold for change of control (1 year): 20% of gross asset value

Threshold for change of control (3 years): 30% of gross asset value

Sale price of Park Hyatt Beaver Creek Resort & Spa: $176 million
