City Invests $1B for New Affordable Housing Preservation Program

Commercial Observer — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 18:08:14 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The New York City Department of Housing Preservation and Development (HPD) has launched the Supportive Preservation Program (SPP), a $1 billion initiative aimed at preserving over 30,000 units of supportive affordable housing across the city. The program will offer tax exemptions, below-market loans, and loan modifications to existing supportive housing projects that have social service contracts with the city. This effort is part of Mayor Zohran Mamdani's broader "Block by Block: The Housing Plan for a New Era," which seeks to create and preserve 400,000 affordable homes over the next decade.
