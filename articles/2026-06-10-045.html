Tech Giants Have Long Kept Data Center Deals Secret. Public Outcry Is Forcing A Rethink

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 09 Jun 2026 12:35:29 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The data center industry is facing increasing public scrutiny over its long-standing practice of requiring local government officials to sign non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) for proposed projects. While these NDAs have historically been used to protect proprietary information and prevent land price inflation, they are now becoming a significant political liability, fueling public opposition to new data center developments. Microsoft has notably ceased using NDAs with local governments, citing a commitment to transparency and trust-building, though other tech giants like Google, Amazon, and Meta continue to defend their use. The debate highlights a tension between corporate confidentiality and public accountability, as developers and tech firms seek to balance strategic interests with community relations amid growing demand for AI infrastructure.
