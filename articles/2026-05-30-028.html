Mag Mile office building careening towards foreclosure

FORECLOSURE

The Real Deal — Chicago — Fri, 29 May 2026 18:39:10 +0000

SUMMARY

The 28-story office building at 625 North Michigan Avenue in Chicago is facing an imminent foreclosure lawsuit, with its owners, Golub and BlueFive Capital, reportedly cooperating with special servicer LNR Partners. The property's $50.6 million CMBS loan, originated in 2019, was transferred to special servicing last December due to significant cash flow issues and a sharp decline in occupancy from 92% in 2019 to 64% in summer 2025. This marks the second Magnificent Mile property for Golub to face foreclosure within a year, highlighting the ongoing challenges in Chicago's downtown office market.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Downtown Chicago office space has been significantly impacted by work-from-home trends, leading to distress in several previously stable buildings. While retail is recovering and residential mixed-use redevelopments are occurring, the office market has not yet rebounded. An office tower at 500 North Michigan Avenue recently sold for $5.1 million for residential conversion.
