Illinois' Bears Bill Stalls, Setting Stage For Potential Indiana Move

Bisnow News Feed — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 13:14:09 -0400

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The Illinois legislature adjourned without passing a bill that would have provided incentives for a new stadium development, potentially jeopardizing the Chicago Bears' plans to remain in the state. Governor JB Pritzker expressed reluctance to commit billions in taxpayer money to a billionaire-owned team. This legislative inaction positions Indiana as a strong contender to attract the team, as its governor recently signed a bill creating a financial framework for a new Bears stadium in Hammond, involving over $2 billion from the team and approximately $1 billion from the state through various taxes.
