The Plan: Lower Manhattan’s Wrey Rental Tower Is Designed Like a Private Club

DEVELOPMENT

Commercial Observer — Wed, 24 Jun 2026 10:00:33 +0000

SUMMARY

GFP Development is converting a 31-story former office building at 222 Broadway in Lower Manhattan into a 788-unit luxury rental tower named The Wrey. The project, which began move-ins in June and is expected to be fully delivered in Q1 next year, aims to meet significant demand from professionals seeking high-quality housing in the area. The building is designed with extensive amenities, including two pools, a bathhouse, coworking spaces, and a private dining room, to create an exclusive club-like atmosphere for residents. The developer is also considering opting into a state tax break program that requires a percentage of units to be affordable.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Property Name: The Wrey

Address: 222 Broadway, New York City, NY

Units: 788

Completion: Q1 2027

Notable Features: Luxury rental tower converted from a former office building, featuring two pools, a bathhouse, coworking space, private dining, and a spa. Located near City Hall Park, overlooking Broadway, Saint Paul's Chapel, and the World Trade Center.

Project Notes: Developer is considering opting into the state's 467-m program, which offers tax breaks for nonresidential-to-housing conversions with an affordable housing component. The building's name is inspired by the Jacob Wrey Mould memorial fountain in City Hall Park.
