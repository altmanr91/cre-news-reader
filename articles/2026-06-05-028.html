Fisher Island fuel depot drama pushes out Miami-Dade’s port director, COO

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Miami — Thu, 04 Jun 2026 21:44:46 +0000

SUMMARY

Miami-Dade County's chief operating officer and PortMiami director have resigned amidst controversy surrounding the county's pending $400 million acquisition of a fuel depot on Fisher Island. The county's proposed deal with HRP Group, which purchased the property for $180 million in October, is facing a lawsuit from the Fisher Island Community Association and Fisher Island Club. The lawsuit seeks to compel HRP Group to honor a prior agreement to demolish the fuel bunk, remediate the land, and develop luxury condominiums, as well as convey 4 acres to the community association. The county is now reportedly considering eminent domain to acquire the property, a move also being challenged in court.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: industrial

Site Area: 10 acres

Project Notes: HRP Group previously acquired the property for $180 million in October. The county's proposed acquisition is for a fuel depot with a lease expiring in 2027. The county is being sued by the Fisher Island Community Association and Fisher Island Club to force HRP Group to honor a prior development agreement.
