“Save Boca law” requires voter approval for future land deals

The Real Deal — Miami — Wed, 13 May 2026 17:45:17 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The Boca Raton City Council unanimously approved an ordinance, known as the "Save Boca law," requiring voter approval for the sale, lease, or alienation of city-owned land larger than half an acre. This ordinance is a direct response to the overwhelming voter rejection of the One Boca megadevelopment in March, which was planned for city-owned land. The council also voted to propose incorporating this law into the city charter via an election before March of next year, reflecting a shift towards greater public accountability in land use decisions.
