Texas Retail Brokers Adjust To New Realities

REBusinessOnline — Wed, 27 May 2026 11:46:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article explores the evolving landscape for retail brokers in Texas, particularly those specializing in tenant representation, highlighting the increased complexities and extended timelines in securing retail leases. Despite a strong resurgence in brick-and-mortar retail, macroeconomic factors like high land and construction costs, coupled with elevated occupancy expenses, are creating significant challenges. Brokers are increasingly adopting advisory roles, educating tenants on market terms, setting realistic expectations, and assisting with due diligence, permits, and build-outs to navigate a tight market where landlords can be selective. The process from initial information gathering to store opening can now take two to four years, demanding greater effort and an anticipatory mindset from brokers.
