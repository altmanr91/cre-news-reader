Build-to-Rent Investors Catch a Break in U.S. House Bill

Commercial Observer — Wed, 20 May 2026 18:44:05 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The U.S. House of Representatives has approved a revised bipartisan housing affordability bill, the 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act, which removes a contentious provision from the Senate version that would have mandated institutional investors sell build-to-rent (BTR) single-family homes to individual buyers after seven years. Commercial real estate industry groups, including CREFC and the MBA, advocated against the divestment clause, arguing it was impractical and would deter capital investment in single-family rental developments, thereby hindering housing supply. The House's more deliberate approach, which clarifies misconceptions about institutional homeownership, is seen as an improvement by industry stakeholders, who emphasize the need to avoid disrupting the broader rental housing market.
