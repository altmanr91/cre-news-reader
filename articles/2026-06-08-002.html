CMBS Distress Rises in 17 of the 25 Largest U.S. Markets

Commercial Observer — Mon, 08 Jun 2026 11:30:24 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / CMBS Distress Report

A May 2026 analysis of commercial mortgage-backed securities (CMBS) distress by CRED iQ reveals a national distress rate of 12.7 percent across the 25 largest U.S. metropolitan statistical areas, an increase from 12.2 percent in June 2025. Seventeen of these markets experienced year-over-year increases, with Minneapolis, Denver, and Rochester, N.Y., showing the highest distress rates. The most significant escalations were observed in St. Louis, Oklahoma City, and Pittsburgh, largely due to concentrated office and mixed-use loan exposure. Conversely, Providence, Charlotte, and Austin saw improvements in their distress rates, reflecting successful loan resolutions and stronger market dynamics in some Sun Belt areas.
