Miami-Dade schools revives HQ land swap with Crescent Heights

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Miami — Fri, 26 Jun 2026 18:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Miami-Dade County Public Schools is proceeding with a land swap agreement with developer Crescent Heights to relocate its headquarters to a new mixed-use tower in Miami's Omni neighborhood. The school district will transfer its parking lot, valued at $27.5 million, to Crescent Heights in exchange for new office space, an auditorium, and 600 parking spaces within the developer's 43-story project. This deal, which involves no monetary exchange, will provide the district with new administrative facilities, expected to be worth more than double the value of the land contributed.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: mixed-use

Notable Features: 43-story mixed-use development with dedicated office space, auditorium, and parking for the school district.

Project Notes: The school district's parking lot (Parcel 7) was valued at $27.5 million last year. The value of the new administrative space is expected to be more than double that. No money will be exchanged in the deal. The district's current headquarters is at 1450 Northeast Second Avenue, Miami, FL.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Crescent Heights

OWNER: Miami-Dade County Public Schools
