The real estate industry picks its candidate in CA-11

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Sun, 03 May 2026 16:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Political & Campaign Finance

This article examines the significant financial contributions from the real estate industry to candidates in San Francisco's competitive CA-11 congressional race. State Sen. Scott Wiener has garnered over $123,000 from real estate professionals, representing nearly 5% of his total individual contributions, reflecting the industry's support for his pro-housing stance. In contrast, Supervisor Connie Chan, who has opposed state housing mandates, received $4,000 from real estate donors, while tech centimillionaire Saikat Chakrabarti, largely self-financing his campaign, received approximately $3,200 from the industry. The article also notes the resurgence of San Francisco's office market, driven by the artificial intelligence sector, with office demand surpassing pre-pandemic levels.

DATA POINTS

Scott Wiener real estate contributions: $123,000

Scott Wiener total individual contributions: $2.7 million

Connie Chan real estate contributions: $4,000

Saikat Chakrabarti real estate contributions: $3,200

Saikat Chakrabarti total individual contributions: $360,000

Saikat Chakrabarti total campaign spending: $5 million

New AI companies touring offices in San Francisco (Jan-Mar): 67

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Office demand in San Francisco surpassed its 2018-2019 average through March, making it the only key U.S. market tracked by VTS to do so. New York City's office demand exceeded pre-pandemic levels at various points but fell short in Q1 this year. Other cities like Boston, Chicago, Washington, D.C., Seattle, and Los Angeles have not seen full recovery in demand. The AI industry is a primary driver, with 67 new AI companies touring offices in San Francisco between January and March.
