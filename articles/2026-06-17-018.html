The Daily Dirt: FARE Act returns just $7 per complaint to consumers

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 11:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article critiques the effectiveness of the FARE Act in its first year, highlighting a significant disparity between the number of complaints filed and the actual financial recoveries for renters. Despite over 2,000 complaints about illegal broker fees, only about $7 per complaint was returned to consumers, totaling $15,500. The author argues that this minimal recovery, representing only 1 in 100 complaints, indicates the act is not effectively addressing the issue, possibly because many complaints did not involve actual payments or were directed at brokers rather than landlords. The piece also touches on the Community Opportunity to Purchase Act and the challenges of developing a parking lot in the East Village due to restrictive R8B zoning, which severely limits potential housing units and revenue for struggling co-ops and NYCHA.
