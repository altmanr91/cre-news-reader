The Daily Dirt: Electrical fires surge after rent law

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 05 Jun 2026 11:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article argues that the 2019 Housing Stability and Tenant Protection Act in New York City has inadvertently led to a surge in electrical fires in older Bronx apartment buildings. The author contends that the rent law's cap on Major Capital Improvement (MCI) rent increases, set at 2 percent, makes it financially unfeasible for landlords to undertake necessary electrical system upgrades, which can cost upwards of $1.5 million for a 75-unit building. Even with phased-in rent hikes, the revenue generated would not cover the monthly payments for a typical 15-year loan, leaving distressed buildings without access to capital as banks are unwilling to lend. The piece also highlights concerns from a property insurance broker about how open violations, which tenants are encouraged to report, can negatively impact property insurance premiums, potentially leading to higher costs or policy cancellations for landlords.
