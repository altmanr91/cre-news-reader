Multiple Factors Drive MOB Growth

Connect CRE — Fri, 26 Jun 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Healthcare Report

A recent Cushman & Wakefield report, "Vital Signs," highlights the continued strong momentum in the U.S. medical office building (MOB) sector, driven by robust demand and constrained supply. The report indicates that investors are focusing on the sector's positive fundamentals, leading to sustained high occupancy and upward pressure on rents. Key factors contributing to demand include a 5.8% increase in healthcare spending over the past decade and significant growth in specialties like endocrinology and psychiatry. Supply remains tight due to decreased construction, aging inventory, and competition for viable development sites, with Q1 absorption up 71% year-over-year and occupancy at 92.5%. While the expiration of ACA subsidies may impact some patient volumes, the rapidly aging U.S. population is expected to drive substantial future demand for healthcare services, largely covered by Medicare, ensuring the sector's resilience and attractive investment profile.
