Universal Music plans first US branded condo project at contentious Hill Country site

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Texas — Thu, 18 Jun 2026 19:49:48 +0000

SUMMARY

Universal Music Group is partnering with Austin-based White Rocks Entertainment to develop UMusic Hotel & Private Residences Austin, a branded condo and hotel project in the Hill Country near Bee Cave. The development will feature 600 condo units, 150 hotel rooms, and a 10,000-seat outdoor venue, with construction anticipated to begin in early 2027 and conclude in 2030, pending regulatory approvals. This marks Universal Music's first hospitality project in the United States, with condo pricing expected to start at $2.3 million.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: condo

Units: 600

Site Area: 71 acres

Notable Features: Proposed amenities include professional recording studios, a nightclub, comedy club, indoor performance venue, and two restaurants. The project is designed to meet a 100 percent water reuse standard, with over 70 percent of the site dedicated to open space.

Project Notes: The project is currently awaiting approval from the Texas Commission on Environmental Quality for its wastewater permit. A similar project, Violet Crown, was previously proposed for the site in 2021 but was withdrawn due to local opposition over environmental concerns.
