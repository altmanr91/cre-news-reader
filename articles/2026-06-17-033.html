SF office leasing activity rides AI wave to near 30-year high

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Tue, 16 Jun 2026 23:37:26 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Office Leasing

San Francisco's office market is experiencing a significant resurgence, with leasing activity nearing a 30-year high, primarily fueled by the rapid expansion of artificial intelligence firms. The first half of the year saw approximately 6.4 million square feet leased, with a projected annual volume of 15.3 million square feet, a pace comparable to 1996. This surge is directly linked to a substantial increase in venture capital funding for AI startups, which has more than doubled compared to last year, driving strong tenant demand. The demand has also shifted tenant preferences towards fully built-out, immediately occupiable spaces, leading to a significant reduction in available sublease inventory.

DATA POINTS

Office leasing volume (H1 this year): 6.4 million square feet

Office leasing volume (April-May this year): 2.1 million square feet

Projected annual office leasing volume: 15.3 million square feet

Venture funding in San Francisco (first five months): $252 billion

Venture funding in San Francisco (all of last year): $117 billion

Tenant requirements: 9.7 million square feet

Tech firms' share of demand: 50%

AI firms' share of demand: 25%

Available sublease space (mid-2023): nearly 10 million square feet

Available sublease space (today): 3.4 million square feet

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Venture capital funding has historically been a strong driver of tenant demand in San Francisco's tech sector. The current AI boom is also impacting the local housing market, leading to rising rents and home prices, tight supply, and rapid trading of high-end residences.
