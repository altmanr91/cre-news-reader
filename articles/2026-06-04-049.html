U.S. Retail Supply Is Tightening, But Few Developers Plan To Build New Product

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 03 Jun 2026 16:16:38 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The U.S. retail sector experienced negative absorption in Q1 2026, leading to high occupancy rates across major landlord portfolios. Despite this tightening supply, new retail development is largely stalled due to tariffs, elevated interest rates, and the specific pre-leasing requirements of retail tenants, making the financial risk unfeasible for most developers. Texas is an exception, with strong population growth driving significant grocery-anchored mixed-use developments and active expansion by major anchor retailers. Nationally, landlords are even buying out leases from bankrupt retailers to control spaces and secure higher rents from new, better-quality tenants.

DATA POINTS

Q1 2026 U.S. retail absorption: -4.4M SF

Weitzman retail space leased in Texas: 44M SF

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The U.S. retail sector experienced negative absorption of 4.4M SF in Q1 2026 after two quarters of increasing occupied space. Dallas-Fort Worth's grocery-anchored retail sector achieved a fourth consecutive year of record occupancy in January 2026.
