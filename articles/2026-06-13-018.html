Charlotte giving red light to data center projects for 150 days

The Real Deal — National — Fri, 12 Jun 2026 16:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The Charlotte City Council has enacted a 150-day moratorium on new data center developments to study their environmental impact and streamline policy. This pause allows city officials to assess concerns such as noise pollution, water supply effects, and increased demand on local power grids, which have become prominent due to the rapid proliferation of data centers. The moratorium will not affect projects that have already received valid building permits or completed development applications, but it is expected to impact proposed projects like American Tower Corporation's 40,000-square-foot data center and the first phase of PowerHouse Charlotte.
