'An Avalanche Of Good': Scottish World Cup Crowds Have Boosted Boston Businesses

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 18:28:57 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The influx of an estimated 50,000 Scottish World Cup fans has provided a significant economic boost to Boston's downtown businesses, including hotels, bars, restaurants, and retail. Despite initial concerns about international tourism and lower-than-forecast hotel bookings in host cities, Boston experienced strong demand, with some establishments tripling sales and hotels seeing increased average rates. This surge in activity, compared to the Celtics' championship parade, highlights the importance of destination-worthy events in revitalizing the city's downtown, which has faced challenges with affordability and nightlife perceptions. Business leaders hope the World Cup's momentum will continue to attract tourists and further stimulate the local economy.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Boston hotel occupancy on Friday and Saturday averaged 79% and 82% respectively, below the occupancy on the same days last year, but average rates were up 28% and 29% those days, and revenue per available room (RevPAR) was up 23% and 24%. Boston ranked No. 2 among the 11 U.S. World Cup host cities for growth in the number of people hosting on Airbnb, with Scottish travelers accounting for nearly a quarter of bookings around their game against Haiti.
