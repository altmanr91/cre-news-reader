Judge says Cook County on the hook for millions in tax sale takings

The Real Deal — Chicago — Tue, 12 May 2026 20:56:34 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

A federal judge has ruled that Cook County is liable for damages related to its delinquent property tax sale system, which allowed investors to seize homes for unpaid taxes and retain the surplus equity. This decision follows the U.S. Supreme Court's 2023 ruling in Tyler v. Hennepin County, which deemed such practices unconstitutional. The ruling could expose Cook County to millions in payouts to nearly 2,500 homeowners who lost properties since 2020, often for relatively small tax debts, while investors profited from the homes' significant equity.

DATA POINTS

Number of homeowners who lost properties since 2020: nearly 2,500

Total initial tax debts for lost homes: $2.3 million

Combined market value of lost homes: exceeding $108 million

Average loss per homeowner: $70,000

Estimated annual liability for Cook County: $15.4 million
