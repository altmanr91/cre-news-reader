Austin’s builders can now build taller. Will they?

The Real Deal — Texas — Fri, 29 May 2026 20:50:14 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

Austin City Council has approved changes to its density bonus program, allowing downtown buildings to exceed current height limits by 400 or 850 feet. This initiative aims to encourage more development within the city center, particularly south of 11th Street, by offering increased density in exchange for affordable housing contributions or fees. However, some local developers express skepticism about the program's immediate impact, citing similar past initiatives that yielded limited results, especially given the current challenges in the office market and the trend of residential growth in the suburbs. The city is also pursuing annexation of a large undeveloped parcel for mixed-use development, anticipating significant long-term tax revenue.

DATA POINTS

Maximum height increase allowed: 400 feet or 850 feet

Number of buildings over 750 feet in Downtown Austin: 2

Estimated tax revenue from "Dog's Head" annexation: Over $3.5 billion over decades
