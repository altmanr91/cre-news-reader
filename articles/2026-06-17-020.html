AG James sues landlords over rent-stabilized building violations

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 00:04:07 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

New York Attorney General Letitia James has filed lawsuits against two Brooklyn landlords, John Anderson and Claudette Henry, alleging violations of rent stabilization laws. The lawsuits claim the landlords failed to properly register regulated units, attempted illegal evictions, and harassed tenants. These actions are part of a broader compliance program targeting buildings with five or fewer units built before 1974 that were later altered to have six or more units, making them de facto rent-stabilized.
