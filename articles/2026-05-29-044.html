Data Center Land Deals Surged 141% In Q1, Warping The Market For Everyone Else

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 28 May 2026 16:10:14 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / Land Sales

Sales of land designated for data center development surged by 141% in Q1 2026 compared to the previous year, reaching approximately $3.3 billion nationwide. This dramatic increase, which accounted for 30% of all development site spending, is driven by the dwindling availability of land with immediate access to power and favorable zoning. The scarcity of powered land has significantly inflated prices, making it challenging for other asset classes like industrial to compete, particularly in markets like Northern Virginia where industrial development has shifted due to data center land pricing. Experts anticipate this trend will continue as demand for computing power grows, but the sector faces a critical challenge in securing sufficient electricity capacity from energy providers.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Data center land deals accounted for 30% of all development site spending in Q1 2026, up from 19% in 2025. The number of data center sites sold decreased from 20 to 16, indicating a rise in value per site. Overall development site sales volume increased 55% year-over-year to $11.1 billion in Q1. In Q4 2025, developers added only 25 gigawatts of electricity capacity to their pipeline, half of the previous quarter. U.S. data center power demand is projected to increase from 31 GW in 2025 to 66 GW in 2027. Industrial development sales volume increased 65% year-over-year to $2.3 billion, with the number of sites sold rising only 19%. Multifamily development sales volume increased from $2 billion to $2.6 billion, and mixed-use site sales climbed from $1.2 billion to $2.4 billion. Private buyers accounted for 70% of Q1 development transactions.
