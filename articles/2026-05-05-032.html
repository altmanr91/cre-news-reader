Texas CRE foreclosures surge past $1B for May auctions, fueled by multifamily distress

FORECLOSURE

The Real Deal — Texas — Mon, 04 May 2026 14:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Commercial real estate foreclosures in Texas surged past $1 billion in May, marking the first time this threshold has been crossed since May 2025, primarily driven by distress in the multifamily sector. The analysis, covering major metros like Houston, San Antonio, Dallas, Fort Worth, and Austin, indicates 43 commercial properties are slated for auction, with apartment complexes forming the majority. Notable properties facing auction include an $80 million office building in Houston, a 248-unit apartment complex in Austin with a $42 million mortgage, and a 352-unit apartment building in San Antonio with a $45 million loan. Additionally, a four-property multifamily portfolio totaling 1,530 units across Dallas and Tarrant counties is facing foreclosure on a $177 million loan.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Commercial real estate distress in Texas reached over $1 billion in May 2026, the highest since May 2025, with multifamily properties being the primary driver. The analysis covers seven counties including Houston, San Antonio, Dallas, Fort Worth, and Austin. A total of 43 commercial properties are scheduled for auction, predominantly apartment complexes. The office building at 3000 Post Oak Boulevard in Houston is facing auction due to the exit of its anchor tenant, Bechtel, which occupied 99% of the building and left in 2022. The $80 million CMBS loan on this property matured in March 2025. The Park at Crestview Apartments in Austin, a 248-unit property, has a $42 million mortgage from 2023, equating to $169,000 per unit. The Summit at Salado Creek Apartments in San Antonio, a 352-unit property, has a $45 million loan, equating to $128,000 per unit. A four-property multifamily portfolio in Dallas and Tarrant counties, totaling 1,530 units, is facing foreclosure on a $177 million loan. Twelve properties scheduled for auction have been flagged multiple times, indicating ongoing distress or litigation.
