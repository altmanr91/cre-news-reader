City leaders consider bolstering affordable housing fund to bring back developers

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Thu, 21 May 2026 23:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

San Francisco leaders are proposing a two-pronged strategy to reactivate the city's stalled housing development pipeline. One initiative involves a charter amendment to significantly increase the annual contribution to the city's Housing Trust Fund from $52 million to $125 million, extending its duration and linking future increases to property tax growth. Concurrently, the city is considering reducing inclusionary housing requirements for market-rate developers, lowering the affordable set-aside from 15% to 5% for projects with 25 or more units, or offering alternative compliance options. These measures aim to inject more public funding into affordable housing while reducing financial burdens on private developers to stimulate new construction.

DATA POINTS

Current Housing Trust Fund annual contribution: $52 million

Proposed Housing Trust Fund annual contribution: $125 million

Proposed reduction in inclusionary housing requirement: From 15% to 5% for projects with at least 25 units

Proposed revenue bond for affordable housing preservation: $70 million
