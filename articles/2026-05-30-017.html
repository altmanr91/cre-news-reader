Housing Notes: Affordability normalizes in only 7-10 years!

The Real Deal — National — Fri, 29 May 2026 14:00:17 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Jonathan Miller, a prominent residential real estate analyst, has partnered with The Real Deal to publish his "Housing Notes" column and quarterly New York City market report. Miller argues that the housing affordability crisis in the U.S. will take seven to ten years to normalize, citing ongoing inflationary policies, geopolitical conflicts, and reduced labor supply. He criticizes the National Association of Realtors' Housing Affordability Index (HAI) for its flawed methodology, which he believes misrepresents true affordability by ignoring crucial costs and assuming unrealistic down payments. Miller contrasts NAR's approach with Oxford Economics' more comprehensive index, which includes property taxes, insurance, and HOA fees, providing a more realistic assessment of the market.

DATA POINTS

Long Island bidding-war market share (excluding East End): 56.9%

Modestly priced Long Island suburbs bidding-war market share: >80%

NAR Housing Affordability Index (HAI) since mid-2022: Straddling 100 (baseline of affordability)

Income required for home purchase (Oxford Economics, 2024 vs. 2019): Twice the income required in 2019
