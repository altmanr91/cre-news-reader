Foreclosures jump as FHA safety net shrinks

The Real Deal — National — Mon, 04 May 2026 21:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Housing Distress

Foreclosure filings increased by 28% year-over-year in March, driven by stricter rules for the Federal Housing Administration's (FHA) "partial claim" program. This program, which allowed delinquent borrowers to defer missed payments, now has tighter restrictions, requiring homeowners to make three consecutive on-time payments and limiting access to once every two years. Analysts predict that up to half of seriously delinquent FHA borrowers may not meet these new requirements, potentially leading to approximately 250,000 distressed sales over the next 12 to 18 months. The impact on home prices will vary by market, with stronger markets potentially absorbing these sales due to existing equity, while weaker markets may see price reductions.

DATA POINTS

Foreclosure filings increase (March YoY): 28%

FHA loans as % of outstanding U.S. mortgages: 10%

Delinquency rate for FHA borrowers (March): 11.6%
