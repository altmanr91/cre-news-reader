How Living-as-a-Service Could Expand Multifamily Bottom Lines

Connect CRE — Fri, 05 Jun 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

A recent Deloitte report highlights "Living-as-a-Service" (LaaS) as an emerging strategy for multifamily owners and operators to attract and retain renters. This model shifts beyond traditional leasing to a subscription-driven, service-oriented platform offering convenience and lifestyle flexibility, including options like furniture packages, flexible leases, and concierge services. The report suggests LaaS could generate additional revenue streams and boost retention for owners, while appealing to renters seeking flexibility and convenience over homeownership commitments. Demographic and economic trends, such as a projected 21.7% increase in U.S. renter households by 2035 and longer renter tenures, are driving interest in this model.

DATA POINTS

Projected U.S. renter households by 2035: 56.3 million

Increase in U.S. renter households by 2035: 21.7%

Current U.S. renter households: 46.2 million

Lease renewals as percentage of leasing activity: 57%

Increase in renters age 65 and older (2013-2023): 30%

Increase in high-net-worth renter households (2019-2023): tripled
