PolicyPro: Council bill would shield “harassed” properties from 311 complaints, progressives push commercial rent control

The Real Deal — New York — Mon, 15 Jun 2026 23:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

A new City Council bill in New York City aims to protect "harassed" properties from repeated, unsubstantiated 311 complaints by requiring agencies to stop inspections after three anonymous complaints within six months that do not result in verified violations. This measure seeks to prevent the misuse of the 311 system, which can negatively impact property owners by raising red flags for lenders, insurers, and potential buyers, even when no violations are found. Separately, progressive City Council members are advocating for the state Legislature to approve commercial rent stabilization for New York City, proposing a system with a Commercial Rent Guidelines Board and minimum 10-year lease renewals to support small businesses facing rising rents.
