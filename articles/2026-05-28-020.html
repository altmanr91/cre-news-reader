NYC’s top deals: Tribeca parking garage sells — finally — for $59M

SALE

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 28 May 2026 11:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Alchemy Properties has acquired a former parking garage at 56 North Moore Street in Tribeca for $58.5 million from the Calicchio family, who had owned the property for decades. The property was previously marketed as a potential office conversion opportunity. Additionally, an LLC tied to Rafael Rabinowitz purchased a five-story, 29,000-square-foot office building at 267 Broadway in Tribeca for $30 million from a company linked to the Roe Corporation. In the Flatiron District, a 10-story multifamily property with eight apartments at 16 East 18th Street, spanning nearly 20,300 square feet, was sold for $16.3 million by an LLC tied to Revere Capital to an LLC linked to Eun Rae Jo.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Ground-up construction in NYC is becoming harder to finance, leading builders to focus on redevelopment projects. Manhattan is seeing large office-to-residential conversions, while Brooklyn's redevelopment involves smaller expansions of residential homes in low-rise neighborhoods.
