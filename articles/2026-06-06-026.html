Bears punt on Illinois, announce move to Indiana

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Chicago — Fri, 05 Jun 2026 18:43:32 +0000

SUMMARY

The Chicago Bears football team has announced its intention to develop a new stadium and entertainment district in Hammond, Indiana, following a prolonged search for a new home. This decision comes after the team's board of directors voted to advance the project in Indiana, citing a failed legislative attempt in Illinois to secure a public-private ownership deal for a stadium in the Chicago suburbs. The proposed Hammond development, which is not yet a finalized deal, is expected to be taxpayer-financed and will include an entertainment area to enhance foot traffic.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: stadium

Notable Features: Proposed development near Wolf Lake and Gary, approximately 20 miles southeast of Chicago, to include an entertainment area.

Project Notes: The Bears previously acquired the former Arlington International Racecourse property in Illinois for $200 million in 2023, but faced hurdles with property tax relief.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Chicago Bears — George McCaskey, Chairman; Kevin Warren, CEO

FINANCING

Taxpayer-financed stadium
