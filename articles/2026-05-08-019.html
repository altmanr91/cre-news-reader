Board paves way for rent freeze as advocates push for money back

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 08 May 2026 00:31:38 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

New York City's Rent Guidelines Board has approved a preliminary rent increase range of 0-2% for one-year leases and 0-4% for two-year leases on regulated apartments, effective October. This decision, which includes the possibility of a rent freeze, comes amidst strong advocacy from tenant groups for no increases and concerns from landlord associations about rising operating costs and potential declines in building conditions. Mayor Zohran Mamdani, who campaigned on a rent freeze platform, expressed encouragement that the board is considering affordability and operating expenses, while real estate groups have criticized the decision as irresponsible and potentially leading to legal challenges.
