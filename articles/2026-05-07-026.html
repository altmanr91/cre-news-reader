Condo project replacing classic Fort Lauderdale hotel moves ahead

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Miami — Wed, 06 May 2026 22:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

A 54-unit condominium project, Opus at 701, received site-plan approval from the Fort Lauderdale City Commission. The 13-story development will replace the former Manhattan Tower hotel and an apartment building on a 0.78-acre site along the Intracoastal Waterway. The developer acquired the site last year for nearly $20 million. The project will feature a fitness center, spa, and a rooftop infinity-edge pool, with a sculpture of the former hotel planned for the lobby entrance to commemorate its architectural identity.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: condo

Property Name: Opus at 701

Address: 632 and 701 Bayshore Drive, Fort Lauderdale, FL

Units: 54

Site Area: 0.78 acres

Notable Features: Boutique condo development with a fitness center, spa, pool deck overlooking the Intracoastal Waterway, and an elevated roof deck with an infinity-edge pool and clubhouse lounge.

Project Notes: The 0.78-acre site was assembled last year for nearly $20 million, including the $11 million purchase of the 1955-built Manhattan Tower hotel and an $8.5 million purchase of a 56-year-old apartment building. A sculpture of the Manhattan Tower will be installed at the lobby entrance to commemorate the property's architectural identity.
