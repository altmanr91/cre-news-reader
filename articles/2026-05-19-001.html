New York City’s Higher-End Retail Deserts, and How to Fill Them

Commercial Observer — Tue, 19 May 2026 10:30:19 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article explores the phenomenon of "retail deserts" in affluent New York City neighborhoods like Windsor Terrace and Kensington, where residents have significant disposable income but a dearth of desirable retail options. The author argues that proximity to established, upscale retail corridors in adjacent neighborhoods, coupled with retailers' preference for co-tenancy with similar brands, creates a self-perpetuating cycle of limited retail development. Zoning requirements, or the lack thereof, for ground-floor retail in residential buildings also play a crucial role in shaping the retail landscape, as evidenced by the contrast between older residential areas and newly rezoned districts like Gowanus.
