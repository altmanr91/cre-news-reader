Google’s listings experiment adds pressure to fractured MLS landscape

The Real Deal — National — Wed, 20 May 2026 20:15:17 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Google is re-testing a program to display home listings directly in its search results, partnering with AI-enabled brokerage HouseCanary. This initiative, which involves direct agreements with Multiple Listing Services (MLSes) like California Regional MLS, San Diego MLS, and My State MLS, as well as brokerages such as eXp Realty, signals a significant shift in listing syndication. The move is seen as a direct challenge to established real estate portals like Zillow, Realtor.com, and Homes.com, which have historically relied on MLS data feeds. Industry experts suggest that Google's immense consumer reach could disrupt the current portal models, especially as the MLS landscape becomes more fractured and brokerages increasingly withhold listings from immediate MLS syndication.
