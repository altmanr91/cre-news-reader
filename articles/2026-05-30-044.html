Startup Looks To Build £200M UK Portfolio In A New Asset Class: American Car Washes

Bisnow News Feed — Fri, 29 May 2026 12:39:32 -0400

SUMMARY

Development

Rays Wash Club, a new startup backed by investors including Formula 1 driver George Russell, is aiming to develop a £200M portfolio of automated car washes across the UK, replicating a successful subscription-based model from the U.S. The company is actively seeking to acquire freehold or leasehold sites, with plans to roll out up to 60 locations over the next five years. Each site, ideally one acre, will feature a 50-130 foot automated wash tunnel capable of servicing 120-150 cars per hour, employing 5-10 staff, and operating on a subscription model for customers.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The U.S. automated car wash industry is a $15B market, attracting private equity firms like KKR and Oaktree Capital Management. Henley Investment Management created a U.S. car wash platform in 2023, aiming to deploy $500M into that market. U.S. car wash memberships typically cost $30-$50 per month.
