Hotel union deal puts NYC owners on notice

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 23 May 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

New York City's hotel industry has reached a tentative eight-year labor agreement with the Hotel and Gaming Trades Council, covering nearly 30,000 workers across more than 250 properties. The deal, which is expected to be ratified, includes significant wage increases, with housekeepers projected to earn over $100,000 annually by the sixth year. While the agreement averts a strike ahead of the 2026 FIFA World Cup, hotel owners are concerned about the substantial increase in payroll obligations, which they believe will impact their bottom line amidst rising inflation, tariffs, and borrowing costs. Operators are expected to attempt to raise room rates to offset these increased expenses.

DATA POINTS

Wage increase over contract term: more than 50 percent on average

Housekeeper hourly wage increase by 2034: from roughly $40 to more than $61

Non-tipped hotel worker additional hourly pay over contract term: $21.20

Manhattan hotel occupancy: mid-80 percent range

Manhattan average hotel rate in 2025: $334 per night

Number of unionized hotels in NYC: roughly 200 of 785 properties
