Talking Local Law 97 with DOB head Ahmed Tigani

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 08 May 2026 21:12:07 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

New York City's Department of Buildings (DOB) Commissioner Ahmed Tigani discussed the implementation and future of Local Law 97, which mandates greenhouse gas emission limits for most buildings over 25,000 square feet. The first phase of compliance, which took effect in 2024 with reports due at the end of last year, has seen a high compliance rate of over 91%. The DOB is currently analyzing this initial data to inform future enforcement and policy adjustments, emphasizing compliance over penalties. The city is exploring financing tools like J-51 renewals and an offset program to support building owners in meeting upcoming emission targets, particularly for affordable housing projects.

DATA POINTS

Local Law 97 fine for non-compliance: $268 for every ton of carbon above a building's limit

Local Law 97 compliance rate (Phase 1): Over 91%
