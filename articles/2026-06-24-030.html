Chicago sues Airbnb and host who allegedly violated short-term rental ordinance hundreds of times

The Real Deal — Chicago — Tue, 23 Jun 2026 17:37:36 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

The city of Chicago has filed a lawsuit against Airbnb and a prominent host, Milan Rubenstein, alleging widespread violations of its short-term rental ordinance. The city claims Rubenstein illegally operated over 200 unregistered units between 2024 and 2025, using a single hotel license for properties across the city instead of individual registrations. The lawsuit also accuses Airbnb of prioritizing profit over compliance by failing to remove non-compliant listings, providing incomplete data, and refusing to utilize the city's data platform to verify registrations. Chicago is seeking the surrender of profits from the illegally rented units and daily fines for both parties, aiming to establish a precedent for better enforcement of its 2016 ordinance designed to regulate the short-term rental market and address housing and safety concerns.
