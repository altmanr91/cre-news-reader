PolicyPro: Development deals hit Council roadblock, new charter commission eyes housing rules

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 28 May 2026 23:11:54 +0000

SUMMARY

Development

The Monitor Point apartment complex, a proposed 1,150-unit development on the Greenpoint waterfront, faces a political stalemate in the City Council. Council member Lincoln Restler has rejected the project without a commitment to more income-restricted housing and the completion of Bushwick Inlet Park. The Gotham Organization, the developer, plans three residential towers with 40% of units (460 apartments) designated as permanently income-restricted for households earning 60% of the area median income, while the remaining 690 units would be market-rate. The developer has invested approximately $130 million in development rights, MTA facilities, and infrastructure, arguing that further affordability requirements are financially unfeasible. The project is now a candidate to test the city's new housing appeals board, highlighting the ongoing YIMBY-versus-NIMBY debate in the city.
