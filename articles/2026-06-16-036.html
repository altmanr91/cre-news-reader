Sunnyvale offices once home to Google poised for housing conversion

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Mon, 15 Jun 2026 21:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Dallas-based real estate firm Beam Reach has proposed demolishing a former Google office building at 215 Bordeaux Drive in Sunnyvale, CA, to construct a 265-unit residential building. The Sunnyvale planning commissioners have unanimously approved the proposal. This project is part of a broader trend in the Bay Area to convert underutilized office space into housing, driven by high housing demand and the city's mandate to plan for new housing units.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Address: 215 Bordeaux Drive, Sunnyvale, CA

Units: 265

Site Area: 2 acres

Notable Features: Eight-story multifamily building replacing a one-story research and development building.

Project Notes: The site was previously a Google office, listed for sublease three years prior as Google trimmed its office footprint.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Beam Reach

OWNER: Google

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Sunnyvale's office vacancy rate is 10.9%, compared to 15.4% across Silicon Valley. San Mateo County is seeing increased demolition of underutilized offices for housing. Sunnyvale is required to plan for 11,966 new housing units by 2031, with Moffett Park identified as a key area for conversion into mixed-use neighborhoods.
