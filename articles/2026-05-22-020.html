The Daily Dirt: The life and death of a landlord

SALE

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 22 May 2026 11:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

This article reports on the sale of a 67-unit rent-stabilized building in the Bronx, which traded for $3.965 million, or $59,000 per unit. The property was part of a 20-building portfolio being sold by the estate of the late Howard Alkoff, who had owned the building for 42 years. The sale highlights the challenges faced by owners of rent-stabilized properties in New York City following the 2019 Housing Stability and Tenant Protection Act, which has impacted revenue and expenses, leading to low valuations despite the building being well-maintained.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Address: 1181 Sheridan Avenue, New York City, NY

Units: 67

Sale Price: $4.0M

Year Built: 1936

Notable Features: Well-maintained building with steel-and-glass entrance doors, tiled entryway, massive lobby, steel and marble staircases, clean common areas, dual elevators, and a silver-coated roof in good condition.

Project Notes: The property was part of a 20-building portfolio owned by Howard Alkoff's H&H Equities, which had owned the building for 42 years. The portfolio had a low number of HPD violations compared to distressed buildings. The property's tax bills increased significantly from 2021 to 2024, and H&H Equities fell behind on payments, owing $314,000 in August 2024 before reaching a payment plan.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

SELLER: H&H Equities

BUYER: Walter Wilfinger

SELLER BROKER: Lala Realty Group — Marco Lala

SELLER BROKER: RM Friedland

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Housing Stability and Tenant Protection Act of 2019 has negatively impacted the financial picture for rent-stabilized buildings in New York City, leading to low revenue and rising expenses. The sale price for 1181 Sheridan Avenue was 3.8 times the rent roll, yielding a cap rate of 10.4 percent.
