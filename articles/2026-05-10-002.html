A solution for vacant units and Mamdani’s trust problem

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 09 May 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Editorial

This article offers an opinion on how the Mamdani administration can build trust with the real estate industry and address the issue of vacant housing units in New York City. The author suggests that despite initial friction, the administration is attempting to engage with industry leaders to achieve its goals of reducing rent growth and improving tenant conditions. A key proposal involves the city issuing an RFP for contractors to renovate vacant rent-stabilized units at scale, charging owners a fixed fee, and guaranteeing legal occupancy. The author argues this approach would be more efficient and cost-effective than new construction, help bring thousands of units back online, create jobs, and foster trust between the city and property owners.
