To build or to buy? After uproar over price, Fort Lauderdale city hall proposal back for a vote

The Real Deal — Miami — Fri, 26 Jun 2026 23:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Government Use

Fort Lauderdale city commissioners are set to vote on a renegotiated proposal for a new city hall, reducing the development cost by over $50 million to $217.1 million. The original $268 million proposal, which would have totaled $725 million over 30 years, faced public backlash due to its high price and unfavorable loan terms. The revised plan eliminates developer equity and fees, with annual payments of $15.8 million over 30 years, totaling $474 million. Concurrently, the city is also evaluating the potential acquisition and renovation of three existing buildings as an alternative to new construction, with costs ranging from $227.4 million to $289 million.

DATA POINTS
