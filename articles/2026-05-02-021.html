Midtown South dominates April leasing activity

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 01 May 2026 18:28:50 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Leasing Report

Midtown South dominated Manhattan's office leasing activity in April, securing four of the five largest leases and accounting for nearly 45% of all demand. The submarket's leasing volume reached 1.6 million square feet, a 65% increase over the 10-year monthly average. Overall Manhattan office leasing totaled 3.6 million square feet, a 38% decrease from March but still 30% above the 10-year average. The borough's availability rate slightly decreased to 13.4%, with total available space at its lowest since October 2020, and sublet inventory also significantly declined.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Midtown South's April leasing volume of 1.6 million square feet was 65% higher than its 10-year monthly average. Manhattan's overall April leasing volume, at 3.6 million square feet, was 30% above the 10-year monthly average and nearly 7% higher than a year ago. The borough's total available space of 69.9 million square feet is the lowest since October 2020 and nearly 29% below the post-pandemic peak. Sublet inventory of 10.5 million square feet is down nearly 30% year-over-year and at its lowest level since 2019.
