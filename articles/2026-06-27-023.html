Habitat for Humanity shifts focus to outer boroughs

CONSTRUCTION

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 26 Jun 2026 14:28:13 +0000

SUMMARY

Habitat for Humanity is significantly expanding its affordable housing initiatives in New York City's outer boroughs, constructing 179 co-op units across two major developments in Brooklyn and the Bronx. The Constellation project in Brooklyn, spanning eight sites, has grown to 108 units, while Claremont House in the Bronx has expanded to 71 units. These projects are designed to address the housing needs of the "missing middle" and are supported by various city and state affordable housing programs, including the Open Door Program and the New Infill Housing Opportunity Program, and facilitated by the City of Yes for Housing Opportunity.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: co-op

Property Name: Constellation / Claremont House

Units: 179

Notable Features: Units are targeted for the "missing middle" income bracket, providing affordable homeownership opportunities.

Project Notes: The Community Land Trust owns the land, leased to Habitat on 99-year deals. Projects are made possible by the City of Yes for Housing Opportunity and utilize city and state programs including the Open Door Program and the New Infill Housing Opportunity Program. Recipients will benefit from tax abatements from the Housing Development Fund Corporation.
