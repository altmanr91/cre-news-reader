“We are in competition”: Eric Johnson decries City Hall gridlock after Mavs move

The Real Deal — Texas — Wed, 03 Jun 2026 20:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Dallas Mayor Eric Johnson has publicly criticized the city's gridlock and its losing battle against suburban areas for residents, businesses, and attractions, following the Dallas Mavericks' decision to relocate their arena to the northern edge of the city. Johnson advocates for the redevelopment or sale of the aging Dallas City Hall, a site previously considered by the Mavericks, citing its extensive repair needs and the city's need to compete for future growth. This move by the Mavericks, along with other major businesses and sports teams, highlights a broader trend of companies migrating from Dallas' urban core to its northern suburbs, raising concerns about the city's economic competitiveness.
