SoCal City Decisively Passes First-In-Nation Data Center Ban

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 03 Jun 2026 14:43:39 -0400

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

Voters in Monterey Park, California, have overwhelmingly approved Measure NDC, enacting the nation's first permanent citywide ban on data centers. The decision follows growing resident opposition to data center development, including a specific proposal by HMC Capital for a 250,000-square-foot facility, which was withdrawn in April. This ban reflects a broader national trend of communities expressing concerns over the environmental and quality-of-life impacts of data centers, leading to moratoriums and proposed bans in several other states.

DATA POINTS

Voter approval for ban: 86%

City population: 62,000 residents
