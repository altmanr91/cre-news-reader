Opinion / Market Commentary

This article outlines the stances of the four leading candidates in the Los Angeles mayoral primary election on key commercial real estate issues, including housing affordability, homelessness, and the controversial Measure ULA real estate transfer tax. Incumbent Mayor Karen Bass, along with challengers Nithya Raman, Spencer Pratt, and Adam Miller, each offer distinct approaches to streamlining housing development, addressing the city's homelessness crisis, and either modifying or enforcing Measure ULA. The candidates' positions highlight the ongoing debate over how best to balance development incentives with social welfare initiatives in Los Angeles.
