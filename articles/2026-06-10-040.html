Lurie, Mahmood backpedal plan to halve SF transfer tax due to city budget deficit

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Tue, 09 Jun 2026 19:04:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

San Francisco Mayor Daniel Lurie and Supervisor Bilal Mahmood have paused their plan to halve the city's transfer tax, known as the BUILD Act, due to the city's significant budget deficit. The proposed legislation aimed to stimulate housing development by reducing transfer taxes on properties over $10 million and $25 million, which were increased by Proposition I in 2020. However, the city's current financial crisis necessitates a focus on revenue generation, leading the officials to delay the tax cut until a revenue-neutral solution can be found, potentially through a ballot measure to tax currently exempt properties like foreclosures.

DATA POINTS

Current transfer tax rate for properties > $25M: 6%

Proposed transfer tax rate for properties > $25M: 3%

Estimated revenue from current tax rate over next few years: $400 million
