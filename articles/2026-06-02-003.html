New York City Housing Development Now Hinges on Certain Trends: Forum

Commercial Observer — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 21:10:48 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

A recent forum highlighted the contrasting development environments in New York City and Florida, with panelists debating the pros and cons of each. While Florida is often lauded for its lower taxes and pro-business stance, developers noted significant bureaucratic hurdles and lengthy permitting processes in South Florida compared to New York City. Panelists also discussed the potential impact of New York's proposed pied-à-terre tax, with some believing its effects would be minimal due to loopholes, but signaling a broader trend of buyers moving to tax-friendlier states. The forum also emphasized the importance of government engagement with the private sector to address housing needs and praised New York's 467m tax incentive for residential conversions as a smart policy that reduces execution risk compared to ground-up development.
