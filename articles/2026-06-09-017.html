Canada, Mexico lead US in World Cup hotel bookings

The Real Deal — National — Mon, 08 Jun 2026 21:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Hospitality Report

Hotel bookings in Canada and Mexico for the upcoming World Cup are significantly outpacing those in most U.S. host cities, according to CoStar Group data. This trend is attributed to several factors, including the greater affordability of Canadian and Mexican cities for travelers, encompassing both hotel rates and ancillary costs like transportation. Additionally, the U.S. political climate and concerns over immigration policies may be deterring some foreign visitors. Despite the current booking patterns, there remains potential for last-minute bookings, as historically up to 40% of hotel reservations materialize within six days of a match.

DATA POINTS

Vancouver World Cup hotel occupancy: 48%

Guadalajara World Cup hotel occupancy: 48%

San Francisco World Cup hotel occupancy: 44%

Historical last-minute hotel bookings for matches: Up to 40% within 0-6 days before the match
