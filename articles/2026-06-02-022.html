Family firm buys mixed-use center near Boca Raton for $40M as CRE deals chill

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Miami — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 19:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Interface Properties, a family firm based in Boca Raton, acquired the 15-acre Fountains Center, a mixed-use retail and office complex near Boca Raton, for $40 million. The transaction was financed with a $31.8 million acquisition loan from City National Bank. The property, consisting of seven buildings totaling 189,500 square feet, was sold by an entity led by James Caprio of Titan General Partners and is 95 percent leased, with a significant portion occupied by medical office tenants. Interface Properties plans a multimillion-dollar renovation of the campus.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

South Florida commercial deal volume dropped 23 percent in Q1 compared to last year and 11 percent from the Q1 average since 2017, due to macroeconomic pressures and uncertainty over interest rates. Some asset classes, including multifamily, are experiencing downward pressure on values. Sellers are often reluctant to sell amid higher cap rates, but some are forced to due to looming debt maturities and higher interest rates.
