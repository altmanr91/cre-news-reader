City says it will halve affordable housing lease-up period

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 14 May 2026 21:34:48 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

New York City's Mamdani administration plans to reduce the average lease-up period for affordable housing projects from 210 days to 100 days. This initiative, part of the SPEED task force's first report, includes shortening lottery application periods, accelerating applicant selection, and piloting direct landlord-shelter collaborations. The reforms aim to balance fairness and transparency for applicants with the urgent need to house residents, addressing concerns raised by various nonprofit organizations regarding the city's lengthy lease-up process.

DATA POINTS

Current average affordable housing lease-up period: 210 days

Target affordable housing lease-up period: 100 days

Lottery application period reduction: From 60 days to 21 days

Number of items in SPEED task force report: 52

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Enterprise Community Partners found that it takes three times longer to fill an affordable housing project in New York City compared to the national median.
