NYC’s top construction permits: Week ending May 29, 2026

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 03 Jun 2026 12:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Construction Permits

The New York City Department of Housing Preservation and Development filed plans for the largest new building permit last week, a 13-story, 137-unit multifamily project spanning 128,102 square feet in Long Island City. Other significant filings included NFS Development Corp.'s plans for a 99-unit building in the Bronx and GP Restoration NYC Inc.'s proposal for a 49-unit property, also in the Bronx. Major alteration permits were filed for a significant residential expansion in Woodstock and for the Hotel Plaza Athénée, while Rabsky Group filed the largest demolition permit for a 29,000-square-foot building in Tribeca.

DATA POINTS

Largest New Building Permit: 128,102 SF, 137-unit building at 32-11 31st Street, Long Island City

New Building Permit: 56,508 SF, 99-unit building at 2805 Morris Avenue, Fordham

New Building Permit: 37,743 SF, 49-unit building at 958 Rogers Place, Longwood

Major Alteration Permit: 33,900 SF to add 88 units at 846 Brook Avenue, Woodstock

Major Alteration Permit: 16,865 SF at 37 East 64th Street, Lenox Hill

Major Alteration Permit: 11,028 SF at 167-01 Union Turnpike, Hillcrest

Largest Demolition Permit: 29,000 SF building at 267 Broadway, Tribeca

Demolition Permit: 10,490 SF across seven buildings on Osborn Street and Watkins Street, Brownsville

Demolition Permit: 9,400 SF building at 490 Eighth Avenue, Penn Plaza
