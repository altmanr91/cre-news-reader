SoftBank tied to detail-sparse land purchase in Milam County

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Texas — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 12:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Entities affiliated with SoftBank Group have acquired 4,709 acres in Milam County, Texas, within the larger 33,000-acre former Sandow Lakes Ranch site. While specific plans for the land are not detailed, the purchase is speculated to be related to data center development, given SoftBank's recent investments in the sector and its partnership with OpenAI for a large data center project in the same county. The acquisition comes amidst a surge in data center development in Texas, which has prompted calls for increased regulation from state officials.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: land

Property Name: Sandow Lakes Ranch

Site Area: 4709 acres

Notable Features: Part of the 33,000-acre former Sandow Lakes Ranch site, which includes Alcoa Corporation’s decommissioned aluminum smelter.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: SoftBank Group

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Xebec Holdings

SPONSOR PIPELINE

Stargate 1.2 gigawatt data center: OpenAI data center in Milam County, developed and operated by SB Energy with a $1 billion joint investment.

280,000 industrial lease: Industrial lease in Hutto, TX, with unconfirmed ties to SoftBank.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Austin Business Journal reported that Xebec Holdings' plans for The Switch campus at the Sandow Lakes Ranch site include a 3,300-acre campus slated for tenant readiness in 2026. SoftBank acquired data center investment firm DigitalBridge for $4 billion in late 2025. Governor Greg Abbott has recommended regulations for future data center developments, including requiring them to fund their own grid interconnection and eliminating sales tax exemptions.
