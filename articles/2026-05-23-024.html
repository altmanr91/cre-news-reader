Forest Development wins bid to build at Riviera Beach’s Marina Village

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Miami — Fri, 22 May 2026 18:03:19 +0000

SUMMARY

Forest Development has been selected by Riviera Beach city council members to develop a 12-acre waterfront site at Marina Village. The proposed $556.1 million phased development will include a Marriott Autograph Collection hotel, a convention center, a condominium with boat storage, restaurant/office buildings, a revamped park, and a culinary school. The developer must finalize an agreement with the city in the coming months, with project funding expected to come from existing loans and a commitment to contribute to a community benefit fund.

Notable Features: Proposed development includes a 20-story Marriott Autograph Collection hotel, a possible 10-story convention center, a 20-story condo with boat storage, and a culinary school run by celebrity chef Richard Burke.

Project Notes: The project is part of an 80-acre assemblage that city officials have been trying to redevelop for over a decade. Forest Development plans to contribute $100,000 annually to a community benefit fund. The development is projected to generate $2.9 million annually in city revenue and create 1,000 permanent jobs. The bid process was preceded by an FBI subpoena for Marina Village documents from city officials.
