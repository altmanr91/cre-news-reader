Digging into BTR and Housing Trends with Kiavi’s Tom Hallock

Connect CRE — Fri, 08 May 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Tom Hallock, head of construction lending at Kiavi, offers insights into the current housing market, describing it as the most stable he has seen despite volatility. He notes that an adequate supply of housing is emerging, with some markets returning to 2019 levels, and buyers are gaining more choices. Hallock anticipates that the remainder of 2026 will bring more affordable options, with interest rates stabilizing in the 6% range, which he views as healthy for the market. He also discusses the build-to-rent (BTR) market, suggesting that proposed federal legislation to force investors to sell BTR properties after seven years is likely political noise that will lead to more sensible compromises, such as exemptions for communities of five or more contiguous units. Hallock believes the BTR model, particularly horizontal multifamily, will continue to thrive as the economic gap between renting and owning narrows, appealing to demographics like single women and young families.
