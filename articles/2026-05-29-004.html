A Big Retail Tenant Kept Saying No. Then AI Closed the Deal.

Commercial Observer — Thu, 28 May 2026 17:55:30 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

NewMark Merrill Companies is leveraging artificial intelligence to enhance its retail leasing and investment strategies, according to CEO Sandy Sigal. The firm uses AI to identify optimal tenants for its shopping centers, analyze market conditions for potential acquisitions, and craft compelling pitch decks for prospective tenants. This approach has reportedly led to successful lease agreements with major retailers who had previously declined, and guided acquisition decisions by identifying the best replacement tenants for vacant spaces. Sigal emphasizes that while AI significantly streamlines the initial stages of deal-making, human expertise remains crucial for refining AI-generated content and ensuring accuracy.
