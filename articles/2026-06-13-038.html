Hudson Pacific lands SF’s biggest office lease in nearly a decade

LEASE

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Fri, 12 Jun 2026 19:06:12 +0000

SUMMARY

The City and County of San Francisco has signed a significant 502,000-square-foot office lease expansion at 1455 Market Street, owned by Hudson Pacific Properties. This deal brings the city government's total footprint in the building to over 900,000 square feet and is the largest office lease in San Francisco in nearly a decade. The 23-year lease term includes options for two five-year extensions and a right for the city to purchase the building, reflecting a strong commitment to consolidating several city departments into a modern, seismically sound facility. This move is expected to boost occupancy in the building to 89% and free up a city-owned property for future housing and mixed-use development.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: office

Address: 1455 Market Street, San Francisco, CA

Size: 502,000 SF

Current Occupancy: 89.0%

Notable Features: 22-story building; city government will consolidate multiple departments into this modern facility.

Project Notes: The City and County of San Francisco already occupies approximately 400,000 square feet in the building from two prior leases signed in 2024 and 2025. The city has the right to purchase the building until March 2027 and a right of first offer thereafter. Hudson Pacific Properties previously acquired the building for $93 million in 2010 and bought out a joint venture partner's interest for $43.5 million in 2024, valuing the property at $96.6 million at that time.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

LANDLORD: Hudson Pacific Properties

TENANT: City and County of San Francisco — Angela Yip, spokesperson for the city’s real estate division
