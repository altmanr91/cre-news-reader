Rockrose reels in $400M loan at 666 Greenwich

REFINANCE

The Real Deal — New York — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 14:32:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Rockrose Development secured a $404 million refinancing loan from PNC Bank for its luxury multifamily property, The Archive, located at 666 Greenwich Street in the West Village. Freddie Mac is slated to purchase the loan. The property, a historic Romanesque Revival-style building, features 479 residential units and 80,000 square feet of retail space.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Property Name: The Archive

Address: 666 Greenwich Street, New York City, NY

Units: 479

Loan Amount: $404.0M

Loan Per Unit: $843,424/unit (calculated)

Year Built: 1892

Notable Features: Romanesque Revival-style red brick building, full block property, internal atrium, in-house cinema, parking, common roof deck, city landmark since 1967.

Project Notes: The property was originally owned by the federal government for customs operations, then turned over to New York City in 1982. A partnership of New York State Urban Development Corporation and Teitelbaum Group was designated to convert the building into apartments. Rockrose joined as a general partner in a 99-year lease in 1985. The operating group pays into a fund for historic preservation and has a full property tax exemption.
