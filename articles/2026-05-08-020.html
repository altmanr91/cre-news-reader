COPA returns, with tenant advocates crafting the bill

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 07 May 2026 22:19:43 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

New York City Council Member Sandy Nurse is reintroducing the Community Opportunity to Purchase Act (COPA), a bill designed to give non-profit organizations a preferential chance to acquire apartment buildings. The revised legislation, which is expected to pass with the support of Mayor Zohran Mamdani, aims to preserve affordable housing by targeting buildings with indicators of physical or financial distress, such as those in enforcement programs or losing subsidies. While the real estate industry previously opposed a similar version, tenant advocates are actively involved in crafting the current bill, which seeks to delay sales for a limited period to allow pre-qualified non-profit buyers to conduct due diligence and make an offer.
