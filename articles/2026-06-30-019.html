NY Dirt: Rutland Plaza tenants take landlord, HPD to court

The Real Deal — New York — Tue, 30 Jun 2026 11:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Tenants at Rutland Plaza, a 438-unit residential complex in Brownsville, Brooklyn, have filed a lawsuit against their landlord, Robyn Lucas-Cora, and city and state housing agencies, alleging neglected conditions despite the landlord receiving $96 million in state preservation funding in 2016. The lawsuit seeks to compel corrective action for issues such as black mold, broken elevators, and raw sewage. Tenants are also considering a rent strike to pressure for improvements, claiming the landlord used the preservation funds for superficial upgrades rather than thorough repairs.
