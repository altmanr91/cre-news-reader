George Armoyan wipes out big Loop office loans as Chicago’s roster of debt-free towers grows

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Chicago — Sat, 06 Jun 2026 12:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Canadian investor George Armoyan, through Clarke Incorporated, has consolidated control over three Chicago-area office properties, including 120 South LaSalle and 20 South Clark streets in the Loop and 275 North Field Drive in Lake Forest. This move is part of Clarke's $1.1 billion takeover of Ravelin Properties, formerly Slate Office REIT, which had been facing financial difficulties and defaulted on debentures. The transaction effectively wipes out significant office loans, allowing the properties, particularly 20 South Clark Street, to operate debt-free and pursue aggressive leasing strategies in a competitive market.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: office

Sale Price: $1.10B

Notable Features: The acquisition includes three Chicago-area office properties: 120 South LaSalle, 20 South Clark, and 275 North Field Drive.

Project Notes: The transaction is a corporate takeover of Ravelin Properties REIT, which included a portfolio of properties in Canada and Ireland in addition to the Chicago assets. The deal resolved Ravelin's financial difficulties and loan defaults.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

LENDER: CIBC

LEASING AGENT: Stream Realty

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

A growing trend among well-capitalized owners in Chicago is to pay off mortgages entirely to maintain a competitive edge, allowing them to offer flexible lease terms and concessions without lender mandates. This strategy is being adopted by landlords like Morgan Stanley at 155 North Wacker Drive, Metropolis Investment Holdings at 455 North Cityfront Plaza, State Teachers Retirement System of Ohio at 77 West Wacker Drive, and Irvine Company at 300 North LaSalle Street.
