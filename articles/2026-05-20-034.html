10-Year Clears 4.5% 'Ceiling,' Forcing Real Estate To Reckon With Rate Reality

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 19 May 2026 17:11:29 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The U.S. 10-Year Treasury yield recently surpassed 4.5%, reaching its highest point since January 2025, prompting varied reactions within the commercial real estate sector. While some experts view this as a psychological rather than a material shift, others consider it a critical juncture, particularly for the substantial volume of pre-2022 debt maturing this year. The elevated rates are making loan sizing challenging and impacting acquisition returns, with concerns that a sustained increase above 5% could significantly impede market improvement. Despite these headwinds, capital is flowing back into real estate, with strong investment sales and office leasing activity reported in the first quarter, suggesting a market that is adapting to the new rate environment.
