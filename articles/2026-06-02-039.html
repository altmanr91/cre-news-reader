Boston's AI Sector Playing Catch-Up, But Office Owners See Some Activity

Bisnow News Feed — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 14:27:49 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Greater Boston's artificial intelligence sector is lagging behind major tech hubs like San Francisco and New York City in terms of venture capital funding and large office leasing activity, despite its historical strength in tech and biotech. While Boston captured only 8% of AI funding in 2025 and 3.7M SF of AI-specific office leasing since 2019 compared to San Francisco's 10M SF, local experts and landlords are observing a nascent but growing momentum. Recent state investments, the formation of the Massachusetts AI Coalition, and an uptick in smaller office leases (2,000-6,000 SF) indicate a building community and increasing interest from AI companies, though the region has yet to attract major expansion deals from large AI firms.
