Austin’s sublease glut is shrinking, but big office blocks still linger

Texas - The Real Deal — Thu, 30 Apr 2026 21:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Office Sublease Report

Austin's sublease market is experiencing a significant reduction in available space, with over 1 million square feet absorbed since September, bringing the total down to 3.3 million square feet. Despite this positive trend, large blocks of office space, particularly those over 100,000 square feet, continue to dominate the market, representing more than half of the total availability. The market activity reflects both a growing demand for discounted, flexible office solutions and ongoing corporate downsizing, with some long-standing listings finding new occupants while new sublease supply continues to emerge.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Austin's sublease market saw 1 million square feet absorbed since September, reducing total availability to 3.3 million square feet from 4.4 million square feet. Subleases larger than 100,000 square feet account for nearly 1.8 million square feet, more than half of the available space. Subleases offer discounted rents, shorter terms, and plug-and-play buildouts, appealing to firms in a cautious leasing environment.
