Amancio Ortega eyes record-setting Chicago office deal at 1 North Wacker

The Real Deal — Chicago — Wed, 24 Jun 2026 20:15:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Property Listing / For Sale

Amancio Ortega, founder of Zara, is reportedly nearing a deal to acquire the 51-story 1 North Wacker Drive office tower in Chicago from Irvine Company. The potential sale, which could exceed $500 million, would be the most expensive post-pandemic office transaction in the city. The deal would involve assuming a $353 million CMBS loan. The building, completed in 2001 and spanning 1.4 million square feet, boasts a 92% occupancy rate, significantly outperforming the downtown Chicago office market's average vacancy of 27%.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Downtown Chicago office buildings average 27% vacancy. The March 2022 sale of a stake in the Bank of America Tower at 110 North Wacker Drive valued the property at over $1 billion, setting a high water mark for Chicago office deals.
