Wellness-focused Coral Gables condo project nabs $68M construction loan

LOAN

The Real Deal — Miami — Mon, 08 Jun 2026 19:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Constellation Group and the Boschetti Group secured a $67.5 million construction loan from BHI for Cora Merrick Park, a 74-unit luxury condominium development in Coral Gables, two months after breaking ground. The project, located at 4241 Aurora Street, is designed with a strong focus on health and wellness, aiming for certification through the International Well Building Institute’s residential program. This financing underscores the developers' strategy to attract affluent buyers with wellness-centric amenities, despite a challenging lending environment for new construction.

Site Area: 0.7 acres

Notable Features: Wellness-focused luxury condo project seeking certification through the International Well Building Institute’s residential program, featuring a rooftop pool deck, yoga and meditation spaces, a fitness center, and Coral Gables’ first rooftop padel court.

Project Notes: The 0.7-acre site was previously acquired for $6.1 million in 2022. The development team originally envisioned the site as an apartment project before pivoting to condos. Construction began in April 2026 and is expected to wrap in 2028, a year later than originally projected. Units are priced from $900,000 to over $4 million for penthouses. The development will also feature a 5,000-square-foot public park with a sculpture by Jaume Plensa, which the developers plan to donate to the city.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Construction financing remains difficult to obtain, particularly as South Florida’s condo market shows signs of slowing after several years of record-breaking growth. Berkadia’s Miami office secured over $680 million in land acquisition, construction, and condo inventory loans across South Florida in 2026, and more than $1.7 billion over the past year.
