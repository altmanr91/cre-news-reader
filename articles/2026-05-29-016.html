HUD trims environmental review for federally-backed multifamily projects

The Real Deal — National — Thu, 28 May 2026 14:32:22 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) has issued an interim rule to streamline the approval process for federally-backed multifamily housing projects, particularly those involving affordable housing. This change eliminates a final environmental review sign-off for developments exceeding 200 units or with mortgages over $5 million, a step HUD deemed redundant and a cause of unnecessary delays. The move aligns with the Trump administration's broader deregulation agenda aimed at reducing federal permitting and environmental hurdles, a long-standing request from housing developers and industry groups who argue that layered reviews complicate financing timelines for affordable housing projects.
