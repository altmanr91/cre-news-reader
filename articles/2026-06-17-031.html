DataBank lands more than $1B boost for Red Oak data center megacampus

LOAN

The Real Deal — Texas — Tue, 16 Jun 2026 19:27:09 +0000

SUMMARY

DataBank has secured an additional $1.45 billion in financing for its Red Oak data center megacampus, bringing the total project funding to $2.65 billion. This new capital includes an $800 million revolving credit facility and a $650 million upsizing for the Red Oak campus. The development, which previously received a $2 billion construction loan for its initial phase, is part of a larger trend of significant data center expansion in the Dallas area.

Phase I: first three of eight planned data centers

Site Area: 292 acres

Notable Features: The campus is planned for eight data centers with 180 megawatts of power capacity.

Project Notes: The first three buildings are already fully leased. This financing follows a $2 billion construction loan received in April for the first phase.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

SPONSOR: DataBank

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Red Oak is emerging as a data center hub, with Compass Datacenters adding over 750,000 square feet across three buildings by 2026, and Google establishing a $600 million data center in 2023. Texas is projected to become the largest data center market in the U.S. by 2030, with grid demand from data centers expected to reach 40 gigawatts by 2028, up from 8 gigawatts in 2025. As of January, Texas had 387 operational data centers.
