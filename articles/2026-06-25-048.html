5 Years After Deadly Surfside Collapse, Investigators Say Building Failure Began Weeks Prior

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 24 Jun 2026 13:11:44 -0400

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Federal investigators have determined that the structural failure of the Champlain Towers South condo building in Surfside, Florida, began weeks before its catastrophic collapse in June 2021, with two connections between garage columns and the pool deck failing in early June 2021. This initial failure led to a redistribution of loads to weaker column connections, ultimately causing the pool deck to collapse onto the parking garage, which then triggered the building's partial collapse. The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) is finalizing its report with recommendations for updated building standards and codes. The tragedy has prompted Florida lawmakers to enact Senate Bill 4-D, requiring structural inspections and fully funded reserves for older condo buildings, which has created financial challenges for many owners. The site of the collapse was acquired by Damac International for $120M, with plans for a luxury condo development, but sales have been slow, and the firm is reportedly considering selling the site or seeking a development partner.
