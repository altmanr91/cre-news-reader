Endless supply: DFW residential rankings

The Real Deal — Texas — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 11:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Residential Rankings

This article ranks the top residential broker teams and brokerages in the Dallas-Fort Worth market by total dollar volume, highlighting the continued strength of the region's housing market despite economic headwinds. Ben Caballero of HomesUSA.com led the individual broker rankings with $2.43 billion in sales, while Compass topped the brokerage list with over $6.2 billion. The report also delves into the unique dynamics of DFW's luxury market, where local firm Allie Beth Allman & Associates maintains a strong presence against national competitors like Compass, often leveraging off-market transactions.

DATA POINTS

Top Broker (Individual): Ben Caballero (HomesUSA.com) - $2.43 billion sales volume, 4,923 transactions, $493,724 average sale price (April 1, 2025 - April 1, 2026)

Second Broker (Individual): Jared Turner (Turner Mangum) - $956 million sales volume, 3,257 deals (April 1, 2025 - April 1, 2026)

Top Brokerage: Compass - $6.2 billion+ sales volume, 7,188 deals (April 1, 2025 - April 1, 2026)

Second Brokerage: Ebby Halliday Realtors - $5.6 billion sales volume, 7,720 deals (April 1, 2025 - April 1, 2026)

Third Brokerage: eXp Realty - $3 billion total sales (April 1, 2025 - April 1, 2026)

Top Luxury Team (Average Sales Price): Perry Wisdom Barrett Narosov Group (Allie Beth Allman) - $281.6 million sales volume, 70 deals, ~$4 million average per deal

Second Luxury Team (Average Sales Price): Detwiler+Wood Group (Compass) - $233.6 million volume, 63 deals, ~$3.7 million average per deal

Most Expensive Residential Sale in Texas (2025): 6601 Hunters Glen Road, University Park, TX - $30.5 million

Ultra-luxury homes sold above $10 million (2025): Dallas-Fort Worth: 15; Houston: 10

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Home construction in Texas has seen a decline from its post-pandemic peak. Interest rate reductions expected for the current year do not appear to be materializing, which is anticipated to lower housing starts and reduce inventory. Dallas-Fort Worth led the state in sales dollar volume for ultra-luxury homes in 2025, with 15 homes selling above $10 million, surpassing Houston's 10. Off-market transactions are common in Dallas, partly to avoid high property tax valuations, and these deals significantly boost top luxury teams.
