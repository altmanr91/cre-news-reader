Fort Chaplin’s affordable housing promise in DC gives way to squalid reality

The Real Deal — National — Thu, 04 Jun 2026 21:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Fort Chaplin Park Apartments, a 549-unit affordable housing complex in Washington, D.C., is facing severe disrepair and hundreds of unresolved building violations, a decade after being celebrated as a model for affordable housing preservation. Residents report issues such as mold, rodent infestations, flooding, and electrical problems, with District records showing approximately 650 open housing code violations. The property's owner, Standard Communities, has acknowledged management shortcomings and is working with regulators and local nonprofits to address the issues, while emphasizing its commitment to long-term affordability.
