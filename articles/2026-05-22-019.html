Elliman’s weak first-quarter performance casts doubt on turnaround

The Real Deal — National — Thu, 21 May 2026 17:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Company Earnings

Douglas Elliman reported a $16 million net loss in the first quarter of 2026, reversing a brief return to profitability in late 2025. The loss was primarily driven by a 15% year-over-year decline in revenue, attributed to fewer home sales and a slowdown in its development marketing division. Despite efforts to reduce operating expenses, the firm faced increased costs from litigation and agent commissions, highlighting ongoing challenges in its recovery from previous losses and leadership changes. The company's new development marketing pipeline, valued at $25 billion, has yet to significantly translate into earnings, with commissions from this sector declining in the first quarter.
