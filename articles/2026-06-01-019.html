“You’re right!” So far, AI’s threat to resi real estate is making clients overconfident

The Real Deal — National — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 11:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article explores how artificial intelligence, particularly generative AI tools like ChatGPT, is impacting residential real estate agents by making clients overconfident and increasing agent workloads. While previous technological disruptions like online listings and iBuying failed to displace agents or significantly reduce commissions, AI presents a new challenge by empowering clients with often inaccurate or legally non-compliant information. Agents are now spending more time correcting AI-generated advice, which could lead to increased workloads, lower commissions, or even jeopardize deals, despite the technology still being in its early stages of adoption.

DATA POINTS

Americans using generative AI often: One quarter (as of April survey), up 40% from six months prior

Americans using AI in home transaction: Half (as of end of last year), likely higher now

Real estate commissions: Hovered between 5% and 6% for decades
