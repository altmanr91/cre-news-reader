Inside Naples’ ultra-luxury shift

The Real Deal — Miami — Mon, 04 May 2026 11:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Naples, FL, traditionally a seasonal market, is rapidly transforming into a year-round ultra-luxury hub, attracting affluent transplants who are spending more time and money in the area. This shift is evidenced by record-breaking residential sales, including a $225 million beachfront estate and an $85 million waterfront mansion in the exclusive Port Royal neighborhood. The market's appeal is further bolstered by new luxury amenities, a rebuilding of the Port Royal private club, and a strong pipeline of high-end condo projects, despite broader market corrections and the impact of Hurricane Ian.

DATA POINTS

Collier County record home sale (April 2025): $85 million

Second-priciest home sale in U.S. (April 2025): $225 million

Most expensive listing in Florida (Gordon Pointe): $271 million

Port Royal private club rebuilding cost: $100 million

Collier County storm repair cost (Hurricane Ian): $2.2 billion

Port Royal private club membership buy-in: Nearly $500,000

Naples housing price drop (2025): 6.5 percent

Single-family closings (Nov 2025 - Apr 1 2026 vs. Nov 2024 - Apr 1 2025): Up slightly

$20M+ market segment closings (Nov 2025 - Apr 1 2026 vs. Nov 2024 - Apr 1 2025): Up 75 percent (from 4 to 7 closings)

Pending sales in Naples (Feb 2026 year-over-year): Up 56 percent

Inventory in Naples (Feb 2026 year-over-year): Down 15 percent

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The ultra-luxury market in Naples is experiencing unprecedented 'buzz' in areas like Port Royal. The overall housing market in Naples saw the largest drop in prices in the country in 2025, down 6.5 percent, but the $20 million-and-up segment is performing strongly. Pending sales in Naples were up 56 percent year-over-year in February 2026, while inventory was down 15 percent in the same period. The JW Marriott Marco Island Beach Resort and two Naples golf courses were under contract for $835 million in March 2026, indicating strong confidence in local tourism.
