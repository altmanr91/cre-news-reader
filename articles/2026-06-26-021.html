Waterton buys for $81M, NADG scores $121M loan amid Palm Beach-area apartment shortage

The Real Deal — Miami — Thu, 25 Jun 2026 21:12:13 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Multifamily

Waterton Residential acquired The Landings at Pembroke Lake Apartments, a 358-unit multifamily complex in Pembroke Pines, for $80.5 million. Separately, North American Development Group secured a $120.6 million construction loan from Wells Fargo Bank to begin building NUVO Delray Beach, a 476-unit apartment complex in Delray Beach. The Delray Beach project will include 25% affordable housing units and is expected to deliver its first apartments by fall 2027, addressing a shortage of multifamily supply in Palm Beach County.

DATA POINTS

Palm Beach County Multifamily Deliveries (2025): Lowest since 2011

Palm Beach County Multifamily Demand vs. Supply (2025): Demand outpaced supply for the first time since 2021

Palm Beach County Multifamily Units Under Construction (Q1 2026): 4,600 units (highest level since 2023)

Broward County Multifamily Units Under Construction (Q1 2026): 6,000 units (lowest level since 2020)

Broward County Multifamily Demand vs. Supply (2025): Demand outweighed supply for the first time in three years

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Multifamily development in Palm Beach County saw its lowest annual delivery since 2011 last year, leading to demand outpacing supply for the first time since 2021. The development pipeline in Palm Beach County reached 4,600 units in Q1 2026, the highest level since 2023. In Broward County, multifamily construction has slowed, with 6,000 units under construction in Q1 2026, the lowest since 2020, and demand outpaced supply last year for the first time in three years.
