States defang housing opponents, but NIMBYs claw back

The Real Deal — National — Sat, 23 May 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article discusses the ongoing legislative battles in states like Florida, California, and New York, where state governments are attempting to streamline housing development by curtailing local obstructionism, often referred to as 'NIMBYism'. Despite state-level efforts to expedite projects, local opposition groups continue to employ various tactics, including environmental concerns and even the presence of wildlife, to delay or halt developments. The piece highlights a specific case in Woodland Hills, California, where a developer's plan to convert a golf course into a residential community is facing significant local resistance, illustrating the challenges in overcoming entrenched 'not in my backyard' sentiments.
