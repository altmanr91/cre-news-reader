Chicago MLS cuts off data feed to Zillow

The Real Deal — Chicago — Wed, 20 May 2026 16:01:08 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Midwest Real Estate Data (MRED), the third-largest Multiple Listing Service in the U.S., has terminated its direct listing feed to Zillow, removing approximately 43,000 Chicago-area homes from the platform. This action is the culmination of a year-long dispute over Zillow's practice of blocking listings that were initially marketed through private channels, which MRED considers a violation of its licensing agreement prohibiting filtering listings based on the associated broker. MRED's CEO, Rebecca Jensen, emphasized the MLS's responsibility to consistently enforce its rules, stating that Zillow's selective exclusion breaches its agreement. Zillow, in response, has filed a federal antitrust lawsuit against MRED and Compass, alleging collusion to stifle competition, and argues that MRED's actions harm buyers, sellers, and agents by reducing access to listings.

DATA POINTS

Listings removed from Zillow (Chicago-area): 43,000

Active listings on Zillow (Chicago, after cutoff): 2,000
