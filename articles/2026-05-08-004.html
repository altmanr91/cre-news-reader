Walker & Dunlop Investment Partners Outlines 2026 CRE Risks

Commercial Observer — Thu, 07 May 2026 20:02:51 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Walker & Dunlop Investment Partners' experts recently discussed the significant risks facing the commercial real estate financial system, particularly within the multifamily sector. They highlighted that multifamily investments from 2019-2022 have largely underperformed due to a shift from low interest rates and cheap capital to today's higher rates, increased debt costs, and muted rent growth. This environment has created a demand for bridge financing as many construction loans mature on properties that are not yet stabilized or meeting original underwriting projections. The panel also emphasized the resilience of real estate-secured private credit compared to corporate private credit, noting that hard assets provide intrinsic value and foreclosure options for lenders. Furthermore, they pointed out a concerning trend of low distributions on paid investment (DPI) for real estate equity funds, impacting investor liquidity and future capital allocation decisions by institutional investors.

DATA POINTS

Real estate funds performance (Preqin Database study): More than 200 funds with $200 billion in invested capital are not returning money to investors at historic levels.

Median Distributions on Paid Investment (DPI) for 2019-2021 vintage funds: 15 percent
