The Wave is Here: What Multifamily Loan Maturities Mean for Owners, Lenders & Brokers in 2026

REBusinessOnline — Fri, 12 Jun 2026 16:02:16 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article analyzes the impending wave of multifamily loan maturities in 2026, highlighting the challenges owners face due to elevated interest rates and tightened underwriting standards. Many owners who secured low-interest loans in 2020-2021 are now over-leveraged, with increased operating expenses, slowed rent growth, and declining valuations making refinancing difficult. This situation is expected to drive a surge in transaction activity, including forced sales, as well-capitalized sponsors re-enter the market to acquire assets that are no longer viable for their current owners. The author advises owners to be proactive, realistic, and plan early for refinancing, selling, or bringing in capital partners.

DATA POINTS

Multifamily mortgages maturing in 2026: 13%

March Consumer Price Index (CPI): 3.3% over prior 12 months

Federal Reserve benchmark funds rate (late April): 3.5% to 3.75%

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Mortgage Bankers Association's 2025 Commercial Real Estate Survey of Loan Maturity Volumes indicates that 13% of multifamily mortgages will mature in 2026. Interest rates reached record lows in 2020-2021, but the Federal Reserve has since raised rates in response to inflation, with the benchmark funds rate between 3.5% and 3.75% in late April. Operating expenses have increased, rent growth has slowed, cap rates have widened, and valuations have declined, leading to tighter underwriting and smaller refinance loan amounts.
