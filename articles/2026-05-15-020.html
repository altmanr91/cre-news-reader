“Everyone says it’s my company”: Will Blodgett wins summary judgement in Fairstead case

The Real Deal — National — Thu, 14 May 2026 13:18:29 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

A Delaware Court of Chancery judge granted summary judgment to William Blodgett, a former founding partner of Fairstead, in a lawsuit brought against him by the affordable housing operator. The court found that while Blodgett violated his employment agreement by sharing confidential information, Fairstead did not have the right to cancel his equity interests. This decision could result in a multi-million dollar payout to Blodgett for improperly canceled equity, with the exact amount yet to be determined.
