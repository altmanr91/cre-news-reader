ICSC 2026: Inflation, Trampoline Parks and the Fate of Luxury

Commercial Observer — Fri, 22 May 2026 18:28:35 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article summarizes key takeaways from the ICSC conference, highlighting that retail real estate is experiencing a strong resurgence in leasing and investment, driven by a long period of underdevelopment and demolition of older centers. However, this strength is not yet fully reflected in Net Operating Income (NOI). Inflation and evolving consumer behavior are major concerns, with shoppers increasingly seeking value and convenience, leading to more frequent visits and a boost for discount stores. The industry is also focused on tenant remixing in shopping centers, a re-evaluation of luxury brand strategies, and the surprising growth of entertainment retail, including trampoline parks. The potential impact of artificial intelligence on real estate data and analytics is also a significant topic of discussion.
