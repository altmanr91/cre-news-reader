Kneejerk opponents wonder why they don’t get early notice

The Real Deal — New York — Sat, 13 Jun 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Editorial

This opinion piece criticizes local activists in Albany, NY, for opposing a proposed $7 million bus terminal project. The author argues that the opposition, led by a county legislator and a community leader, is a "NIMBY" (Not In My Backyard) reaction, primarily objecting to the project's location and the timing of community input rather than its merits. The article highlights the project's necessity, citing the impending closure of the current Greyhound station due to other construction and the need for a modern, equitable transportation hub for bus riders. The author suggests that the project, which has been developed through extensive collaboration among various government and economic development agencies, will serve a vital public need and improve regional connectivity.
