Recovering Value from Underutilized Campus Space

Connect CRE — Fri, 29 May 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Higher Education

JLL's inaugural 2025 Higher Education Portfolio Benchmarking Survey highlights that universities and colleges are struggling with underperforming physical campuses, averaging $4.9 billion in value per institution, due to a significant gap between planned and actual classroom utilization. The survey found that 81% of institutions lack key performance indicators for space management, and only 50% track occupancy data, leading to anecdotal rather than evidence-based space decisions. JLL analysts suggest that systematic space management could generate annual cost savings of $2 million to $4 million, emphasizing that higher education treats its largest capital investment differently from corporations, which view space as a measurable, optimizable asset. The report proposes a phased solution involving sensor deployment, KPI establishment, and data integration into decision-making, asserting that optimizing real estate assets is the largest untapped funding source for institutional excellence.

DATA POINTS

Average campus value per institution: $4.9 billion

Gap between planned and actual classroom utilization: 33%

Institutions without space management KPIs: 81%

Institutions tracking occupancy data: 50%

Potential annual cost savings from systematic space management: $2 million to $4 million
