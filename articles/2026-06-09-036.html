Co-living startup sells Sutter Mansion at 52% markup a year after buying

SALE

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Mon, 08 Jun 2026 23:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Neighbourgood, a co-living and hospitality company, has sold the Sutter Mansion, a boutique hotel in San Francisco, for $7.3 million to Dharma Bums Holdings. This transaction represents a 52% markup from the $4.8 million Neighbourgood paid for the property just one year prior. Neighbourgood divested the asset to reallocate capital towards larger-scale adaptive reuse opportunities that better align with its long-term investment strategy.
