Sale-Leasebacks Surge As Companies Eye Ways To Raise Cash

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 04 Jun 2026 08:07:19 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Sale-leaseback transactions are experiencing a resurgence as companies seek to unlock capital from their real estate assets amid a unique economic environment characterized by stabilizing interest rates and increased merger and acquisition activity. This strategy allows businesses to raise cash without incurring debt, improving their balance sheets, and providing immediate cash flow for buyers. The trend is driven by both corporate M&A deals, where real estate is often divested post-acquisition, and by companies looking to fund expansion or provide exit strategies for owners. Despite broader economic uncertainties, sale-leasebacks offer a flexible financing alternative, though some critics, particularly in the healthcare sector, are pushing for regulatory oversight.
