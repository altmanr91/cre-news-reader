PolicyPro: J-51 reboot flops for rent-regulated landlords, boosts co-ops and condos

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 20 May 2026 22:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

The New York State Legislature is poised to approve a reboot of the J-51 property tax abatement program, extending it through 2036 and broadening eligibility for co-ops and condos. While the revamped program increases the benefit cap to 100% of reasonable rehabilitation costs and mandates biennial updates to cost schedules, it maintains the 50% affordability requirement for rental buildings, effectively excluding many rent-stabilized properties. This outcome is seen as a significant disappointment for owners of rent-regulated buildings, who sought a broader overhaul to include properties with at least 90% rent-regulated units. Conversely, co-ops and condos will benefit from a raised assessed value threshold for eligibility, which will also be indexed to inflation, and a cap on abatements at 50% of annual property tax, a limit not expected to deter participation.

DATA POINTS

J-51 program extension: through 2036

J-51 benefit cap increase: up to 100% of reasonable rehabilitation cost (from 70%)

J-51 annual abatement cap: 8.33% of renovation costs over life of benefit (up to 20 years)

J-51 rental building affordability requirement: at least 50% affordable

J-51 co-op/condo assessed value threshold: $60,000 (from $45,000), indexed to CPI

J-51 co-op/condo abatement cap: 50% of building's annual property tax bill
