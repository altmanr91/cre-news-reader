Banks are back, baby

The Real Deal — National — Fri, 29 May 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

After a two-year period of reduced activity, banks are re-entering the commercial real estate lending market, driven by improved market conditions and a greater willingness to work with borrowers on refinancing terms. This shift follows a period where banks focused on cleaning up their loan books, leading to an 80% increase in CRE loan originations in Q1 2026 compared to the previous year. While some banks are still cautious, particularly regarding certain asset classes and markets, the overall sentiment indicates a return to more active lending, with some institutions reporting significant increases in their deal pipelines.
