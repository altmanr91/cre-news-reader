A life sciences sector that’s finally stabilizing? That’s what JLL is predicting

REJournals — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 10:02:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Life Sciences Report

JLL's 2026 U.S. Lab Property Report indicates that the U.S. life sciences real estate sector is showing signs of stabilization, with tenant demand for laboratory space beginning to outpace new supply in key markets. This shift is attributed to improving biotech funding, stronger capital flows, and increased tenant activity, suggesting the market may be entering an early recovery phase after a prolonged downturn. However, the sector still faces a significant supply imbalance, particularly in older properties, and recovery is uneven across the country, with primary markets like Boston, San Francisco Bay Area, San Diego, and Raleigh-Durham outperforming secondary markets.

DATA POINTS

U.S. lab availability decrease since mid-2025: 2 million square feet

Absorption in buildings completed since 2020 (last 9 months): 2.6 million square feet

Increase in available space in properties built before 2000 (last 9 months): 700,000 square feet

Share of lab leases by AI, robotics, and 'tough tech' companies in Boston (2025): 30%

Year-over-year demand increase in Boston, San Francisco Bay Area, San Diego, and Raleigh-Durham (Q1 2026): 44%

Combined demand in Boston, San Francisco Bay Area, San Diego, and Raleigh-Durham (Q1 2026): nearly 8 million square feet

Tenant demand decrease in secondary markets (last 3 years): nearly 3 million square feet

Available inventory increase in secondary markets (last 3 years): 4.4 million square feet

Vacancy rates in Boston, San Diego, and Bay Area: 32%

Total U.S. lab market size: more than 200 million square feet

Supply-to-demand ratio in U.S. lab market: approaching 6-to-1
