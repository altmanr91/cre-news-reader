New York State Lawmakers Pass One-Year Data Center Construction Moratorium

Commercial Observer — Fri, 05 Jun 2026 17:37:34 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

New York State lawmakers have passed a one-year moratorium on the construction of new large data centers, aiming to establish regulations for the rapidly expanding industry. The legislation, which passed both the Senate and Assembly on the final day of the legislative session, seeks to address concerns regarding environmental impact, infrastructure capacity, and the protection of local communities. While Governor Kathy Hochul's office has not indicated whether she will sign the bill, the administration has previously emphasized the importance of ensuring that data centers cover the costs of their utility expansion to prevent rising expenses for consumers, especially with the increasing demand driven by artificial intelligence.
