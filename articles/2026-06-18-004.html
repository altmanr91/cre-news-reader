Warsh’s First Fed Meeting as Chair Produces a Result Many in CRE Expected

Commercial Observer — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 19:22:15 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The Federal Reserve, under new Chairman Kevin Warsh, unanimously voted to maintain its benchmark interest rate between 3.5 percent and 3.75 percent for the fourth consecutive meeting, signaling a continued 'higher-for-longer' environment due to inflation uncertainty and geopolitical tensions. The updated 'dot plot' projections removed previous forecasts for a rate cut this year and indicated potential rate hikes, with the federal funds rate now projected at 3.8 percent by the end of 2026. While the commercial real estate industry desires lower rates, some market participants believe a quarter-point reduction would not significantly impact deal pricing, and many are prepared to transact. The market has largely priced in these higher-for-longer conditions, with the 10-Year Treasury yield being a more critical factor for refinancing economics than the federal funds rate.
