PGIM bets big on homebuilder land banks with $4B deployment

LOAN

The Real Deal — National — Wed, 27 May 2026 17:38:58 +0000

SUMMARY

PGIM, the asset management arm of Prudential Financial, has deployed approximately $4 billion into residential land-bank deals through a partnership with Domain Real Estate Partners. This strategy involves homebuilders selling undeveloped lots to a financing partner and then repurchasing them as construction progresses, allowing builders to maintain liquidity and a future housing pipeline without tying up capital in land holdings. The move reflects a broader institutional investor trend towards asset-based finance, providing exposure to long-term housing demand while mitigating direct development risks.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: land

Loan Amount: $4.00B

Project Notes: PGIM and Domain Real Estate Partners launched their partnership last year and have completed seven residential land-bank transactions.
