Chicago’s cold storage reset: Modern product rises as aging inventory lags

REJournals — Tue, 19 May 2026 10:02:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Chicago's cold storage market is undergoing a significant structural reset, with modern, purpose-built facilities outperforming an aging inventory. Despite a rise in overall vacancy to 7.6% in Q1 2026 and a 65% drop in construction activity, demand for state-of-the-art cold storage remains strong, evidenced by tight vacancy rates below 1% for modern product and a 48% increase in net absorption over the past year. This divergence is driven by tenants seeking automation-ready, energy-efficient, and compliance-forward facilities, leading to a migration away from older, less sophisticated spaces. While rental rates are stagnant due to operator economics and an oversupply of pallet positions, Chicago's fundamental advantages, such as limited land and development constraints, continue to attract institutional capital, particularly for build-to-suit projects with long-term leases.
