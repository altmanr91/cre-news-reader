Who is Buffy Wicks? Meet the legislator dividing California real estate

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Thu, 25 Jun 2026 22:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

California legislator Buffy Wicks has introduced AB 736, a bill that proposes to cap real estate transfer taxes statewide at 1.5 percent for most jurisdictions and 3 percent for cities like San Francisco and Culver City. This legislation aims to stimulate housing development by addressing developer concerns about high transfer taxes, which can hinder the conversion of commercial properties into housing. The bill seeks to find a middle ground between a more aggressive taxpayer-backed measure to cap taxes at 0.11 percent and existing higher rates, particularly impacting San Francisco's 'mansion tax' (Measure ULA) and potentially costing the city $100 million annually. While supported by some major developers, the proposal faces opposition from progressives who argue it limits local government revenue for affordable housing and from realtors concerned it could increase homebuying costs in some general-law cities.
