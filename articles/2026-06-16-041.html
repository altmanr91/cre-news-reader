Michael Puline: Principal’s Mindset is Essential to Modern Brokerage

Connect CRE — Mon, 15 Jun 2026 21:20:16 +0000

SUMMARY

Feature / Profile

Michael Puline, a veteran in retail real estate, has joined Marcus & Millichap and Institutional Property Advisors (IPA) to spearhead the strategic growth of their retail divisions and lead investment sales professionals. With a 25-year background in closing over 2,500 leasing and sale transactions totaling $8.5 billion, Puline aims to leverage his principal's mindset, gained from two decades on the ownership side, to enhance client service and execution. He believes the future of brokerage requires a deep understanding of the capital stack, operational challenges, and long-term business strategy, moving beyond mere transaction execution. Puline was drawn to the firm's extensive network, proprietary data, and integrated research, which he sees as crucial advantages in the fast-paced retail market. He notes that retail has transitioned from a doubted asset class to one with strong fundamentals, driven by consistent demand for physical footprints, including last-mile retail and service-oriented businesses. While grocery-anchored and net-lease properties remain strong, small shop demand is significantly driving rental income growth, leading to increased interest in unanchored strips. Retailers are also showing greater flexibility in formats and market strategies, adapting to limited supply and becoming more receptive to higher rents due to improved sales and margins.
