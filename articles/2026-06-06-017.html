The Data Drop: In a pricey world, NYC’s builders pivot to redevelopments

The Real Deal — National — Fri, 05 Jun 2026 12:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Development Trends

An analysis of New York City permit data reveals a significant shift in development trends, with builders increasingly prioritizing redevelopment projects over new ground-up construction due to persistently high financing costs. This trend is particularly evident in Manhattan, where office-to-residential conversions are prevalent, and in Brooklyn, where expansions of single and two-family homes are becoming common. This pivot allows developers to maintain a steady construction pipeline despite rising interest rates and construction expenses.

DATA POINTS

Refinancing originations vs. new purchase loans (Q1): Refinancing originations outpaced new purchase loans for the second consecutive quarter, a trend not seen since early 2022.

New home purchase loans (Q1): Dropped by 12% year-over-year to a 12-year quarterly low.

Dallas-Fort Worth top 20 residential brokers deal volume (April 2025-April 2026): Nearly $7.7 billion

Dallas-Fort Worth top 20 residential brokers sales increase (April 2025-April 2026): 6% more sales compared to the prior year

Dallas-Fort Worth top 20 residential brokers average sale price (April 2025-April 2026): $485,500, down from $516,300 the prior year

Port St. Lucie, FL renter mobility (2019-2024): Rose 8.6 percentage points

Port St. Lucie, FL homeowner mobility (2019-2024): Fell by 1.3 points

Potential multifamily loan defaults with 5-year rent freeze (NYC): 6% of multifamily loans (including market-rate buildings), rising to 8% (excluding market-rate buildings)

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

High mortgage rates are making it increasingly difficult for homeowners to give up their low-interest-rate loans, contributing to lower homeowner mobility in Sun Belt markets. The Dallas-Fort Worth housing market is normalizing post-pandemic, with increased sales volume but a dip in average prices for top brokers. A five-year rent freeze in New York City is projected to have a limited impact on multifamily loan defaults, affecting only 6-8% of loans according to Moody's.
