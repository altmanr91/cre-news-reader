LISTEN: Inside David and Michael Shabsels’ summer camp empire

The Real Deal — National — Mon, 15 Jun 2026 18:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

This article discusses the financial troubles of Simad Holdings, an operator of approximately 30 summer camps, which recently filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy. The company defaulted on Israeli bonds and transferred $34 million to its owners' private companies, leading to significant liabilities. The situation highlights the risks associated with aggressive financing strategies and has drawn attention from Israeli securities investigators.

DATA POINTS

Number of creditors: 50,000 to 100,000

Liabilities: $500 million to $1 billion

Israeli bond market raise (December): $195 million

Transferred funds to private companies: $34 million
