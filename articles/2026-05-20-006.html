New Jersey Retail: The Juice Is Worth The Squeeze

REBusinessOnline — Wed, 20 May 2026 11:49:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article argues that New Jersey presents a unique and attractive retail market for operators despite its challenges, which include tight space availability, high rents, and complex regulatory processes. The author highlights that retailers often need to adapt their store formats to fit the market's constraints, particularly in Northern New Jersey where large boxes are rare. Brokers emphasize the importance of local expertise to navigate the state's diverse submarkets and municipal nuances. Despite initial sticker shock on rents, tenants often find strong store performance and favorable occupancy costs, making the investment worthwhile. The article also points out the distinct advantage of New Jersey's retail landscape, which often includes ample parking and a suburban feel even in urban environments, fostering cross-shopping and longer customer stays.
