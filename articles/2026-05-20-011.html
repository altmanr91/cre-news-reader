Plenty of momentum: Suburban Chicago multifamily market gains strength in first quarter of 2026

REJournals — Wed, 20 May 2026 10:08:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Sales Report

The suburban Chicago multifamily market experienced significant growth in the first quarter of 2026, with total sales volume surging by 56.9% year-over-year to $360.1 million. This increase was driven by investors pursuing larger apartment assets, as the number of transactions remained flat at 54 deals compared to the previous year. The average transaction size rose to $6.6 million, and the total number of apartment units traded increased by 50.4% to 2,430 units, indicating a trend towards larger, more institutional-quality properties.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Chicago suburban multifamily market saw a significant increase in sales volume and average transaction size in Q1 2026, indicating a shift towards larger, more institutional-quality properties. Cook County accounted for half of all suburban multifamily transactions, with DuPage and Kane counties showing larger-than-average deal sizes.
