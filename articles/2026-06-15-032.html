Godwin Group Directors Sued For £155M, Assets Frozen

Bisnow News Feed — Sun, 14 Jun 2026 07:17:09 -0400

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

The directors of Godwin Group's collapsed property companies are facing a High Court lawsuit for approximately £155 million, with their assets frozen. The claim, lodged by administrators from MHA, alleges breach of directors' fiduciary duties, fraudulent trading, and knowing receipt and dishonest assistance, stemming from the mismanagement of Godwin Capital No. 8 Ltd. This action follows the company's administration in June 2025, after raising £162 million from over 2,000 individual investors through loan notes for property developments, which were not secured on assets as promised.
