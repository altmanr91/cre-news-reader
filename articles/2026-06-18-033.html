Double-dipping: Walker & Dunlop alleges investor committed “fraudulent misrepresentation” to get inflated loans for West Side Chicago apartments

FORECLOSURE

The Real Deal — Chicago — Wed, 17 Jun 2026 20:24:11 +0000

SUMMARY

Walker & Dunlop, on behalf of Freddie Mac, has filed two foreclosure lawsuits against multifamily investor Chaim Bialostozky, alleging "fraudulent misrepresentation" in securing inflated loans for West Side Chicago apartment properties. The lawsuits claim Bialostozky orchestrated same-day, back-to-back sales at higher prices to obtain larger Freddie Mac loans, without disclosing the initial, lower-priced transactions to the lender. The properties, which include a four-building portfolio and a separate apartment building, have allegedly fallen into severe disrepair, with Bialostozky ceasing debt payments last July. Walker & Dunlop is seeking at least $7.1 million to cover unpaid debt and legal fees, and is requesting a receiver be appointed to manage the properties, which require an estimated $7.8 million in repairs.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Loan Amount: $7.1M

Project Notes: The lawsuits allege that Chaim Bialostozky purchased a four-building portfolio for $4.6 million, which had traded for $3.5 million earlier the same day, and secured a $3.4 million loan. A separate property at 45-52 North Waller Avenue was purchased for $3.6 million, after an earlier $2.7 million transaction, with a $2.9 million loan secured. The properties are in severe disrepair, requiring an estimated $7.8 million in repairs.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Multifamily distress has been increasing in Chicago's South and West Sides, where out-of-state investors acquired apartment buildings when interest rates were low and subsequently defaulted on debt payments. Walker & Dunlop previously disclosed potential exposure to up to $100 million in fraudulent mortgages.
