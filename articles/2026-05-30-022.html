Nonprofit brings affordable homes, grocer to publicly owned site as part of Coleman Park revival

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Miami — Fri, 29 May 2026 22:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

The Quantum Foundation, through its Palm Beach Venture Philanthropy division, is spearheading a mixed-use residential development in West Palm Beach's Coleman Park neighborhood. The city commissioners have transferred 0.85 acres of publicly owned land to the nonprofit, which will combine it with additional parcels to create a project featuring residential units, a grocery store, a library, community offices, and gathering spaces. A significant portion of the residential units will be designated as affordable or workforce housing, aiming to address the persistent poverty and health disparities in the historically Black community.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: mixed-use

Site Area: 0.85 acres

Notable Features: Includes a grocery store, library, community organization offices, and gathering space. At least 40% of residential units will be affordable or workforce housing.

Project Notes: The project is part of an ongoing revitalization effort for the Coleman Park neighborhood. Quantum Foundation is seeking a developer for the project, with completion expected in approximately five years. The nonprofit has already invested over $8 million into the neighborhood.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Quantum Foundation

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Palm Beach Venture Philanthropy — Raphael Clemente, executive director
