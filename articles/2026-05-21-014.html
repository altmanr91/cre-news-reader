Milwaukee’s industrial market entering a period of steady growth? That’s what Marcus & Millichap is predicting

REJournals — Thu, 21 May 2026 05:32:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Industrial Outlook

Milwaukee's industrial market is anticipated to enter a period of steady growth, driven by strengthening tenant demand and increasing investor interest, according to a new report from Marcus & Millichap. The market is transitioning from a supply-driven environment, where significant new construction had pushed vacancy rates higher, towards a more balanced state. The report highlights easing vacancy pressures, robust demand for smaller industrial buildings, and the positive impact of infrastructure investments around Milwaukee Mitchell International Airport as key drivers for this optimistic outlook. A projected slowdown in new construction, with completions expected to fall by 35% in 2026, is also expected to contribute to declining vacancy and support rent growth.

DATA POINTS

Industrial vacancy (H1 2025): 6%

Industrial completions (2026 forecast): 1.3 million square feet

Projected decline in industrial completions (2026): 35%

Average asking rents (2026 forecast): $6.23 per square foot

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

More than 4 million square feet of industrial space was vacated during the first half of 2025. Demand for industrial buildings under 50,000 square feet remains strong, with vacancy levels below market averages. Institutional investment activity in Milwaukee's industrial sector is increasing, with a growing number of properties trading above $10 million, particularly in Milwaukee and Waukesha counties.
