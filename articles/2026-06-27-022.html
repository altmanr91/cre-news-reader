Lender can’t sidestep foreclosure at stalled Garment District condo project, judge rules

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 26 Jun 2026 19:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

A New York State Supreme Court judge has ruled that Shanghai Commercial Bank must pursue a traditional foreclosure process to take control of a stalled Garment District condo conversion project, reversing an earlier decision that would have allowed the bank to acquire the property via a deed-in-lieu of foreclosure. The dispute involves a $60.6 million loan provided by the bank to an entity tied to the Chen family, who defaulted on the loan in 2022. The ruling means that the current note buyers, Empire Capital Holdings and Hakimian Organization, who acquired the distressed debt for approximately $15 million, will also need to complete a foreclosure to gain ownership.
