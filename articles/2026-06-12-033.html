Inside the deal: How R2 completed Gold Coast condo deconversion after Fifield tower proposal got shot down twice

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Chicago — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 16:39:54 +0000

SUMMARY

R2 Companies has successfully completed the deconversion of a 20-unit condominium building at 127 Maple Street in Chicago's Gold Coast, acquiring all units for $4.7 million after four years of negotiations. The firm plans to renovate and rent out the units, with potential future redevelopment of the accompanying 20-spot parking lot. This acquisition follows a failed attempt by Fifield Companies to develop a high-rise apartment tower on the site, highlighting the challenges and opportunities in Chicago's tight multifamily market and the growing trend of condo deconversions.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Address: 127 Maple Street, Chicago, IL

Units: 20

Sale Price: $4.7M

Year Built: 1886

Notable Features: Prime Gold Coast location with flexible zoning and a 20-spot parking lot.

Project Notes: The acquisition involved a multi-year negotiation process to buy out individual condo owners, some of whom were involved in legal disputes. The property was previously difficult to sell individually due to disrepair and a high percentage of rental units, limiting mortgage underwriting.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: R2 Companies — Zack Cupkovic, Partner; Matt Garrison, CEO

SELLER BROKER: Triton Real Estate — Nicole Linares, Broker

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Fifield Companies

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Chicago's multifamily market is experiencing a historic low in new development, with new apartments completed over the past 12 months amounting to just 0.8% of existing inventory. This scarcity is driving up leases and fueling rapid rent growth, with the city's average rent reaching $1,956 per month at the end of 2025 (CoStar data). The condo deconversion market is gaining traction as developers seek opportunities to create rental units from older properties.
