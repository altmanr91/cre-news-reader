Cloudburst Data Centers secures incentives for $14B project between Austin and San Antonio

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Texas — Fri, 01 May 2026 20:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Cloudburst Data Centers secured a significant tax abatement deal from Guadalupe County commissioners for a planned $14.5 billion data center campus in Central Texas, located within the Austin-San Antonio corridor. The project, spanning 220 acres across Guadalupe and Hays counties, will feature 10 to 12 buildings ranging from 70,000 to 250,000 square feet each. This incentive package, which includes an estimated $500 million tax abatement and a 10-year development agreement, is crucial for one of the region's largest industrial developments, with the first phase of four buildings targeted for completion in 2027.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Communities in Texas have recently pushed back on new data center developments due to concerns over water and power usage. Hays County tabled a proposed moratorium on data center development, and the city of San Marcos denied a $1.5 billion data center project. Brazoria County commissioners rejected a tax abatement for Nightpeak Energy's proposed $3.5 billion Old Ocean Data Center campus.
