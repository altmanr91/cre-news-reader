Jane Kim leads CA insurance chief race, boasts $30K in real estate donations

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 20:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Political & Crime News

Jane Kim, a former San Francisco Board of Supervisors member, is leading the primary race for California's state insurance commissioner, a position gaining prominence due to recent wildfire-related insurance shortfalls. Kim is proposing a single-payer, state-backed disaster insurance program, inspired by New Zealand's model, where homeowners would pay a levy to the state via their private insurers for disaster coverage. The state would then invest these funds in the bond market, using earnings to finance fire safety initiatives. This approach aims to stabilize insurance costs for homeowners and prevent insurers from exiting the California market or raising premiums due to increased wildfire risk.
