Chicago’s industrial market: A calmer sector after so many boom years?

REJournals — Tue, 12 May 2026 10:03:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Industrial Report

The Chicago industrial market is stabilizing after a period of rapid growth, with steady demand for warehouse and distribution space, according to Transwestern's Q1 2026 report. While leasing activity, totaling 16.3 million square feet, remains below pandemic-era highs, it shows a significant year-over-year increase. Market fundamentals are improving, evidenced by a drop in direct industrial vacancy to 4.5% and declining sublease availability. Construction has slowed, with only 1.9 million square feet delivered in Q1, the lowest in a decade, and over half of the current 17.2 million square feet under construction is preleased. Asking rents continue to grow, albeit at a more moderate pace, reaching an average of $9.58 per square foot triple net.

Strongest Absorption Submarket: I-55/I-80 Southwest Corridors with nearly 5 million square feet

Largest Negative Absorption Submarket: South Suburban with negative 786,200 square feet

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Direct leasing activity in Chicago's industrial market increased 10.3% from Q4 2025 and 40.7% year-over-year in Q1 2026. The direct industrial vacancy rate fell 30 basis points to 4.5% in Q1 2026. Total availability declined to 8.6%, down 10 basis points from the previous quarter. Sublease availability has retreated from its 10-year high in Q3 2023. Asking rents rose 4.5% year-over-year to an average of $9.58 per square foot triple net.
