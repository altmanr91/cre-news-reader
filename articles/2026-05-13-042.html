Apartment Construction Starts Plummet To 15-Year Low

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 12 May 2026 15:06:17 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / Construction Report

Multifamily construction starts in the U.S. have fallen to a 15-year low, with only 55,000 units breaking ground in Q1 2026, a volume not seen since 2011. This decline is attributed to higher development and financing costs, coupled with a slowdown in rent growth. Despite a record wave of office-to-residential conversions, the overall pipeline of units under construction has decreased by 50% from its 2023 peak. While some Sun Belt markets like Miami, Charlotte, and Tampa show strong development relative to their existing inventory, many metros are still absorbing the high volume of units delivered in 2024.
