Affordable housing project rises from SiliconSage wreckage in Fremont

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Thu, 21 May 2026 18:36:58 +0000

SUMMARY

A long-stalled development site in Fremont, previously associated with the collapse of SiliconSage Builders, has been acquired by USA Properties Fund and Riverside Charitable Corporation for $11.5 million. The developers plan to construct Centerville Plaza Apartments, a 290-unit fully affordable housing complex with ground-floor retail. The project will be built in two phases, with construction on the first phase expected to begin in June, following the anticipated closing of construction financing. The project is notable for its use of 4 percent low-income housing tax credits and a significant boost from the Housing Trust of Silicon Valley for its second phase.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Property Name: Centerville Plaza Apartments

Units: 290

Sale Price: $11.5M

Site Area: 3 acres

Notable Features: Fully affordable housing complex with ground-floor retail.

Project Notes: The 2.9-acre site was previously approved for a mixed-use project by SiliconSage Builders in 2019, but the developer later imploded due to fraud allegations and filed for bankruptcy. Los Angeles-based Bolour Associates acquired the property through foreclosure. The project is being financed with 4 percent low-income housing tax credits, and the second phase will receive a $10.3 million boost from the Housing Trust of Silicon Valley. Phase 1 includes 140 apartments and 1,600 SF of retail; Phase 2 includes 150 apartments and 3,500 SF of retail.
