Peruvian developer expands Miami condo pipeline with 62-story tower

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Miami — Wed, 03 Jun 2026 18:01:11 +0000

SUMMARY

Peruvian developer Grupo T&C, led by William Ticona, is planning a 62-story mixed-use tower named 13th Edge near Miami's Brickell district. The project will feature 400 apartments and 400 hotel rooms, replacing a 36-unit condo building that Grupo T&C acquired last year for $27.6 million. This development marks a significant expansion of the developer's real estate activities in South Florida.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: mixed-use

Property Name: 13th Edge

Address: 237 Southwest 13th Street, Miami, FL

Size: 648,700 SF

Units: 400

Keys: 400

Notable Features: Proposed 62-story tower with 234 parking spaces, a pool, bar, outdoor lounge for hotel guests, and a pool and rooftop yoga lawn for residents. Hotel rooms range from 400-900 SF, apartments from 400-860 SF.

Project Notes: The site previously held El Vedado, a 36-unit condo built in 1972, which will be demolished. The developer acquired the site for $27.6 million last year. A pre-application has been filed with Miami-Dade County officials.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Grupo T&C — William Ticona

ARCHITECT: Kobi Karp Architecture

SELLER: Blue Palm Capital

SPONSOR PIPELINE

Edgewater residential development site: Acquired in 2024 for $15.1 million, zoned for a 36-story tower with 124 units or 248 hotel rooms.

Edge House Miami: 57-story, 608-unit luxury resort-style tower in Edgewater, approved for short-term rentals and slated for 2028 completion.
