Chicago’s industrial market remains a steady bet post pandemic

LEASE

Chicago Real Estate News - The Real Deal — Thu, 30 Apr 2026 21:48:48 +0000

SUMMARY

Chicago's industrial market has demonstrated resilience and a healthy supply-demand balance in the first quarter of the year, according to NAI Hiffman research. Unlike other sectors facing oversupply or undersupply issues, the industrial market has seen vacancy rates improve to 5.9 percent, even as new construction balances between speculative and built-to-suit projects. Leasing activity for existing properties remains robust, with a notable 11.9 million square feet leased in the first quarter, indicating a transition towards a more sustainable growth cycle.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Regional industrial vacancy improved by 10 basis points quarter-over-quarter to 5.9 percent in Q1. In Q1 2020, pre-pandemic, regional vacancy was just over 6 percent. In Q1, 53 percent of industrial developments were speculative and 47 percent were built-to-suit, compared to Q2 2020 when approximately 90 percent of new developments were speculative. New leases totaled 11.9 million square feet in Q1, up from 10.9 million square feet in Q1 2025.
