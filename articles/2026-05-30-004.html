PNC Bank Refis West Village Apartment Building With $404M Loan

REFINANCE

Commercial Observer — Fri, 29 May 2026 15:26:11 +0000

SUMMARY

Rockrose Development affiliates have secured $404 million in agency debt from PNC Bank, which will be purchased by Freddie Mac, to refinance The Archive, a 472-unit luxury apartment building in Manhattan's West Village. The financing highlights the continued strength of the New York City multifamily market and the enduring appeal of this landmarked property, which Rockrose originally converted into apartments in 1988.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Property Name: The Archive

Address: 666 Greenwich Street, New York City, NY

Units: 472

Loan Amount: $404.0M

Loan Per Unit: $855,932/unit (calculated)

Year Built: 1988

Notable Features: Landmarked luxury apartment building, formerly a U.S. Appraiser's warehouse, located a half mile from New York University.

Project Notes: The property was designated as a New York City landmark in 1966. Rockrose signed an eight-year retail lease for 25,000 square feet on the ground floor in early 2024.
