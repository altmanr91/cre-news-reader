Not Your Parents’ Lecture Hall

Connect CRE — Fri, 12 Jun 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article, drawing on insights from JLL, argues that traditional university lecture halls are outdated and ineffective for modern learning styles. The author contends that educational institutions should transform these static spaces into multi-functional environments, much like contemporary workplaces have evolved from 'cube farms' to adaptable settings. By offering diverse zones for individual work, collaboration, and presentations, universities can enhance student engagement and accommodate various pedagogical methods, including neuro-inclusive design principles. The article concludes that flexible, reconfigurable spaces will be utilized more consistently throughout the day, supporting a broader range of learning needs.
