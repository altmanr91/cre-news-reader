CRE Capital Flows Unevenly in a K-Shaped Economy

Connect CRE — Fri, 29 May 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article analyzes the persistent K-shaped economic recovery, five years after its emergence during the COVID-19 recession, and its uneven impact on commercial real estate capital flows. The author argues that while capital is available, it is highly selective, favoring strong sponsors and high-quality assets in top-performing markets. This divergence is evident across sectors, with luxury retail and top-tier multifamily performing well, while lower- and middle-market retail and renter-by-necessity multifamily struggle with oversupply and weaker fundamentals. Lenders are prioritizing assets with clear business plans and strong sponsorship, leading to creative deal structures that price risk accordingly.
