It’s official: New York City is getting a rent freeze

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 25 Jun 2026 23:51:53 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

New York City's Rent Guidelines Board has voted to freeze rents on rent-stabilized apartments for both one-year and two-year leases, a decision that fulfills a campaign promise by Mayor Zohran Mamdani and marks a significant victory for tenant advocates. This unprecedented freeze on two-year leases has drawn strong criticism from landlord groups, who argue it will lead to reduced investment in maintenance and repairs, potentially accelerating the deterioration of the city's housing stock. Legal challenges from landlord organizations are anticipated, with some board members and industry representatives decrying the process as politically motivated rather than fact-based.
