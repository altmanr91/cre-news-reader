Construction Costs Rise 6.2% Through April

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 13 May 2026 11:23:21 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / Construction Costs

Construction input prices in the U.S. have risen 6.2% since January, with April seeing a 1.7% increase over March, according to an analysis by Associated Builders and Contractors based on Bureau of Labor Statistics data. This surge, driven by inflation, tariffs, and geopolitical factors, marks a more significant increase in the first four months of 2026 than in the preceding three years combined. Energy costs, particularly crude petroleum, natural gas, and unprocessed energy materials, are the primary drivers of these price hikes, alongside notable increases in softwood lumber and steel products.
