How an alleged California fraudster seized a regional bank and swindled a seasoned landlord

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 18 Jun 2026 21:57:13 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Federal agents have arrested Mahender Makhijani for allegedly defrauding Western Alliance Bancorp out of $100 million through a scheme involving real estate plays and manipulated title policies. Veteran landlord Gerald Marcil, who owns thousands of multifamily units in Southern California, claims he was duped by Makhijani into guaranteeing loans and signing documents without full disclosure, leading to significant debt and legal bills. Marcil has filed two lawsuits alleging fraud and elder abuse, detailing how Makhijani secretly placed junior liens on properties, pocketed sale proceeds, and diverted funds from capital calls and rental income. The alleged scheme also involved Makhijani's control over Nano Banc, which Marcil claims was used to manipulate him into signing a $19.2 million loan and an indemnity agreement, ultimately threatening foreclosure on his portfolio.
