NY Dirt: Can’t pay, won’t pay

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 24 Jun 2026 11:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article explores the reasons behind declining rent collections in New York's affordable housing sector, a trend that began during the pandemic and continues to impact operators' financial stability. While no single cause has been identified, theories include tenants facing increased economic hardship, a shift in tenant attitudes towards rent payments influenced by pandemic-era assistance and eviction moratoriums, and city policies that inadvertently incentivize tenants to fall deeply into arrears before receiving financial aid. The variability in collections across similar properties suggests complex social dynamics at play within the rental system.
