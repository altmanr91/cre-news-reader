Claudio Soifer’s endless Park Slope saga faces another setback

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 16:20:22 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Hanover Capital has filed a lawsuit against investor Claudio Soifer, alleging he backed out of a $21.7 million mortgage agreement for a planned 135-unit rental development at 167 Fourth Avenue in Park Slope, Brooklyn. Hanover Capital is seeking $218,000 in damages, plus interest and legal fees, claiming it expended significant resources on the prospective financing. The site, acquired by Soifer in 2015, has been vacant since 2019, with development plans shifting from condominiums to the current rental project, which has an estimated total cost of $140 million.
