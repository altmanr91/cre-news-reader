Housing Notes: Wall Street is adding more finance jobs to NYC than anywhere else

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 19:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article argues that the narrative of New York City's decline is unfounded, presenting data to show the city's economic resilience and evolution. The author highlights that NYC has gained the most finance jobs nationally since 2019, significantly outpacing Miami, and emphasizes the substantial growth of the tech sector, which now employs more private-sector workers than Wall Street. The piece also notes a strong return-to-office trend, with Manhattan office visitation exceeding pre-pandemic levels, driven by both finance and tech sectors. This diversification and job growth, particularly among younger, high-wage earners, is expected to sustain strong demand in NYC's housing market.
