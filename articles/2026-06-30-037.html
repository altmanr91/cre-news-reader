Cinemark founder sells Preston Hollow mansion

SALE

The Real Deal — Texas — Mon, 29 Jun 2026 16:45:00 +0000

SUMMARY

The Preston Hollow mansion of Cinemark founder Lee Roy Mitchell and his wife Tandy was sold to the Kevin and Amanda Moore Living Trust. The 14,300-square-foot luxury residence, situated on 3.4 acres, was originally listed for $22 million in September 2025 before its price was reduced to $18 million in January. The final sale price was not disclosed, but the asking price suggests it could be one of Dallas-Fort Worth's top residential transactions of 2026.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: single family

Address: 9769 Audubon Place, Dallas, TX

Size: 14,300 SF

Year Built: 1995

Site Area: 3.4 acres

Notable Features: The six-bedroom, 12-bathroom home includes nine fireplaces, a 750-bottle wine cellar, and a 20-seat Art Deco-style media room.

Project Notes: The asking price at the time of sale was $18 million, or $1,255 per square foot.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

SELLER: Individual — Lee Roy Mitchell; Tandy Mitchell

BUYER: Kevin and Amanda Moore Living Trust

ARCHITECT: Robbie Fusch
