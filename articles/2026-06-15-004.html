New York City’s 485-x vs. 467-m: Two Very Different Development Incentives

Commercial Observer — Mon, 15 Jun 2026 12:00:13 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article contrasts two New York City development incentives, 485-x and 467-m, highlighting their differing impacts on housing production. The author argues that 485-x, intended to promote affordable housing, is failing due to stringent construction wage requirements that make large-scale projects unfeasible for most developers. In contrast, 467-m, which facilitates office-to-residential conversions, is successfully adding new housing units. The article cites TF Cornerstone as a rare developer undertaking large 485-x projects, but even they acknowledge reduced profitability, indicating the policy's unsustainability for the broader market.

DATA POINTS

485-x minimum construction wage (100+ units): $40 per hour

485-x maximum construction wage (150+ units): $72.45 per hour

Estimated increase in construction wages due to 485-x (100+ units): 5-10%

Estimated increase in construction wages due to 485-x (150+ units): 15-20%

Estimated yield reduction for TF Cornerstone due to 485-x: 25-50 basis points

Estimated IRR reduction for TF Cornerstone due to 485-x: a few hundred basis points
