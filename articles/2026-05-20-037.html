ULI Shuffles Offices For Global HQ, Washington Chapter

LEASE

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 19 May 2026 11:02:55 -0400

SUMMARY

The Urban Land Institute (ULI) has relocated its global headquarters to 2101 L St. NW in Washington, D.C., securing 26,000 square feet. This move, from its previous 33,000-square-foot space at 2001 L St. NW, aims to foster team collaboration and strengthen ULI's mission. Concurrently, ULI Washington, the local chapter, has moved into an 800-square-foot sublease within Hickok Cole's headquarters in the NoMa submarket, a strategic shift to decentralize its regional events.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: office

Address: 2101 L St. NW, Washington, D.C.

Size: 26,000 SF

Year Built: 1970

Notable Features: Building renovated in 2007, designed for natural light, accessibility, and a balance of shared and focused workspaces.

Project Notes: ULI Washington's 800 SF sublease is at Hickok Cole's headquarters, a former 1930s-era printing press warehouse.
