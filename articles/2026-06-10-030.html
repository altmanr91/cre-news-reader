As Obama Presidential Center opens, South Side residents fear Airbnb boom

The Real Deal — Chicago — Tue, 09 Jun 2026 19:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The opening of the Obama Presidential Center on Chicago's South Side has led to a significant increase in short-term rental licenses in the adjacent 20th Ward, contrasting with a city-wide decline. While the Obama Foundation has supported this growth, residents in historically affordable areas like Woodlawn fear displacement due to rising housing costs. Housing advocates and researchers argue that an influx of short-term rentals can inflate rents and home prices, with one study suggesting a direct correlation. Airbnb representatives, however, contend that the impact on affordability is not significant and that short-term rentals provide substantial income for local hosts.

DATA POINTS

Chicago-wide short-term rental license drop: 38%

20th Ward short-term rental license increase: 46%

Impact of 1% increase in Airbnb listings on rents (2019 study): 0.018% increase

Typical Airbnb host earnings in South Side Chicago (2025): $20,000

Projected annual visitors to Obama Presidential Center: 700,000
