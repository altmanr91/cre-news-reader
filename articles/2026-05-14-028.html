Inside the year-long Chicago standoff that ended in Zillow’s antitrust suit

The Real Deal — Chicago — Wed, 13 May 2026 23:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Zillow has filed an antitrust lawsuit against Midwest Real Estate Data (MRED), a Chicago-area MLS, and Compass, alleging that the two companies colluded to restrict Zillow's access to listing data. The lawsuit claims that MRED and Compass leveraged their market power to pressure Zillow into displaying listings that were initially marketed off-platform, threatening to cut off Zillow's access to a significant portion of Northern Illinois real estate listings. This legal action is the culmination of a year-long dispute over listing data control in Chicago, with Zillow asserting that rule changes and coordinated actions by MRED and Compass were designed to undermine its Listing Access Standards. The outcome of this case could significantly impact competitive strategies and alliances between brokerages and MLSs as technology continues to reshape the real estate industry.
