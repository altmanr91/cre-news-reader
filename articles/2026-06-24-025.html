Stonerock loses downtown office buildings in $66M foreclosure

FORECLOSURE

The Real Deal — Miami — Tue, 23 Jun 2026 21:36:57 +0000

SUMMARY

Two downtown Miami office buildings, previously acquired by Stonerock Capital in 2022 with a mortgage from Ardent Companies, are now facing foreclosure. The foreclosure judgment, totaling $65.7 million, was issued after Stonerock Capital allegedly defaulted on loan payments and failed to repay the mortgage upon maturity, as well as neglecting property tax payments. The properties are scheduled for an online auction in July.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: office

Size: 322,800 SF

Loan Amount: $65.7M

Notable Features: The 200 Southeast First Street building includes a data center and ground-floor retail. The 44 West Flagler Street building is anchored by a bank.

Project Notes: The original mortgage of $58.3 million was provided by Ardent Companies in 2022 for Stonerock Capital's acquisition of the buildings for $56.7 million. Triple Double Real Estate was a partner in the initial acquisition but left the venture in 2023. The foreclosure complaint was filed in 2025, alleging default on a $49.4 million mortgage and missed property tax payments for 2023.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Miami office leasing reached 918,000 square feet in the first quarter, with the tech industry accounting for 37% and banking, finance, insurance, and real estate accounting for 29%. Downtown Miami had a 19% direct vacancy, which is among the highest of the city's submarkets.
