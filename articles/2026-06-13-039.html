Native American tribe grows Seattle-area holdings with $53M apartment buy

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Seattle — Fri, 12 Jun 2026 14:00:02 +0000

SUMMARY

The Spokane Tribe of Indians, through its Spokane Indian Housing Authority, has acquired the 200-unit Waterford Apartments in Everett, WA, for $53.3 million. This transaction marks the largest multifamily sale in Everett this year and continues the housing authority's strategy of expanding its real estate holdings in the Seattle metropolitan area. The property, built in 1989 and renovated in 2014, features a strong amenity package. The acquisition aligns with the buyer's recent trend of purchasing multifamily assets in the region, often with plans to convert market-rate units into income-restricted affordable housing.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Everett is experiencing strong short- and long-term rent growth due to limited new multifamily deliveries and steady renter demand. Everett's occupancy rate was 94.9% in Q1, compared to Seattle's 94.2%. While Seattle saw 524 new multifamily units completed in Q1, Everett had zero.
