Wall Street crowds into niche corner of real estate private credit

The Real Deal — National — Mon, 18 May 2026 14:05:26 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Wall Street firms are increasingly launching non-traded mortgage REITs to tap into the growing demand for private credit in real estate. These semi-liquid vehicles, which raise capital from wealthy individual investors to finance real estate loans, mirror structures common in corporate private credit. The rapid emergence of these REITs, with seven of the top ten in fundraising launched in the past two years, indicates a burgeoning and competitive market. This shift is driven by an improving real estate picture and potential capital reallocation from corporate private credit, positioning mortgage REITs favorably as interest rates fluctuate.
