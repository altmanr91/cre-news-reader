Pompano Beach mansion sells for record $14M as high-end development takes hold

SALE

The Real Deal — Miami — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 19:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

An oceanfront mansion in Pompano Beach, FL, sold for a record-setting $13.8 million, marking the highest single-family home sale in the city's history. The 7,760-square-foot property was acquired by Seattle developer Harold Griffith and his wife, Joan Griffith, from an affiliate of developer Leathem Stearn. This transaction highlights Pompano Beach's emergence as a luxury destination, attracting high-end development and a new wave of wealth due to its relative affordability compared to nearby markets.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The previous record for a single-family home sale in Pompano Beach was $10 million for a property at 2593 Southeast Eighth Street. The prior record-holder, sold in 2023, is now back on the market for $15 million. Pompano Beach has invested millions in infrastructure upgrades and public space revitalization.
