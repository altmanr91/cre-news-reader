“Why wouldn’t you come to Austin?” Dutch architecture firm chooses Austin for first U.S. office

The Real Deal — Texas — Mon, 11 May 2026 19:58:09 +0000

SUMMARY

Lease

Dutch architecture firm UNS has selected Austin, TX, for its inaugural U.S. office, citing the city's dynamic environment and growth potential. The firm, which previously collaborated with Austin on a light rail system, is currently operating from a co-working space downtown and plans to secure a larger permanent office in the downtown area or near Lady Bird Lake as it expands. This move comes despite a broader trend of cooling corporate relocation enthusiasm in Austin, with the city's office market experiencing increased sublease availability.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Austin's office market is experiencing a glut of sublease space due to tech companies retracting large office footprints. Austin's corporate relocation stream has slowed, prompting Mayor Kirk Watson to outline a plan to target sectors like AI and offer incentives to attract corporate tenants.
