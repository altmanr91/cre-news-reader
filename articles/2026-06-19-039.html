Housing Affordability Through RVs and Travel Trailers

Connect CRE — Fri, 19 Jun 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article explores the concept of recreational vehicles (RVs), travel trailers, and tiny homes as a potential solution to the housing affordability crisis. It highlights the increasing number of people living in RVs full-time, often due to economic necessity, but notes that current U.S. Building Codes classify RVs as vehicles, not year-round housing. The piece discusses existing RV communities in states with more lenient zoning and advocates for regulatory changes, such as removing the "no year-round" sticker on RVs and updating building codes to create standalone standards for tiny homes, to facilitate their use as permanent dwellings.
