Housing Notes: The whining meant it wasn’t about the pied-à-terre tax

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 08 May 2026 18:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Editorial

This opinion column argues that the real estate industry's backlash against New York City's proposed pied-à-terre tax was not primarily about the tax itself, but rather a reaction to a specific social media video by the mayor. The author suggests that the criticism, particularly from Ken Griffin, stemmed from being singled out for his record-setting residential purchase, which touched a nerve amidst a K-shaped economic recovery. The author also questions Griffin's stated reasons for potentially withdrawing from a major office project, noting inconsistencies in his arguments regarding safety and business environment compared to his actual investment patterns and the relative talent pools in New York City and Miami.
