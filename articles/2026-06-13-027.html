Todd Boehley’s Eldridge in talks to lease at OKO, Cain’s 830 Brickell

LEASE

The Real Deal — Miami — Fri, 12 Jun 2026 13:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Eldridge Industries, founded by Todd Boehly, is reportedly in negotiations to lease approximately 8,000 square feet at the 830 Brickell office tower in Miami. This potential move would see the investment firm relocate from its current nearby Miami office. The 57-story tower, developed by OKO Group and Cain, was completed in 2024 and has achieved the highest office rents in Miami-Dade County, benefiting from strong demand driven by an influx of out-of-state firms and favorable tax policies in Florida.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Miami-Dade County office rents have reached a high of $250 per square foot, gross, at 830 Brickell. South Florida is experiencing increased demand for office space due to an influx of out-of-state firms and migration from states with higher taxes, such as California and New York. Florida has no personal income tax, and a ballot question to increase the homestead exemption for property taxes is pending for the November election.
