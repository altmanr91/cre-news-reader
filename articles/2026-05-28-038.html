CA condo developers may see litigation relief as reform bill breezes through Assembly

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Wed, 27 May 2026 22:06:40 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

California lawmakers are advancing AB 1903, a bill aimed at reforming the state's condo defect liability law, which has been blamed for a significant decline in condo construction since 2003. The proposed legislation, which passed the State Assembly unanimously, would grant developers and general contractors a right to repair defects before facing lawsuits and offers a path to liability release with third-party quality control certification. This reform seeks to alleviate the high litigation and insurance costs that have stifled condo development, potentially boosting homeownership opportunities in a state with high housing costs.
