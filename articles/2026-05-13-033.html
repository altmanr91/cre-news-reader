Oakland leads nation in home value loss as median reaches lowest point in a decade

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Tue, 12 May 2026 19:30:30 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Housing Report

Oakland, CA, is experiencing the steepest decline in home values among major U.S. cities, with the typical home value falling to approximately $716,000 in March, an 11.4% year-over-year decrease. This downturn, which began after a mortgage rate peak in 2022, has pushed values 28.1% below 2019 levels, reaching a decade-low. The city's housing market struggles are attributed to broader urban challenges, including crime concerns, a less vibrant downtown, and a shift towards suburban living, contrasting with San Francisco's AI-driven housing boom.
