Paxton wins real estate industry-dividing Texas Republican primary runoff

The Real Deal — Texas — Wed, 27 May 2026 17:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Political & Crime News

Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton secured a decisive victory over incumbent Sen. John Cornyn in the Republican primary runoff for the U.S. Senate, a race that became the most expensive in Texas history due to its perceived significance for the future direction of the Republican party. The real estate industry's endorsements were notably split, with prominent Dallas and Houston developers backing Cornyn, while other Texas-based developers supported Paxton. The outcome is seen as a bellwether for the GOP's future, attracting significant out-of-state real estate industry investment, particularly from South Florida.

DATA POINTS

Paxton's runoff vote share: nearly 64 percent

Cornyn's March primary vote share: roughly 42 percent

Paxton's March primary vote share: roughly 40 percent

Paxton's South Florida donations (April): 14

Cornyn's South Florida donations (April): 43
