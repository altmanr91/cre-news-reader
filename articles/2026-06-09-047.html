OceanFirst Purges $1.4B In Rent-Stabilized Multifamily Loans Following Flushing Financial Merger

SALE

Bisnow News Feed — Mon, 08 Jun 2026 12:01:15 -0400

SUMMARY

OceanFirst Financial Corp. is divesting $1.4 billion in multifamily loans, primarily from Flushing Financial Corp.'s portfolio, shortly after completing their merger. This strategic move aims to significantly reduce OceanFirst's exposure to rent-regulated properties in New York City, a segment that has faced challenges since the 2019 Housing Stability and Tenant Protection Act. The bank had anticipated substantial losses on this portfolio, underwriting the deal with loss assumptions four times higher than Flushing's existing reserves and devaluing the rent-regulated apartment loan portfolio by over 10%. The sale is expected to conclude by the end of Q2.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: multifamily

Sale Price: $1.40B

Project Notes: The sale represents the bulk of Flushing Financial Corp.'s multifamily loan portfolio, with an average loan size of $1.3M. Of these loans, 48% were tied to fully regulated buildings, 35% were between 50% and 99% regulated, and 17% were less than half regulated. The weighted average loan-to-value ratio at origination was 55%, and the weighted average debt coverage ratio was 1.7x.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

SELLER: OceanFirst Financial Corp.

OWNER: Flushing Financial Corp.
