Texas built-to-rent investors remain wary of federal housing bill

The Real Deal — Texas — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 22:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

A federal housing bill aimed at improving affordability and curbing institutional investment in single-family homes has significantly impacted the built-to-rent (BTR) development pipeline in Texas. While recent revisions to the 21st Century Renewing Opportunity in the American Dream (ROAD) to Housing Act, which removed a provision requiring large investors to sell BTR homes to individuals within seven years, are seen as a positive step, investors remain hesitant. Developers indicate that capital deployment for new BTR projects is largely frozen, awaiting final legislative approval and clarity from the White House. This cautious approach is particularly evident in Texas, a leading state for BTR construction, where several projects with substantial investment have stalled.
