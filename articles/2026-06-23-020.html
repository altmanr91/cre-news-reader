Michelin star leasing: Real estate realities behind South Florida’s dining boom

The Real Deal — Miami — Mon, 22 Jun 2026 21:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

South Florida's restaurant scene is experiencing a wave of closures, particularly among Michelin-starred establishments, due to escalating rental rates and razor-thin profit margins. Restaurateurs like Alvaro Perez Miranda emphasize the importance of landlords understanding the value a restaurant brings and the strategic advantage of operating in emerging neighborhoods with more affordable rents. Industry experts note that average restaurant net profit margins are around 3%, with rent ideally not exceeding 10% of projected sales. The market has seen restaurant rents in South Florida surge from approximately $60 per square foot to over $100 per square foot, with premier locations exceeding $200 per square foot, making it challenging for many operators to sustain their businesses.
