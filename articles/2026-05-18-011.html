Why this West Village “townhouse” was a hard sell

SALE

The Real Deal — New York — Sun, 17 May 2026 17:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

A unique tenancy-in-common townhouse unit in New York City's West Village, located at 197 West Houston Street, finally sold for $2.1 million after a year on the market. The property, which was listed at a significant discount to area averages, presented challenges due to its shared ownership structure with an upstairs unit, making traditional mortgage financing difficult and requiring a cash buyer. The sale highlights the complexities of co-ownership arrangements, especially those established decades ago, and the increasing interest in shared property ownership as a solution to high housing costs in competitive markets.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: single family

Address: 197 West Houston Street, New York City, NY

Sale Price: $2.1M

Notable Features: Features real oak floors, a working wood-burning fireplace, and a hidden back garden.

Project Notes: The property is a two-family home with a tenancy-in-common deed, where two parties have 50-50 ownership. The downstairs unit was sold, while the upstairs unit remains occupied by the original co-owner. The sellers previously acquired their unit in 2017 for $2 million and invested approximately $500,000 in renovations.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The cost of buying in New York City is expensive, leading to increased interest in shared property ownership arrangements. Some brokers are developing a niche in helping friends buy townhouses together, often utilizing state attorney general's office routes to convert buildings into condos for easier future sales.
