World Cup Demand For Houston Hotels Falling Short Despite Double-Digit Increases In Bookings

Bisnow News Feed — Mon, 11 May 2026 14:51:54 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / Hospitality Report

A month before the 2026 FIFA World Cup, Houston's hotel demand is falling short of initial expectations, with approximately 70% of hotels reporting booking paces below forecasts, though still consistent with typical June and July levels. While overall bookings for June and July are up 9.1% and 11% respectively compared to last year, the Downtown Houston submarket shows a more significant increase of 38% for June and 63% for July, driven by its proximity to event venues. Hoteliers note that while current bookings are an improvement over the previous year, they do not reflect the substantial spike anticipated for a global event of this magnitude. Across all U.S. host cities, 80% of hotels report bookings below initial forecasts, with visa barriers and geopolitical concerns cited as primary factors impacting international travel demand.

DATA POINTS

Houston hotels reporting bookings below World Cup expectations: 70%

June bookings increase (Houston): 9.1%

July bookings increase (Houston): 11%

July bookings increase (Downtown Houston): 63%

U.S. international travelers short of expectations last year: 11 million
