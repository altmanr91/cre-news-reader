GSA hits another hurdle in push to shrink federal real estate

The Real Deal — National — Fri, 26 Jun 2026 17:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The General Services Administration (GSA) is facing challenges in its efforts to downsize the federal real estate portfolio, with the Wilbur J. Cohen Federal Building in Washington, D.C., potentially being removed from the disposition list. A senior GSA official cited the prohibitive cost of separating the building's infrastructure from the adjacent Mary E. Switzer Memorial Federal Building as a major hurdle. Despite a congressional directive to sell the property, the GSA is considering reinvesting in the Cohen Building to improve its utilization, highlighting the broader issue of deferred maintenance across the federal portfolio that impedes sales.

DATA POINTS

Federal real estate repair costs: $25.8 billion

Number of 'non-core' assets on rescinded disposition list: 443

Total square footage of 'non-core' assets on rescinded disposition list: nearly 80 million square feet
