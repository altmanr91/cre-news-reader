$1.34B Settlement Awarded in SoCal Property Fraud Case

Commercial Observer — Tue, 09 Jun 2026 21:55:56 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

A Southern California real estate investor, Mohammad Honarkar and his company 4G Wireless, has been awarded a $1.34 billion arbitration settlement in a property fraud case. The award, one of the largest of its kind, stems from a joint venture formed in 2021 with Mahender Makhijani and Continuum Analytics, who were found liable for fraud, breach of contract, and forcible entry and detainer. The respondents allegedly failed to make a promised $30 million equity contribution and incurred significant encumbrances on Honarkar's commercial real estate portfolio, leading to foreclosures and receiverships.
