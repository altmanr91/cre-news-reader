Canadian firm buys Lincoln Park self storage and office building for $31M

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Chicago — Mon, 22 Jun 2026 21:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Avenue Living Asset Management, a Canadian real estate investment firm, has acquired a self-storage and office building in Chicago's Lincoln Park neighborhood for $30.5 million. The seller, LSC Development, had successfully repositioned the property after purchasing it out of foreclosure in 2012, converting a significant portion into a storage facility while retaining office and retail space. This transaction highlights the continued investor interest in the highly sought-after Lincoln Park market.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: self storage / office

Address: 1030 West North Avenue, Chicago, IL

Sale Price: $30.5M

Project Notes: The seller, LSC Development, acquired the property out of foreclosure in 2012 and converted part of it into a storage facility after an electronics store tenant vacated in 2009.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: Avenue Living Asset Management

SELLER: LSC Development

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The per-unit price for mid-sized apartment complexes on Chicago's North Side has been trending upward past $500,000 per unit over the past year. Approximately 70% of homes sold in Lincoln Park earlier this spring went over their asking price, with an average premium of more than $60,000.
