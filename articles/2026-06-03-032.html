Abandoned hospital in Oak Cliff to get $60M makeover

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Texas — Tue, 02 Jun 2026 21:44:55 +0000

SUMMARY

Options Real Estate has acquired a 14-acre former hospital site in Dallas's Oak Cliff neighborhood from the City of Dallas for approximately $6.9 million. The developer plans a $60 million redevelopment to transform the long-vacant property into a mixed-use "village" featuring townhomes, apartments, and retail space. This project aims to revitalize a historically disadvantaged area, with the developer emphasizing community involvement and leveraging existing infrastructure.

Notable Features: Site is adjacent to Kiest Park, a library, and an elementary school; plans include green spaces and potential reuse of existing water lines and elevators.

Project Notes: The City of Dallas previously acquired the property for $6.5 million in 2022. The hospital closed in 2014. The developer has a 90-day due diligence period and must close the deal by April 30, 2027.
