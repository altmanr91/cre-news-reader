Housing Notes: With an 80% Manhattan market share, what happens if Compass quits REBNY?

The Real Deal — National — Tue, 09 Jun 2026 16:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article, authored by Jonathan Miller, analyzes the antitrust implications of the Compass-Anywhere merger, particularly focusing on the Federal Trade Commission's (FTC) merger guidelines and the subsequent investigation by the New York State Attorney General. Miller argues that the merger, despite avoiding extensive federal scrutiny, exhibits market share concentrations in several key submarkets that exceed the FTC's presumptively illegal thresholds. He highlights concerns that such dominance could lead to higher commissions, limited listing access, and conflicts of interest for consumers, emphasizing the importance of regulatory oversight in maintaining a competitive real estate environment.
