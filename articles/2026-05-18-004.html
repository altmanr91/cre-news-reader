Retail Leasing, Development Activity ‘Slow and Steady’ in Baltimore Market

REBusinessOnline — Mon, 18 May 2026 11:52:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The Baltimore retail market is experiencing a "slow and steady" pace in leasing and new development due to elevated interest rates, higher construction costs, and economic uncertainties, mirroring national trends. Despite these challenges, the market remains optimistic for a rebound in the latter half of 2026, driven by strong local fundamentals and consumer resilience, particularly in the food-and-beverage sector. Permitting delays in Baltimore City and surrounding counties continue to hinder tenant build-out processes, causing frustration and potentially derailing transactions. However, some projects are making positive strides, and the overall 4% retail vacancy rate is contributing to continued rental growth.
