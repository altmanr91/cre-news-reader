Meet Poorvesh Thakkar, the Dallas owner Safehold is trying to eject from a Manhattan tower

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 22 May 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Safehold, the ground lessor of the 135 West 50th Street office tower in Midtown Manhattan, has filed a lawsuit to eject Thakkar Developers from the property. Safehold alleges that the Dallas-based developer, led by Poorvesh Thakkar, failed to meet its obligations under a long-term ground lease, resulting in nearly $28 million in unpaid property taxes, interest, and penalties. Thakkar Developers had acquired the ground lease for the nearly 1 million-square-foot building for $8.5 million and announced plans for an office-to-residential conversion. This lawsuit adds to a series of legal challenges for Thakkar, including recent fraud charges from the SEC and multiple lawsuits related to his Dallas-area projects.
