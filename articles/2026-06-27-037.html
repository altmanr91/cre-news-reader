Lane wants more homes, less office at SRI campus makeover in Menlo Park

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Fri, 26 Jun 2026 23:30:27 +0000

SUMMARY

Lane Partners has filed revised plans for the redevelopment of the 64-acre SRI International campus in Menlo Park, shifting the focus from office space to a greater emphasis on residential development. The updated proposal for the project, known as Parkline, will feature 1,082 homes, including apartments, single-family homes, and townhomes, a significant increase from the previous plan's 800 apartments. This adjustment reflects current market trends, with reduced demand for office space and strong pricing for residential properties. The project will also include office space and a retail village, with a master plan by Skidmore Owings & Merrill and residential designs by TCA Architects and KTGY.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: mixed-use

Property Name: Parkline

Address: 333 Ravenswood Avenue, Menlo Park, CA

Units: 1,082

Site Area: 64 acres

Notable Features: The project includes five open spaces, including a dog park, and plans to replace 1,100 trees while preserving 228 mature trees.

Project Notes: The revised plans call for 44 acres of housing and 14.6 acres of offices and retail, compared to the old proposal of 37.1 acres of housing and 16.5 acres of offices. The housing mix includes 754 apartments, 220 single-family homes, and 108 townhomes. The project would include 1,550 residential parking spaces and 2,006 commercial parking spaces. The first phase will build the two largest apartment complexes at Ravenswood Avenue and Laurel Street.
