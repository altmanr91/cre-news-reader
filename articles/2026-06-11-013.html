BELL Construction breaks ground on 230,000-square-foot adult detention center in Tennessee’s Williamson County

CONSTRUCTION

REJournals — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 09:05:00 +0000

SUMMARY

BELL Construction has broken ground on the new Williamson County Adult Detention Center, a 230,000-square-foot facility in Williamson County, Tennessee. This project is designed to enhance justice services by providing a more secure and streamlined environment for both staff and inmates, featuring 596 beds, 18 housing pods, and comprehensive support facilities. The new center is a key part of the county's multi-phase initiative to modernize its justice and detention infrastructure amidst ongoing growth.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: government

Property Name: Williamson County Adult Detention Center

Size: 230,000 SF

Beds: 596

Notable Features: Includes 18 housing pods, 260 prefabricated metal cells, and a 70,000-square-foot parking garage with 156 secure parking stalls. Features central intake, booking, court services, laundry, food service, medical/dental facilities, infirmary, mental health housing, and recreation areas.

Project Notes: This is the second project to break ground as part of Williamson County's multi-phase effort to update county facilities.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

GENERAL CONTRACTOR: BELL Construction

OWNER: Williamson County
