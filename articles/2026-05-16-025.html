Miami Beach board greenlights plans for Terra, partners’ bayfront condo tower

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — Miami — Fri, 15 May 2026 16:36:28 +0000

SUMMARY

Terra and its partners, RG Development Group and GV Development, received unanimous approval from the Miami Beach Design Review Board for a 330-foot-tall luxury condo tower in South Beach. The project, located at 1250 West Avenue, will feature 106 condo units averaging nearly 3,500 square feet, replacing a 1964-built condo building that Terra and former partner Michael Stern acquired last September for $120 million. The development also includes a ground-floor commercial space and tropical gardens, with the developer committed to mitigating construction impacts and completing a baywalk behind the site.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: condo

Address: 1250 West Avenue, Miami Beach, FL

Size: 371,000 SF

Units: 106

Notable Features: Luxury condo tower with units averaging nearly 3,500 square feet, including two penthouses with rooftop pools and private amenity decks. Features underground parking, a 2,685-square-foot commercial space, and tropical gardens with pollutant-filtered bio swales.

Project Notes: The site was previously acquired for $120 million in September, financed with a $98 million loan from Northwind, and included the Bikini Hostel property across the street. The project's original developer, Michael Stern, secured an overlay zoning district allowing for the 330-foot height in exchange for completing a baywalk and redeveloping the Bikini Hostel into a city park. Stern exited the project earlier this year.
