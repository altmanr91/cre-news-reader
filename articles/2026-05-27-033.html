Basis, Lessen put Loop office space on sublease market as vacancy crisis drags on

The Real Deal — Chicago — Tue, 26 May 2026 15:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / Office Vacancy

Basis Technologies and Lessen are subleasing significant office space in Chicago's Loop, reflecting the ongoing challenges in the downtown commercial property market. Basis Technologies is offering its entire 70,000-square-foot space at the Sullivan Center, while Lessen is subleasing either 37,000 or 39,000 square feet across two floors at 203 North LaSalle Street. These moves highlight the trend of companies optimizing their office footprints in a remote-first environment and the broader market's flight to quality, with high vacancy rates persisting in both downtown and suburban Chicago.

DATA POINTS

Suburban office vacancy (April): 33.4 percent

Downtown office vacancy: 28.6 percent

Downtown office space vacant for over three years: Half

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Loop office market is experiencing a flight to quality, with increasing demand for full-amenitized, top-tier office spaces. The pandemic's ripple effect continues to impact commercial properties, leading to high vacancy rates.
