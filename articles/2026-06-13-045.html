'Infrastructure Of The Future': $75B SpaceX IPO To Fuel Data Center Expansion On Land And In Orbit

Bisnow News Feed — Fri, 12 Jun 2026 12:53:48 -0400

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Business News

SpaceX's recent $75 billion initial public offering, which valued the company at $1.7 trillion, is expected to significantly fuel its expansion in the data center sector, both on Earth and in orbit. The company, which has operations across 12 major properties in six states, plans to leverage this capital to further develop its terrestrial data center infrastructure, particularly in Texas, Tennessee, and Mississippi, where it is already building some of the country's largest facilities. Additionally, a substantial portion of the IPO proceeds will support Elon Musk's ambitious plans to launch 1 million solar-powered satellites into low Earth orbit to serve as AI computing data centers, a move he believes will be more energy-efficient than ground-based alternatives.
