Demand for Logistics, Manufacturing Space Drives Wichita’s Pipeline of Projects

REBusinessOnline — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 12:39:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Wichita's industrial real estate market concluded 2025 with stable fundamentals, driven by strong demand for large-format logistics and manufacturing space. Despite a slight increase in overall vacancy to 9%, this was primarily due to smaller bay turnover and properties being marketed but not yet move-in ready. The vacancy rate for larger industrial buildings (over 100,000 square feet) remained tight at 2.8%, indicating a persistent shortage of modern bulk space. Asking rents averaged $6.07 per square foot triple net annually, holding steady due to robust interest in quality warehouse and manufacturing facilities. The market's strategic position as a logistics and manufacturing hub continues to attract both regional and national firms, with a healthy development pipeline addressing the demand for modern facilities.
