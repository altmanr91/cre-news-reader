California’s transfer tax fight just got upended. And Bay Area sellers want to be paid in AI stock

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Sun, 28 Jun 2026 15:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

California recently experienced significant legislative activity regarding real estate transfer taxes, with a citizen-led initiative to cap taxes at 0.11% qualifying for the November ballot. Assemblymember Buffy Wicks introduced AB 736, proposing caps of 1.5% for most jurisdictions and 3% for cities like San Francisco and Culver City, aiming to address developer concerns about high taxes hindering commercial-to-housing conversions. However, the citizen initiative was withdrawn after a deal with state lawmakers, leaving the future of Wicks' bill uncertain. Meanwhile, the Bay Area housing market is experiencing a frenzy driven by the AI industry boom, with some luxury home sellers attempting to trade properties for pre-IPO stock in AI companies like Anthropic and OpenAI, reflecting a broader scramble to capitalize on the AI gold rush.
