Reactions to Zillow-MRED lawsuit expose fault lines in fight over listing data

The Real Deal — Chicago — Sun, 17 May 2026 14:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Zillow has filed a lawsuit against Midwest Real Estate Data (MRED), the Chicago-area Multiple Listing Service, and Compass, alleging that MRED colluded with Compass to force Zillow to display Compass's private listings nationwide or lose access to its Chicago data feed. This legal action highlights a broader industry debate over the control and transparency of listing data, questioning whether homesellers should be able to market properties privately or if a fully public market best serves consumers. Zillow argues that MRED, whose board includes Compass-affiliated brokers, is acting as a competitor rather than a neutral marketplace, potentially harming broader market access and consumer interests. The outcome of this lawsuit could significantly impact how MLSs nationwide interact with listing portals and brokerages, potentially leading to more fragmented listing data if Zillow loses, or setting a precedent for broader data distribution if Zillow prevails.
