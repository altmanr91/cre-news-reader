Blood vs sweat: Succession at Majestic Realty holds up Fort Worth’s billion-dollar Stockyards expansion

The Real Deal — Texas — Mon, 04 May 2026 11:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Business News

A legal dispute between Majestic Realty and former executive vice president Craig Cavileer has stalled the second phase of the $1 billion Fort Worth Stockyards expansion. Cavileer, who spearheaded the initial revitalization of the historic district, was fired by Majestic amid a leadership transition within the family-owned firm. He alleges that Majestic foreclosed on his ownership stake in projects, including the Stockyards, demanding immediate repayment of $76 million in loans, thereby denying him promised sweat equity. Majestic Realty has not commented on the litigation, but the company is reportedly shifting to a more gradual development approach for the district, while Cavileer has launched his own firm, Delightful Development, to pursue individual projects within the area.
