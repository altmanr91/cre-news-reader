San Francisco sent its two polarizing housing views to the November ballot

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Mon, 08 Jun 2026 15:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Political Commentary

San Francisco voters have advanced two candidates to the November general election who represent the city's conflicting views on housing policy. State Sen. Scott Wiener, a proponent of pro-growth housing policies, secured a significant lead, advocating for increased housing production and reduced local permitting hurdles. His opponent, Supervisor Myrna Chan, represents a more traditional progressive stance, emphasizing subsidized affordable housing and expressing concerns about gentrification from market-rate development. The primary results highlight the ongoing debate within San Francisco regarding housing supply, affordability, and the role of state versus local control in development.

DATA POINTS
