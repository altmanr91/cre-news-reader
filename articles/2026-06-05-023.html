PolicyPro: Mamdani targets broker fees to curb CityFHEPS costs

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 04 Jun 2026 23:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The Mamdani administration in New York City is targeting broker fees paid through the CityFHEPS housing voucher program to curb soaring costs. The city's Human Resources Administration plans to cap broker fees at one month's rent, a significant reduction from the current allowance of up to 15 percent of annual rent. This move is part of a broader effort to contain the program's expenses, which are projected to reach $1.7 billion this fiscal year. While the city aims to reduce spending, industry groups argue that maintaining market-rate fees is crucial to ensure broker participation and help voucher holders navigate the housing search process.
