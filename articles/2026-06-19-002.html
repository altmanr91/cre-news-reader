L.A. Closer to Scaling Back ‘Mansion Tax’

Commercial Observer — Thu, 18 Jun 2026 21:05:31 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Los Angeles lawmakers are considering a ballot measure to amend Measure ULA, the city's controversial "mansion tax," by exempting newly constructed multifamily properties sold within 10 years of completion. The tax, implemented in April 2023, imposes a 4% to 5.5% levy on property sales above certain thresholds, and while intended for affordable housing and homelessness prevention, it has been criticized for significantly deterring multifamily development. Opponents cite data indicating a substantial drop in high-value real estate deals and new multifamily production, arguing it exacerbates the affordability crisis. Proponents, however, attribute the construction slowdown to broader market conditions and warn that exemptions could reduce funding for housing initiatives.
