Walmart Pays $223M for SoCal Cold-Storage Facility

ACQUISITION

Commercial Observer — Thu, 28 May 2026 19:30:24 +0000

SUMMARY

Walmart Realty, a subsidiary of Walmart, has acquired a 507,000-square-foot cold-storage facility in Riverside, CA, for $223 million from State Street Corporation. Walmart had been leasing the property since 2019, and the acquisition solidifies its grocery and food distribution operations in the Southern California market. The transaction highlights the strong demand for existing large-format cold-storage facilities in the Inland Empire, a critical hub for the region's food supply network.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: industrial

Address: 1001 Columbia Avenue, Riverside, CA

Size: 507,000 SF

Sale Price: $223.0M

$/SF: $440/SF (calculated)

Notable Features: Temperature-controlled distribution facility with 42-foot clear height ceilings, 120 trailer stalls, 98 dock-high doors, and 22,500 square feet of office space. Located less than four miles from Interstates 215, State Route 60, and State Route 91.

Project Notes: Walmart had been leasing the property since 2019.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: Walmart Realty

SELLER: State Street Corporation

BUYER BROKER: JLL — Scott Coyle; Peter McWilliams; Tim O’Rourke

SELLER BROKER: Eastdil Secured

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Inland Empire is a premier component in Southern California’s grocery supply network. Demand for temperature-controlled logistics space continues to outpace supply. Limited speculative construction coupled with robust tenant demand is expected to positively impact net absorption, vacancy, and availability in the Inland Empire industrial market over the coming quarters.
