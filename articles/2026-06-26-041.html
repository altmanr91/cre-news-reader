Report: Has Brexit Been a Boon to the UK?

Connect CRE — Thu, 25 Jun 2026 21:37:25 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Economist Ray Perryman argues that Brexit has severely harmed the UK economy, citing data indicating a significant reduction in GDP by 6% to 8% and a fall in investment by 12% to 18%. He attributes these declines to the uncertainty and disruptions caused by leaving the EU, despite acknowledging other contributing factors like the pandemic and energy policy. Perryman concludes that restricting trade and the flow of capital and labor through Brexit was an unfortunate choice with long-term implications for growth, especially given existing global economic challenges.

DATA POINTS

Brexit vote in favor of departing: 51.89%

Brexit vote to remain: 48.11%

UK citizens surveyed in June 2026 who believe the country shouldn't have left the EU: 57%

Reduction in UK's Gross Domestic Product due to Brexit: 6% to 8%

Fall in investment due to Brexit: 12% to 18%

Fall in employment and productivity due to Brexit: 3% to 4%
