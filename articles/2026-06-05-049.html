Data Center Lobbying Muscle Shifts To Statehouses As Local Battles Boil

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 04 Jun 2026 15:17:44 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The data center industry is shifting its lobbying efforts from federal to state and local levels, as community opposition and regulatory scrutiny intensify in key markets. This pivot is driven by the fact that local officials control critical aspects like zoning, tax incentives, and interconnection approvals, which are essential for data center development. States like Texas and California are becoming central battlegrounds, with new regulations and moratoriums emerging, and significant political spending by tech-aligned PACs in state and local elections. The industry faces increasing public pushback, leading to project cancellations and ballot measures banning data centers, while developers in Texas are racing to secure power grid approvals amidst surging demand.
