Lafayette to consider upzoning to meet state housing requirements after lawsuit

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 20:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

The City of Lafayette has settled a lawsuit with the Housing Action Coalition, agreeing to consider upzoning 130 acres along Mt. Diablo Boulevard and add more land for future housing development to meet state housing requirements. The settlement, which also includes a payment of $120,080 in attorney fees, aims to avoid prolonged litigation and increase the city's development potential by hundreds of units. Lafayette is mandated to entitle 2,114 new housing units by 2031.

DATA POINTS

Attorney Fees Paid by City: $120,080

State-Mandated Housing Units by 2031: 2,114

Density Increase North of Mt. Diablo Boulevard: 60 to 65 units per acre

Density Increase Adjacent to and North of Mt. Diablo Boulevard: 35 to 45 units per acre

Density Increase South Side of Mt. Diablo Boulevard: 35 to 45 units per acre

Condominiums Delivered Spring 2026: 85

Mount Diablo Condos (Approved): 31 units
