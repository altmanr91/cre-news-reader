Dems running for California governor spar over housing

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Tue, 12 May 2026 15:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Democratic candidates for California governor debated housing policy, focusing on strategies to increase housing production and address affordability. Key discussion points included the role of state government in enforcing housing mandates, the impact of labor costs and prevailing wage requirements, and the potential for modular construction to reduce development expenses. Candidates also discussed reforming Proposition 13 to generate revenue for infrastructure and the challenges cities face in financing housing-related costs.

DATA POINTS

Steyer's housing development promise: 1 million new homes over four years

Newsom's 2018 housing development pitch: 3.5 million new homes over eight years

Actual housing units produced in California (2018-2026): fewer than 900,000 units

Estimated cost reduction from modular construction: at least 20 percent

Estimated annual cost of commercial property tax loophole (Prop 13): $20 billion

Average housing project construction time in California: 49 months

Average housing project construction time in Colorado: 37 months

Average housing project construction time in Texas: 27 months
