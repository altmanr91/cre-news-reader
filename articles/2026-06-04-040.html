Former Greyhound station in downtown San Jose slated to become concert venue

DEVELOPMENT

The Real Deal — San Francisco — Wed, 03 Jun 2026 19:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

A former Greyhound station and an adjacent vacant property in downtown San Jose are slated for redevelopment into an entertainment district named San Pedro South. The project, led by local entrepreneur Mauricio Mejia, will feature an outdoor concert venue called The Greyhound at the former bus depot site and another venue, The Domes, across the street. The initiative aims to activate these properties and create a walkable neighborhood while nearby multifamily developments await financing.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: retail

Property Name: The Greyhound / The Domes (San Pedro South)

Address: 60-70 South Almaden Avenue, San Jose, CA

Total Project Cost: $3.5M

Notable Features: The Domes venue will be designed with inspiration from the Sphere in Las Vegas.

Project Notes: The Greyhound site was seized by lenders out of foreclosure last fall after the former owner filed for bankruptcy. The project aims to activate the properties while adjacent multifamily developments secure financing.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Mill Creek Residential Trust
