Texas officials’ legal campaign against Muslim-centric project decried as “Islamophic witch hunts”

The Real Deal — Texas — Sun, 03 May 2026 14:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Non-CRE / Political & Crime News

Texas state officials, including Governor Greg Abbott and Attorney General Ken Paxton, are engaged in a protracted legal battle to block The Meadow, a Muslim-centric community development in North Texas. The developer, Community Capital Partners, recently secured a win in Travis County, compelling the Texas Workforce Commission to review updated housing policies for the project. However, Paxton plans to appeal this ruling and continues to pursue two additional lawsuits against the developer, alleging securities law violations and evasion of state oversight by the project's municipal utility district. Critics have condemned the state's actions as "Islamophobic witch hunts" and "politically driven regulatory harassment."
