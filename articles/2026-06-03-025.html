DeSantis’ property tax cuts head to statewide vote in November

The Real Deal — Miami — Tue, 02 Jun 2026 20:27:02 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

Florida voters will decide in November whether to approve a constitutional amendment proposed by Governor Ron DeSantis that would significantly increase the homestead exemption on primary residences. The measure, which passed both legislative chambers, would raise the exemption from $50,000 to $150,000 in 2027 and $250,000 in 2028, with future increases tied to the consumer price index. Local governments and public services have expressed strong concerns, warning of potential major funding cuts if the amendment passes, while critics also suggest it could lead to a cost shift to other taxes or reduce housing affordability.

DATA POINTS

Current homestead exemption: $50,000

Proposed homestead exemption (2027): $150,000

Proposed homestead exemption (2028): $250,000

Required voter approval: 60 percent

Potential funding gap for public services: $55 billion

Annual assessment cap on non-homesteaded property (current): 5 percent

Annual assessment cap on non-homesteaded property (proposed): 10 percent
