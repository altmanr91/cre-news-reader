Real estate has a Ponzi problem

The Real Deal — National — Sun, 21 Jun 2026 13:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

This article highlights a recurring theme in recent real estate fraud cases: the alleged sale of trust rather than tangible assets. It details several high-profile cases, including Ken Mattson's alleged $100 million Ponzi scheme and Josh Schuster's $10 million scheme, where investors were reportedly swayed by personal relationships and perceived credibility. The author argues that the private nature, complex ownership structures, and potential for outsized returns in real estate can make it particularly susceptible to such schemes, making it difficult for even experienced investors to distinguish legitimate opportunities from fraud. The article concludes by emphasizing that credibility can be as persuasive as collateral in real estate dealings.
