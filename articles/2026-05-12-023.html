LISTEN: City, state politics dominate real estate today

The Real Deal — New York — Mon, 11 May 2026 20:42:06 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article summarizes discussions from The Real Deal's NYC Real Estate Forum, highlighting the significant influence of city and state politics on New York's real estate industry. Key topics included a proposed $2 billion tax subsidy for Related Companies' Hudson Yards expansion, which faced protests, and the controversial pied-à-terre tax, which drew strong opposition from residential brokerage leaders. The forum also addressed the 485x tax abatement program, with city officials acknowledging developer concerns but indicating no immediate plans for changes. The article notes that political issues, such as these tax policies, continue to be central to real estate discussions.
