Toyota’s $2B San Antonio expansion hangs on incentive vote

The Real Deal — Texas — Thu, 18 Jun 2026 14:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

San Antonio city leaders are optimistic about securing a proposed $2 billion manufacturing expansion by Toyota, which would double the size of the automaker's South Side campus and create approximately 2,000 jobs. The City Council is preparing to vote on an economic incentives package to support "Project Orca," which would be one of the largest industrial investments in the city in recent years. This expansion would further solidify Toyota's two-decade presence in the region and enhance San Antonio's reputation as a manufacturing hub with strong cross-border supply chain access.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Interest from major employers remains strong in markets with available industrial land and workforce capacity. San Antonio's proximity to northern Mexico, extensive interstate connectivity, and growing advanced manufacturing workforce are key selling points for corporate prospects.
