T2 escalates lenders’ clash over distressed Great Central Brewing property in wake of 1901 Project groundbreaking

FORECLOSURE

The Real Deal — Chicago — Sat, 13 Jun 2026 14:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

A foreclosure battle is escalating over the distressed Great Central Brewing Company properties in Chicago's Near West Side, an area experiencing significant development interest due to the adjacent 1901 Project. T2 Capital has filed a $2.1 million foreclosure lawsuit against an adjacent lot at 1745 West Walnut Street, naming developer David Avram. This action follows Harvest Commercial Capital's earlier $4.7 million foreclosure filing against the main brewery building at 221 North Wood Street. T2 Capital is seeking a joint sale of both the real estate and brewing infrastructure, aiming to maximize recovery in a market where industrial properties are under pressure from redevelopment.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: industrial

Property Name: Great Central Brewing Company

Address: 221 North Wood Street, Chicago, IL

Loan Amount: $2.1M

Project Notes: The T2 Capital debt stems from a $1.85 million compromise deficiency note to settle a previous $12 million foreclosure in 2023. The developer defaulted on the workout agreement by missing the first payment in March 2025. The properties are located within the Kinzie Corridor Planned Manufacturing District, an area protected by zoning laws.
