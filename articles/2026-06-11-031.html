Houston Astros tee up entertainment district with purchase of Salvation Army shelter

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Texas — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 12:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

An entity associated with the Houston Astros acquired a 0.6-acre property in Houston, TX, previously owned by the Salvation Army. This acquisition is part of the Major League Baseball franchise's ongoing efforts to develop a large-scale entertainment district across from its home stadium, Daikin Park. The planned district will feature retail, a hotel, and an open viewing area, aiming to create a vibrant, year-round destination similar to other successful sports-anchored developments.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: land

Address: 1717 Congress Street, Houston, TX

Site Area: 0.6 acres

Notable Features: Located across the street from Daikin Park (formerly Minute Maid Park).

Project Notes: The property was previously home to the Salvation Army's Center for Hope. The Astros announced plans for an entertainment district in 2023, with a goal to start construction by late 2024. The broader entertainment district plans include 60,000 square feet of retail, a 17-story, 300-key hotel, and an open area for game viewing.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: Dugout Properties

SELLER: The Salvation Army

DEVELOPER/SPONSOR: Houston Astros — Jim Crane, Owner
