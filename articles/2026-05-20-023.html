Evanston moves forward with housing plan focused on raising housing density, cutting red tape

The Real Deal — Chicago — Tue, 19 May 2026 15:30:58 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The Evanston City Council has approved its Housing4All plan, a 134-page blueprint aimed at increasing housing affordability and density. The plan seeks to preserve 1,000 existing affordable units, construct 4,000 new ones, and protect residents from displacement. A key component involves pursuing greater housing density across all neighborhoods, with the council rejecting amendments that would have limited redevelopment in lower-density areas. This initiative comes as Evanston grapples with rising housing costs, which have contributed to economic and racial segregation, with an estimated 35% of households considered cost-burdened in 2025.
