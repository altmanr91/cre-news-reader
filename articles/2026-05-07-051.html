Commercial Property Prices Fall In April, Still Up 3.1% YOY

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 06 May 2026 12:23:44 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / Price Index

Commercial property prices experienced a slight dip of 0.1% in April, marking the first decline of 2026, according to Green Street's Commercial Property Price Index. Despite this monthly decrease, overall commercial property values have shown a 3.1% increase over the past 12 months. The market is characterized by disciplined buyers, uninspiring rent growth in many sectors, and a 10-year Treasury yield in the mid-4s, which is dampening investor enthusiasm. The life sciences sector saw a 0.7% decline in April but remains up 5% year-over-year, trailing only data centers and strip retail, both up 6%.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The 10-year Treasury yield, a key determinant for CRE borrowing costs, has been in the mid-4s, influenced by the Iran conflict. The Federal Open Market Committee maintained its benchmark interest rate for the third consecutive meeting in April, with the Federal Reserve adopting a wait-and-see approach due to economic disruptions from immigration raids and changing tariff policies, exacerbated by oil price spikes.
