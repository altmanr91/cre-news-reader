Starwood notches win against Alan Stalcup in personal guarantee suit

Latest New York Real Estate News — Thu, 30 Apr 2026 21:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Legal Ruling / Personal Guarantee

A New York court has granted summary judgment to Starwood in three cases against Alan Stalcup, CEO of multifamily syndicator GVA, over alleged breaches of "bad boy" recourse guarantee agreements. Starwood claims Stalcup is personally liable for loans tied to properties in Atlanta, Orlando, and Nashville due to GVA-connected borrowers failing to address property liens. While the total amount Stalcup may owe is still being determined, Starwood has sought up to $110 million across the suits, with one case resulting in a $7.9 million judgment against Stalcup.

DATA POINTS

Amount sought by Starwood: $110 million

Judgment in one case: $7.9 million
