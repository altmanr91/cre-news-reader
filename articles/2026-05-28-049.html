Developers Race To Grab Spots Near TSMC's Chipmaking Factories

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 27 May 2026 14:41:37 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The Phoenix real estate market is experiencing a significant boom across all asset classes, driven by Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co.'s (TSMC) $165 billion investment in new chipmaking plants. Developers are actively acquiring industrial sites and land parcels, with some even demolishing existing office buildings to secure prime locations. This activity is fueled by the anticipated influx of workers and the broader ecosystem of suppliers and support services attracted to the area, creating substantial demand for industrial, multifamily, and office space. Brokers note sustained demand from direct TSMC suppliers and a wider range of users seeking proximity to the massive manufacturing project.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Phoenix is undergoing one of the nation's most aggressive industrial build-outs. In the past year, the city reported the third-largest decline in industrial vacancy among major markets. For properties over 250K SF, vacancy dipped more than 500 basis points to below 12%. Semiconductor-adjacent land in North Phoenix is commanding unprecedented premiums due to TSMC-related demand.
