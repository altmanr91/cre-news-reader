Operational Expenses Are Becoming 'Full Deal Breakers'

Bisnow News Feed — Tue, 09 Jun 2026 15:01:45 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Rising operational expenses, including insurance, labor, and property taxes, are making real estate investment deals increasingly difficult to underwrite, according to industry experts. Property managers are under pressure to identify value-driving initiatives, such as renovations or amenity additions, while also being transparent with owners about realistic cash flow and disposition expectations. The current market, characterized by elevated interest rates, stagnant apartment occupancy, and oversupply in some markets like Houston, is forcing difficult conversations between developers, investors, and lenders about underperforming legacy deals and future investment viability. Experts anticipate that positive rent growth in markets like Houston may not return until 2027, prompting a focus on increasing tenant renewal rates to mitigate turnover costs.
