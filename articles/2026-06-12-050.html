HUD Halts LA Homeless Agency Funding Pending Investigation

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 18:12:31 -0400

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

The Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) has suspended a significant portion of its funding to the Los Angeles Homeless Services Authority (LAHSA), a joint city and county agency, pending an investigation by HUD's Office of Inspector General. The investigation will determine if LAHSA violated federal laws, with the outcome dictating whether funding is reinstated or the agency is permanently debarred. This federal action follows years of local scrutiny and comes shortly before Los Angeles County plans to divert hundreds of millions of dollars from LAHSA to a new county agency due to concerns about contract oversight and spending. HUD Secretary Scott Turner stated that taxpayers would no longer fund an organization prioritizing self-interest over public service, while LAHSA expressed concern that the funding suspension could displace thousands of formerly homeless individuals.

DATA POINTS

Federal funds as percentage of LAHSA's annual budget (July 2025): 7%

Los Angeles County funding as percentage of LAHSA's budget: 46%

Los Angeles County funding amount to LAHSA: $379M

LAHSA's budget in last fiscal year: $875M
