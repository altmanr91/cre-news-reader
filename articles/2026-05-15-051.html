NYC Unveils Reforms To Slash Housing Development Timelines

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 14 May 2026 14:04:00 -0400

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

New York City's Mamdani administration has unveiled a comprehensive set of recommendations aimed at significantly reducing the timelines for housing development, particularly for affordable housing projects. The "Streamlining Procedures to Expedite Equitable Development" (SPEED) task force, formed on Mayor Zohran Mamdani's first day in office, published a 36-page report detailing reforms that could cut eight months off affordable housing schedules and up to two years for projects requiring zoning changes. The proposed changes focus on reducing bureaucratic delays, increasing staffing at key agencies, and upgrading technology, including a centralized permitting website. Industry groups have praised the initiative, noting that current processes often lead to project cancellations or downsizing, resulting in a loss of thousands of proposed apartments.
