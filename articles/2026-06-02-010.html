Housing Notes: Removing Florida property taxes will reduce housing affordability

The Real Deal — National — Mon, 01 Jun 2026 21:30:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Jonathan Miller, a prominent residential real estate analyst, argues that Florida Governor DeSantis' proposal to eliminate property taxes for primary residences, while seemingly beneficial, would ultimately reduce housing affordability. Miller contends that the plan, which would exempt the first $250,000 (and eventually $500,000) of a homesteaded home's value, primarily benefits older, long-tenured homeowners and shifts the tax burden to other revenue sources, such as sales taxes or new creative taxes. He highlights that the proposal would strip local governments of a significant portion of their controllable revenue, potentially leading to reduced services or increased taxes elsewhere. Furthermore, Miller suggests that the enhanced exemption, by disincentivizing home turnover, could exacerbate the already rapid home price appreciation in Florida, making homeownership less attainable for new buyers and first-timers.
