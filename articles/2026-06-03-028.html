New Illinois bill helps homeowners at risk of losing home over unpaid property tax

The Real Deal — Chicago — Tue, 02 Jun 2026 22:00:13 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Illinois lawmakers have passed a bill designed to protect homeowners from losing their properties over unpaid property taxes, bringing the state into compliance with a 2023 Supreme Court ruling. The new legislation, awaiting the governor's signature, overhauls the previous system in Cook County where tax liens could be sold, often resulting in homeowners losing their surplus equity. The revised process introduces public auctions for delinquent certificates, allows property owners to participate, and establishes a surplus equity fund, offering greater leniency and protection for those at risk of foreclosure.
