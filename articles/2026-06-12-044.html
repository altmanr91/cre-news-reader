Summer Vacations and Hotels. Or Not

Connect CRE — Fri, 12 Jun 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

A recent analysis by Marcus & Millichap suggests that while inflation and energy costs are influencing summer travel plans, the impact on the hotel sector is nuanced. Demand for limited-service hotels has softened, continuing a multi-year trend, while select-service hotels have maintained stable demand and full-service hotels have seen rising demand. Despite a potentially non-blockbuster summer, the overall hotel market is expected to be steady, with modest gains in the upper-echelon full-service segments, supported by a slowdown in new construction. On the investment front, property sales are up significantly from 2024, with stable pricing and cap rates, indicating a cautiously positive outlook for investors.
