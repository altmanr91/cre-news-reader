Firm led by former Blackstone Exec buys two Waukegan industrial properties for $47M

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — Chicago — Tue, 09 Jun 2026 12:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Centaur Capital Partners, led by Blackstone co-founder John Schreiber, acquired two industrial properties in Waukegan, IL, from VentureOne Real Estate for a total of $46 million. The portfolio includes a 133,000-square-foot distribution center and a 271,000-square-foot manufacturing building, both fully leased to packaging companies. This acquisition reflects continued investor interest in the Chicago industrial market as it normalizes post-pandemic, with speculative development evening out and existing inventory absorbing tenants.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: industrial

Size: 404,000 SF

Sale Price: $46.0M

Site Area: 18.7 acres

Project Notes: VentureOne Real Estate previously acquired these two properties in 2023 for $39 million.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: Centaur Capital Partners — John Schreiber

SELLER: VentureOne Real Estate

SPONSOR PIPELINE

5959 West Howard Street, Niles, IL: 314,028-square-foot FedEx facility acquired in a joint venture with Talos Capital for $72 million.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Chicago industrial market is normalizing post-pandemic, with speculative development evening out and existing inventory absorbing tenants. As of Q2, 11.4 million square feet of industrial space was under construction in the Chicago area, with 50.5% being speculative. Regional vacancy improved by 10 basis points quarter-over-quarter to 5.9% in Q2, compared to just over 6% in Q1 2020.
