With Rent Control Looming, Investors Say It's Hard To Bet On Boston

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 28 May 2026 17:47:20 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

Real estate executives in Massachusetts are expressing significant concern over the potential impact of a statewide rent control ballot measure, which is creating regulatory uncertainty and making long-term investment planning difficult for investors in Boston and across the state. The proposed measure, capping rent increases at 5% or the consumer price index (whichever is lower), is seen by many as a major risk that could fundamentally alter the multifamily market. Investors are already reportedly diverting capital elsewhere, with some institutional buyers halting investments in Massachusetts due to the chilling effect of the proposed policy, compounded by negative macroeconomic factors like high construction costs and stagnant population growth. While some argue the impact might be limited to markets with high rent growth, the prevailing sentiment among Boston-based real estate professionals is that rent control would stifle new housing supply, which is already slowing due to high interest rates and construction costs.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Boston has experienced a sharp drop in new multifamily projects constructed, with deliveries decreasing from 2,765 units in Q1 2025 to 1,736 units last quarter. Year-over-year rent growth in Boston's first quarter flattened to 0.3%, marking a nearly five-year low.
