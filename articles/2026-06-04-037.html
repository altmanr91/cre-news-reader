The Line, Hyatt Centric go back to lenders in foreclosure auction

FORECLOSURE

The Real Deal — Texas — Wed, 03 Jun 2026 18:08:43 +0000

SUMMARY

Two prominent Downtown Austin hotels, The Line Austin and the Hyatt Centric, were returned to their respective lenders in a foreclosure auction. JP Morgan reacquired The Line Austin after the previous owner defaulted on a $172 million loan. Separately, an entity affiliated with Peachtree Group took back the Hyatt Centric following a default on an $84.6 million loan. The Line Austin, a 428-key hotel, had opened in 2018 and was recently recognized as a top hotel in Texas, making its foreclosure particularly significant.

DATA POINTS

Address: 111 East Cesar Chavez Street, Austin, TX

Size: 169,586 SF

Keys: 428

Sale Price: $172.0M

Project Notes: The Line Austin was appraised for tax purposes at $168.9 million. The previous ownership defaulted on a $172 million loan issued in late 2023. The Hyatt Centric owner and developer Realberry defaulted on an $84.6 million loan from 2024.
