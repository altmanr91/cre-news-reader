Lamar flips slice of Geneva Commons to CenterSquare for $39M

SALE

The Real Deal — Chicago — Mon, 29 Jun 2026 20:22:28 +0000

SUMMARY

Lamar Companies sold an 87,000-square-foot portion of Geneva Commons, an open-air retail center in Geneva, IL, to CenterSquare Investment Management for nearly $39 million. This transaction allowed Lamar to recoup over 60 percent of its initial investment from two years prior, demonstrating the viability of distressed retail turnarounds in suburban Chicago. The deal highlights institutional investor interest in modernized open-air retail, contrasting with the struggles of enclosed malls in the region.

DATA POINTS

Property Type: retail

Property Name: Geneva Commons

Address: 602 Commons Dr, Geneva, IL*

Size: 87,000 SF

Sale Price: $39.0M

Occupancy at Sale: 95.0%

Notable Features: Open-air lifestyle center with a mix of dining, wellness, and apparel tenants.

Project Notes: Lamar Companies acquired the full 440,370-square-foot property in April 2024 for $63.8 million, financing it with a $60.2 million loan from Banc of California. CenterSquare's acquisition was wrapped into a $78 million revolving credit agreement with CIBC Bank.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The suburban Chicago shopping center market is showing signs of strength, with institutional buyers seeking modernized open-air retail hubs. This contrasts with enclosed suburban malls facing financial distress and requiring expensive redevelopment. The Streets at Woodfield in Schaumburg, a 693,000-square-foot plaza, was sold for $69 million in January, representing a 59 percent loss for Blackstone's affiliate Perform Properties, which had acquired it for $168.5 million in 2015.
