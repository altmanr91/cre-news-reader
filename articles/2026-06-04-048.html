Bond Market Turbulence Saps CRE Momentum

Bisnow News Feed — Wed, 03 Jun 2026 16:12:41 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The commercial real estate sector's recovery ambitions for 2026 are being challenged by bond market turbulence and elevated Treasury yields, despite a strong first quarter. Economists anticipate sustained high debt costs due to ongoing geopolitical conflicts, leading to a revision of earlier optimistic forecasts. While a new real estate cycle has begun, macroeconomic pressures are expected to keep cap rates flat, making it harder for investors to generate returns. Transaction volumes are likely to be smaller, with a shallower pool of bidders, emphasizing the importance of strong property management over relying on interest rate reductions. Office and retail sectors are identified as offering the best opportunities for returns, with office landlords increasingly willing to accept losses and retail benefiting from historically low new construction.
