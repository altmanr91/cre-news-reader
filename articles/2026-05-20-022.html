Iconic Mag Mile venue converting 400K sf into a Marriott

The Real Deal — Chicago — Tue, 19 May 2026 16:21:44 +0000

SUMMARY

Development

Hearn is in advanced discussions with Marriott International to convert approximately 400,000 square feet of office space within the former John Hancock Center into a 350-key Edition-branded luxury boutique hotel. This proposed conversion, spanning floors 23 through 34, represents a strategic pivot for the iconic Magnificent Mile property, which has faced significant office vacancy challenges since the pandemic. The move aligns with broader efforts to revitalize Chicago's tourism industry, as other downtown venues are also being repurposed for hotel use.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Chicago City Council approved a 1.5 percent surcharge on downtown hotel room rates in May, adding to an existing 17.5 percent tax, to boost the Choose Chicago tourism budget. The lower 10 floors of the BMO Harris Bank building are also being converted into hotels.
