Congress Passes ROAD to Housing Act; President Expected to Sign into Law

Connect CRE — Wed, 24 Jun 2026 11:19:52 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

The U.S. Congress has passed the 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act, which is expected to be signed into law by the President. The legislation includes a provision to increase banks' public welfare investment (PWI) cap from 15% to 20%, aiming to boost private capital for affordable housing development. While it restricts institutional ownership of single-family homes, build-to-rent housing is exempt, and existing institutional holdings are not required to be sold. The Act also features reforms to modernize federal housing programs, reduce development barriers, and expand housing supply and affordability nationwide.

DATA POINTS

House Vote: 358-52

Senate Vote: 85-5

Public Welfare Investment (PWI) Cap Increase: From 15% to 20%

Last PWI Cap Increase: 2006
