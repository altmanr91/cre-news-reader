Tenants Gain Even More Power In Bay Area Lab Market

Bisnow News Feed — Sun, 03 May 2026 17:17:26 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The Bay Area's life sciences real estate market experienced a challenging first quarter, with negative net absorption of 453,000 SF and availability rising to 32.3%. Sublease space remains abundant, particularly in South San Francisco, leading to aggressive pricing by sublandlords. Despite a 9% year-over-year decline in asking rents to $5.57 per SF and a marketwide vacancy rate of 29%, positive indicators in the broader biotech industry suggest a potential rebound in leasing activity by late 2026 or early 2027. Landlords are focusing on occupancy to generate cash flow, while tenants are leveraging the market conditions to secure favorable terms, including significant concessions and tenant improvement allowances.

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Net absorption of lab space in the Bay Area turned negative in Q1 2026, with 453,000 SF of occupancy losses. Availability increased to 32.3%. Sublease space in South San Francisco totaled 630,000 SF in Q1, with 2.1M SF of sublease space available throughout the Bay Area. Base asking rents declined by 9% year-over-year to $5.57 per SF in Q1. Marketwide life sciences vacancy ticked up to 29% in Q1, from 28% in Q1 2025. The construction pipeline for labs in the Bay Area remains at zero. Approximately 14.1M SF of lab space is available in the Bay Area, including 2M SF of spec lab space delivered between 2022 and 2025.
