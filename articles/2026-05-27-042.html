2028 Olympics and Beyond: Talking with Exposition Park’s General Manager

Connect CRE — Tue, 26 May 2026 22:23:24 +0000

SUMMARY

Infrastructure Investment / Government Policy

Exposition Park, a 160-acre state-owned campus in South Los Angeles, is preparing to host its third Olympic Games in 2028. General Manager Andrea Ambriz highlighted the park's extensive experience with large-scale events, including previous Olympiads, NASCAR, and major public gatherings, as a key asset in managing the upcoming Games. A significant focus is on transforming Los Angeles into a "transit-first" city for the event, which will require extensive public training and security protocols due to limited on-site parking and multiple activated venues. The park is also leveraging partnerships with local community organizations, leaders, and the city to balance the needs of the surrounding residential neighborhood with the international spotlight of the Games. Early and frequent conversations with stakeholders have been crucial, leading to a proposed $100 million in state funding for infrastructure improvements, including sidewalk and roadway repairs, to enhance accessibility and compliance.
