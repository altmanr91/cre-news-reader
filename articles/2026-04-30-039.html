HUD, USDA Seek To Roll Back Energy-Efficiency Building Requirements

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 30 Apr 2026 12:46:29 -0400

SUMMARY

Government Policy / Housing Regulation

The U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) and Department of Agriculture (USDA) are rolling back Biden-era energy-efficiency building standards, arguing that these regulations add significant costs to home construction and hinder housing production. The agencies claim the standards increased homebuilding costs by $20,000 to $31,000, making homes less affordable and slowing development. This move aligns with the current administration's broader strategy to reduce regulatory burdens on housing development and mortgage lending, aiming to increase housing supply and promote homeownership.

DATA POINTS

Estimated cost increase from energy-efficiency standards: $20,000 to $31,000 per home

U.S. housing shortage: 10 million houses
