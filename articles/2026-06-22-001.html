CMBS Distress Rate Climbed to 11.86% in May

Commercial Observer — Mon, 22 Jun 2026 13:06:19 +0000

SUMMARY

Market Research / CMBS Report

The overall commercial mortgage-backed securities (CMBS) distress rate climbed to 11.86% in May 2026, an increase from 11.08% in April, as both special servicing and delinquency rates rose across the conduit and single-asset, single-borrower universe. This reversal erased April's brief improvement and pushed distress back toward cyclical highs, more than doubling since mid-2022. The persistent gap between the special servicing rate and the delinquency rate indicates that a significant portion of distressed loans are being actively managed by servicers before or instead of becoming payment-delinquent, serving as a leading indicator for investors as upcoming maturities approach.

Delinquency Rate (April 2026): 8.95%

Mixed-Use Distress Rate: 16.12%

Lodging Distress Rate: 12.27%

Multifamily Distress Rate: 10.95%

Self-Storage Distress Rate: 0.15%

Industrial Distress Rate: 1.04%

Manufactured Housing Distress Rate: 1.19%

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The overall CMBS distress rate has more than doubled since mid-2022 when it was near 5%. The gap between the special servicing rate and the delinquency rate was approximately 170 basis points in May 2026. Office remains the most troubled major segment with a 17.11% distress rate, followed by mixed-use at 16.12% and lodging at 12.27%. Self-storage (0.15%), industrial (1.04%), and manufactured housing (1.19%) show strong resilience.
