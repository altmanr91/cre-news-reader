RealPage rent-fixing settlements pass $200M (and counting)

The Real Deal — New York — Wed, 20 May 2026 16:17:17 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Multiple landlords have reached settlement agreements totaling over $218 million in the ongoing class-action lawsuit alleging a rent-fixing scheme involving RealPage's YieldStar software. These settlements, disclosed in U.S. District Court filings, require the landlords to cease using nonpublic rental information from RealPage databases for pricing and prohibit them from using the software to set rental prices. The litigation against RealPage and various landlords began after a 2022 investigative article highlighted how the software's algorithm could potentially inflate rents and suppress competition. While the settling firms deny liability, the Department of Justice also pursued its own suit against RealPage, which was settled in November with commitments for platform changes and independent oversight.
