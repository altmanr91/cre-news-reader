Wall Street Is Funding The AI Build-Out With Bonds The SEC Never Sees

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 11 Jun 2026 13:47:10 -0400

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The article discusses the increasing use of 144A bonds, a type of private placement debt, to finance data center development, particularly for AI infrastructure. This financing method, which bypasses SEC registration and is sold to institutional investors, offers speed, scale, and flexibility, including longer loan terms that allow projects to stabilize before debt maturity. While some investors express concern about the concentration of AI-related risk in private credit, the underlying assets are often pre-leased data centers with strong cash flow, making them attractive to conservative institutional investors like pension funds and insurance companies seeking long-term, predictable returns. This shift in financing reflects a growing perception of data centers as essential infrastructure rather than traditional industrial assets.
