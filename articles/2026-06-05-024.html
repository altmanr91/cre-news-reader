Lawmakers urge FTC scrutiny of real estate marketplaces

The Real Deal — New York — Thu, 04 Jun 2026 22:25:16 +0000

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Two U.S. lawmakers have urged the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) to investigate online real estate marketplaces, alleging that their practices contribute to housing unaffordability. Democratic Reps. Jennifer McClellan and Don Beyer sent a letter to FTC Chairman Andrew Ferguson, expressing concerns that platforms steering prospective homebuyers to buyer agents via referral fees may lead to higher transaction costs or less favorable financial terms. The lawmakers highlighted features like "contact agent" buttons on listings, which they believe can mislead buyers. This call for scrutiny comes amidst increasing public and regulatory examination of the residential real estate sector, driven by industry consolidation, high interest rates, and a shortage of affordable housing inventory.
