Waymo drives into $220M land buy outside Phoenix

ACQUISITION

The Real Deal — National — Wed, 10 Jun 2026 15:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Waymo, the autonomous vehicle subsidiary of Alphabet, has acquired a sprawling 5,458-acre proving grounds site in Surprise, Arizona, for $220 million. The property, previously used by Chrysler and Apple for vehicle testing, will serve as a dedicated facility for Waymo to advance its self-driving technology. This significant investment underscores Waymo's deepening commitment to the Phoenix area, which has become a key hub for autonomous vehicle development and testing.

DATA POINTS

Notable Features: Site includes several circular tracks and grid-like streets for simulating urban and high-speed driving conditions.

Project Notes: The City of Surprise approved the transfer of a development agreement from the prior owner to Waymo. The site was previously used by Chrysler and Apple for vehicle testing.

COMPANIES/PEOPLE

BUYER: Waymo

SELLER: Route 14 Investment Partners LLC

MARKET INTELLIGENCE

The Phoenix area has been a premier autonomous vehicle testing ground since Waymo's early rider program launched in 2018. Toyota also operates an 11,000-acre proving grounds near Wittmann, which it upgraded with a $50 million investment last year and opened to other companies in 2021. BNSF Railway has a proposed $3.2 billion logistics park and intermodal facility nearby.
