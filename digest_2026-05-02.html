For co-ops, pied-à-terre tax leaves more questions than answers

The Real Deal — New York — Fri, 01 May 2026 22:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

New York lawmakers are once again attempting to implement a pied-à-terre tax on non-primary residences valued at $5 million or more in New York City, a proposal that could generate between $340 million and $510 million annually. The primary challenge lies in the city's existing property tax system, which significantly undervalues co-ops and condos, making it difficult to accurately assess the tax at the unit level. Experts and industry professionals are raising concerns about the administrative complexities, including how to define primary residency, how to split a single tax bill for co-ops, and the potential for numerous challenges from property owners. The Department of Finance would need to develop new metrics and processes to overcome these systemic issues, which could involve tailoring thresholds by borough or requiring managing agents to assist with residency verification, a prospect that property managers find impractical.

Illinois Municipal League pitches alternative housing plan, drawing Realtor backlash

The Real Deal — Chicago — Fri, 01 May 2026 23:09:52 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The Illinois Municipal League has introduced its "Real Housing Act" as an alternative to Governor JB Pritzker's "Build plan," aiming to address housing affordability while preserving local zoning authority. The proposal suggests voluntary incentives for local governments to adopt denser housing options and includes controversial measures such as capping residential real estate commissions at 3% and removing the state ban on local rent stabilization. The Illinois Realtors trade association has strongly criticized the proposal, arguing that the commission cap could violate antitrust laws and that other measures might limit housing supply and increase costs. The Municipal League contends its plan prioritizes housing affordability over real estate industry profits.

Market Research / Housing Report
