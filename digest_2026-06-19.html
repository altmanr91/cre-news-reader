20 Attorneys General Sue To Block Executive Order Targeting DEI In Federal Contracts

Bisnow News Feed — Thu, 18 Jun 2026 12:16:56 -0400

SUMMARY

CRE Legal / Regulatory

Attorneys general from 19 states and the District of Columbia have filed a lawsuit against the Trump administration, seeking to block an executive order that targets diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) efforts among federal contractors. The lawsuit argues that the executive order, titled "Addressing DEI Discrimination by Federal Contractors," and its resulting rules are arbitrary, capricious, and unlawfully implemented without proper public comment periods. The states contend that the new rules, which require contractors to avoid "racially discriminatory DEI activities" and make internal records available for compliance, are vague, increase compliance costs, and hinder economic growth, potentially leading to contract termination or exclusion from government leasing opportunities for non-compliance. The legal challenge highlights the significant impact on federal contracts, including construction work and all General Services Administration leases, and seeks an injunction to prevent the order's implementation.
