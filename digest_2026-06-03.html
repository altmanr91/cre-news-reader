The Growing Divide Between Credit Funds and Specialty Finance Lenders

Commercial Observer — Wed, 03 Jun 2026 04:00:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

This article analyzes the growing divergence between private credit funds and specialty finance lenders in the commercial real estate market, particularly in an environment of rising interest rates and reduced traditional bank lending. The author argues that while both operate outside conventional banking, their capital structures dictate their behavior in volatile markets. Private credit funds, often backed by institutional capital, face pressure to deploy funds within fixed timelines, leading to less flexibility. In contrast, balance sheet lenders, like Hall Structured Finance, deploy their own capital, enabling a more adaptable, deal-specific approach and greater freedom to make thoughtful decisions independent of external pressures. The article highlights Hall Structured Finance's unique position as a lender with a developer's perspective, allowing for a holistic evaluation of projects and a focus on appropriate deal sizing to ensure borrower success.

CRE Legal / Regulatory

New York lawmakers have passed two significant real estate bills. One bill aims to curb private listings by requiring agents to post listings on an MLS or public website in a timely manner, unless the seller opts out in writing with a disclosure. This measure applies to both sales and rental listings, increases penalties for violations, and offers liability protection for agents acting in good faith.
