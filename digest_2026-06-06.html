Hotel Performance Surges After Memorial Day Weekend Slump

Bisnow News Feed — Fri, 05 Jun 2026 12:22:00 -0400

SUMMARY

Market Research / Hospitality Report

U.S. hotels experienced their slowest Memorial Day weekend in six years, with occupancy at 71.1% and demand down 1% year-over-year, marking the lowest rates since 2020. However, performance rebounded sharply in the subsequent days, driven by business travel and major entertainment events. This trend reinforces the K-shaped recovery in hospitality, where luxury and upper-upscale segments continue to grow, while economy hotels face declining demand. Post-holiday, revenue per available room (RevPAR) increased by 8.1% and average daily rates (ADR) by 4.5%, with luxury and upper-upscale leading the recovery.

New York’s Pied-à-Terre Tax Is Bad Policy. But It Shouldn’t Stop Development Land Sales.

Commercial Observer — Fri, 05 Jun 2026 17:38:30 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The author argues that New York's new pied-à-terre tax, while disruptive to the luxury condominium and cooperative market in the short term due to uncertainty, should not significantly impact the value of development land being acquired today. This is because current land buyers are underwriting projects that will be completed years from now, by which time the tax's initial phase will have ended, and legal challenges or amendments are likely. The author suggests that existing condominium inventory will face near-term headwinds, but development land values are more influenced by future market conditions than current ones. However, a key caveat is that if the state legislature extends the current high tax rates beyond 2028, the equation for development land values would change dramatically.

Housing Notes: Amazonification of summer rentals

The Real Deal — National — Fri, 05 Jun 2026 19:13:50 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

The East End summer rental market is experiencing a significant shift from early, predictable leasing to a last-minute, "just-in-time" model, driven by a generational change in consumer behavior. Renters are increasingly treating the process like ordering from Amazon, waiting until closer to key holidays like Memorial Day and July 4th to book properties. This pattern, observed by local real estate experts, suggests a behavioral evolution since the pandemic rather than a weakening market, though it introduces greater anxiety for landlords. The trend is also noted in other high-priced, second-home markets across the U.S., where segments less dependent on rising mortgage rates are seeing more rental activity.
