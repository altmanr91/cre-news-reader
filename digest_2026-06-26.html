Is CRE Truly an Inflation Hedge?

Connect CRE — Fri, 26 Jun 2026 05:01:00 +0000

SUMMARY

Opinion / Market Commentary

A recent analysis by Marcus & Millichap challenges the blanket assumption that commercial real estate (CRE) universally acts as an inflation hedge, revealing that the relationship between rent growth and inflation varies significantly by property type. The study, which examined 25 years of data, found that lease length, contract terms, and demand shifts are key factors influencing inflation pass-through. While office properties showed the weakest correlation due to longer lease terms and limited escalators, retail and industrial sectors demonstrated moderate links. Multifamily, self-storage, and hospitality assets exhibited the strongest resistance to inflation, primarily due to shorter lease terms or daily rate adjustments allowing for more frequent rental resets. Despite these differences, the report concludes that CRE generally offers more inflation resistance than equities, particularly in sustained inflationary periods.
